## Question 1 [nelson]

> Must newly baptized positions always extend beyond all previously baptized positions at the same level, or can gaps in the position space ever be filled retroactively?

### Nelson's Answer

Nelson describes forking as sequential, forward-only growth:

> "One digit can become several by a forking or branching process. This consists of creating successive new digits to the right; we call these 'under' the previous digit." (4/20)

> "For instance, if there is an item 2, it can branch into several more items, each of which is a daughter item. Thus 2.1, 2.2, 2.3, 2.4... are successive items being placed under 2." (4/20)

The word "successive" implies sequential production — 2.1, then 2.2, then 2.3. No retroactive filling.

This is reinforced by the permanence guarantee:

> "New items may be continually inserted in tumbler-space while the other addresses remain valid. This means that any address of any document in an ever-growing network may be specified by a permanent tumbler address." (4/19)

The system never revisits assigned space. Growth is append-only, forward-only. Baptism extends beyond all previous positions at the same level; gaps cannot be filled retroactively because the mechanism is "successive" creation, not arbitrary placement.
