## Question 4 [nelson]

> Does baptizing a position at any level carry a permanent commitment about the range of positions available beneath it for future baptism?

### Nelson's Answer

Yes. Baptism is irrevocable and the subtree beneath a baptized position remains permanently available for future forking:

> "Any address, once assigned, remains valid forever." (4/19)

> "New items may be continually inserted in tumbler-space while the other addresses remain valid. This means that any address of any document in an ever-growing network may be specified by a permanent tumbler address." (4/19)

The commitment is both upward and downward. Upward: the address never expires, never becomes invalid, can never be reclaimed. Downward: the owner permanently controls the subtree and can continue to fork new positions beneath it indefinitely.

> "Whoever owns a specific node, account, document or version may in turn designate (respectively) new nodes, accounts, documents and versions, by forking their integers." (4/17)

> "A tumbler consists of a series of integers. Each integer has no upper limit." (4/19)

Since each digit has no upper limit, the range of positions available beneath any baptized parent is unbounded. Baptism at position X permanently commits that X exists and that the infinite sub-space beneath X is available for future forking by X's owner.
