# Sub-Questions — Permanence and Economic Obligations

**Inquiry:** What must the system guarantee about its permanence invariants when economic obligations are not met — are address permanence, content immutability, and publication irrevocability unconditional or conditioned on continued payment?

1. [nelson] Must an address assigned to content remain permanently valid even if the content's owner ceases all payment to the system?
2. [nelson] Can stored content ever be deleted or modified by the system as a consequence of unpaid economic obligations, or is immutability unconditional once content is accepted?
3. [nelson] Once a document is published, can the system revoke its published status due to the author's failure to meet ongoing financial obligations?
4. [nelson] If content becomes inaccessible due to non-payment, must the system distinguish between content that still exists but is withheld and content that has been destroyed?
5. [nelson] What must the system guarantee about links that reference content whose owner has stopped paying — must those links remain valid and resolvable?
6. [nelson] If one author's content is shared into another author's document through transclusion, does non-payment by the original author affect the integrity of the second author's document?
7. [nelson] Must the identity association between a creator and their content survive the creator's failure to meet economic obligations?
8. [nelson] Must the version history of a document remain intact and retrievable even when the document's owner has abandoned all financial commitment to the system?
9. [nelson] Does the system owe any permanence obligation to third parties — readers, linkers, transcluders — that is independent of the original author's payment status?
10. [nelson] If content carries royalty or micropayment relationships with other parties, must those economic structures be preserved even when the content's owner defaults on storage or hosting obligations?
