**T10a.6 (DomainDisjointness).** For any two distinct allocators `X` and `Y`, their domains do not overlap: `dom(X) ∩ dom(Y) = ∅`.

The disjointness follows from prior consequences. Distinct allocators stand in one of two relationships in the allocator tree: either they share a common lineage (one is an ancestor of the other), or they do not. We treat each case.

*Case 1: X and Y are in an ancestor–descendant relationship.* Without loss of generality, X is the ancestor and Y the descendant on a common lineage at strictly greater depth. By T10a.1, every output in `dom(X)` has the uniform sibling length `γ_X`, and every output in `dom(Y)` has the uniform sibling length `γ_Y`. By T10a.3 (local monotonicity along a lineage), `γ_Y > γ_X`. Were `t ∈ dom(X) ∩ dom(Y)`, we would have both `#t = γ_X` and `#t = γ_Y`, forcing `γ_X = γ_Y` and contradicting the strict inequality. Hence `dom(X) ∩ dom(Y) = ∅`.

*Case 2: X and Y are not in an ancestor–descendant relationship.* By T10a.5, every `x ∈ dom(X)` is prefix-incomparable with every `y ∈ dom(Y)`: `x ⋠ y ∧ y ⋠ x`. Were `t ∈ dom(X) ∩ dom(Y)`, instantiate this with `x = y = t` to obtain `t ⋠ t`. But the Prefix definition gives `t ≼ t` immediately — `#t ≤ #t` and `tᵢ = tᵢ` for `1 ≤ i ≤ #t` — contradicting `t ⋠ t`. Hence `dom(X) ∩ dom(Y) = ∅`. ∎

*Consequence (witness uniqueness).* The disjointness elevates `same_allocator(a, b)` from a bare existential to a witness-unique predicate. When `same_allocator(a, b)` holds, the allocator `A` with `a, b ∈ dom(A)` is unique: were two distinct allocators `X` and `Y` both to contain `a`, the element `a` would lie in `dom(X) ∩ dom(Y)`, contradicting disjointness. Disjointness thus settles the *cross-allocator* question — exactly one allocator witnesses the pair — but it does not by itself make the enumeration indices `(i, j)` with `a = tᵢ, b = tⱼ` single-valued: even with `A` fixed, a chain `t₀, t₁, t₂, …` would need to be non-repeating for the index of `a` within it to be unique. That *within-allocator* injectivity is the subject of T10a.7 (EnumerationInjectivity). The two results are complementary: T10a.6 removes cross-allocator ambiguity, T10a.7 removes within-allocator ambiguity, and together they make any predicate phrased over the enumeration indices — `allocated_before(a, b)` in T9 — well-defined on pairs `(a, b)` satisfying `same_allocator(a, b)`.

*Formal Contract:*
- *Precondition:* `X` and `Y` are distinct allocators conforming to T10a.
- *Postcondition:* `dom(X) ∩ dom(Y) = ∅`. Witness-uniqueness corollary: when `same_allocator(a, b)` holds, the witnessing allocator `A` with `a, b ∈ dom(A)` is uniquely determined by the pair `(a, b)`. Single-valuedness of the enumeration indices `i, j` with `a = tᵢ, b = tⱼ` further requires the within-allocator injectivity supplied by T10a.7.
- *Depends:* T10a (AllocatorDiscipline) — supplies the identity criterion on allocators (`X = Y` iff both are the root or both are non-root with matching spawning triples; distinctness is `X ≠ Y`) and the tree relations (parent, child, depth, ancestor/descendant) on which the case-split "ancestor–descendant vs. not" and the phrase "distinct allocators" rest; also supplies the per-allocator domain `dom(·)` whose disjointness is the claim. T10a.1 (UniformSiblingLength) — supplies the per-domain uniform length used in case 1; T10a.3 (LengthSeparation) — gives the strict length inequality `γ_Y > γ_X` between ancestor and descendant sibling streams, contradicting any shared element in case 1; T10a.5 (CrossAllocatorIncomparability) — gives prefix-incomparability of all outputs across non-ancestor–descendant allocators, which together with reflexivity of `≼` excludes any shared element in case 2; Prefix (PrefixRelation) — reflexivity `t ≼ t`, used to derive the contradiction in case 2.
