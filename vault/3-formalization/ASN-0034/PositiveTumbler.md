## Zero tumblers and positivity

**TA-Pos (PositiveTumbler).** A tumbler `t ∈ T` is *positive*, written `Pos(t)`, iff at least one of its components is nonzero: `(E i : 1 ≤ i ≤ #t : tᵢ ≠ 0)`. A tumbler is a *zero tumbler* iff every component is zero: `(A i : 1 ≤ i ≤ #t : tᵢ = 0)`.

The predicate `Pos(t)` is not written `t > 0`, because `>` already denotes T1's lexicographic ordering, and the two notions diverge. The all-zero tumbler `[0, 0]` exhibits the divergence concretely: under T1, `[0] < [0, 0]` by case (ii) — the prefix rule, since `[0]` is a proper prefix of `[0, 0]` — yet `¬Pos([0, 0])`, since every component of `[0, 0]` is zero. The converse direction does hold — `Pos(t)` implies `t` is T1-greater than every zero tumbler — and is established downstream as TA-PosDom (PositiveDominatesZero), which consumes `actionPoint(t)` as its T1 divergence witness and therefore depends on ActionPoint. Splitting TA-PosDom off from TA-Pos keeps TA-Pos's own Depends (T0 only) acyclic with ActionPoint's: the prior mutual citation TA-Pos ↔ ActionPoint — in which TA-Pos cited ActionPoint for the theorem and ActionPoint cited TA-Pos for the `Pos(w)` definition, justified in prose as "two logical layers … flattened into a mutual citation without a content-level cycle" — is now carried by two distinct Depends edges on distinct properties (TA-Pos → ActionPoint via ActionPoint's `Pos(w)` precondition, and ActionPoint → TA-PosDom via TA-PosDom's proof consuming `actionPoint(t)`), each machine-readable as a DAG edge rather than requiring the reader to unpack a prose distinction between definition-level and theorem-level consumption at property granularity.

*Formal Contract:*
- *Definition:* `Pos(t)` (positive) iff `(E i : 1 ≤ i ≤ #t : tᵢ ≠ 0)`. Zero tumbler: `(A i : 1 ≤ i ≤ #t : tᵢ = 0)`.
- *Depends:* T0 (CarrierSetDefinition) — the carrier set `T`, the length `#t`, and the component projection `tᵢ` used in the Definition (`Pos(t)` iff `(E i : 1 ≤ i ≤ #t : tᵢ ≠ 0)`) and in the zero-tumbler companion definition (`(A i : 1 ≤ i ≤ #t : tᵢ = 0)`) all come from T0's characterisation of T as finite sequences over ℕ with length ≥ 1. T0 is the sole dependency of the TA-Pos definition: the predicate `Pos(t)` is a pure T0-level existential over component equality, and the zero-tumbler companion is its universal dual; no ordering, arithmetic, or well-ordering axiom is consumed by the definition itself. The T1 ordering consequence (`Pos(t) ⟹ z < t` for every zero tumbler `z`), and the ActionPoint consumption it rests on, are carried by TA-PosDom (PositiveDominatesZero) as a separate theorem with its own Depends list.
