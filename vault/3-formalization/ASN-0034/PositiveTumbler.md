## Zero tumblers and positivity

Under T3, the tumblers `[0]`, `[0, 0]`, `[0, 0, 0]`, etc., are *distinct* elements of T вҖ” they have different lengths. Under T1, they form a chain: `[0] < [0, 0] < [0, 0, 0] < ...` by the prefix rule. There is no single "zero tumbler"; there are infinitely many all-zero tumblers.

**Definition (PositiveTumbler).** A tumbler `t вҲҲ T` is *positive*, written `Pos(t)`, iff at least one of its components is nonzero: `(E i : 1 вүӨ i вүӨ #t : tбөў вү  0)`. A tumbler is a *zero tumbler* iff every component is zero: `(A i : 1 вүӨ i вүӨ #t : tбөў = 0)`.

The predicate `Pos(t)` is not written `t > 0`, because `>` already denotes T1's lexicographic ordering, and the two notions diverge. The all-zero tumbler `[0, 0]` exhibits the divergence concretely: under T1, `[0] < [0, 0]` by case (ii) вҖ” the prefix rule, since `[0]` is a proper prefix of `[0, 0]` вҖ” yet `В¬Pos([0, 0])`, since every component of `[0, 0]` is zero. The converse direction does hold: `Pos(t)` implies `t` is T1-greater than every zero tumbler, as the following proof establishes.

Every positive tumbler is greater than every zero tumbler under T1.

*Proof.* Let `t вҲҲ T` with `Pos(t)` and let `z вҲҲ T` be a zero tumbler; we show `z < t`. Since `Pos(t)`, the set `{i : 1 вүӨ i вүӨ #t вҲ§ tбөў вү  0}` is a nonempty subset of в„• вҖ” nonempty by `Pos(t)`'s existential, a subset of в„• because its elements are indices in the range `1 вүӨ i вүӨ #t` вҖ” so T0's well-ordering of в„• furnishes a smallest element `k` with `1 вүӨ k вүӨ #t` and `tвӮ– вү  0`; for all `i < k` we have `tбөў = 0`, by minimality of `k`. Since `z` is a zero tumbler, `zбөў = 0` for all `i вүӨ #z`. We distinguish two cases by the relationship between `#z` and `k`.

*Case `#z вүҘ k`.* For `1 вүӨ i < k` we have `zбөў = 0 = tбөў`, establishing the T1 agreement condition. Since `k вүӨ #z` and `k вүӨ #t`, we have `k вүӨ min(#z, #t)`, and `zвӮ– = 0 < tвӮ–` because `tвӮ– вүҘ 1` as a nonzero natural number. By T1 case (i) with witness `k`, `z < t`.

*Case `#z < k`.* For `1 вүӨ i вүӨ #z` we have `i < k` (since `i вүӨ #z < k`), whence `tбөў = 0 = zбөў`, establishing the T1 agreement condition at every position of `z`. From `#z < k вүӨ #t` we obtain `#z + 1 вүӨ #t`, so by T1 case (ii) with witness `#z + 1`, `z < t`. вҲҺ
