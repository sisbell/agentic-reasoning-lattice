## Zero tumblers and positivity

**TA-Pos (PositiveTumbler).** A tumbler `t ∈ T` is *positive*, written `Pos(t)`, iff at least one of its components is nonzero: `(E i : 1 ≤ i ≤ #t : tᵢ ≠ 0)`. A tumbler `t ∈ T` is a *zero tumbler*, written `Zero(t)`, iff every component is zero: `(A i : 1 ≤ i ≤ #t : tᵢ = 0)`. The two predicates are introduced as a paired pair of symbols so that downstream properties — TA-PosDom below, TA6 in the ordinal section, and any future property wishing to quantify over zero tumblers — can bind `Zero(z)` in a precondition or postcondition guard uniformly, rather than spelling the universal `(A i : 1 ≤ i ≤ #z : zᵢ = 0)` out inline at each site. The symbol is the dual of `Pos(t)` at the definitional layer, not a derived abbreviation: `Zero(t) ⟺ ¬Pos(t)` holds unconditionally as the DeMorgan dual `(A i : 1 ≤ i ≤ #t : tᵢ = 0) ⟺ ¬(E i : 1 ≤ i ≤ #t : tᵢ ≠ 0)`, with no side-condition on `#t` — in particular, were `#t` zero, both `Zero(t)` (empty universal, vacuously true) and `¬Pos(t)` (negation of an empty existential, which is false, so its negation is true) would hold, so the predicates would *agree* in that degenerate case rather than diverge; T0's `#t ≥ 1` eliminates the empty-index case from the carrier but is not what makes the two predicates coincide. The reason to state `Zero(t)` with a universal over components as its defining clause rather than as the negation `¬Pos(t)` is therefore not the equivalence itself — unconditional, it does not distinguish the two presentations — but the downstream unpacking profile: consumers that need component-level facts `zᵢ = 0` at specific indices can read them off `Zero(z)` directly, whereas extracting the same facts from `¬Pos(z)` would require an intermediate trichotomy step negating the existential into its universal dual, a routing the universal presentation bypasses.

The predicate `Pos(t)` is not written `t > 0`, because `>` already denotes T1's lexicographic ordering, and the two notions diverge. The all-zero tumbler `[0, 0]` exhibits the divergence concretely: under T1, `[0] < [0, 0]` by case (ii) — the prefix rule, since `[0]` is a proper prefix of `[0, 0]` — yet `Zero([0, 0])` holds, since every component of `[0, 0]` is zero (equivalently, `¬Pos([0, 0])` holds, the two coinciding unconditionally by the DeMorgan duality noted above; we write `Zero([0, 0])` in preference to `¬Pos([0, 0])` to keep the positive-case and zero-case symbols at the same syntactic footing). The converse direction does hold — `Pos(t)` implies `t` is T1-greater than every zero tumbler — but establishing it requires a T1 divergence witness together with a case split on whether the zero tumbler's length reaches the positive tumbler's first nonzero position, so its proof consumes ActionPoint and T1 on top of TA-Pos, none of which are available at the definition layer; the proof is therefore carried out downstream by a separate ordering theorem (TA-PosDom) with its own Depends list, not stated here.

Confining the definition layer to the predicate alone is what breaks the prior mutual-citation pattern between TA-Pos and ActionPoint. In earlier drafts the two cited each other at property granularity — TA-Pos citing ActionPoint for the ordering theorem and ActionPoint citing TA-Pos for the `Pos(w)` definition — whose machine-readable DAG form was a cycle, justified in prose as "two logical layers … flattened into a mutual citation without a content-level cycle". The present structure replaces that with a single directed edge ActionPoint → TA-Pos (ActionPoint's precondition `Pos(w)` binding TA-Pos's predicate) and no return edge: TA-Pos's Depends lists T0 only and reaches no property that transitively reaches ActionPoint, so the edge set of the same-ASN Depends DAG restricted to `{TA-Pos, ActionPoint}` is acyclic as a single edge. The downstream ordering theorem that the previous paragraph defers to sits further along the DAG — it depends on both TA-Pos and ActionPoint — but imposes no return edge on either, so the cycle is genuinely broken at the level of Depends edges rather than hidden inside a cross-citation.

*Formal Contract:*
- *Definition:* `Pos(t)` (positive) iff `(E i : 1 ≤ i ≤ #t : tᵢ ≠ 0)`; `Zero(t)` (zero tumbler) iff `(A i : 1 ≤ i ≤ #t : tᵢ = 0)`. The two predicates are defined as a pair at this site so that downstream properties can bind `Zero(z)` uniformly in their contracts without spelling the universal out inline; they are DeMorgan duals so that `Zero(t) ⟺ ¬Pos(t)` holds unconditionally on sequences of any length (the empty-index case falls outside the carrier by T0's `#t ≥ 1`, but the equivalence itself is not contingent on that restriction — both sides hold vacuously when `#t = 0`), and `Zero(t)` is introduced with its universal as its defining clause rather than as `¬Pos(t)` so that downstream consumers can read `zᵢ = 0` off the definition directly without routing through a negated existential.
- *Depends:* T0 (CarrierSetDefinition) — the carrier set `T`, the length `#t`, and the component projection `tᵢ` used in both companion definitions (`Pos(t)` iff `(E i : 1 ≤ i ≤ #t : tᵢ ≠ 0)` and `Zero(t)` iff `(A i : 1 ≤ i ≤ #t : tᵢ = 0)`) all come from T0's characterisation of T as finite sequences over ℕ with length ≥ 1. T0 is cited solely for this length-and-component machinery that both predicates quantify over; it is *not* cited for making `Zero(t)` and `¬Pos(t)` coincide — the equivalence `(A i : 1 ≤ i ≤ #t : tᵢ = 0) ⟺ ¬(E i : 1 ≤ i ≤ #t : tᵢ ≠ 0)` is an unconditional DeMorgan dual that holds for any sequence, including the empty one (where both sides hold vacuously), so it imposes no side-condition on `#t` and no dependency on T0's `#t ≥ 1` beyond the carrier/length/projection machinery already listed. T0 is the sole dependency of the TA-Pos definition: the predicate `Pos(t)` is a pure T0-level existential over component equality, and `Zero(t)` is its universal dual at the same T0-level; no ordering, arithmetic, or well-ordering axiom is consumed by the definition itself. The T1 ordering consequence (`Pos(t) ∧ Zero(z) ⟹ z < t`), and the ActionPoint consumption it rests on, are carried by a separate downstream ordering theorem (TA-PosDom) with its own Depends list.
