**NAT-addassoc (NatAdditionAssociative).** Addition on ℕ is associative: `(m + n) + p = m + (n + p)` for every `m, n, p ∈ ℕ`.

This is one of the NAT-* axioms in T0's enumeration of the ℕ properties available to downstream proofs. T0 lists `NAT-addassoc (NatAdditionAssociative)` explicitly alongside the eight other NAT-* axioms — NAT-closure, NAT-order, NAT-discrete, NAT-addcompat, NAT-wellorder, NAT-zero, NAT-sub, NAT-cancel — and declares the combined list exhaustive: "the ASN states no NAT-* axiom outside it." This file states the formal content of NAT-addassoc that downstream proofs cite; the axiom is registered inside T0's enumeration, not added alongside it. The ASN's convention (T0) is that each proof cites only the ℕ facts it actually uses; within this ASN, associativity is invoked at a single site — GlobalUniqueness Case 5's length-collision argument, after the sub-cases `k'₁ = k'₂` and `k'₁ < k'₂` are excluded and the remaining trichotomy outcome `k'₁ > k'₂` forces `(k'₁, k'₂) = (2, 1)` (the values live in `{1, 2}` by T10a), whence the assumed equality `#p₁ + k'₁ = #p₂ + k'₂` reads `#p₁ + 2 = #p₂ + 1`. Combined with the numeral-unfolding `2 = 1 + 1` — the definitional identification of the numeral 2 with the successor of 1, successor coinciding with `+1` — associativity instantiated at `m = #p₁, n = 1, p = 1` regroups `#p₁ + (1 + 1)` as `(#p₁ + 1) + 1`, rewriting `#p₁ + 2` as `(#p₁ + 1) + 1` so the equality becomes `(#p₁ + 1) + 1 = #p₂ + 1`, whence NAT-cancel's right cancellation at the trailing `+1` extracts `#p₂ = #p₁ + 1` — the length-extension identity that underwrites the subsequent zero-count argument excluding the `(k'₁, k'₂) = (2, 1)` configuration from Case 5. Without an explicit axiom that citation would appeal to background arithmetic, breaking the citation policy that the other NAT-* axioms enforce.

The criterion governing T0's enumeration is *demonstrated downstream consumption*: an ℕ fact enters the enumeration when at least one proof in this ASN consumes it, and is held out otherwise. Associativity meets the criterion at the GlobalUniqueness Case 5 site enumerated above — three ℕ-valued summands (`#p₁`, `1`, `1`) are combined at a single length-comparison step, and the regrouping from `#p₁ + (1 + 1)` to `(#p₁ + 1) + 1` is precisely the operation NAT-cancel's right cancellation then consumes at the trailing `+1`. Commutativity of addition on ℕ — `m + n = n + m` — does *not* meet the criterion: no proof in this ASN reorders two summands. Its omission is load-bearing at two sites within this cone that would otherwise collapse. NAT-addcompat states left *and* right order-compatibility clauses independently, because the two cannot be derived from each other without commutativity; GlobalUniqueness Case 5's sub-case `k'₁ < k'₂` consumes both clauses at opposite placements of the fixed summand — left placement (instantiated at `m = #p₁, n = k'₂, p = k'₁`) lifts `k'₁ ≤ k'₂` to `#p₁ + k'₁ ≤ #p₁ + k'₂`, right placement (instantiated at `m = k'₂, n = #p₂, p = #p₁`) lifts `#p₁ ≤ #p₂` to `#p₁ + k'₂ ≤ #p₂ + k'₂` — and neither step can be routed through the other without a commutativity axiom to align the two placements. Symmetrically, NAT-cancel states left cancellation, right cancellation, and both summand-absorption forms independently under the same non-commutativity rationale; within this cone the right-cancellation clauses of NAT-cancel are consumed four times (once in T1's part (c) Transitivity sub-case (ii, ii) at the fixed summand `1`, and three times in GlobalUniqueness Case 5's length-collision argument at the fixed summands `k'₁`, `k'₂`, and `1`), each at a right-side placement of the cancelling summand that no commutative rewrite is available to supply. The exhaustiveness claim T0 records therefore registers a closed list of admitted ℕ facts whose membership is decided by this same criterion: associativity in, commutativity out.
