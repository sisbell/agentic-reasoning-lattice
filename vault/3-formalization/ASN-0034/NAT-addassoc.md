**NAT-addassoc (NatAdditionAssociative).** Addition on ℕ is associative: `(m + n) + p = m + (n + p)` for every `m, n, p ∈ ℕ`.

This is one of the NAT-* axioms in T0's enumeration of the ℕ properties available to downstream proofs. T0 lists `NAT-addassoc (NatAdditionAssociative)` explicitly alongside the eight other NAT-* axioms — NAT-closure, NAT-order, NAT-discrete, NAT-addcompat, NAT-wellorder, NAT-zero, NAT-sub, NAT-cancel — and declares the combined list exhaustive: "the ASN states no NAT-* axiom outside it." This file states the formal content of NAT-addassoc that downstream proofs cite; the axiom is registered inside T0's enumeration, not added alongside it. The ASN's convention (T0) is that each proof cites only the ℕ facts it actually uses; associativity is used in TS3 (to reconcile `(vₘ + n₁) + n₂` with `vₘ + (n₁ + n₂)` at the shared action point) and in TA-assoc (to reconcile `(aₖ + bₖ) + cₖ` with `aₖ + (bₖ + cₖ)` in the coincident-action-point case). Without an explicit axiom those citations would appeal to background arithmetic, breaking the citation policy that the other NAT-* axioms enforce.

The criterion governing T0's enumeration is *demonstrated downstream consumption*: an ℕ fact enters the enumeration when at least one proof in this ASN consumes it, and is held out otherwise. Associativity meets the criterion at TS3 and TA-assoc — both combine three ℕ-valued summands at a single action-point component and so require regrouping `(m + n) + p` as `m + (n + p)`. Commutativity of addition on ℕ — `m + n = n + m` — does *not* meet the criterion: no proof in this ASN reorders two summands, and its omission is what licenses TumblerAdd's dominance argument to rule out the symmetric proof route through `1 + wₖ > wₖ` (which would need commutativity to align with NAT-addcompat's strict successor inequality `wₖ < wₖ + 1`) in favour of the asymmetric route through NAT-addcompat's right order-compatibility, which suffices everywhere `wₖ` appears as a summand. The exhaustiveness claim T0 records and TumblerAdd consumes therefore registers a closed list of admitted ℕ facts whose membership is decided by this same criterion: associativity in, commutativity out.

- *Axiom:* `(A m, n, p ∈ ℕ :: (m + n) + p = m + (n + p))` (associativity of addition on ℕ).
