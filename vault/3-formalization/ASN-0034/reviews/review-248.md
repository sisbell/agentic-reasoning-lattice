# Cone Review — ASN-0034/T12 (cycle 5)

*2026-04-18 06:12*

### "Exactly TA0's preconditions" undercounts the carrier-membership conditions
**Foundation**: TA0 (WellDefinedAddition). TA0's Formal Contract Preconditions list four entries: `a ∈ T`, `w ∈ T`, `Pos(w)`, `actionPoint(w) ≤ #a`. TA-strict's contract has the parallel four-precondition list. Per the ASN's own discipline, a precondition chain from caller to callee must be unbroken — every callee precondition must be discharged by the caller, not just the "interesting" two.
**ASN**: The phrasing "exactly TA0's preconditions" (and its TA-strict analogue) is asserted at four sites, each enumerating only Pos and the action-point bound:
- Definition (Span) body: "The two conditions `Pos(ℓ)` and `actionPoint(ℓ) ≤ #s` are precisely TA0's preconditions for `s ⊕ ℓ ∈ T`".
- Definition (Span) Depends, TA0 entry: "supplies the postcondition `s ⊕ ℓ ∈ T` under the preconditions `Pos(ℓ)` and `actionPoint(ℓ) ≤ #s`, which are exactly TA0's preconditions".
- T12 proof part (a): "The preconditions of Definition (Span) supply `Pos(ℓ)` and `k ≤ #s`, where `k` is the action point of `ℓ`. These are precisely the preconditions of TA0".
- T12 proof part (b) (re TA-strict): "since `Pos(ℓ)` and `k ≤ #s`, TA-strict gives `s ⊕ ℓ > s`".
**Issue**: TA0's (and TA-strict's) precondition lists each contain four entries, not two. The carrier-membership preconditions `a ∈ T` and `w ∈ T` (instantiated as `s ∈ T` and `ℓ ∈ T` at every call site) are silently dropped in each "exactly the preconditions" claim. Definition (Span)'s premise does supply `s ∈ T` and `ℓ ∈ T` — and T12's premise re-inherits them — so the chain is in fact unbroken; but the prose claim "exactly two are TA0's preconditions" misrepresents the callee's contract and trains a downstream reader to discharge a 2-precondition handle when TA0 actually has 4. A cross-cutting reviser auditing TA0's preconditions against the discharge sites here will count a mismatch.
**What needs resolving**: At each of the four sites, either (a) extend the enumeration to all four preconditions (e.g., "the four conditions `s ∈ T`, `ℓ ∈ T`, `Pos(ℓ)`, `actionPoint(ℓ) ≤ #s` are exactly TA0's preconditions, instantiated at `(a, w) := (s, ℓ)`"), or (b) replace "exactly TA0's preconditions" with "the two non-membership preconditions of TA0 — the membership preconditions are inherited from Definition (Span)'s `s ∈ T`, `ℓ ∈ T`" and apply that convention uniformly. Whichever framing is chosen, apply it consistently across all four sites and the parallel TA-strict invocation in T12 part (b).

### T12 postcondition (b) annotation "(by TA-strict)" undercredits T1 reflexivity
**Foundation**: T1 (LexicographicOrder), Abbreviations block — defines `a ≤ b` as `a < b ∨ a = b`, supplying reflexivity of `≤` (since `s = s` immediately yields `s ≤ s` via the equality disjunct).
**ASN**: T12 Postconditions: "(a) `s ⊕ ℓ ∈ T` (endpoint exists, by TA0). (b) `s ∈ span(s, ℓ)` (non-empty, by TA-strict). (c) `span(s, ℓ)` is order-convex under T1 ...". The proof of (b), however, decomposes membership into two defining conditions: "First, `s ≤ s` holds by reflexivity of `≤` (from T1: `a ≤ b` iff `a < b ∨ a = b`, and `s = s`). Second, since `Pos(ℓ)` and `k ≤ #s`, TA-strict gives `s ⊕ ℓ > s`". The Depends T1 entry correctly enumerates "At the start of (b) Non-emptiness, the reflexivity of `≤` ... rests on T1's definition of `≤` as `< ∨ =`."
**Issue**: Postcondition (a)'s "(by TA0)" is single-source-correct — TA0 supplies `s ⊕ ℓ ∈ T` directly. Postcondition (b)'s "(by TA-strict)" mimics the same single-source pattern, but the proof shows two distinct citations are load-bearing: TA-strict for the right defining condition `s < s ⊕ ℓ`, and T1's Abbreviations block for the left defining condition `s ≤ s`. The Depends list correctly carries both T1 and TA-strict; the postcondition annotation does not. A reviser adjusting TA-strict — or, worse, T1's `≤` abbreviation — would, on the strength of the postcondition annotation alone, audit only one half of (b)'s discharge.
**What needs resolving**: Either extend (b)'s annotation to credit both citations (e.g., "(non-empty, by T1's `≤`-reflexivity for `s ≤ s` and TA-strict for `s < s ⊕ ℓ`)"), or drop the inline source annotations from postconditions entirely and let the Depends list carry the per-step crediting. Whichever convention is chosen, apply it uniformly across (a), (b), and (c) so the postcondition annotations and the Depends per-site enumeration agree on what is load-bearing.
