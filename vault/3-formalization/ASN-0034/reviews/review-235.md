### T10a.5 omits NAT-addcompat for `#inc(tᵢ, k') > #tᵢ` at the ancestor-descendant nesting step
**Foundation**: TA5's Depends and T10a.3's Depends establish the per-step convention that `#t + k' > #t` for `k' > 0` is a NAT-addcompat inference (strict successor plus left order-compatibility lifting `1 ≤ k'` to `#t + 1 ≤ #t + k'`), not a direct consequence of TA5(d). TA5(d) gives the *equation* `#inc(t, k') = #t + k'`; the strict inequality is NAT-level.
**ASN**: T10a.5 (CrossAllocatorIncomparability), ancestor-descendant case: "when a parent spawns a child from output `tᵢ` via `inc(tᵢ, k')` with `k' > 0`, TA5(b) gives agreement on all positions `1..#tᵢ` and TA5(d) gives `#inc(tᵢ, k') > #tᵢ`, so `tᵢ ≼ inc(tᵢ, k')` — the spawning output is a proper prefix of the child's base address". T10a.5's Depends lists T10a, T10a.1, T10a.3, T10a.4, T4, TA5, TA5-SigValid, T3, Prefix — no NAT-addcompat.
**Issue**: The step "`#inc(tᵢ, k') > #tᵢ`" attributed to TA5(d) is actually TA5(d)'s equation `#inc(tᵢ, k') = #tᵢ + k'` plus the NAT-level inference `k' > 0 ⟹ #tᵢ + k' > #tᵢ` — exactly the strict-successor / order-compatibility of addition inference TA5's Depends and T10a.3's Depends discharge from NAT-addcompat. The strict inequality carries the proper-prefix (as opposed to merely non-strict prefix) claim for ancestor-descendant pairs. This is a distinct citation site from Previous Finding 6 (iterated `+1` strict positivity in the inductive step) — it concerns the single-step length increase at each spawning event, load-bearing for establishing `tᵢ ≺ inc(tᵢ, k')` and for the argument that T10a.3 then propagates through the descendant subtree.
**What needs resolving**: T10a.5's proof attribution and Depends must cite NAT-addcompat at the step sharpening TA5(d)'s equation `#inc(tᵢ, k') = #tᵢ + k'` to the strict inequality `#inc(tᵢ, k') > #tᵢ` at the ancestor-descendant case, matching the per-step convention TA5 and T10a.3 apply for the same NAT-level inference.

### T10a.3 local-monotonicity step `d_B > d_A ⟹ d_B − d_A ≥ 1` omits NAT-discrete
**Foundation**: TA5's Depends establishes that the inference "`k > 0` in ℕ ⟹ `k ≥ 1`" is sourced from NAT-zero (lower bound `0 ≤ k`) plus NAT-discrete (no natural strictly between `0` and `0 + 1`), "matching the per-step citation convention established for `T1`, `TumblerAdd`, `ActionPoint`, and `TA-Pos`." The structurally identical sharpening `d_B > d_A ⟹ d_B − d_A ≥ 1` — or equivalently `d_B > d_A ⟹ d_A + 1 ≤ d_B` — consumes the same ℕ-discreteness fact.
**ASN**: T10a.3 (LengthSeparation), local-monotonicity paragraph: "The difference `k'_{d_A+1} + … + k'_{d_B}` is a sum of `d_B − d_A` terms, each `k'_i ≥ 1`; iterated NAT-addcompat order-compatibility applied term-by-term … lifts these per-term bounds to `k'_{d_A+1} + … + k'_{d_B} ≥ d_B − d_A`, and since `d_B > d_A` this gives `d_B − d_A ≥ 1`, hence `1 ≤ k'_{d_A+1} + … + k'_{d_B}`." Depends lists T10a, T10a.1, TA5, NAT-addcompat, T3 — no NAT-zero and no NAT-discrete.
**Issue**: Two places in this paragraph consume NAT-discrete (together with NAT-zero). First, framing the sum as "`d_B − d_A` terms" with indices `d_A + 1, …, d_B` presupposes the index range is non-empty when `d_B > d_A`, i.e., `d_A + 1 ≤ d_B` — exactly the NAT-discrete sharpening of `d_A < d_B` in ℕ. Second, the explicit claim "`d_B > d_A` … gives `d_B − d_A ≥ 1`" is the same NAT-discrete inference stated directly. NAT-addcompat supplies the order-compatibility step that lifts per-term `1 ≤ k'_i` across the sum, but NAT-addcompat alone does not convert a strict order between naturals (`d_B > d_A`) into a lower bound on their difference (`d_B − d_A ≥ 1`) — that is NAT-discrete's content, and NAT-zero provides the `0 ≤ d_B − d_A` lower bound the NAT-discrete instantiation requires. The convention the ASN (and TA5) applies elsewhere would name T0 + NAT-zero + NAT-discrete jointly at this step.
**What needs resolving**: T10a.3's local-monotonicity proof and Depends must cite NAT-zero and NAT-discrete (alongside the already-cited NAT-addcompat) at the step sharpening `d_B > d_A` in ℕ to `d_B − d_A ≥ 1`, matching the per-step citation convention TA5 explicitly names for the structurally identical "strict order in ℕ ⟹ successor-bounded difference" inference.
