### GlobalUniqueness Case 5 mis-cites T4a for content that is T4's axiom
**Foundation**: Self-review (T4 is an axiom in this ASN; T4a is a stated theorem here).
**ASN**: GlobalUniqueness, Case 5 length-collision argument, after deriving `#p₂ = #p₁ + 1`: "By T10a.4, every domain prefix is a T4-valid address (it is a domain element of a conforming allocator). By T4a (condition (iii)), the last component of a T4-valid address is non-zero: p₂[#p₂] ≠ 0."
**Issue**: T4a's postcondition is a biconditional — "the three positional conditions of T4's field-segment constraint — (i) no two zeros are adjacent, (ii) `t₁ ≠ 0`, (iii) `t_{#t} ≠ 0` — hold if and only if every field segment of `t` is non-empty." T4a does not itself assert that T4-valid tumblers satisfy (iii); it asserts that (i)–(iii) are equivalent to segment non-emptiness. The proposition "the last component of a T4-valid address is non-zero" is T4's own axiom content — clause (iii) of the field-segment constraint — which T4 imposes on every admitted address tumbler. To extract `p₂[#p₂] ≠ 0` from T4a instead of T4, a consumer would have to first establish segment non-emptiness of p₂, then apply T4a's Forward direction to recover (iii) — a detour that T4 makes unnecessary. As written, "By T4a (condition (iii))" references the label T4a uses internally for a position in its biconditional, not a postcondition T4a supplies. The direct source is T4, and citing T4a shifts the dependency edge onto a theorem that is not actually the origin of the fact being used. This also misaligns GlobalUniqueness's Depends list (which cites T4a "for condition (iii)") with the load-bearing source of the step.
**What needs resolving**: Either recharacterise the citation as a T4 axiom appeal (with the Depends edge moved to T4 at this site) or make explicit why T4a is the intended source — e.g., by routing through an independent prior derivation of segment non-emptiness for p₂ so that T4a's Forward direction can legitimately deliver (iii).

### NAT-* axioms' justification prose references proofs that are nowhere in scope
**Foundation**: Self-review (the stated NAT-* axioms — NAT-addcompat, NAT-cancel, NAT-order, NAT-addassoc — justify their formulation by pointing at downstream consumers).
**ASN**: NAT-addcompat body: "downstream proofs supply the fixed summand on either side: TumblerAdd, TA5, T10a-N, and GlobalUniqueness Case 5 add the fixed term on the left… while TA1's sub-case `j = k`, TA1-strict's Case 1, and GlobalUniqueness Case 5 add the fixed increment on the right…". NAT-cancel body: "cancellation is used in TA-LC (both to pass from `aₖ + xₖ = aₖ + yₖ` to `xₖ = yₖ`…), in TA-MTO (to pass from `aₖ + wₖ = bₖ + wₖ` to `aₖ = bₖ`…), and in TumblerAdd's dominance proof…". NAT-addassoc body: "associativity is used in TS3 (to reconcile `(vₘ + n₁) + n₂`…) and in TA-assoc…". T0 enumeration (as reproduced in NAT-addassoc) names NAT-closure, NAT-wellorder, NAT-zero, NAT-sub alongside the stated four.
**Issue**: The bodies of the stated NAT-* axioms justify *why each clause is axiomatised the way it is* by pointing at specific downstream proofs — TumblerAdd, TA1, TA1-strict, TA-LC, TA-MTO, TS3, TA-assoc, and others. None of these proofs is present in the ASN, not as a stated theorem and not as a declared external dependency. The justifications are therefore un-auditable: a reader cannot confirm, for instance, that TA1 actually uses right order compatibility at the site the axiom-bundling argument relies on, or that TumblerAdd's dominance proof invokes summand absorption's symmetric form rather than the commutativity the ASN has deliberately withheld. This is distinct from PF7–PF9: those findings flag *load-bearing* absent theorems (TA5a, T4b, T8, etc.) whose content is consumed by proofs in this ASN. The present issue is that the *axiomatisation design choices* — which clauses to bundle, which to state symmetrically, which ℕ facts to admit as NAT-* members — are defended by appeal to consumption patterns in proofs that the reader cannot inspect, and the exhaustiveness claim ("the ASN states no NAT-* axiom outside [the enumerated nine]") is made in the same vacated namespace.
**What needs resolving**: Either state (or declare and link) the downstream proofs the axiom-design prose cites, so the "used by X to do Y" justifications can be verified, or rewrite the NAT-* axiom bodies so the clause-by-clause motivation is self-contained within this ASN's own consumers (e.g., by citing only the uses in T1, TA5, T10a.3, T10a.5, T10a.8, T10a-N, GlobalUniqueness Case 5, and the other properties actually stated here).
