### TA6 Conjunct 2 attributes `tⱼ > 0 ⟺ tⱼ ≠ 0` to T0 alone, but the equivalence requires NAT-zero
**Foundation**: TA6 (ZeroTumblers), Formal Contract *Depends* — T0 entry and (absent) NAT-zero entry.
**ASN**: TA6 Conjunct 2 proof: "By T0, every tumbler component lies in ℕ, so for components the conditions `tⱼ > 0` and `tⱼ ≠ 0` coincide; the hypothesis is therefore literally `(E j : 1 ≤ j ≤ #t : tⱼ ≠ 0)`, which is the definition of `Pos(t)` recorded in TA-Pos." TA6 *Depends* entry for T0: "Conjunct 2 uses T0's constraint `aᵢ ∈ ℕ` to equate the hypothesis form `tⱼ > 0` with `tⱼ ≠ 0`, matching the TA-Pos definition of `Pos(t)`." TA6's Depends list enumerates T0, T1, T4, TA-Pos — NAT-zero is not cited.
**Issue**: The equivalence `n > 0 ⟺ n ≠ 0` for `n ∈ ℕ` is not a consequence of T0. T0 places components in ℕ but supplies no ordering facts about 0; the lower bound `0 ≤ n` for `n ∈ ℕ` is precisely NAT-zero (NatZeroMinimum), which T0's axiom list enumerates as a *separate* axiom alongside NAT-order, NAT-discrete, etc. Without NAT-zero, nothing in TA6's cited foundation rules out negative elements of ℕ, and the step "`> 0` coincides with `≠ 0`" is unjustified. The gap is particularly striking because TA-Pos — cited by TA6 and immediately adjacent — handles the analogous step correctly: TA-Pos's proof of Case `#z ≥ k` writes "NAT-zero furnishes `0 ≤ tₖ` since `tₖ ∈ ℕ`" and lists NAT-zero in its Depends. TA6 performs the same inference and attributes it to T0, diverging from the sibling property's citation discipline for an identical step.
**What needs resolving**: Either (a) add NAT-zero (NatZeroMinimum) to TA6's Depends with a note that it licenses the `tⱼ > 0 ⟺ tⱼ ≠ 0` equivalence on ℕ-valued components required to bridge the TA6 hypothesis form to the TA-Pos `Pos(t)` definition; or (b) restructure Conjunct 2 to avoid needing the equivalence — for instance, by restating TA6 Conjunct 2's hypothesis directly as `tⱼ ≠ 0` so the alignment with `Pos(t)` is syntactic and no ordering fact about 0 is invoked.
