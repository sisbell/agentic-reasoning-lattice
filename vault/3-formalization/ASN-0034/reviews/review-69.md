### T8's proof and Frame clause enumerate an operation vocabulary that conflates property labels with operations and includes undefined terms

**Foundation**: (internal — foundation ASN)
**ASN**: T8 (AllocationPermanence), proof body: "Read-only operations (T1, T2, T4) and pure arithmetic (TumblerAdd, TumblerSub, and inc via TA5 when used as a pure function) leave the allocated set unchanged." Frame clause: "read-only operations (T1, T2, T4) and pure arithmetic (⊕, ⊖, inc via TA5) preserve the allocated set exactly, while allocation transitions (T10a) extend it."
**Issue**: The proof and Frame clause claim to enumerate "the system's currently defined operations" and verify each preserves or extends `allocated(s)`. This enumeration has two problems. First, T1 is defined in this document as LexicographicOrder — a mathematical theorem establishing that `<` is a strict total order, with postconditions irreflexivity, trichotomy, and transitivity. It is not an operation in any state-transition vocabulary. T4 is referenced elsewhere in the document as HierarchicalParsing — a structural validity constraint on tumblers, not an operation. T10a is defined as AllocatorDiscipline — a behavioral axiom. The Frame clause repurposes all three labels to denote system operations ("read-only operations (T1, T2, T4)", "allocation transitions (T10a)") without acknowledging the dual use. A formalizer looking up "T1" in the Frame finds a theorem about the ordering relation, not an operation specification with preconditions, postconditions, and an effect on `allocated(s)`. Second, T2 appears nowhere else in this document — it has no definition, no formal contract, and no other reference. TumblerAdd (⊕) and TumblerSub (⊖) likewise appear only in T8's proof and Frame; neither is defined or referenced in any other property. The Frame's claim that these five entities "leave the allocated set unchanged" is unverifiable: three are defined as non-operations (properties/constraints), and three have no definition at all. The Frame additionally claims "The monotonicity conclusion holds for any future operation as well, since NoDeallocation constrains the entire operation vocabulary" — asserting scope over a vocabulary that has never been formally specified.
**What needs resolving**: Either (a) the operation vocabulary referenced in the Frame and proof must be formally declared — with each operation given a name, signature, and effect on `allocated(s)` — distinct from the property labels that share the same identifiers, or (b) the confirmatory enumeration should be removed from both the proof body and the Frame clause, leaving T8's argument to rest on NoDeallocation alone (which is its sole formal dependency). If retained, T2 must be defined or cited; TumblerAdd and TumblerSub must be cited; and the distinction between "T1 the property" and "T1 the comparison operation" (and similarly for T4, T10a) must be made explicit.
