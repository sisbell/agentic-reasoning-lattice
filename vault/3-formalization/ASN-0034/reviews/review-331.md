**Foundation**: Self-review (foundation ASN); no external foundations declared ("this is a foundation ASN; review internal consistency only").
**ASN**: TA5's summary table lists "TA5-SIG (LastSignificantPosition) — definition (separate property)" and "TA5a — proved (separate property)". TA5-SIG is cited in TA5's Definition/Depends ("the symbol `sig(t)` in the definition, postcondition (b)'s exclusion quantifier, and postcondition (c)'s increment target all resolve against this definition") and in TA5-SigValid's proof ("by TA5-SIG, `sig(t) = max({i : 1 ≤ i ≤ #t ∧ tᵢ ≠ 0})`"). **Issue**: Neither TA5-SIG nor TA5a is stated in the ASN content. The header declares no external foundations, and the "(separate property)" language in TA5's table parallels TA5-SigValid's row (which *is* stated in the ASN), so TA5-SIG and TA5a appear to be promised as in-ASN siblings. Without them, the preservation-envelope claims at `k' ∈ {0, 1}` unconditional and `k' = 2` gated by `zeros(t) ≤ 2` — load-bearing in T10a.4's inductive step and therefore in every downstream property that depends on T4-validity of allocator outputs (T10a.5, T10a.8, GlobalUniqueness Case 5's T4a routing) — have no stated source. Similarly, `sig(t)`'s definition is used in TA5, TA5-SigValid, T10a.8, and TA5(b)/(c)'s modification clauses, but its formal statement (including the fallback for all-zero tumblers) is nowhere in scope. This is structurally parallel to PF2 on T8/NoDeallocation but concerns different properties with different downstream chains.
**What needs resolving**: Either state TA5-SIG and TA5a inline as part of this ASN (consistent with the "separate property" table labels and the "review internal consistency only" header), or declare them as external foundations and record the dependency edges in the ASN metadata so the chains through T10a.4, T10a.8, TA5-SigValid, and GlobalUniqueness Case 5 terminate at a stated source.
