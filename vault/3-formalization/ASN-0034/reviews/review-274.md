### T10a body's T10a.5 Formal Contract omits NAT-closure while the standalone T10a.5 theorem cites it
**Foundation**: NAT-closure (NatArithmeticClosureAndIdentity) supplies closure of ℕ under successor, placing `n + 1 ∈ ℕ` for `n ∈ ℕ`. The ASN follows a per-step citation convention (as T10a.8 and TA5's Case `k = 0` do) whereby a `+1` step that requires the post-increment value to lie in ℕ cites NAT-closure alongside NAT-zero and NAT-addcompat.
**ASN**: T10a body, Formal Contract entry for T10a.5 (Cross-allocator prefix-incomparability). Its Depends list enumerates "T10a, T10a.1, T10a.3, T10a.4, T4, T0, NAT-zero, NAT-discrete, NAT-addcompat, TA5, TA5-SigValid, T3, Prefix" — NAT-closure is not named. The standalone T10a.5 theorem's Formal Contract Depends, by contrast, explicitly includes: "NAT-closure (NatArithmeticClosureAndIdentity — invoked at the same inductive step's `+1` advance to confirm via closure of ℕ under successor that `(tₙ)_{#s+1} + 1 ∈ ℕ`; without NAT-closure the post-increment value would escape T0's carrier and the subsequent strict-positivity claim would speak about a quantity outside ℕ — matches TA5 and T10a.8's citation of NAT-closure for the structurally identical sibling-construction step".
**Issue**: Consequence 5's Case 2 (body prose) claims that elements of `dom(Cₓ)` produce values `1, 2, 3, …` at position `j = #bₓ` via successive `inc(·, 0)` increments. The standalone T10a.5 formalizes this as an induction whose step relies on `(tₙ)_{#s+1} + 1 ∈ ℕ` — sourced from NAT-closure. The T10a body's Formal Contract summary for T10a.5 attributes NAT-zero for the lower bound and NAT-addcompat for the strict successor, but silently drops NAT-closure, breaking the three-axiom pairing the per-step convention (T0 + NAT-zero + NAT-discrete for non-zero-to-positive; NAT-closure + NAT-addcompat for `+1`-in-ℕ) that the standalone theorem and the parallel T10a.8 Formal Contract consistently follow. The T10a body's T10a.5 Depends list is therefore narrower than the standalone theorem's, presenting two different citation obligations for the same property.
**What needs resolving**: Add NAT-closure to the T10a body's Formal Contract Depends list for T10a.5, with the same per-step attribution the standalone T10a.5 theorem records (the `+1` advance at position `#s + 1` in Case 2's inductive step), so the body's summary and the standalone theorem agree on what T10a.5's proof consumes. Alternatively, justify why the body may abbreviate this particular citation chain while T10a.8's Formal Contract and the standalone T10a.5 may not.
