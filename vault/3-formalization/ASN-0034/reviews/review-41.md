# Cone Review — ASN-0034/GlobalUniqueness (cycle 6)

*2026-04-14 07:30*

Looking at this ASN as a complete system, I've verified the proofs of T1, T3, T9, T10, TA5, and the structural arguments in T10a and GlobalUniqueness against the definitions and contracts in scope.

### GlobalUniqueness Case 5 — length-separation claim depends on an unstated T4a chain to exclude same-length pairs
**Foundation**: (internal consistency — Case 5 proof completeness)
**ASN**: GlobalUniqueness Case 5: *"Allocators at different nesting depths produce outputs of different lengths, so they cannot collide by T3."* Supported by: *"A descendant d nesting levels below the parent produces outputs of length at least γ + d > γ."*
**Issue**: The claim is false for the system as defined. Two allocators at different depths can produce outputs of the same length when their k' sequences sum to the same total: an allocator at depth 1 spawned with k'=2 produces outputs of length γ+2, while an allocator at depth 2 spawned via k'₁=1 then k'₂=1 also produces outputs of length γ+2. Their domain prefixes can nest (the depth-1 allocator's domain prefix, a root sibling, is a prefix of the depth-2 allocator's domain prefix). The proof provides only the lower bound "at least γ + d," which gives overlapping ranges for different depths and does not establish uniqueness. Within Case 5's scope (same zero count), the claim is rescued by a chain the proof does not articulate: output zero count = zeros(prefix) + (1 if k'=2, else 0). For two nesting-prefix allocators to have the same output length, the prefix length gap must be exactly 1 with k'-values (2,1). Same zero count then requires zeros(longer prefix) = zeros(shorter prefix) + 1. But the longer prefix extends the shorter by exactly one component, and that component is the last position of a valid address — positive by T4a (no trailing zero, via T10a.4). So the extension adds no zero: zeros(longer) = zeros(shorter), making the same-zero-count equation 0 = 1, a contradiction. The same-length scenario is thus excluded from Case 5 by T4a and T10a.4, not by the "at least γ + d" bound. Neither T4a nor T10a.4 is cited in Case 5.
**What needs resolving**: Case 5 must either invoke T4a (valid addresses have no trailing zero) and T10a.4 (domain prefixes are valid addresses) to show that same-output-length nesting-prefix pairs always have different zero counts — routing them to Case 4 and leaving Case 5 with only different-length pairs — or restructure the argument so that the length claim is justified within the same-zero-count scope by connecting zero count to the exact length formula γ + d + z (where z = number of k'=2 steps), from which same z and different d immediately gives different lengths.

### GlobalUniqueness exhaustiveness equates nesting domain prefixes with ancestor–descendant, which is false
**Foundation**: (internal consistency — exhaustiveness characterization)
**ASN**: GlobalUniqueness exhaustiveness paragraph: *"if nesting (one allocator is an ancestor of the other along the spawning lineage), the addresses have different zero counts (Case 4) or the same zero count (Case 5)."*
**Issue**: The parenthetical presents "nesting domain prefixes" as equivalent to "one allocator is an ancestor of the other." This equivalence is false. Concrete counterexample: root (base length γ=1) spawns child A from sibling (1) with k'=2 (base (1,0,1), domain prefix (1)) and child Q from sibling (1) with k'=1 (base (1,1), domain prefix (1)). Q's child B, spawned from Q's sibling (1,2) with k'=1, has domain prefix (1,2). Then A's domain prefix (1) ≼ B's domain prefix (1,2), so they nest — but A is B's uncle, not its ancestor. (If T10a is read as one child per sibling, the scenario requires spawning A and Q from the same sibling with different k'; but even under the stricter one-child-per-sibling reading, the equivalence is a non-trivial structural property of the allocator tree that requires proof, not a definitional consequence of nesting.) The proof is not endangered — the uncle-nephew pair has different zero counts (A's outputs have zeros=1, B's have zeros=0) and routes to Case 4 — but the characterization gives a false model of which pairs reach Cases 4 and 5. Case 5's proof frames its argument as "parent vs. descendant" and relies on the chain structure of the ancestor–descendant relationship for the additive length argument; if the characterization were taken at face value and a non-ancestor pair entered Case 5, the chain argument would not apply.
**What needs resolving**: Either prove the equivalence "nesting domain prefixes ⟹ ancestor–descendant" (likely requiring T10a to constrain child-spawning to at most one per sibling, plus a structural induction on the allocator tree), or remove the parenthetical and restructure Case 5's argument so it does not depend on the ancestor–descendant framing — for instance, by showing directly that nesting-prefix same-zero-count pairs always have different output lengths (via the T4a chain identified in the previous finding).
