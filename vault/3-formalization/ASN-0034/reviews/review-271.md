# Cone Review — ASN-0034/T10a.5 (cycle 1)

*2026-04-18 10:35*

### T10a.3 local-monotonicity proof uses partial subtraction without citing NAT-sub
**Foundation**: T0 (CarrierSetDefinition) enumerates NAT-sub (NatPartialSubtraction) as the axiom supplying "partial subtraction (with its conditional closure, inverse characterisations, strict positivity, right telescoping, and strict monotonicity clauses)". The closure clause `m ≥ n ⟹ m − n ∈ ℕ` is what places any subtraction expression inside ℕ, which is in turn what licenses NAT-zero's `0 ≤ ·` to apply to it.
**ASN**: T10a.3 (LengthSeparation), local-monotonicity paragraph and its postcondition. The proof writes: "The hypothesis `d_B > d_A` on naturals sharpens to `d_B − d_A ≥ 1`", then "the difference `k'_{d_A+1} + … + k'_{d_B}` is a well-formed sum of `d_B − d_A` terms", then "lifts these per-term bounds to `k'_{d_A+1} + … + k'_{d_B} ≥ d_B − d_A`, and chaining with `d_B − d_A ≥ 1`". The Depends entry for NAT-zero explicitly says "NAT-zero supplies the premise `0 ≤ d_B − d_A` required to instantiate NAT-discrete at `m = 0`". The postcondition similarly states "`#output(B) − #output(A) = k'_{d_A+1} + … + k'_{d_B} ≥ d_B − d_A ≥ 1`".
**Issue**: The expressions `d_B − d_A` and `#output(B) − #output(A)` presuppose that subtraction on ℕ yields a natural number; that claim is supplied by NAT-sub's conditional-closure clause, not by NAT-addcompat, NAT-zero, or NAT-discrete. In particular, NAT-zero's `0 ≤ n` axiom requires `n ∈ ℕ` as its quantification premise — so invoking "NAT-zero supplies `0 ≤ d_B − d_A`" silently assumes `d_B − d_A ∈ ℕ`, which is exactly NAT-sub's content. The Depends list of T10a.3 enumerates NAT-addcompat, NAT-zero, NAT-discrete, but omits NAT-sub; the per-step citation convention the ASN is following elsewhere (T0 + NAT-zero + NAT-discrete cited jointly for the structurally identical inference) would require NAT-sub here as the fourth citation.
**What needs resolving**: Either cite NAT-sub in T10a.3's Depends at the local-monotonicity step (covering both the hypothesis-sharpening and the postcondition's subtraction-form statement), or rewrite the proof and postcondition in the additive form `d_A + 1 ≤ d_B` and `#output(A) + 1 ≤ #output(B)` so that no subtraction expression appears and NAT-sub is genuinely not consumed.
