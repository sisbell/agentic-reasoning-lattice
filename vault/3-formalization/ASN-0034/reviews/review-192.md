# Cone Review — ASN-0034/TA5a (cycle 7)

### TA5 attributes `0 ∈ ℕ` to "NAT-closure's additive identity", a clause NAT-closure does not carry
**Foundation**: NAT-closure (NatArithmeticClosure) — per T0's enumeration, stated as "closure under successor and addition". No "additive identity" clause appears in NAT-closure's listed content. The canonical source of `0 ∈ ℕ` under the ASN's own inventory is T0's carrier definition (or, derivatively, NAT-zero's naming of 0 as the minimum of ℕ).
**ASN**: TA5's Depends entry for NAT-closure:
> "the child construction's designated components — the `k − 1` field separators `0` at positions `#t + 1, ..., #t + k − 1` and the first child `1` at position `#t + k` ... name members of ℕ, with `0` supplied by NAT-closure's additive identity and `1 = 0 + 1` supplied by NAT-closure's closure of ℕ under successor, so that the ℕ-membership of the components set in the child branch is discharged from NAT-closure rather than left implicit."
**Issue**: T0 introduces NAT-closure as "closure under successor and addition" — a *closure* axiom (`m, n ∈ ℕ ⟹ m + n ∈ ℕ`), not an identity axiom. "Additive identity" is not one of the clauses the ASN assigns to NAT-closure, and `0 ∈ ℕ` cannot be read off the closure statement alone. Under the per-step citation convention the ASN enforces elsewhere (T4a, T4c, TA5-SigValid, TA5a's preface), the membership `0 ∈ ℕ` used by TA5's child construction should route through T0's carrier (ℕ is the declared carrier, containing 0 by definition) or NAT-zero (naming 0 as minimum of ℕ) — not through NAT-closure, whose content does not license the step.
**What needs resolving**: TA5's Depends entry must either (a) reattribute `0 ∈ ℕ` to the foundation axiom whose stated content actually licenses the step (T0's carrier definition, or NAT-zero), removing the "additive identity" attribution from NAT-closure, or (b) coordinate with T0's enumeration to add an "additive identity" clause to NAT-closure's listed content — the latter would, however, require parallel updates to T0's contract so the two sites agree on NAT-closure's exported scope.

### TA5a's iff carries a side condition on `k = 1` that is already guaranteed by the stated precondition
**Foundation**: T4 (HierarchicalParsing) — imposes `zeros(t) ≤ 3` on every T4-valid tumbler as part of the axiom. TA5a's stated precondition is "`t` satisfies T4 (valid address tumbler)".
**ASN**: TA5a's theorem statement and Guarantee both read:
> "The operation `inc(t, k)` on a valid address `t` preserves T4 if and only if `k = 0`, or `k = 1` with `zeros(t) ≤ 3`, or `k = 2` with `zeros(t) ≤ 2`."
> "*Guarantee:* `inc(t, k)` satisfies T4 iff `k = 0`, or `k = 1 ∧ zeros(t) ≤ 3`, or `k = 2 ∧ zeros(t) ≤ 2`."

Case `k = 1` in the proof closes with: "T4 is preserved when the existing zero count is within bound: `zeros(t) ≤ 3`."
**Issue**: Under TA5a's own precondition (T4-validity), `zeros(t) ≤ 3` is already guaranteed by T4's zero-count clause. Stating the `k = 1` branch as "`k = 1` with `zeros(t) ≤ 3`" makes the conjunct `zeros(t) ≤ 3` redundant under the scope in which the iff is being asserted: every `t` that satisfies the precondition already satisfies the conjunct. The `k = 2` branch, by contrast, carries a genuine additional constraint (`zeros(t) ≤ 2`, strictly tighter than T4's `≤ 3`). Presenting the two positive-`k` branches in parallel syntactic form — each with its own `zeros(t)` side condition — obscures the asymmetry the proof establishes (`k = 1` is unconditional on the precondition domain; `k = 2` is not) and contradicts the closing paragraph's characterisation that "`inc(·, 1)` steps are zero-separator-neutral and so may be interleaved without consuming any of that budget". A downstream reader reconstructing `inc(·, 1)`'s admissibility from TA5a's Guarantee alone could conclude that `k = 1` is subject to an independent structural cap, when the proof shows no such cap exists under T4-validity.
**What needs resolving**: TA5a's iff statement and Guarantee must either (a) drop the redundant `zeros(t) ≤ 3` conjunct from the `k = 1` branch — so the iff reads "`k ∈ {0, 1}`, or `k = 2` with `zeros(t) ≤ 2`" — matching the asymmetry the proof establishes and the closing paragraph's narrative; or (b) reformulate the theorem's precondition so that the iff range includes tumblers with `zeros(t) > 3` (i.e., weaken to a hypothesis under which the `k = 1` conjunct is load-bearing), simultaneously reckoning with whether the `k = 0` case — whose proof routes through T4-validity via TA5-SigValid and T4's boundary clause — survives the weakened precondition.
