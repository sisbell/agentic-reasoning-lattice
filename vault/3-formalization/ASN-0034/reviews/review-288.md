### TA4 invokes ZPD's least-disagreement-position and case-split clauses to apply TumblerSub's construction without listing ZPD in Depends
**Foundation**: ZPD (ZPD) — supplies the case-split clause (`zpd(a, w)` is defined iff the padded sequences disagree somewhere) and the minimality clause (when defined, `zpd(a, w)` is the least padded-divergence position). TumblerSub's Definition is keyed on `k = zpd(a, w)`: the three-region piecewise rule (`rᵢ = 0` for `i < k`, `rₖ = aₖ − wₖ`, `rᵢ = aᵢ` for `i > k`) and the no-divergence branch (`a ⊖ w = [0, …, 0]` of length `L`) cannot be invoked without identifying `zpd` for the operand pair. TumblerSub's own Depends enumerates ZPD with two coupled sites for exactly this reason.
**ASN**: TA4 (PartialInverse), proof Step 2. Case 1: "At each position `i < k`, both `rᵢ = 0` … and `wᵢ = 0` …, so `rᵢ = wᵢ` and no divergence occurs before position `k`. … Hence `rₖ ≠ wₖ` and the first divergence is at position `k`. … TumblerSub produces: `sᵢ = 0` for `i < k` (zeroing pre-divergence positions), `sₖ = rₖ - wₖ = (aₖ + wₖ) - wₖ = aₖ` (reversing the advance) … and `sᵢ = rᵢ` for `i > k` (tail copy)." Case 2: "Now `s = r ⊖ w = w ⊖ w`: the sequences agree at every position, so no divergence exists and TumblerSub yields the zero tumbler of length `L = k`". TA4's Depends enumerates TA-Pos, ActionPoint, TumblerAdd, TA0, T1, TumblerSub, T3, NAT-closure, NAT-addcompat, NAT-cancel, NAT-zero, NAT-order, NAT-sub — ZPD is absent.
**Issue**: TumblerSub's Postconditions (membership, `#(a ⊖ w) = L`, conditional Pos and actionPoint = zpd) do not surface the *componentwise* result, so TA4 must consume TumblerSub's Definition — which is the case analysis on `k = zpd(r, w)` — to conclude `s = [0, …, 0, aₖ]` in Case 1 and the zero tumbler in Case 2. The two argument moves "the first divergence is at position `k`" (Case 1) and "no divergence exists" (Case 2) are precisely ZPD's minimality clause and case-split clause respectively. The same per-step citation convention TumblerSub adopts when it cites ZPD with separate non-emptiness and minimality sites — and that TA4 follows for NAT-zero, NAT-order, NAT-closure, NAT-addcompat, NAT-cancel — requires ZPD to be cited here as well; transitive availability through TumblerSub does not satisfy the convention (T0, NAT-zero, etc. are reachable through TumblerSub or TA0 yet still listed at TA4).
**What needs resolving**: TA4 must either add ZPD to its Depends list with per-site entries naming the Case 1 minimality use (justifying that the *named* `k` is the least padded-disagreement position of `(r, w)`) and the Case 2 case-split use (justifying that no `zpd` exists when `r = w`), or restructure Step 2 to derive `s` solely from TumblerSub's exported postconditions plus an independent componentwise argument that does not invoke the term "divergence."
