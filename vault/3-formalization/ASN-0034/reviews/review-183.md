### T6(d)'s Postcondition omits node- and user-field matching while its preamble and closing commentary invoke full-tumbler "document family" / "shared-prefix" semantics
**Foundation**: T6(d) preamble ‚Äî "Whether `a` and `b` both carry a document field and the document field of `a` is a prefix of the document field of `b` ‚Äî the prefix relation on document projections *under shared presence of both projections* (*structural subordination within a document family*, strictly weaker than the exact equality of (c))"; T6(c) vs (d) terminological contrast ‚Äî "(c) establishes the symmetric relation `D(a) = D(b)` under shared presence, whereas (d) establishes the asymmetric relation `D(a) ‚™Ø D(b)` under shared presence; `D(a) = D(b)` implies `D(a) ‚™Ø D(b)` but not conversely"; T6 closing commentary ‚Äî Nelson: "shared prefix means shared containing scope" and "The owner of a given item controls the allocation of the numbers under it", and Gregory's `tumbleraccounteq` which "truncates the candidate to the length of the parent and checks for exact match ‚Äî this is the operational realization of T6".
**ASN**: T6(d) proof body ‚Äî "Extract `D(a)` and `D(b)` via `fields` ‚Ä¶ both must possess document fields ‚Äî `zeros(a) ‚â• 2` and `zeros(b) ‚â• 2` ‚Ä¶ When both presence thresholds hold, the sequence `D(a)` is a prefix of `D(b)` iff two conditions hold: `Œ≥‚Çê ‚â§ Œ≥·µ¶` and `(A k : 1 ‚â§ k ‚â§ Œ≥‚Çê : D‚Çñ·µÉ = D‚Çñ·µá)`"; T6(d) Postcondition ‚Äî "returns YES iff `zeros(a) ‚â• 2 ‚àß zeros(b) ‚â• 2 ‚àß #D(a) ‚â§ #D(b) ‚àß (A k : 1 ‚â§ k ‚â§ #D(a) : D(a)‚Çñ = D(b)‚Çñ)`".
**Issue**: T6(d) checks only the D-field prefix relation ‚Äî it extracts only `D(a)` and `D(b)` and compares only those two sequences. Its Postcondition never mentions `N(a) = N(b)` or `U(a) = U(b)`. But three framing sources around (d) invoke full-tumbler semantics: (1) the preamble calls the relation "structural subordination within a document family", and "document family" in this ASN's own scheme requires same node and same user (per T4's hierarchy discussion); (2) the closing commentary cites Nelson's "shared prefix means shared containing scope" and identifies T6 with `tumbleraccounteq`, which truncates and checks full-prefix equality, not just D-field equality; (3) the case is positioned as "strictly weaker than (c)", which does require `N(a) = N(b) ‚àß U(a) = U(b) ‚àß D(a) = D(b)`. Under T6(d)'s actual Postcondition, two tumblers from *different* user accounts (e.g., `1.0.2.0.5.0.1` with `U=(2), D=(5)` and `1.0.3.0.5.0.1` with `U=(3), D=(5)`) both satisfy `D(a) = (5) = D(b)` and so return YES ‚Äî yet they belong to different "document families" by any reading of the preamble. The cross-cutting gap is that the informal framing and the Nelson/Gregory commentary treat (d) as full-tumbler containment while the formal statement isolates D alone; per-property review of (d) sees a coherent D-prefix procedure, but the ASN as a system makes incompatible claims about what (d) computes.
**What needs resolving**: Either strengthen T6(d)'s Postcondition (and proof body) to carry `N(a) = N(b) ‚àß U(a) = U(b)` so it genuinely captures "structural subordination within a document family" and aligns with the `tumbleraccounteq` identification in the closing commentary, or weaken the preamble and closing commentary so (d) is stated as a narrow claim about D-field prefix alone ‚Äî detached from "document family" / "shared containing scope" / `tumbleraccounteq` framing ‚Äî and make explicit that full-tumbler containment at document granularity would require a separate case combining the conjuncts.
