### T10a.3 body Consequence and Formal Contract under-attribute strict inequality and omit NAT-axiom citations that the standalone T10a.3 theorem discharges
**Foundation**: NAT-addcompat (NatAdditionOrderAndSuccessor) supplies the strict successor inequality `n < n + 1` and left/right order-compatibility that sharpen TA5(d)'s *equation* `#inc(t, k') = #t + k'` to the *strict inequality* `#inc(t, k') > #t`. TA5(d) alone gives only the equation. The per-step citation convention in the ASN (as applied in the standalone T10a.3, the standalone T10a.5, and T10a-N) requires NAT-addcompat to be cited at every strict-inequality step through addition. NAT-sub (NatPartialSubtraction) supplies conditional closure and right telescoping used by the local-monotonicity `#output(B) − #output(A) = k'_{d_A+1} + … + k'_{d_B}` subtraction form (already flagged in Previous Findings for the standalone T10a.3, now similarly needed in the body).
**ASN**: T10a body, Consequence 3 prose: "When the parent performs `inc(·, k')` with `k' ∈ {1, 2}`, TA5(d) gives `#inc(t, k') = #t + k'`, so the child's base address has length strictly exceeding any parent sibling." And T10a body Formal Contract entry for T10a.3: "Depends: T10a (AllocatorDiscipline — child-spawning bounded to `k' ∈ {1, 2}`…), T10a.1 (uniform sibling length), TA5 (TA5(d): #inc(t, k') = #t + k' for k' > 0)."
**Issue**: (i) The body Consequence 3 prose attributes the strict inequality "length strictly exceeding any parent sibling" to TA5(d) alone, reproducing the same attribution error that Previous Findings identified in Consequence 5 and that cycle 3 fixed *only* in Consequence 5 — the NAT-addcompat step that lifts `1 ≤ k'` to `#t + 1 ≤ #t + k'` and the strict-successor step that delivers `#t < #t + 1` are suppressed in Consequence 3's prose. (ii) The T10a body Formal Contract summary for T10a.3 lists only `T10a, T10a.1, TA5` as Depends, whereas the standalone T10a.3 theorem enumerates `T10a, T10a.1, TA5, NAT-addcompat, NAT-sub, NAT-zero, NAT-discrete, T3` with per-step attribution. The body summary understates T10a.3's consumption the same way Previous Finding #5 flagged for T10a.5's NAT-closure omission. The additive strict-increase claim "across d nesting levels the separation is additive" and the "local monotonicity: #output(B) > #output(A)" clause in the body contract both require NAT-addcompat (and NAT-sub for the subtraction-form) that the Depends list does not cite.
**What needs resolving**: Rewrite Consequence 3's body prose so the strict inequality is discharged from NAT-addcompat (with T0 + NAT-zero + NAT-discrete for the `k' > 0 ⟹ 1 ≤ k'` sharpening), paralleling the fix applied to Consequence 5 in cycle 3. Extend the T10a body Formal Contract entry for T10a.3 to include NAT-addcompat, NAT-sub, NAT-zero, NAT-discrete (and T3 for the distinctness step from different lengths) with per-step attribution, matching the standalone T10a.3 theorem's Depends so body and standalone agree on what T10a.3's proof consumes.

### T10a-N body proof and Formal Contract Depends omit T0 + NAT-zero + NAT-discrete sourcing for the `k' > 0 ⟹ 1 ≤ k'` step
**Foundation**: The per-step citation convention established in TA5's Case `k > 0`, T10a.5, T10a.8, TA5-SigValid, T4a, and T4c requires the "non-zero ⇒ strictly positive on ℕ" inference (equivalently `k > 0 ⟹ k ≥ 1` on ℕ) to be sourced jointly from T0 (carrier ℕ), NAT-zero (`(A n ∈ ℕ :: 0 ≤ n)` as lower bound premise for NAT-discrete), and NAT-discrete (instantiated at `m = 0` to rule out `0 ≤ k < 1`). NAT-addcompat alone does not license this sharpening — it supplies the order-compatibility and strict-successor clauses but not the conversion of `k > 0` on ℕ into `k ≥ 1` on ℕ.
**ASN**: T10a body, the "Necessity" paragraph: "From `k' > 0` in ℕ we have `1 ≤ k'`; NAT-addcompat's left order-compatibility…" And the T10a Formal Contract entry for T10a-N, Depends list: "TA5 (…), NAT-addcompat (… lifts `1 ≤ k'` (from `k' > 0` in ℕ)…), Prefix (prefix definition), T10a.2 (…), T10a.5 (…), and T10 (…)." No T0, NAT-zero, or NAT-discrete is cited.
**Issue**: The body proof's step "From `k' > 0` in ℕ we have `1 ≤ k'`" is the same inference sourced in TA5, T10a.5, T10a.8 from T0 + NAT-zero + NAT-discrete. T10a-N glosses the step without citation — and its Depends list omits all three of T0, NAT-zero, NAT-discrete. NAT-addcompat's parenthetical "(from `k' > 0` in ℕ)" acknowledges the premise but routes it through an uncited inference. Under the per-step convention the rest of the ASN follows, T10a-N must cite the same trio at this step. Because T10a-N is the sole necessity argument for the sibling restriction and is referenced by Consequence 8's paragraph-structure remark, leaving its precondition chain unsourced weakens the axiom's necessity derivation relative to the sufficiency derivations (Consequences 1–8) that do source this inference.
**What needs resolving**: Either (i) add an explicit per-step citation in the Necessity paragraph that discharges `k' > 0 ⟹ 1 ≤ k'` from T0 + NAT-zero + NAT-discrete (paralleling TA5's Case `k > 0` proof and T10a.5's body Case 2 inductive step), and extend T10a-N's Formal Contract Depends list with these three foundations with per-step attribution; or (ii) record a convention exempting T10a-N from the per-step sourcing convention and explain why necessity-direction proofs may cite NAT-addcompat's `(from k' > 0 in ℕ)` premise without sourcing that premise's own conversion on ℕ.
