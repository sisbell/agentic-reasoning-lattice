### TA-Pos Ôåö ActionPoint Depends citation forms a declared cycle
**Foundation**: TA-Pos (this ASN) Depends lists ActionPoint; ActionPoint (this ASN) Depends lists TA-Pos.
**ASN**: TA-Pos's Depends: "ActionPoint (ActionPoint) ÔÇö the postcondition proof consumes `actionPoint(t)` as its T1 divergence witness"; ActionPoint's Depends: "TA-Pos (PositiveTumbler) ÔÇö supplies the predicate `Pos(w)` in the precondition and guarantees the existence of an index `i` with `wßÁó Ôëá 0`, making the set `{i : 1 Ôëñ i Ôëñ #w Ôêğ wßÁó Ôëá 0}` nonempty". TA-Pos further annotates: "the two YAML `depends` entries reflect two logical layers ÔÇö the definition consumption ActionPoint ÔåÆ TA-Pos and the theorem consumption TA-Pos ÔåÆ ActionPoint ÔÇö flattened into a mutual citation without a content-level cycle."
**Issue**: The project declares that "ASNs form a dependency DAG". The same structural commitment applies at the property level within an ASN ÔÇö Depends is the machine-readable topological-ordering signal a reviser (or a formalization tool) uses to determine what must be verified before what. The current revision resolves Previous Finding #6's duplication concern by having both properties cite each other, with a prose paragraph arguing the cycle is "logically non-circular" because one citation is definitional and the other is theorem-level. But the distinction "definition consumption vs. theorem consumption" is not encoded in the Depends field itself; any consumer of the Depends metadata (TLA+ extraction, topological sort for verification order, cross-reference audit) sees a cycle at property granularity. The prose justification is unverifiable without the reader already knowing which citation is definitional ÔÇö the very information the Depends format is supposed to make explicit. Critically, this is the only mutual Depends citation in the ASN: TumblerAdd cites TA-Pos and ActionPoint without either citing back; T1 cites T3 without T3 visible to cite back; TA0/TA-strict cite TumblerAdd without TumblerAdd citing them. The TA-Pos Ôåö ActionPoint pair is a structural exception justified only in prose.
**What needs resolving**: Either (a) restructure so Depends is acyclic ÔÇö e.g., split TA-Pos into a definition block (no Depends beyond T0) and a theorem `PositiveDominatesZero` that depends on ActionPoint, so ActionPoint cites only the definition and the theorem cites ActionPoint; or (b) extend the Depends format to distinguish definitional from theorem-level citations (e.g., tagged citations or a separate `uses-definition:` field) so the claimed "two logical layers" are machine-readable; or (c) if the mutual citation is retained as an exception, the ASN must articulate the tooling convention under which declared cycles are acceptable and document why this exception doesn't generalize.
