### `depth-1 child` / `depth-2 child` labels collide with the depth function defined in the same block
**Foundation**: (none — internal consistency within T10a's Definitions)
**ASN**: T10a, *Definitions* → *Derived tree relations*: "The *depth* function `depth : 𝒯 → ℕ` is defined by `depth(root) = 0` and `depth(Y) = depth(parent(Y)) + 1` for non-root `Y`" and, two sentences later, "A *depth-1 child* of `X` is a child `C` of `X` with `spawnParam(C) = 1`, and a *depth-2 child* is one with `spawnParam(C) = 2`."
**Issue**: The word *depth* is given two incompatible meanings within one definition block. Under the first reading, every *child* of X has `depth(Y) = depth(X) + 1`, so "depth-1 child" should pick out the children of the root. Under the second reading, "depth-1 child" means `spawnParam = 1` and is independent of `depth(Y)` — a node with `depth(X) = 5` can still have a "depth-1 child" whose `depth` in the depth-function sense is `6`. The two are not even type-compatible: the depth function ranges over ℕ (tree levels), while the "depth-N" in "depth-N child" ranges over `{1, 2}` (spawn parameters). A downstream property citing "depth-1 child" in PartitionMonotonicity has to guess which reading is intended, and a reader who follows the depth-function definition literally will read "depth-1 child" as "child at tree-depth 1" and be silently wrong.
**What needs resolving**: Either rename one of the two notions so the word *depth* is single-meaning in T10a (e.g., "k=1 child" / "k=2 child" or "param-1 child" / "param-2 child"; or, alternatively, reserve *depth* for spawn-parameter and rename the tree-level function), or add an explicit disambiguating gloss at the "depth-N child" definition that states the clash and pins the intended meaning.
