### T10a.3 omits NAT-addcompat for `+1`/order-compatibility arithmetic
**Foundation**: TA5's Depends establishes the per-step convention that NAT-addcompat is cited explicitly for both the strict successor inequality (`n < n + 1`) and order-compatibility of addition (`p ≤ n ⟹ m + p ≤ m + n`), "rather than [leaving] it as an implicit appeal to ℕ-arithmetic." Previous Findings 4 and 6 already flagged identical omissions in T10a.8 and T10a.5.
**ASN**: T10a.3 (LengthSeparation) proof: "Since `k' ≥ 1`, every child output has length at least `γ + 1 > γ` — strictly longer than any parent sibling"; "Since each `k'_i ≥ 1`, the cumulative sum `k'₁ + … + k'_d` is strictly increasing with `d`"; "The difference `k'_{d_A+1} + … + k'_{d_B}` is at least `d_B − d_A ≥ 1` (since each `k'_i ≥ 1`)." Postcondition: "output length exactly `γ + k'₁ + … + k'_d ≥ γ + d`." Depends lists T10a, T10a.1, TA5, T3 — no NAT-addcompat.
**Issue**: The step `γ + 1 > γ` is precisely the `n < n + 1` strict successor inequality TA5 discharges from NAT-addcompat. The step "sum of d terms each ≥ 1 is ≥ d" is order-compatibility of addition applied d − 1 times — the same lemma TA5 cites to lift `1 ≤ k` to `#t + 1 ≤ #t + k`. T10a.3 performs these inferences repeatedly (single-step, additive-lineage, local-monotonicity, and in the postcondition's `≥ γ + d` clause) but its Depends lists no NAT-arithmetic authority. TA5(c)/(d) deliver length equations `#inc(t, 0) = #t` and `#inc(t, k') = #t + k'`; they do not on their own supply the order inferences `#t + k' > #t` or `γ + k'₁ + … + k'_d ≥ γ + d`.
**What needs resolving**: T10a.3's proof and Depends must cite NAT-addcompat at each step that invokes strict-successor `n < n + 1` or order-compatibility of addition — the single-step `γ + 1 > γ`, the additive-lineage strict-increase claim, the local-monotonicity difference-≥-depth-gap claim, and the postcondition's `γ + k'₁ + … + k'_d ≥ γ + d` clause — matching the per-step convention TA5 applies and Previous Findings 4 and 6 flagged for T10a.8 and T10a.5.
