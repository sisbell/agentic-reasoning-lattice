# Cone Review — ASN-0034/TS5 (cycle 5)

*2026-04-18 14:29*

### TS5's OrdinalShift citation omits per-precondition discharge at the invocation site
**Foundation**: The per-step discharge discipline this ASN enforces — articulated at TS3's Depends (which discharges each TA0 precondition and each OrdinalShift precondition at each of three invocation sites), at TS4's Depends (which discharges OrdinalShift's `n ∈ ℕ` at its invocation site and all four of OrdinalDisplacement's preconditions at that site), and at OrdinalShift's own Depends (which discharges all four of OrdinalDisplacement's preconditions one-for-one). TS5's Depends itself follows this discipline at its TS3 entry ("TS3's six preconditions at the instantiation `u = v, a = n₁, b = d` are discharged to named sources: `v ∈ T` from TS5's own precondition... `n₁ ∈ ℕ`... `d ∈ ℕ`... `n₁ ≥ 1`... `d ≥ 1`... `#v = m`...") and at its TS4 entry ("TS4's four preconditions at the instantiation `u = shift(v, n₁), n = d` are discharged to named sources: `u ∈ T`... `d ∈ ℕ`... `d ≥ 1`... `#u = m`...").
**ASN**: TS5's Depends entry for OrdinalShift: "OrdinalShift (OrdinalShift) — invoked between the TS3 and TS4 calls to license both `u ∈ T` and `#u = m` for `u = shift(v, n₁)` ('By OrdinalShift, u is a tumbler with #u = m (shift preserves length)'); TS4's `u ∈ T` precondition (under the substitution `v = u`) and `#u = m` precondition both depend on this length-and-carrier-preservation fact."
**Issue**: OrdinalShift is invoked at `(v, n₁)`, so its three preconditions `v ∈ T`, `n ∈ ℕ`, `n ≥ 1` must be discharged under the substitution `v = v, n = n₁`. TS5's TS3 and TS4 entries spell out each precondition's named source; the OrdinalShift entry names none. The transfers are trivial (all three come from TS5's own preconditions), but under the per-step discipline "trivial transfer from TS5's own precondition" must still be the *named* source. Without that enumeration, a reviser tightening OrdinalShift's preconditions has no Depends-backed record of which TS5 precondition flows to which OrdinalShift precondition at this invocation.
**What needs resolving**: TS5's Depends entry for OrdinalShift should enumerate OrdinalShift's three preconditions at `(v, n₁)` and name the source for each (paralleling TS5's own treatment of TS3 and TS4), or articulate the convention by which this invocation's preconditions are treated differently.
