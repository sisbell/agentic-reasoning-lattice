# Cone Review — ASN-0034/TumblerAdd (cycle 8)

*2026-04-16 20:17*

### T1's trichotomy proof invokes well-ordering of ℕ without citation
**Foundation**: `T0 (CarrierSetDefinition)` — well-ordering of ℕ (added cycle 7): "every nonempty subset `S ⊆ ℕ` has a least element."
**ASN**: `T1 (LexicographicOrder)`, proof part (b) Trichotomy: "Define the *first divergence position* `k` as the least positive integer at which `a` and `b` disagree — either because `aₖ ≠ bₖ` at some `k ≤ min(m, n)`, or because one tumbler is exhausted at position `k = min(m, n) + 1` while the other continues." T1's `Depends` T0 entry names only length, component projection, and the three strict-total-order properties of `<` on ℕ (irreflexivity, trichotomy, transitivity). Well-ordering is not cited.
**Issue**: The phrase "the least positive integer at which `a` and `b` disagree" asserts existence of the minimum of a nonempty subset of ℕ — exactly the well-ordering principle T0 now enumerates. The proof's case split in (b) then relies on this minimum (Case 2 invokes "by minimality of `k`, `aᵢ = bᵢ` for all `i < k`"; Case 3 ranges over the same minimality). Without well-ordering, "first divergence position" is not a defined construct. T1's `Depends` does not record well-ordering among the T0 properties it invokes, even though cycle 7 introduced the axiom precisely because downstream proofs in this ASN depend on it. Analogous citations were added for `PositiveTumbler`'s "smallest index `k`" step and `ActionPoint`'s `min` well-definedness, but T1's matching step was not updated.
**What needs resolving**: T1's `Depends` T0 entry should be widened to record T0's well-ordering of ℕ as the licensing source for the "least positive integer at which `a` and `b` disagree" step in the trichotomy proof (and for the corresponding minimality appeals in Cases 2 and 3), matching the per-step citation convention established for `ActionPoint` and `PositiveTumbler`.

### TumblerAdd's dominance proof invokes well-ordering of ℕ without citation
**Foundation**: `T0 (CarrierSetDefinition)` — well-ordering of ℕ.
**ASN**: `TumblerAdd`, dominance-over-displacement proof (`a ⊕ w ≥ w`): "For `i < k`, `rᵢ = aᵢ` and `wᵢ = 0` (by definition of action point); if some `aⱼ > 0` for `j < k`, the least such `j` is a divergence point with `rⱼ > wⱼ`, so T1 case (i) gives `r > w`." TumblerAdd's `Depends` T0 entry cites T0 for carrier-set membership, order-compatibility of `+`, strict successor inequality, and additive identity — but not well-ordering.
**Issue**: The step "the least such `j`" asserts existence of the minimum of the set `{j : 1 ≤ j < k ∧ aⱼ > 0}`, a nonempty subset of ℕ in the branch where some `aⱼ > 0` exists. This is an independent use of T0's well-ordering distinct from the well-ordering use propagated into TumblerAdd through its `actionPoint(w)` preconditions (which ActionPoint discharges internally). A formalizer scanning TumblerAdd's `Depends` to license this proof step has no source in scope for "least such `j` exists" — the four T0 facts recorded (carrier set, `+`-monotonicity, successor, identity) do not entail the least-element principle. Cycle 7 widened T0 and cited the principle in `PositiveTumbler` and `ActionPoint`, but the analogous step inside `TumblerAdd`'s own proof was not updated.
**What needs resolving**: TumblerAdd's `Depends` T0 entry should be widened to record T0's well-ordering of ℕ as the licensing source for the "least such `j`" step in the dominance proof, matching the per-step citation convention now used elsewhere in the ASN.
