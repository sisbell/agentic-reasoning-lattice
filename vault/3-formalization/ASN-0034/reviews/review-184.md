### T0's NAT-* axioms live only in body prose, yet every downstream proof consumes them while citing only "T0"
**Foundation**: T0 (CarrierSetDefinition), body — "The standard properties of ℕ that downstream proofs cite — closure under successor and addition, strict total order, discreteness, order compatibility of addition, well-ordering, zero as lower bound of ℕ, partial subtraction … and associativity of addition — are stated as separate axioms (NAT-closure, NAT-order, NAT-discrete, NAT-addcompat, NAT-wellorder, NAT-zero, NAT-sub, NAT-cancel, NAT-addassoc) … Each proof cites only the ℕ facts it actually uses."
T0 (CarrierSetDefinition), Formal Contract — "*Axiom:* T is the set of all finite sequences over ℕ with length ≥ 1, equipped with length `#· : T → ℕ` … and component projection `·ᵢ` yielding `aᵢ ∈ ℕ` … The subcarrier `ℕ⁺ = {n ∈ ℕ : n > 0}` denotes the strictly positive naturals …"
**ASN**: T4 body — "T0 already fixes the carrier as ℕ, so a component is either zero or strictly positive — no further clause is needed to force non-zero components to be positive" (uses zero-as-lower-bound of ℕ). T4a body — "since T0 fixes the carrier as ℕ, such a position carries a strictly positive value" (uses NAT-zero). T4b body — "T0's carrier ℕ makes every non-zero component positive" and "Non-emptiness of `N(t)` follows from `#t ≥ 1` (T0)". T4c body — "the `zeros` function is non-negative by construction — it counts indices, so its image lies in the non-negative integers of T0's carrier ℕ" (uses NAT-zero). T6 Ingredient 1 — "by T0, every component lies in ℕ, so every non-zero component is strictly positive"; Ingredient 3 — equality and order on ℕ is "decidable" (uses NAT-discrete, NAT-order). None of T4, T4a, T4b, T4c, T6 names any NAT-* axiom in its Depends list — each one cites "T0 (CarrierSetDefinition)" alone.
**Issue**: T0's body sets up a convention — NAT-* are stated as *separate* axioms and "each proof cites only the ℕ facts it actually uses" — but (i) T0's *Formal Contract Axiom clause* does not include any NAT-* statement; the NAT-* axioms exist only as named items in T0's body prose with no individual formal content, so there is no contract-level object for downstream proofs to cite; (ii) consequently every downstream theorem that relies on a property of ℕ (non-negativity of counts, "non-zero ⇒ positive", decidable equality on ℕ, order comparisons) bundles that reliance under a single "T0" citation rather than the specific NAT-* axiom(s) it actually consumes. The chain from T4c's exhaustion step, T4a's positivity step, T4b's case `k = 0`, and T6's Ingredient 3 each terminates at "T0" — but T0's formal Axiom clause, read strictly, supplies only the carrier, length, component projection, and ℕ⁺ subcarrier, not the order, zero-lower-bound, or decidability properties those downstream steps actually invoke. Per-property review at T0 sees a tidy carrier axiom; per-property review at T4a/T4b/T4c/T6 sees consistent "T0" citations. The cross-cutting gap is that T0's own stated convention (specific NAT-* citations, each proof citing only what it uses) is uniformly unfollowed, and the NAT-* axioms that would underwrite the cited facts are not promoted into any formal contract a downstream proof could point at.
**What needs resolving**: Either promote each NAT-* axiom into a separate formal-contract statement (with its own name and content) that downstream theorems can cite individually, and update T4, T4a, T4b, T4c, T6 to replace their bundled "T0" citations with the specific NAT-* axiom(s) they consume; or retract T0's "stated as separate axioms … each proof cites only the ℕ facts it actually uses" convention and restate T0's Axiom clause so that the ℕ properties downstream proofs consume (order, zero-lower-bound, decidability) are formally included under the single T0 citation those proofs already make.
