### "Span may be empty" prose conflicts with T12's non-emptiness postcondition
**Foundation**: T12 (SpanWellDefinedness), postcondition (b): "`s ∈ span(s, ℓ)` (non-empty, by T1's `≤`-reflexivity for `s ≤ s` and TA-strict for `s < s ⊕ ℓ`)." Definition (Span): `span(s, ℓ) = {t ∈ T : s ≤ t < s ⊕ ℓ}` — the address-set fixed by the definition.
**ASN**: The "Spans" narrative between Definition (Span) and T12 asserts: "And a span may be empty — populated by nothing at present — yet valid: 'A span that contains nothing today may at a later time contain a million documents.' The range is determined by the endpoints; what is actually stored within that range is a question about the current state of the system, not about the tumbler algebra."
**Issue**: The word "span" carries two different referents across the document. In Definition (Span) and in T12, `span(s, ℓ)` names the address-set `{t ∈ T : s ≤ t < s ⊕ ℓ}` — the range — and T12(b) proves this set is always non-empty (contains at least `s`). In the narrative paragraph, "a span may be empty" uses "span" for the *content* populating that range, which may indeed be empty. The paragraph's own follow-up sentence ("The range is determined by the endpoints; what is actually stored within that range is a question about the current state of the system") disambiguates, but only after using the same word "span" for both senses in adjacent sentences. A reader cross-checking the Nelson quote against T12(b) sees "a span... populated by nothing" in the prose and `s ∈ span(s, ℓ)` formally, and must infer that "empty" refers to content-emptiness while `span(s, ℓ)` refers to address-non-emptiness — a sense distinction that neither Definition (Span) nor T12 names.
**What needs resolving**: Either (a) reserve the term "span" in the prose for the formal address-set `span(s, ℓ)` and name the content separately (e.g., "a span's content may be empty", "a span may contain no documents today") so that "span" has one meaning throughout; or (b) introduce an explicit disambiguating convention at the narrative's opening — distinguishing *span* (address-set) from *span's contents* (populated subset) — and apply it uniformly in every subsequent use, so that T12's "non-empty" and the narrative's "may be empty" cannot be read against each other under the same term.
