### TA5(d) cited as supplying strict inequality `#inc(tᵢ, k') > #tᵢ` in T10a body, inconsistent with standalone T10a.5
**Foundation**: TA5's postcondition (d) reads: "when `k > 0`: `#t' = #t + k`, the `k - 1` intermediate positions `#t + 1, ..., #t + k - 1` are set to `0`…" — an equation on lengths, not a strict inequality. The strict inequality `#t + k > #t` for `k ≥ 1` is a NAT-level consequence (NAT-addcompat's strict successor `n < n + 1` combined with left order-compatibility lifting `1 ≤ k`), not part of TA5(d)'s own content. The standalone T10a.5 theorem's introductory paragraph correctly separates these: "TA5(d) gives the *equation* `#inc(tᵢ, k') = #tᵢ + k'`; since `k' ∈ {1, 2}` by T10a we have `1 ≤ k'`, and NAT-addcompat's strict successor inequality… sharpens TA5(d)'s equation to the strict inequality". T10a.3's standalone theorem applies the same convention (TA5(d) for the equation, NAT-addcompat for the strict sharpening), and T10a.3's Depends notes this matches "the per-step citation convention TA5's Case `k > 0` applies".
**ASN**: T10a body, Consequence 5 introductory paragraph: "For ancestor-descendant allocator pairs, prefix-nesting holds by construction: when a parent spawns a child from output `tᵢ` via `inc(tᵢ, k')` with `k' > 0`, TA5(b) gives agreement on all positions `1..#tᵢ` and TA5(d) gives `#inc(tᵢ, k') > #tᵢ`, so `tᵢ ≺ inc(tᵢ, k')` — the spawning output is a proper prefix of the child's base address, and positional inheritance propagates this through the child's entire subtree."
**Issue**: T10a body attributes the strict inequality `#inc(tᵢ, k') > #tᵢ` to TA5(d), but TA5(d) supplies only the equation `#inc(tᵢ, k') = #tᵢ + k'`. The strict sharpening consumes `1 ≤ k'` (from T10a's `k' ∈ {1, 2}`) together with NAT-addcompat's strict successor and left order-compatibility — content the T10a body's Consequence 5 prose suppresses and the T10a.5 standalone theorem (as well as T10a.3, T10a.5 Formal Contract, and T10a-N) explicitly lays out. Because Consequence 5's prose is also the only place in T10a's body that establishes the ancestor-descendant nesting claim `tᵢ ≺ inc(tᵢ, k')`, and this nesting claim is then consumed in the same paragraph's "length separation between parent and child inherently nests" argument and carried forward to T10a.6's Case 1 (ancestor-descendant disjointness), the body's attribution chain is understating the NAT-level step the standalone theorems correctly source. The Formal Contract's Depends for T10a.5 lists TA5 without NAT-addcompat, NAT-zero, NAT-discrete, or T0 — matching the body prose's loose attribution rather than the standalone T10a.5 theorem's precise per-step sourcing.
**What needs resolving**: Either (i) rewrite Consequence 5's introductory paragraph so that the strict inequality is discharged from NAT-addcompat (with NAT-zero/NAT-discrete for the `k' > 0 ⟹ k' ≥ 1` sharpening) rather than from TA5(d) alone — paralleling the standalone T10a.5 theorem's introduction and T10a.3's single-step claim — and extend T10a.5's Depends in the T10a Formal Contract to include NAT-addcompat (and the supporting T0/NAT-zero/NAT-discrete trio for the `k' ≥ 1` step); or (ii) justify why T10a body's Consequence 5 prose may cite TA5(d) for the strict inequality while T10a.5's standalone theorem may not, and record the convention explicitly so the two representations do not contradict each other on what TA5(d) supplies.
