### `+1` / `+` operator and literal `1` in case-(ii) values are not discharged by Depends
**Foundation**: Divergence's internal convention — "every operator occurring in the Definition is either T0's length `#·`, T0's component projection, or the strict order `<` supplied by NAT-order". T0's exhaustive NAT-* list names NAT-closure ("closure under successor and addition"), NAT-addassoc, and NAT-zero among the arithmetic axioms; no axiom is explicitly cited as establishing the literal `1 ∈ ℕ`.
**ASN**: Divergence Definition case (ii): "`divergence(a, b) = #a + 1`" (sub-case ii-a) and "`divergence(a, b) = #b + 1`" (sub-case ii-b), repeated in the Formal Contract Definition and in the postcondition discussion. ZeroPaddedDivergence Relationship-to-Divergence postcondition: "≥ `#a + 1` under (β), ≥ `#w + 1` under (γ), each replacing `min(#a, #w) + 1` for its sub-case".
**Issue**: Both Definitions produce values via the additive expression `length + 1`, introducing (i) the binary operator `+` on ℕ and (ii) the literal `1 ∈ ℕ`. Neither operator appears in Divergence's stated operator inventory (which explicitly lists only `#·`, projection, and `<`), and neither is discharged by its Depends (T0, T1, T3, NAT-order, NAT-wellorder). Likewise ZPD's Depends (T0, NAT-zero, NAT-order, NAT-wellorder, Divergence) cites no addition axiom and no axiom establishing `1 ∈ ℕ`. This is a structural parallel to the Previous Findings' `max`/`min` and `0 ∈ ℕ` gaps — addressed for those operators but left open for `+1` — and is especially conspicuous given that Divergence's convention explicitly forbids operators outside the enumerated list.
**What needs resolving**: Either (a) extend Divergence's stated operator inventory and add an addition axiom (e.g., NAT-closure or NAT-addassoc) plus a discharging citation for `1 ∈ ℕ` to both Divergence's and ZPD's Depends, or (b) restate case-(ii)'s value without `+`, e.g., as the successor of the shorter length anchored to an existing axiom. As written, the Depends lists do not cover every operator the case-(ii) values and the Relationship-to-Divergence postcondition use.
