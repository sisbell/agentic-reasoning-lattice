**T10a-N (AllocatorDisciplineNecessity).** T10a restricts the sibling stream of an allocator to `inc(·, 0)` — "Each allocator produces its sibling outputs exclusively by repeated application of `inc(·, 0)`." Relaxing this restriction to permit `inc(·, k)` with any `k ≥ 0` in the sibling stream admits prefix nesting among co-sibling outputs of a single allocator, violating the non-nesting precondition of T10.

Suppose the discipline is relaxed in this way. Let `t₀ ∈ T` be an allocator's base, fix any `k > 0` that the relaxation admits into the sibling stream, and consider the two outputs `t₁ = inc(t₀, 0)` and `t₂ = inc(t₁, k)`, both admitted as sibling outputs of this single allocator under the relaxation. The argument is parametric in `k`: no step below depends on any specific value beyond `k > 0`, and the witness `k = 1` is merely the minimal such value. By TA5(c), `#t₁ = #t₀`. By TA5(d), since `k > 0` at the second step, `#t₂ = #t₁ + k`; three T0 facts together convert this equation into the strict inequality `#t₁ < #t₂`. T0's discreteness — at `m = 0`, any `n ∈ ℕ` satisfying `0 ≤ n < 0 + 1` is `n = 0` — rules out natural numbers strictly between `0` and `1`, so `k > 0` in ℕ sharpens to `k ≥ 1`. T0's order-compatibility of addition (`p ≤ n ⟹ m + p ≤ m + n`), instantiated at `m = #t₁`, `p = 1`, `n = k`, lifts `1 ≤ k` to `#t₁ + 1 ≤ #t₁ + k`. T0's strict successor inequality `n < n + 1` (for every `n ∈ ℕ`), instantiated at `n = #t₁`, gives `#t₁ < #t₁ + 1`. T0's defining clause `m ≤ n ⟺ m < n ∨ m = n` chains these into `#t₁ < #t₁ + 1 ≤ #t₁ + k = #t₂`, yielding `#t₁ < #t₂`. By TA5(b) for `k > 0`, `t₂` agrees with `t₁` at every position `1 ≤ i ≤ #t₁` — the agreement clause of TA5(b) is quantified over all original positions of `t₁` independently of `k`, so this step is itself parametric in `k`. The Prefix definition's length precondition is the non-strict `#t₁ ≤ #t₂` rather than the strict inequality just established; T0's defining clause `m ≤ n ⟺ m < n ∨ m = n` weakens `#t₁ < #t₂` into `#t₁ ≤ #t₂`, supplying the exact length conjunct Prefix requires. These two facts — agreement on all `#t₁` positions of the shorter tumbler and `#t₁ ≤ #t₂` — are precisely the conditions of the Prefix definition, so `t₁ ≼ t₂`.

The nesting `t₁ ≼ t₂` is itself a defining consequence of applying `inc(·, k)` with `k > 0`: TA5(b) and TA5(d) together force it on any such pair, so its *occurrence* between `t₁` and `t₂` is not in question. What the relaxation breaks is the *role* the pair plays. Under T10a the nesting is harmless because `t₂ = inc(t₁, k)` would be the base address of a child allocator in a separate domain — T10a.6 places parent and child disjointly, and T10's precondition is not sought between parent and child outputs. Under the relaxation, however, `t₁` and `t₂` are co-sibling outputs of one allocator, so the pair `(t₁, t₂)` is offered to T10 as candidate prefixes of independent allocation; the T10 precondition `p₁ ⋠ p₂ ∧ p₂ ⋠ p₁` then fails on this pair because `t₁ ≼ t₂`. T10 can no longer separate the two domains — every address extending `t₂` also extends `t₁` — and the partition-independence guarantee collapses. The `k = 0` sibling restriction of T10a is therefore necessary: since the construction above is parametric in `k > 0`, any relaxation that admits some `inc(·, k)` with `k > 0` into the sibling stream admits the construction at that specific `k`, and hence admits a co-sibling pair at which T10's precondition fails. ∎

*Formal Contract:*
- *Preconditions:* T10a's sibling restriction is relaxed to permit `inc(·, k)` with any `k ≥ 0` in the sibling stream of a single allocator. `t₀ ∈ T`; `k > 0` is arbitrary; the allocator emits `t₁ = inc(t₀, 0)` and `t₂ = inc(t₁, k)` as co-sibling outputs under the relaxation.
- *Postconditions:* `t₁ ≼ t₂` — the two co-sibling outputs stand in a prefix relation, violating T10's non-nesting precondition (`p₁ ⋠ p₂ ∧ p₂ ⋠ p₁`) on this pair. Since the construction is parametric in `k > 0`, the `k = 0` sibling restriction of T10a is necessary for T10's precondition to hold among co-sibling outputs of a single allocator: every `k > 0` admitted by a relaxation witnesses a failing pair.
- *Depends:* T0 (CarrierSetDefinition) — the parametric length step rests on four ℕ-level facts that T0 enumerates. First, from `k > 0` in ℕ, T0's discreteness (`m ≤ n < m + 1 ⟹ n = m`, instantiated at `m = 0`) gives `k ≥ 1`: no natural number lies strictly between `0` and `0 + 1`, so the only way `k > 0` can hold is `k ≥ 1`. Second, T0's order-compatibility of addition (`p ≤ n ⟹ m + p ≤ m + n`, applied with `m = #t₁`, `p = 1`, `n = k`) lifts this `1 ≤ k` to `#t₁ + 1 ≤ #t₁ + k` — the length-level non-strict step that feeds the transitivity chain. Third, T0's strict successor inequality `n < n + 1` (for every `n ∈ ℕ`), instantiated at `n = #t₁`, gives `#t₁ < #t₁ + 1`: the length equation supplied by TA5(d) names a ℕ-sum, and T0 is the source of the strict inequality between `n` and `n + 1` at the length level. Fourth, T0's defining clause `m ≤ n ⟺ m < n ∨ m = n` discharges two roles here: it combines `#t₁ < #t₁ + 1` with `#t₁ + 1 ≤ #t₁ + k` to produce the strict inequality `#t₁ < #t₁ + k = #t₂`, and it subsequently weakens `#t₁ < #t₂` to the non-strict `#t₁ ≤ #t₂` that Prefix's length precondition demands — the same `<`-to-`≤` step that Prefix's own Depends attributes to T0 — so the strict ℕ-inequality flows into Prefix's non-strict conjunct rather than being silently identified with it. These appeals are discharged from T0 rather than left implicit, matching the per-step citation convention established for `T1`, `TA5`, `Prefix`, and `T10` on comparable uses of length comparison, ℕ-successor, order-compatibility, discreteness, and `≤`-unfolding. T10a (AllocatorDiscipline) — supplies the `k = 0` sibling restriction whose relaxation this argument considers; without T10a the clause "relaxing the sibling restriction" has no referent. T10a.6 (DomainDisjointness) — distinct allocators have disjoint domains (`dom(X) ∩ dom(Y) = ∅`); this places the child allocator's base address `t₂ = inc(t₁, k)` (for any `k > 0`) outside the parent's domain, so under T10a the nesting `t₁ ≼ t₂` crosses the parent–child domain boundary rather than lying within the parent's sibling stream, and therefore does not offer the pair to T10's non-nesting precondition among co-sibling outputs. TA5 (HierarchicalIncrement) — TA5(c) gives `#t₁ = #t₀`, TA5(d) gives the length equation `#t₂ = #t₁ + k` for arbitrary `k > 0` (the strict inequality `#t₁ < #t₂` that follows is discharged by the T0 chain cited above), and TA5(b) for `k > 0` gives agreement of `t₂` with `t₁` on positions `1..#t₁` — an agreement clause quantified over all original positions irrespective of `k`, so parametric in `k`; these are the three TA5-supplied facts that, combined with the T0-supplied discreteness, order-compatibility, strict successor, and `<`-to-`≤` steps, reconstruct the Prefix conditions on the pair `(t₁, t₂)`. Prefix (PrefixRelation) — its definition is invoked to convert "agreement on all `#t₁` positions and `#t₁ ≤ #t₂`" into `t₁ ≼ t₂`. T10 (PartitionIndependence) — supplies the non-nesting precondition `p₁ ⋠ p₂ ∧ p₂ ⋠ p₁` that the conclusion is shown to falsify.
