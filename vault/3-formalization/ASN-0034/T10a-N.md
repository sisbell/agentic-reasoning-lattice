**T10a-N (AllocatorDisciplineNecessity).** T10a restricts the sibling stream of an allocator to `inc(·, 0)` — "Each allocator produces its sibling outputs exclusively by repeated application of `inc(·, 0)`." Relaxing this restriction to permit `inc(·, k)` with any `k ≥ 0` in the sibling stream admits prefix nesting among co-sibling outputs of a single allocator, falsifying T10a.2 (NonNestingSiblingPrefixes) — T10a's within-allocator consequence that distinct sibling outputs of one allocator are prefix-incomparable.

Suppose the discipline is relaxed in this way. Let `t₀ ∈ T` be an allocator's base, fix any `k > 0` that the relaxation admits into the sibling stream, and consider the two outputs `t₁ = inc(t₀, 0)` and `t₂ = inc(t₁, k)`, both admitted as sibling outputs of this single allocator under the relaxation. The argument is parametric in `k > 0`: no step below depends on any specific value. By TA5(d), since `k > 0` at the second step, `#t₂ = #t₁ + k`; three NAT-level facts together convert this equation into the strict inequality `#t₁ < #t₂`. NAT-discrete — at `m = 0`, any `n ∈ ℕ` satisfying `0 ≤ n < 0 + 1` is `n = 0` — rules out natural numbers strictly between `0` and `1`, so `k > 0` in ℕ sharpens to `k ≥ 1`. NAT-addcompat's order-compatibility of addition (`p ≤ n ⟹ m + p ≤ m + n`), instantiated at `m = #t₁`, `p = 1`, `n = k`, lifts `1 ≤ k` to `#t₁ + 1 ≤ #t₁ + k`. NAT-addcompat's strict successor inequality `n < n + 1` (for every `n ∈ ℕ`), instantiated at `n = #t₁`, gives `#t₁ < #t₁ + 1`. NAT-order's defining clause `m ≤ n ⟺ m < n ∨ m = n` chains these into `#t₁ < #t₁ + 1 ≤ #t₁ + k = #t₂`, yielding `#t₁ < #t₂`. By TA5(b) for `k > 0`, `t₂` agrees with `t₁` at every position `1 ≤ i ≤ #t₁` — the agreement clause of TA5(b) is quantified over all original positions of `t₁` independently of `k`, so this step is itself parametric in `k`. The Prefix definition's length precondition is the non-strict `#t₁ ≤ #t₂` rather than the strict inequality just established; NAT-order's defining clause `m ≤ n ⟺ m < n ∨ m = n` weakens `#t₁ < #t₂` into `#t₁ ≤ #t₂`, supplying the exact length conjunct Prefix requires. These two facts — agreement on all `#t₁` positions of the shorter tumbler and `#t₁ ≤ #t₂` — are precisely the conditions of the Prefix definition, so `t₁ ≼ t₂`.

The nesting `t₁ ≼ t₂` is itself a defining consequence of applying `inc(·, k)` with `k > 0`: TA5(b) and TA5(d) together force it on any such pair, so its *occurrence* between `t₁` and `t₂` is not in question. What the relaxation breaks is the *role* the pair plays. Under T10a the nesting is harmless because `t₂ = inc(t₁, k)` would be the base address of a child allocator in a separate domain — T10a.6 places parent and child disjointly, and T10's precondition is not sought between parent and child outputs. Under the relaxation, however, `t₁` and `t₂` are co-sibling outputs of one allocator, and the strict length inequality `#t₁ < #t₂` established above forces `t₁ ≠ t₂`, so `(t₁, t₂)` is a pair of *distinct* sibling outputs of one allocator standing in a prefix relation `t₁ ≼ t₂`. T10a.2 (NonNestingSiblingPrefixes) — T10a's within-allocator consequence — asserts exactly the negation: for any two distinct sibling outputs `tᵢ`, `tⱼ` of a single allocator, `tᵢ ⋠ tⱼ ∧ tⱼ ⋠ tᵢ`. The pair `(t₁, t₂)` is therefore a counterexample to T10a.2, and the refutation stays internal to T10a as a within-allocator claim: the pair is co-sibling, not cross-allocator, so it is not a candidate pair of independent-allocator domain prefixes to which T10 would be applied. T10's precondition `p₁ ⋠ p₂ ∧ p₂ ⋠ p₁` is sought at the domain prefixes of two distinct allocators — as in Case 3 of GlobalUniqueness, where `p₁` and `p₂` are the spawning parent domain elements of two non-nesting branches — and that cross-allocator precondition is supplied downstream by T10a.5 (CrossAllocatorIncomparability), not by T10a.2. T10a.2 concerns co-siblings of one allocator, a within-domain integrity claim whose failure breaks T10a's own structural guarantee that distinct sibling outputs of a single allocator are prefix-incomparable rather than mediating a failure at T10's inputs. The clause the relaxation strips is precisely the one that sustained the falsified postcondition, since T10a.2's proof rests on T10a.1's uniform sibling length and T10a.1 is the direct consequence of the `inc(·, 0)` restriction; removing that restriction removes T10a.1 and with it T10a.2. The `k = 0` sibling restriction of T10a is therefore necessary for T10a.2: since the construction above is parametric in `k > 0`, any relaxation that admits some `inc(·, k)` with `k > 0` into the sibling stream admits the construction at that specific `k`, and hence admits a co-sibling pair at which T10a.2 fails. ∎

*Formal Contract:*
- *Preconditions:* T10a's sibling restriction is relaxed to permit `inc(·, k)` with any `k ≥ 0` in the sibling stream of a single allocator. `t₀ ∈ T`; `k > 0` is arbitrary; the allocator emits `t₁ = inc(t₀, 0)` and `t₂ = inc(t₁, k)` as co-sibling outputs under the relaxation.
- *Postconditions:* `t₁ ≼ t₂` — the two distinct co-sibling outputs stand in a prefix relation, falsifying T10a.2 (NonNestingSiblingPrefixes), which asserts that distinct sibling outputs of one allocator are prefix-incomparable (`tᵢ ⋠ tⱼ ∧ tⱼ ⋠ tᵢ`). T10a.2 is T10a's within-allocator consequence; the falsification is a within-allocator failure and does not propagate through T10, since T10 is applied at the domain prefixes of two distinct allocators — a cross-allocator precondition supplied by T10a.5 (CrossAllocatorIncomparability) for non-ancestor-descendant pairs, and co-siblings of one allocator are not such a pair. Since the construction is parametric in `k > 0`, the `k = 0` sibling restriction of T10a is necessary for T10a.2 to hold among co-sibling outputs of a single allocator: every `k > 0` admitted by a relaxation witnesses a failing pair.
- *Depends:* T0 (CarrierSetDefinition) — supplies the carrier set T, the length `#·`, and component projection used throughout (preconditions `t₀ ∈ T`, lengths `#t₁`, `#t₂`); the four ℕ-level facts the parametric length step rests on are attributed to the NAT-* axioms below. NAT-discrete (NatDiscreteness) — from `k > 0` in ℕ, NAT-discrete (`m ≤ n < m + 1 ⟹ n = m`, instantiated at `m = 0`) gives `k ≥ 1`: no natural number lies strictly between `0` and `0 + 1`, so the only way `k > 0` can hold is `k ≥ 1`. NAT-addcompat (NatAdditionOrderCompatibility) — two clauses are cited here. First, NAT-addcompat's order-compatibility of addition (`p ≤ n ⟹ m + p ≤ m + n`, applied with `m = #t₁`, `p = 1`, `n = k`) lifts `1 ≤ k` to `#t₁ + 1 ≤ #t₁ + k` — the length-level non-strict step that feeds the transitivity chain. Second, NAT-addcompat's strict successor inequality `n < n + 1` (for every `n ∈ ℕ`), instantiated at `n = #t₁`, gives `#t₁ < #t₁ + 1`: the length equation supplied by TA5(d) names a ℕ-sum, and NAT-addcompat is the source of the strict inequality between `n` and `n + 1` at the length level. NAT-order (NatStrictTotalOrder) — its defining clause `m ≤ n ⟺ m < n ∨ m = n` discharges two roles here: it combines `#t₁ < #t₁ + 1` with `#t₁ + 1 ≤ #t₁ + k` to produce the strict inequality `#t₁ < #t₁ + k = #t₂`, and it subsequently weakens `#t₁ < #t₂` to the non-strict `#t₁ ≤ #t₂` that Prefix's length precondition demands — the same `<`-to-`≤` step that Prefix's own Depends attributes to NAT-order — so the strict ℕ-inequality flows into Prefix's non-strict conjunct rather than being silently identified with it. These appeals are discharged from the NAT-* axioms rather than left implicit, matching the per-step citation convention established for `T1`, `TA5`, `Prefix`, and `T10` on comparable uses of length comparison, ℕ-successor, order-compatibility, discreteness, and `≤`-unfolding. T10a (AllocatorDiscipline) — supplies the `k = 0` sibling restriction whose relaxation this argument considers; without T10a the clause "relaxing the sibling restriction" has no referent. T10a.6 (DomainDisjointness) — distinct allocators have disjoint domains (`dom(X) ∩ dom(Y) = ∅`); this places the child allocator's base address `t₂ = inc(t₁, k)` (for any `k > 0`) outside the parent's domain, so under T10a the nesting `t₁ ≼ t₂` crosses the parent–child domain boundary rather than lying within the parent's sibling stream, and therefore does not offer the pair to T10's non-nesting precondition among co-sibling outputs. TA5 (HierarchicalIncrement) — TA5(d) gives the length equation `#t₂ = #t₁ + k` for arbitrary `k > 0` (the strict inequality `#t₁ < #t₂` that follows is discharged by the NAT chain cited above), and TA5(b) for `k > 0` gives agreement of `t₂` with `t₁` on positions `1..#t₁` — an agreement clause quantified over all original positions irrespective of `k`, so parametric in `k`; these are the two TA5-supplied facts that, combined with the NAT-supplied discreteness, order-compatibility, strict successor, and `<`-to-`≤` steps, reconstruct the Prefix conditions on the pair `(t₁, t₂)`. Prefix (PrefixRelation) — its definition is invoked to convert "agreement on all `#t₁` positions and `#t₁ ≤ #t₂`" into `t₁ ≼ t₂`. T10a.2 (NonNestingSiblingPrefixes) — T10a's within-allocator consequence that distinct sibling outputs of one allocator are prefix-incomparable (`tᵢ ⋠ tⱼ ∧ tⱼ ⋠ tᵢ`); the pair `(t₁, t₂)` produced by the relaxation is distinct (the strict length inequality `#t₁ < #t₂` forces `t₁ ≠ t₂`) and nesting (`t₁ ≼ t₂`), so it is exactly a counterexample to T10a.2. Citing T10a.2 here names the clause that the conclusion directly falsifies; the refutation is internal to T10a and does not involve T10, because T10's precondition `p₁ ⋠ p₂ ∧ p₂ ⋠ p₁` is applied at the domain prefixes of two distinct allocators (as in GlobalUniqueness Case 3) and co-siblings of one allocator are not such a pair. T10a.5 (CrossAllocatorIncomparability) — named here to fix the placement of T10's cross-allocator precondition: for non-ancestor-descendant allocator pairs T10a.5 supplies `p₁ ⋠ p₂ ∧ p₂ ⋠ p₁` at the domain-prefix level, whereas T10a.2 supplies the disjoint within-allocator guarantee, so this argument's direct falsification of T10a.2 leaves T10's inputs themselves untouched by the necessity construction. T10 (PartitionIndependence) — cited only as orientation: its precondition `p₁ ⋠ p₂ ∧ p₂ ⋠ p₁` is a cross-allocator condition and is therefore not the clause this argument falsifies, nor is T10a.2 a bridge to it; the distinct downstream property whose cross-allocator scope does supply T10's preconditions is T10a.5.
