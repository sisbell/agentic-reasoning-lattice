**T10a-N (AllocatorDisciplineNecessity).** T10a restricts the sibling stream of an allocator to `inc(·, 0)` — "Each allocator produces its sibling outputs exclusively by repeated application of `inc(·, 0)`." Relaxing this restriction to permit `inc(·, k)` with any `k ≥ 0` in the sibling stream admits prefix nesting among co-sibling outputs of a single allocator, violating the non-nesting precondition of T10.

Suppose the discipline is relaxed in this way. Let `t₀ ∈ T` be an allocator's base, and consider the two outputs `t₁ = inc(t₀, 0)` and `t₂ = inc(t₁, 1)`, both admitted as sibling outputs of this single allocator under the relaxation. By TA5(c), `#t₁ = #t₀`. By TA5(d), since `k = 1 > 0` at the second step, `#t₂ = #t₁ + 1`, so `#t₁ < #t₂`. By TA5(b) for `k > 0`, `t₂` agrees with `t₁` at every position `1 ≤ i ≤ #t₁`. These two facts — agreement on all `#t₁` positions of the shorter tumbler and strict length excess `#t₁ < #t₂` — are precisely the conditions of the Prefix definition, so `t₁ ≼ t₂`.

The nesting `t₁ ≼ t₂` is itself a defining consequence of applying `inc(·, k)` with `k > 0`: TA5(b) and TA5(d) together force it on any such pair, so its *occurrence* between `t₁` and `t₂` is not in question. What the relaxation breaks is the *role* the pair plays. Under T10a the nesting is harmless because `t₂ = inc(t₁, 1)` would be the base address of a child allocator in a separate domain — T10a.6 places parent and child disjointly, and T10's precondition is not sought between parent and child outputs. Under the relaxation, however, `t₁` and `t₂` are co-sibling outputs of one allocator, so the pair `(t₁, t₂)` is offered to T10 as candidate prefixes of independent allocation; the T10 precondition `p₁ ⋠ p₂ ∧ p₂ ⋠ p₁` then fails on this pair because `t₁ ≼ t₂`. T10 can no longer separate the two domains — every address extending `t₂` also extends `t₁` — and the partition-independence guarantee collapses. The `k = 0` sibling restriction of T10a is therefore necessary: any relaxation that admits some `inc(·, k)` with `k > 0` into the sibling stream admits this construction, and hence admits a co-sibling pair at which T10's precondition fails. ∎

*Formal Contract:*
- *Preconditions:* T10a's sibling restriction is relaxed to permit `inc(·, k)` with any `k ≥ 0` in the sibling stream of a single allocator. `t₀ ∈ T`; the allocator emits `t₁ = inc(t₀, 0)` and `t₂ = inc(t₁, 1)` as co-sibling outputs under the relaxation.
- *Postconditions:* `t₁ ≼ t₂` — the two co-sibling outputs stand in a prefix relation, violating T10's non-nesting precondition (`p₁ ⋠ p₂ ∧ p₂ ⋠ p₁`) on this pair. The `k = 0` sibling restriction of T10a is therefore necessary for T10's precondition to hold among co-sibling outputs of a single allocator.
- *Depends:* T10a (AllocatorDiscipline) — supplies the `k = 0` sibling restriction whose relaxation this argument considers; without T10a the clause "relaxing the sibling restriction" has no referent. TA5 (HierarchicalIncrement) — TA5(c) gives `#t₁ = #t₀`, TA5(d) gives `#t₂ = #t₁ + 1 > #t₁`, and TA5(b) for `k > 0` gives agreement of `t₂` with `t₁` on positions `1..#t₁`; these are the three facts that jointly reconstruct the Prefix conditions on the pair `(t₁, t₂)`. Prefix (PrefixRelation) — its definition is invoked to convert "agreement on all `#t₁` positions and `#t₁ < #t₂`" into `t₁ ≼ t₂`. T10 (PartitionIndependence) — supplies the non-nesting precondition `p₁ ⋠ p₂ ∧ p₂ ⋠ p₁` that the conclusion is shown to falsify.
