**T10a-N (AllocatorDisciplineNecessity).** T10a restricts the sibling stream of an allocator to `inc(¬∑, 0)` ‚ÄĒ "Each allocator produces its sibling outputs exclusively by repeated application of `inc(¬∑, 0)`." Relaxing this restriction to permit `inc(¬∑, k)` with any `k ‚Č• 0` in the sibling stream admits prefix nesting among co-sibling outputs of a single allocator, violating the non-nesting precondition of T10.

Suppose the discipline is relaxed in this way. Let `t‚āÄ ‚ąą T` be an allocator's base, and consider the two outputs `t‚āĀ = inc(t‚āÄ, 0)` and `t‚āā = inc(t‚āĀ, 1)`, both admitted as sibling outputs of this single allocator under the relaxation. By TA5(c), `#t‚āĀ = #t‚āÄ`. By TA5(d), since `k = 1 > 0` at the second step, `#t‚āā = #t‚āĀ + 1`, so `#t‚āĀ < #t‚āā`. By TA5(b) for `k > 0`, `t‚āā` agrees with `t‚āĀ` at every position `1 ‚Č§ i ‚Č§ #t‚āĀ`. These two facts ‚ÄĒ agreement on all `#t‚āĀ` positions of the shorter tumbler and strict length excess `#t‚āĀ < #t‚āā` ‚ÄĒ are precisely the conditions of the Prefix definition, so `t‚āĀ ‚Čľ t‚āā`.

The nesting `t‚āĀ ‚Čľ t‚āā` is itself a defining consequence of applying `inc(¬∑, k)` with `k > 0`: TA5(b) and TA5(d) together force it on any such pair, so its *occurrence* between `t‚āĀ` and `t‚āā` is not in question. What the relaxation breaks is the *role* the pair plays. Under T10a the nesting is harmless because `t‚āā = inc(t‚āĀ, 1)` would be the base address of a child allocator in a separate domain ‚ÄĒ T10a.6 places parent and child disjointly, and T10's precondition is not sought between parent and child outputs. Under the relaxation, however, `t‚āĀ` and `t‚āā` are co-sibling outputs of one allocator, so the pair `(t‚āĀ, t‚āā)` is offered to T10 as candidate prefixes of independent allocation; the T10 precondition `p‚āĀ ‚č† p‚āā ‚ąß p‚āā ‚č† p‚āĀ` then fails on this pair because `t‚āĀ ‚Čľ t‚āā`. T10 can no longer separate the two domains ‚ÄĒ every address extending `t‚āā` also extends `t‚āĀ` ‚ÄĒ and the partition-independence guarantee collapses. The `k = 0` sibling restriction of T10a is therefore necessary: any relaxation that admits some `inc(¬∑, k)` with `k > 0` into the sibling stream admits this construction, and hence admits a co-sibling pair at which T10's precondition fails. ‚ąé
