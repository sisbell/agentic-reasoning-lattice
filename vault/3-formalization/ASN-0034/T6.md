## Decidable containment

The total order T1 determines *sequence* (which address comes first). But the system also needs *containment* — does address `a` belong to account `b`? Is document `d₁` under the same server as document `d₂`? These are not ordering questions; they are prefix questions.

**T6 (DecidableContainment).** For any two tumblers `a, b ∈ T`, the following are decidable from the addresses alone:

  (a) Whether `a` and `b` share the same node field.

  (b) Whether `a` and `b` both carry a user field and those present user fields have matching components — "sharing a *present* user field of matching value" — alongside matching node fields. Symmetric absence (neither tumbler carries a user field) fails this shared-presence requirement, as does asymmetric absence.

  (c) Whether `a` and `b` both carry a document field and the node, user, and document fields have pairwise matching components — "sharing *present* node, user, and document fields of matching value" (*exact equality of the full document projection, under shared presence of both document projections*). Symmetric or asymmetric absence of the document field fails this shared-presence requirement.

  (d) Whether `a` and `b` both carry a document field and the document field of `a` is a prefix of the document field of `b` — the prefix relation on document projections *under shared presence of both projections* (*structural subordination within a document family*, strictly weaker than the exact equality of (c)). Symmetric or asymmetric absence of the document field fails this shared-presence requirement.

*Proof.* We show that each of (a)–(d) admits a terminating decision procedure whose only inputs are the tumbler representations of `a` and `b`. The argument rests on three ingredients, which we establish first.

*Ingredient 1 (field extraction).* By T4, valid address tumblers have at most three zero-valued components, no two zeros are adjacent, and the tumbler neither begins nor ends with zero; by T0, every component lies in ℕ, so every non-zero component is strictly positive. The partial function `fields : T ⇀ Seq(ℕ⁺) × Seq(ℕ⁺) × Seq(ℕ⁺) × Seq(ℕ⁺)` introduced in T4b decomposes a tumbler into its node, user, document, and element fields by locating all zero-valued components — which, by T4's definitional role-assignment, are exactly the field separators, leaving the strictly positive components (via T0) as field contents. T4b fixes the domain of `fields` as exactly the T4-valid subset of `T`; outside that subdomain — tumblers with `zeros(t) ≥ 4` or violating the field-segment constraint — `fields(t)` is not assigned a value. T4b's own contract lists T3 (CanonicalRepresentation) as a per-tumbler precondition, because the uniqueness half of T4b's conclusion — that `fields(t)` is not just defined but *uniquely determined* by `t` — rests on T3: if two encodings of `t` could yield different component values, the scan for zero positions could yield two different separator sets and therefore two different field decompositions. The T6 precondition therefore requires `a, b` to satisfy both T3 and T4: T4-validity places each of `a` and `b` in the domain of `fields`, and T3 fixes their component sequences so that `fields(a)` and `fields(b)` are each a *unique* value on that subdomain — so both invocations below are well-defined and uniquely determined, and every consumer of a projection in cases (a)–(d) inherits this discharge. By T4(b), `fields(t) = (N(t), U(t), D(t), E(t))` is well-defined and uniquely determined by `t` alone on this subdomain, with absent fields represented by the empty sequence `ε` (unambiguous because, by T4a, every *present* field segment is non-empty). Since `t` is a finite sequence, the extraction terminates: scan `t` once, record the positions of zero-valued components, and partition the remaining components into the corresponding field sub-sequences. Write `N(t)`, `U(t)`, `D(t)`, `E(t)` for the node, user, document, and element projections of `fields` respectively — each a partial map `T ⇀ Seq(ℕ⁺)` defined on the same T4-valid subdomain. Each projection returns a finite sequence of strictly positive natural numbers, equal to `ε` iff the corresponding field is absent from `t`.

*Ingredient 2 (field presence).* Define `zeros(t) = #{i : 1 ≤ i ≤ #t ∧ tᵢ = 0}`, the count of zero-valued components in a tumbler `t`; the count is computable from `t` alone by a single finite scan. The biconditionals that tie this count to *field presence* — whether a projection of `fields(t)` equals `ε` — live in T4b's Postconditions, not in T4c: T4c defines the four hierarchical *level labels* (*node, user, document, element address*) by zero count on the T4-valid subdomain, and its Postcondition explicitly disclaims the field-presence reading ("any independent characterisation of the levels — for example, *user address* as 'the tumbler has exactly `N(t)` and `U(t)` non-empty in T4b's `fields(t)` decomposition' — is not the content of T4c"). The field-presence biconditionals we need for cases (b), (c), (d) are therefore sourced from T4b's absence-pattern postcondition, which fixes, for every T4-valid tumbler: `N(t) ≠ ε` unconditionally (the node field is always present — `zeros(t) = 0` still has `N(t) = (t₁, ..., t_{#t})` non-empty); `U(t) ≠ ε` iff `zeros(t) ≥ 1`; `D(t) ≠ ε` iff `zeros(t) ≥ 2`; `E(t) ≠ ε` iff `zeros(t) = 3`. Each presence check thus reduces to a comparison of `zeros(t)` against a fixed natural-number threshold, decidable in finitely many steps.

*Ingredient 3 (finite-sequence equality).* Two finite sequences of natural numbers `S = (s₁, ..., sₘ)` and `R = (r₁, ..., rₙ)` are equal iff `m = n` and `(A i : 1 ≤ i ≤ m : sᵢ = rᵢ)`. This check requires one length comparison and at most `m` equality tests on natural numbers, each decidable. The procedure terminates in at most `m + 1` steps. We call this *componentwise comparison* and use it in every case below.

With these three ingredients, each case proceeds by extraction, presence check (where needed), and componentwise comparison.

*(a) Same node field.* Extract `N(a)` and `N(b)` via `fields` (Ingredient 1). Every tumbler has a node field (Ingredient 2), so both `N(a)` and `N(b)` are present. Apply componentwise comparison (Ingredient 3): check `#N(a) = #N(b)` and, if so, `(A i : 1 ≤ i ≤ #N(a) : N(a)ᵢ = N(b)ᵢ)`. This requires at most `#N(a) + 1` comparisons, each decidable. The procedure terminates and returns *yes* iff the node fields are identical.

*(b) Shared presence of matching user fields alongside matching node fields.* Extract `N(a), U(a)` and `N(b), U(b)` via `fields` (Ingredient 1). The preamble asks for *shared presence* of a user field of matching value — both tumblers must contribute a present user field, and those present fields must agree componentwise — so the procedure decides presence before value. Both `a` and `b` must possess user fields — that is, `zeros(a) ≥ 1` and `zeros(b) ≥ 1` — which is decidable (Ingredient 2). Two distinct failure modes return *no*. *Asymmetric absence*, where exactly one tumbler lacks a user field, leaves no common present field to compare — the side whose `U`-projection is `ε` contributes only the absence marker, not a field, so the preamble's shared-presence demand cannot be met. *Symmetric absence*, where neither tumbler carries a user field, forces `U(a) = U(b) = ε` by T4b's absence-marker clause — the two projections agree as symbols, but the preamble has asked for a *present* shared user field, and the symbol-level equality of two absence markers does not furnish one; consequently the answer is again *no*. When both presence thresholds hold, apply componentwise comparison (Ingredient 3) to each pair: first `N(a) = N(b)`, then `U(a) = U(b)` — checking `#U(a) = #U(b)` and `(A j : 1 ≤ j ≤ #U(a) : U(a)ⱼ = U(b)ⱼ)`. The procedure terminates and returns *yes* iff both tumblers carry user fields and both pairs are componentwise identical.

*(c) Shared presence of matching node, user, and document fields.* This case asks for *exact equality of the full document projection under shared presence of both document projections* — `zeros(a) ≥ 2 ∧ zeros(b) ≥ 2` together with `D(a) = D(b)` componentwise — and is terminologically disjoint from the prefix relation of (d): (c) establishes the symmetric relation `D(a) = D(b)` under shared presence, whereas (d) establishes the asymmetric relation `D(a) ⪯ D(b)` under shared presence; `D(a) = D(b)` implies `D(a) ⪯ D(b)` but not conversely. Extract `N(a), U(a), D(a)` and `N(b), U(b), D(b)` via `fields` (Ingredient 1). Both must possess document fields — `zeros(a) ≥ 2` and `zeros(b) ≥ 2` — decidable by Ingredient 2. Two distinct failure modes return *no*. *Asymmetric absence*, where exactly one tumbler lacks a document field, leaves no common present document field to compare — the side whose `D`-projection is `ε` contributes only the absence marker. *Symmetric absence*, where neither tumbler carries a document field, forces `D(a) = D(b) = ε` by T4b's absence-marker clause — two projections equal as symbols, but the preamble has asked for a *present* shared document field, and the symbol-level equality of two absence markers does not furnish one; consequently the answer is again *no*. When both presence thresholds hold, apply componentwise comparison (Ingredient 3) to each of the three pairs: `N(a) = N(b)`, `U(a) = U(b)`, `D(a) = D(b)`. The total number of comparisons is bounded by the sum of the field lengths plus three length checks, all finite. The procedure terminates and returns *yes* iff both tumblers carry document fields and all three pairs are componentwise identical.

*(d) Document-field prefix within a shared document family.* The preamble positions this case as "structural subordination within a document family, strictly weaker than the exact equality of (c)" — *strictly weaker* being understood in the logical sense of (c)'s conjuncts relaxed along a single coordinate, which in our three-field decomposition means carrying `N(a) = N(b) ∧ U(a) = U(b)` forward from (c) as the structural prerequisites of the "document family" and replacing the D-field clause `D(a) = D(b)` with the asymmetric prefix relation `D(a) ⪯ D(b)`; dropping the N and U conjuncts would produce a claim incomparable with (c) rather than strictly weaker, and would sever the "document family" framing from the rest of this ASN, where a document family is precisely the sub-allocation tree under a given (node, user) pair. Nelson's "shared prefix means shared containing scope" and Gregory's `tumbleraccounteq` — which truncates the candidate to the parent length and checks for exact match across node, user, *and* document components together — both pivot on the same N, U agreement, so carrying those conjuncts forward is also what aligns (d) with the operational reading in the closing commentary. Extract `N(a), U(a), D(a) = (D₁ᵃ, ..., Dᵧₐᵃ)` and `N(b), U(b), D(b) = (D₁ᵇ, ..., Dᵧᵦᵇ)` via `fields` (Ingredient 1). Both tumblers must possess document fields — `zeros(a) ≥ 2` and `zeros(b) ≥ 2`, decidable as in (c); and by T4b's absence-pattern postcondition `zeros(t) ≥ 2` entails `N(t) ≠ ε` and `U(t) ≠ ε`, so once both D-presence thresholds hold, all four of `N(a), U(a), N(b), U(b)` are automatically present and non-empty, and no separate presence check on them is required. Two distinct failure modes on D-presence return *no*. *Asymmetric absence*, where exactly one tumbler lacks a document field, leaves one side without a present document field to form or consume a prefix — the preamble's shared-presence demand is unmet. *Symmetric absence*, where neither tumbler carries a document field, collapses both projections to `ε`, and although `ε ⪯ ε` holds at the symbol level under T4b's absence-marker clause, the preamble has asked for a prefix relation between *present* document fields within a shared document family rather than between two absence markers outside any document family; consequently the answer is again *no*. When both D-presence thresholds hold, apply componentwise comparison (Ingredient 3) first to the two framing pairs: `N(a) = N(b)`, then `U(a) = U(b)`. If either fails, return *no* — two tumblers from different node/user combinations cannot lie within the same document family regardless of how their D-projections relate, and the D-prefix relation in isolation would not witness the "structural subordination" the preamble asks for. When both framing checks succeed, the sequence `D(a)` is a prefix of `D(b)` iff two conditions hold: `γₐ ≤ γᵦ` and `(A k : 1 ≤ k ≤ γₐ : Dₖᵃ = Dₖᵇ)`. Check the length condition first (one comparison of natural numbers); if it fails, return *no*. Otherwise verify componentwise agreement up to position `γₐ` (at most `γₐ` comparisons, each decidable). The total cost is bounded by `#N(a) + #U(a) + γₐ` componentwise comparisons plus three length checks, all finite. The procedure terminates and returns *yes* iff both tumblers carry document fields, `N(a) = N(b)`, `U(a) = U(b)`, and `D(a)` is a prefix of `D(b)`.

In every case the procedure examines only the finite sequence of components in `a` and `b`, performs finitely many equality or ordering tests on natural numbers, and terminates. No mapping tables, version graphs, or system state are consulted — the tumbler representation alone suffices. ∎

*Formal Contract:*
- *Preconditions:* `a, b ∈ T` are valid tumblers satisfying T4 (at most three zeros, no adjacent zeros, no leading or trailing zero) and satisfying T3 (CanonicalRepresentation) — the component sequences of `a` and `b` are each fixed by sequence identity, with no alternative encoding yielding different component values, so that the scan for zero positions carried out in Ingredient 1 below reads a unique separator set at each argument and `fields(a)`, `fields(b)` are therefore uniquely determined as well as defined. Positivity of non-zero components is supplied by T0's carrier ℕ.
- *Depends:* T0 (CarrierSetDefinition) — invoked at Ingredient 1 ("by T0, every component lies in ℕ, so every non-zero component is strictly positive") and again in the precondition note that T0's carrier ℕ supplies positivity rather than a separate T4 clause; T0 licenses the strictly-positive-non-zero claim used to distinguish field components from separator components throughout. T3 (CanonicalRepresentation) — invoked at Ingredient 1 to discharge T4b's per-tumbler T3 precondition at both arguments `a` and `b` ("T4b's own contract lists T3 (CanonicalRepresentation) as a per-tumbler precondition, because the uniqueness half of T4b's conclusion — that `fields(t)` is not just defined but *uniquely determined* by `t` — rests on T3 … T3 fixes their component sequences so that `fields(a)` and `fields(b)` are each a *unique* value on that subdomain"), and again in the Preconditions clause that carries T3 through alongside T4 so the chain T6 → T4b → T3 is unbroken at each invocation of `fields`; without T3, T4b would deliver definedness of `fields(a)` and `fields(b)` but not uniqueness, and the componentwise comparisons of cases (a)–(d) would be comparisons against projections whose values were not pinned down by the tumbler representation alone. T4 (HierarchicalParsing) — invoked at Ingredient 1 to fix the zero-count bound, the no-adjacent-zeros clause, and the boundary clauses ("By T4, valid address tumblers have at most three zero-valued components, no two zeros are adjacent, and the tumbler neither begins nor ends with zero"), and again at the role-assignment claim ("which, by T4's definitional role-assignment, are exactly the field separators"); T4 supplies the structural conditions on which all four decision procedures rely. T4a (SyntacticEquivalence) — invoked at Ingredient 1 to license the unambiguity of the ε-marker for absent fields ("absent fields represented by the empty sequence `ε` (unambiguous because, by T4a, every *present* field segment is non-empty)"); T4a converts T4's positional clauses into the segment-non-emptiness statement that justifies treating ε as a definite absence indicator. T4b (UniqueParse) — invoked at Ingredient 1 to fix `fields : T ⇀ Seq(ℕ⁺) × Seq(ℕ⁺) × Seq(ℕ⁺) × Seq(ℕ⁺)` as the partial function whose domain is exactly the T4-valid subset of `T` ("The partial function `fields : T ⇀ Seq(ℕ⁺) × Seq(ℕ⁺) × Seq(ℕ⁺) × Seq(ℕ⁺)` introduced in T4b … T4b fixes the domain of `fields` as exactly the T4-valid subset of `T`") and to fix `fields(t) = (N(t), U(t), D(t), E(t))` as well-defined and uniquely determined on that subdomain ("By T4(b), `fields(t) = (N(t), U(t), D(t), E(t))` is well-defined and uniquely determined by `t` alone on this subdomain"); invoked again at Ingredient 2 for the *absence-pattern postcondition* that ties field presence to zero count — `N(t) ≠ ε` unconditionally, `U(t) ≠ ε` iff `zeros(t) ≥ 1`, `D(t) ≠ ε` iff `zeros(t) ≥ 2`, `E(t) ≠ ε` iff `zeros(t) = 3` (stated as the case analysis "`zeros(t) = 0` → only `N(t)` non-empty; `zeros(t) = 1` → `N(t), U(t)` non-empty, `D(t) = E(t) = ε`; `zeros(t) = 2` → `N(t), U(t), D(t)` non-empty, `E(t) = ε`; `zeros(t) = 3` → all four non-empty" in T4b's Postconditions) — which supplies the presence-check thresholds (`zeros(a) ≥ 1`, `zeros(a) ≥ 2`) consumed by cases (b), (c), (d); and once more in the closing post-condition referencing `fields(t)` (T4(b)); T4b therefore supplies both the partial-function signature / field-extraction action of Ingredient 1 and the field-presence biconditionals of Ingredient 2, with T6's precondition that `a, b` satisfy T4 discharging the domain restriction so that `fields(a)`, `fields(b)`, and each presence-threshold check are well-defined throughout cases (a)–(d).
- *Postconditions:* (a) The procedure terminates and returns YES iff `N(a) = N(b)` (componentwise). (b) The procedure terminates and returns YES iff `zeros(a) ≥ 1 ∧ zeros(b) ≥ 1 ∧ N(a) = N(b) ∧ U(a) = U(b)`; returns NO whenever at least one of `a, b` lacks a user field — covering both *asymmetric absence* (`zeros(a) ≥ 1 ⊕ zeros(b) ≥ 1`) and *symmetric absence* (`zeros(a) = 0 ∧ zeros(b) = 0`), the latter case refusing to equate the two absence markers `U(a) = U(b) = ε` (supplied by T4b) with a present shared user field. (c) The procedure terminates and returns YES iff `zeros(a) ≥ 2 ∧ zeros(b) ≥ 2 ∧ N(a) = N(b) ∧ U(a) = U(b) ∧ D(a) = D(b)`; returns NO whenever at least one of `a, b` lacks a document field — covering both asymmetric absence and the symmetric case `zeros(a) ≤ 1 ∧ zeros(b) ≤ 1`, where the symbol-level equality `D(a) = D(b) = ε` does not count as a present shared document field. (d) The procedure terminates and returns YES iff `zeros(a) ≥ 2 ∧ zeros(b) ≥ 2 ∧ N(a) = N(b) ∧ U(a) = U(b) ∧ #D(a) ≤ #D(b) ∧ (A k : 1 ≤ k ≤ #D(a) : D(a)ₖ = D(b)ₖ)`; returns NO whenever at least one of `a, b` lacks a document field — covering both asymmetric absence and the symmetric case `zeros(a) ≤ 1 ∧ zeros(b) ≤ 1`, where although `ε ⪯ ε` holds at the symbol level the preamble refuses to read this as a prefix relation between present document fields within a shared document family — and also returns NO whenever both document fields are present but `N(a) ≠ N(b)` or `U(a) ≠ U(b)`, because different node/user combinations cannot lie within the same document family regardless of how their D-projections relate, so the D-prefix check in isolation would not witness the "structural subordination" that the preamble and the closing commentary's `tumbleraccounteq` identification both demand. All decisions use only the tumbler representations of `a` and `b`, via `fields(t)` (T4(b)) and componentwise comparison on finite sequences of natural numbers.

T6 is a corollary of the T4-family (T4, T4a, T4b) together with T3 and T0, rather than of T4 alone: the proof consumes T4's zero-count and adjacency constraints at Ingredient 1, T4a's unambiguity of the ε-marker for absent fields at Ingredient 1, and T4b's partial function `fields` together with its absence-pattern postcondition at Ingredients 1 and 2; T3 discharges T4b's per-tumbler canonical-representation precondition at each of `a` and `b`, supplying the uniqueness half of `fields(a)` and `fields(b)` that the componentwise comparisons below rely on; T0 additionally supplies the carrier ℕ from which the strictly-positive subcarrier ℕ⁺ used by the projections is carved. Beyond these dependencies the proof adds only field extraction, presence checks, and componentwise comparison on finite sequences, each decidable in finitely many steps. The hierarchical level-label vocabulary of *node*, *user*, *document*, and *element address* — introduced by T4c on the T4-valid subdomain — is exposition around T6 only: the four cases are stated and proved in terms of T4b's field projections `N, U, D, E` rather than in terms of level labels, and neither the proof body nor any formal contract clause cites T4c. T4c is therefore framing, not a load-bearing dependency, and is deliberately absent from T6's declared depends. We state T6 separately because the decidability claim is load-bearing for decentralized operation, but it introduces no independent content beyond what T0, T3, T4, T4a, and T4b jointly supply.

We must note what T6(d) does NOT capture. The document field records the *allocation hierarchy* — who baptised which sub-number — not the *derivation history*. Version `5.3` was allocated under document `5`, but this tells us nothing about which version's content was copied to create `5.3`. Nelson: "the version, or subdocument number is only an accidental extension of the document number, and strictly implies no specific relationship of derivation." Formal version-derivation requires the version graph, not just the address.

Nelson confirms that shared prefix means shared containing scope: "The owner of a given item controls the allocation of the numbers under it." The prefix IS the path from root to common ancestor. But he cautions: "Tumblers do not affect the user-level structure of the documents; they only provide a mapping mechanism, and impose no categorization and no structure on the contents of a document." Shared prefix guarantees containment and ownership, never semantic categorization.

Gregory's implementation confirms the distinction between ordering and containment. The codebase provides both `tumblercmp` (total order comparison) and `tumbleraccounteq` (prefix-matching predicate with zero-as-wildcard semantics). The latter truncates the candidate to the length of the parent and checks for exact match — this is the operational realization of T6, and it is a genuinely different algorithm from the ordering comparison.
