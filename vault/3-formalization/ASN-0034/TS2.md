**TS2 (ShiftInjectivity).**

`(A v₁, v₂, n : n ≥ 1 ∧ #v₁ = #v₂ = m : shift(v₁, n) = shift(v₂, n) ⟹ v₁ = v₂)`

*Proof.* Fix v₁, v₂ ∈ T with #v₁ = #v₂ = m, and fix n ≥ 1. Assume shift(v₁, n) = shift(v₂, n). Since v₁, v₂ ∈ T, T0 (CarrierSetDefinition) gives m = #v₁ = #v₂ ≥ 1, so no empty-sequence case arises.

By OrdinalShift, the assumption rewrites as v₁ ⊕ δ(n, m) = v₂ ⊕ δ(n, m). We apply TA-MTO (ManyToOne) with w = δ(n, m), a = v₁, b = v₂, verifying its six preconditions one-for-one in the order TA-MTO's contract states them — `w ∈ T, Pos(w), a ∈ T, b ∈ T, #a ≥ actionPoint(w), #b ≥ actionPoint(w)` — with each of the three membership preconditions and each of the two length bounds discharged separately rather than fused into a composite item:

(i) δ(n, m) ∈ T — by OrdinalDisplacement's exported postcondition `δ(n, m) ∈ T`, discharging TA-MTO's first precondition `w ∈ T`.

(ii) Pos(δ(n, m)) — by OrdinalDisplacement's exported postcondition `Pos(δ(n, m))`. TA-MTO's second precondition `Pos(w)` is thereby discharged one-for-one under its actual name rather than under an order comparison `δ(n, m) > 0`: the notation `>` in this ASN denotes T1's lexicographic ordering on equal-typed tumblers, not a scalar comparison, and no distinguished zero-tumbler is in scope to stand on the right-hand side (T0 stipulates length ≥ 1 with components in ℕ but names no such tumbler). The predicate `Pos(·)` is TA-Pos's defining existential `(E i : tᵢ ≠ 0)`, witnessed at `i = m` inside OrdinalDisplacement's proof through the `n ≥ 1 ⟹ n ≠ 0` promotion and exported as a named postcondition so TS2 cites the exported predicate directly rather than re-deriving from δ's component pattern inline, matching the routing sister call-site OrdinalShift applies at its analogous TA0-precondition-three consumption site.

(iii) v₁ ∈ T — given by hypothesis (this is one of TS2's own preconditions), discharging TA-MTO's third precondition `a ∈ T` one-for-one by direct transfer without routing through any intermediate foundation. The three membership preconditions of TA-MTO — its first, third, and fourth conjuncts `w ∈ T`, `a ∈ T`, `b ∈ T` — are thereby each discharged to a named source (OrdinalDisplacement, hypothesis, hypothesis) rather than fused into a single "all operands well-typed" item, matching the per-step discharge discipline sister call-site OrdinalShift applies at its four TA0 preconditions — where OrdinalShift's own `v ∈ T` check is likewise discharged "by assumption" as a direct transfer from its own precondition.

(iv) v₂ ∈ T — given by hypothesis (this is one of TS2's own preconditions), discharging TA-MTO's fourth precondition `b ∈ T` one-for-one by direct transfer without routing through any intermediate foundation.

(v) #v₁ ≥ actionPoint(δ(n, m)) — the action point of δ(n, m) is m (OrdinalDisplacement), and #v₁ = m by hypothesis, so m ≥ m holds.

(vi) #v₂ ≥ actionPoint(δ(n, m)) — the action point of δ(n, m) is m (OrdinalDisplacement), and #v₂ = m by the hypothesis #v₁ = #v₂ composed with #v₁ = m, so m ≥ m holds.

All preconditions are satisfied. TA-MTO's converse direction gives v₁ᵢ = v₂ᵢ for all 1 ≤ i ≤ actionPoint(δ(n, m)) = m. Since #v₁ = #v₂ = m and all m components agree, T3 (CanonicalRepresentation) gives v₁ = v₂. ∎
