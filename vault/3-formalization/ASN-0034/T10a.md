**T10a (AllocatorDiscipline).** The root allocator begins from a base address satisfying T4. Each allocator produces its sibling outputs exclusively by repeated application of `inc(В·, 0)` вҖ” shallow increment at the last significant position. To spawn a child allocator, the parent performs one `inc(В·, k')` with `k' вҲҲ {1, 2}`, subject to the runtime precondition вҖ” imposed by the discipline and checked before each spawn вҖ” that TA5a's zero-count bound holds on the chosen domain element `t`: `zeros(t) вүӨ 2` when `k' = 2`, where `zeros(В·)` is the zero-count function defined in T4. No runtime zero-count bound is imposed at `k' = 1`: TA5a establishes that `inc(В·, 1)` preserves T4 unconditionally on any T4-valid `t` (the `+1` appended component introduces no zero, so `zeros(inc(t, 1)) = zeros(t)`, and T4's `zeros(t) вүӨ 3` clause on `t` вҖ” supplied by the T4-validity that T10a.4 guarantees at every parent domain element вҖ” carries through unchanged), so the preservation envelope at `k' = 1` is already closed by T4-validity of `t` and no further discipline-level bound is required. The axiom borrows `zeros(В·)` from T4 as a precondition symbol whose meaning the axiom itself relies on to be well-formed, and from TA5a it adopts the preservation bound `zeros(t) вүӨ 2` (for `k' = 2`) as the sole runtime precondition on child-spawning; a `k' = 2` spawning event failing this bound is not admissible under the discipline. The `inc(В·, k')` thus chosen establishes the child's prefix, then further allocation is delegated to the child. Each pair `(t, k')` вҖ” a domain element `t` of the parent together with a spawning parameter `k' вҲҲ {1, 2}` вҖ” produces at most one child-spawning event; the parent may not invoke `inc(t, k')` for the same `t` and `k'` in two distinct spawning operations. The parent's own sibling stream resumes with `inc(В·, 0)`.

T10a constrains what would otherwise be an unregulated choice. Without it, an allocator could intermix shallow and deep increments, producing outputs of varying lengths whose prefix relationships would be uncontrolled. The child-spawning operation вҖ” a single deep increment with `k' вҲҲ {1, 2}` вҖ” establishes a new prefix at a deeper level, from which a new allocator continues with its own `inc(В·, 0)` stream. The restriction to `k' вҲҲ {1, 2}`, combined with TA5a's `zeros(t) вүӨ 2` bound at `k' = 2` (and TA5a's unconditional T4-preservation at `k' = 1` on any T4-valid `t`), ensures that child-spawning preserves T4; unrestricted `k' вүҘ 3` would introduce adjacent zeros, violating T4's field-segment constraint (TA5a, case `k вүҘ 3`).

*Definitions.* The consequences below quantify over distinct allocators, ancestor-descendant pairs, and parent-child relationships, so before giving an allocator's observable output we must first say what an allocator *is* as an object, when two allocators are considered equal, and how the tree relations are derived. We formalize allocators as nodes of a tree induced by the axiom's spawning rule, then build the per-allocator domain and the `same_allocator` predicate on top of that structure.

*Allocator tree.* The *allocator tree* рқ’Ҝ consists of a distinguished *root allocator* together with every allocator reachable by finite iteration of the child-spawning rule. Each non-root allocator `A` carries a *spawning triple* `(parent(A), spawnPt(A), spawnParam(A))`, where `parent(A) вҲҲ рқ’Ҝ` is the allocator that spawned `A`, `spawnPt(A) вҲҲ dom(parent(A))` is the parent domain element from which `A` was spawned, and `spawnParam(A) вҲҲ {1, 2}` is the spawning parameter. `A`'s *base address* is then `inc(spawnPt(A), spawnParam(A))`, determined from the triple by TA5, with `A`'s subsequent outputs produced by iterated `inc(В·, 0)`. The root carries no spawning triple; its base address is the T4-valid address fixed by the initialization constraint stated in the axiom. The tree is defined by induction on depth: level 0 is the singleton `{root}`, and level `d + 1` consists of every allocator whose spawning triple's parent lies at level `d` вҖ” a well-founded construction because `dom(parent(A))` is already defined at the earlier level.

*Identity and distinctness.* Two allocators `X` and `Y` are *equal* вҖ” written `X = Y` вҖ” iff either both are the root, or both are non-root with `parent(X) = parent(Y)` (allocator equality at lesser depth, defined inductively), `spawnPt(X) = spawnPt(Y)` (equality of tumblers), and `spawnParam(X) = spawnParam(Y)` (equality of spawning parameters). Equivalently, `X = Y` iff the finite sequence of spawning triples on the root-path to each is identical. Two allocators are *distinct* iff `X вү  Y`. The axiom's at-most-once child-spawning constraint вҖ” "each pair `(t, k')` yields at most one child-spawning event" at a given parent вҖ” is the dynamic counterpart of this identity criterion: it forbids a single parent from producing two distinct children that share the same `(t, k')`, so the spawning-triple identification over рқ’Ҝ does not over-count children of any parent. Under this identity criterion, "distinct allocators" in T10a.6 and GlobalUniqueness, and "allocators not in an ancestor-descendant relationship" in T10a.5, each denote well-defined relations on рқ’Ҝ.

*Derived tree relations.* `Y` is a *child* of `X` iff `parent(Y) = X`. The *depth* function `depth : рқ’Ҝ вҶ’ в„•` is defined by `depth(root) = 0` and `depth(Y) = depth(parent(Y)) + 1` for non-root `Y`; the finite length of every root-path makes this well-defined. `X` is an *ancestor* of `Y` iff `X = Y` or `X` is an ancestor of `parent(Y)` вҖ” equivalently, iff a finite chain `X = AвӮҖ, AвӮҒ, вҖҰ, AвӮҷ = Y` exists with `parent(Aбөў) = AбөўвӮӢвӮҒ` for `1 вүӨ i вүӨ n`. `Y` is a *descendant* of `X` iff `X` is an ancestor of `Y`. `X` and `Y` are *in an ancestor-descendant relationship* iff one is an ancestor of the other; otherwise they are *not in an ancestor-descendant relationship*. These derived relations fix the denotation of "parent allocator", "param-1 child", and "param-2 child" used in PartitionMonotonicity: a param-1 child of `X` is a child `C` of `X` with `spawnParam(C) = 1`, and a param-2 child is one with `spawnParam(C) = 2`. The prefix *param-* names the spawn parameter and is disjoint from the tree-level `depth` function defined above: the depth of any child `C` of `X` is `depth(X) + 1` regardless of whether `C` is a param-1 or a param-2 child.

*Domain.* For each allocator `A` with base address `tвӮҖ` вҖ” the root's T4-valid initialization when `A` is the root, or `tвӮҖ = inc(spawnPt(A), spawnParam(A))` when `A` is non-root вҖ” its *domain* is the set

  `dom(A) = {tвӮҷ : n вүҘ 0}` where `tвӮҷвӮҠвӮҒ = inc(tвӮҷ, 0)`,

the base address together with every iterated sibling output; the axiom's restriction of sibling production to `inc(В·, 0)` makes this enumeration well-defined, and `inc` itself is supplied by TA5. Child-spawning outputs are excluded from the parent's domain by construction: when `A` performs `inc(t, k')` with `k' > 0`, the resulting address `cвӮҖ = inc(t, k')` is not an element of `dom(A)` but serves as the base of the child allocator `C` with `parent(C) = A`, `spawnPt(C) = t`, `spawnParam(C) = k'`, becoming the initial element of `dom(C)`. We further define the predicate

  `same_allocator(a, b) вүЎ вҲғA вҲҲ рқ’Ҝ : a вҲҲ dom(A) вҲ§ b вҲҲ dom(A)`,

asserting that two addresses both lie in the domain of a single allocator; the existential quantifier now ranges over the well-defined tree рқ’Ҝ. All definitions are well-founded relative to T10a's own dependencies: the tree is induced inductively by the axiom's spawning rule, the domain construction uses `inc` from TA5, and the base-address and sibling-production restrictions are supplied by the axiom itself.

*Justification.* T10a is a design axiom вҖ” it constrains allocator behavior rather than following from prior properties. The consequences below rest on eight foundations: TA5, which defines `inc` and supplies the length postconditions `#inc(t, 0) = #t` (TA5(c)) and `#inc(t, k) = #t + k` for `k > 0` (TA5(d)), together with the strict monotonicity `inc(t, 0) > t` (TA5(a)); TA5a (IncrementPreservesT4), which establishes that `inc(t, k)` preserves T4 iff `k вҲҲ {0, 1}`, or `k = 2 вҲ§ zeros(t) вүӨ 2`, and fails for `k вүҘ 3` вҖ” the `k = 1` branch is unconditional on any T4-valid `t` because `inc(В·, 1)` appends a non-zero component and introduces no separator zero, so `zeros(inc(t, 1)) = zeros(t)` and T4's `zeros(t) вүӨ 3` clause on `t` closes the preservation envelope without a further runtime bound; TA5-SigValid (SigOnValidAddresses), which fixes `sig(t) = #t` for every T4-valid tumbler and thereby identifies the terminal component as the single position advanced by `inc(В·, 0)`, feeding the inductive step of Consequence 5 (where the divergence position is propagated by distinguishing interior from sig-modified positions) and the zero-count preservation argument of Consequence 8 (where the advanced position is located at the non-zero terminal component); T4 (HierarchicalParsing), whose field-segment constraint is the invariant that the discipline must preserve; T3 (CanonicalRepresentation), whose component-wise equality criterion, read contrapositively, guarantees that two distinct tumblers of equal length differ at some position вҖ” the divergence supplying the branching-point argument in the base case of Consequence 5 when the lowest common ancestor spawns its two differing children from distinct parent tumblers; T10 (PartitionIndependence), whose precondition вҖ” non-nesting prefixes вҖ” is the guarantee that T10a's discipline establishes for all non-ancestor-descendant allocator pairs (ancestor-descendant pairs inherently nest, as shown in Consequence 5); Prefix (PrefixRelation), from which it follows that tumblers of equal length are prefix-related only if identical, and whose definition underwrites the necessity argument (T10a-N); and T1 (LexicographicOrder), whose irreflexivity (T1 (a)) and transitivity (T1 (c)) lift the per-step monotonicity of `inc(В·, 0)` to the strict increase of the entire enumeration chain (Consequence 7). We justify the constraint by establishing seven consequences on which the coordination-free uniqueness guarantees depend, then proving that the sibling restriction is necessary for prefix-incomparability.

**Consequence 1: Uniform sibling length.** Since T10a restricts sibling production to `inc(В·, 0)`, and TA5(c) guarantees `#inc(t, 0) = #t`, every sibling output has the same length as the allocator's base address (T10a.1).

**Consequence 2: Non-nesting sibling prefixes.** By T10a.1, all siblings have equal length; tumblers of equal length are prefix-related only if identical (Prefix), so distinct siblings are prefix-incomparable (T10a.2).

**Consequence 3: Length separation between parent and child domains.** When the parent performs `inc(В·, k')` with `k' вҲҲ {1, 2}`, TA5(d) gives `#inc(t, k') = #t + k'`, so the child's base address has length strictly exceeding any parent sibling. All child siblings then preserve this length by T10a.1, and across `d` nesting levels the separation is additive (T10a.3).

**Consequence 4: T4 preservation under the discipline.** The proof proceeds by induction on the allocator tree. *Base:* the root allocator's base address satisfies T4 by the initialization constraint. *Step:* given an allocator whose base address satisfies T4, TA5a guarantees that `inc(В·, 0)` and `inc(В·, 1)` unconditionally preserve T4 on any T4-valid input (cases `k = 0` and `k = 1`), and the axiom restricts child-spawning at `k' = 2` to inputs satisfying TA5a's `zeros(t) вүӨ 2` bound; the inductive hypothesis supplies T4-validity of every `t вҲҲ dom` of the parent allocator, closing the premise of the `k' вҲҲ {0, 1}` branches and supplying the T4-validity under which the explicit `zeros(t) вүӨ 2` runtime check at `k' = 2` lands inside TA5a's preservation envelope. Every child-spawning output `inc(t, k')` therefore satisfies T4 вҖ” and this output serves as the child allocator's base, closing the induction. Every output of a conforming allocator satisfies T4. Properties T4aвҖ“T4c and all downstream consumers of T4 are guaranteed their precondition by the discipline itself (T10a.4).

**Consequence 5: Cross-allocator prefix-incomparability.** Consequences 1вҖ“2 guarantee non-nesting among siblings of a single allocator; Consequence 3 separates parent from child by length. For ancestor-descendant allocator pairs, prefix-nesting holds by construction: when a parent spawns a child from output `tбөў` via `inc(tбөў, k')` with `k' > 0`, TA5(b) gives agreement on all positions `1..#tбөў` and TA5(d) gives `#inc(tбөў, k') > #tбөў`, so `tбөў вүј inc(tбөў, k')` вҖ” the spawning output is a proper prefix of the child's base address, and positional inheritance propagates this through the child's entire subtree. T10's non-nesting precondition therefore does not hold for such pairs, nor is it needed: their outputs have distinct lengths by T10a.3 and hence are distinct. For the remaining case вҖ” allocators not in an ancestor-descendant relationship вҖ” T10's precondition must be established. We prove that every such pair produces prefix-incomparable outputs (T10a.5).

Let X and Y be allocators not in an ancestor-descendant relationship. They share a lowest common ancestor P in the allocator tree; let CвӮ“ be the child of P on the path to X and Cбө§ the child on the path to Y. Were CвӮ“ = Cбө§, that child would be a lower common ancestor, contradicting minimality, so CвӮ“ вү  Cбө§. Write m for the common length of P's domain elements (T10a.1). P spawns CвӮ“ from `(tвӮ“, k'вӮ“)` and Cбө§ from `(tбө§, k'бө§)` with `k'вӮ“, k'бө§ вҲҲ {1, 2}`, and the at-most-once constraint requires distinct spawning pairs: `(tвӮ“, k'вӮ“) вү  (tбө§, k'бө§)`. Let `bвӮ“ = inc(tвӮ“, k'вӮ“)` and `bбө§ = inc(tбө§, k'бө§)`.

*Base case: the branching point produces a divergence position.* Since `(tвӮ“, k'вӮ“) вү  (tбө§, k'бө§)`, either `tвӮ“ вү  tбө§` or `k'вӮ“ вү  k'бө§`. If `tвӮ“ вү  tбө§`: both are domain elements of P with `#tвӮ“ = #tбө§ = m` (T10a.1), so by T3 they differ at some position `j вүӨ m`; TA5(b) for `k > 0` preserves these positions, giving `bвӮ“[j] = tвӮ“[j] вү  tбө§[j] = bбө§[j]`, with `j < #bвӮ“` and `j < #bбө§`. If `tвӮ“ = tбө§` and `k'вӮ“ вү  k'бө§`: one of `{k'вӮ“, k'бө§}` is 1 and the other 2; take `k'вӮ“ = 1` and `k'бө§ = 2` without loss of generality. TA5(d) places a 1 at position `m + 1` of `bвӮ“` (the single appended position) and a 0 at position `m + 1` of `bбө§` (the zero-valued separator preceding the final 1), so `j = m + 1` with `bвӮ“[j] = 1 вү  0 = bбө§[j]` and `j = min(#bвӮ“, #bбө§)`. In both cases a position `j вүӨ min(#bвӮ“, #bбө§)` exists at which `bвӮ“[j] вү  bбө§[j]`.

*Inductive step: divergence propagates through the allocator tree.* We show by induction on depth that every output in the subtree rooted at CвӮ“ preserves the value `bвӮ“[j]` at position j вҖ” or, in the second case, preserves a strictly positive value there вҖ” and symmetrically for Cбө§. *Closure.* For any `x вҲҲ domain(X)` and `y вҲҲ domain(Y)`: X is CвӮ“ or a descendant of CвӮ“, so T10a.3 (local monotonicity) gives `#x вүҘ #bвӮ“` вҖ” with equality when X = CвӮ“, by T10a.1; symmetrically `#y вүҘ #bбө§`. Since `j вүӨ min(#bвӮ“, #bбө§)` (established in the base case), both `#x вүҘ j` and `#y вүҘ j`, and `x[j] вү  y[j]` by the inductive step. Were `x вүј y`, Prefix would require `x[i] = y[i]` for all `i вүӨ #x`, giving `x[j] = y[j]` вҖ” a contradiction. Symmetrically `y вӢ  x`. вҲҺ

**Consequence 6: Domain disjointness.** Consequences 1, 3, and 5 together imply that distinct allocators have disjoint domains. For an ancestorвҖ“descendant pair, T10a.1 gives uniform length within each domain and T10a.3 gives a strict length inequality between ancestor and descendant streams, so no element can lie in both. For a non-ancestorвҖ“descendant pair, T10a.5 gives prefix-incomparability of all outputs, contradicted by the trivial reflexivity `t вүј t` were `t` shared. The disjointness elevates `same_allocator(a, b)` from a bare existential to a witness-unique predicate: when `same_allocator(a, b)` holds, the witnessing allocator `A` with `a, b вҲҲ dom(A)` is uniquely determined by the pair `(a, b)` (T10a.6). Witness-uniqueness alone does not yet make the enumeration indices single-valued вҖ” a single allocator's chain `tвӮҖ, tвӮҒ, tвӮӮ, вҖҰ` could in principle revisit the same tumbler under two indices вҖ” so the well-definedness of any predicate phrased over those indices, `allocated_before(a, b)` in T9 in particular, requires a complementary within-allocator injectivity, supplied next.

**Consequence 7: Enumeration injectivity.** Within any single allocator's chain `tвӮҖ, tвӮҒ, tвӮӮ, вҖҰ`, the indexing map `n вҶҰ tвӮҷ` is injective: `m вү  n` implies `tвӮҳ вү  tвӮҷ`. By TA5(a), each step produces a strictly greater tumbler, `tвӮҷвӮҠвӮҒ = inc(tвӮҷ, 0) > tвӮҷ`; induction on the gap using T1 (c) transitivity lifts this per-step increase to `tвӮҳ < tвӮҷ` for every `m < n`; T1 (a) irreflexivity then excludes `tвӮҳ = tвӮҷ`, yielding distinctness. Combined with Consequence 6, this secures single-valuedness of the enumeration indices `i, j` with `a = tбөў, b = tвұј` as functions of the pair `(a, b)` whenever `same_allocator(a, b)` holds: the witnessing allocator is unique (Consequence 6), and within it the index assigned to each element is unique (Consequence 7). Any predicate phrased over those indices вҖ” `allocated_before(a, b)` in T9 вҖ” therefore receives an unambiguous interpretation (T10a.7).

**Consequence 8: Uniform sibling zero count.** Since T10a restricts sibling production to `inc(В·, 0)` and every output of a conforming allocator satisfies T4 (Consequence 4), TA5-SigValid fixes `sig(tвӮҷ) = #tвӮҷ` at every step; T4's field-segment constraint forces the terminal component non-zero, hence strictly positive (T0's carrier в„•). The `inc(В·, 0)` step then advances only that position (TA5(b), TA5(c)): the post-increment value `(tвӮҷ)_{sig(tвӮҷ)} + 1` remains strictly positive вҖ” NAT-addcompat's strict successor inequality `n < n + 1` for every `n вҲҲ в„•` gives `(tвӮҷ)_{sig(tвӮҷ)} + 1 > (tвӮҷ)_{sig(tвӮҷ)} вүҘ 0`, matching the per-step citation convention TA5 and T10a.8's proof already follow for this `+1` arithmetic вҖ” and TA5(c) preserves length, so no position enters or leaves the zero set. Every sibling output therefore shares the base address's zero count (T10a.8). This is the zero-count parallel of Consequence 1's length uniformity; together they supply the full *base address вҶ’ every sibling* extension of per-base-address TA5(d) bookkeeping on both length (Consequence 1) and zero count (Consequence 8) that cross-allocator reasoning consumes at the domain level rather than only at base addresses.

**Necessity.** Suppose the discipline is relaxed to permit any k вүҘ 0 in the sibling stream. Let an allocator with base address b produce aвӮҒ = inc(b, 0), then aвӮӮ = inc(aвӮҒ, k') with k' > 0 вҖ” both legitimate sibling outputs under the relaxed rule. By TA5(b), since k' > 0, aвӮӮ agrees with aвӮҒ on every position 1 вүӨ i вүӨ #aвӮҒ. By TA5(d), #aвӮӮ = #aвӮҒ + k'; the strict inequality `#aвӮҒ < #aвӮӮ` that the Prefix definition consumes is NAT-level rather than a consequence of TA5(d)'s equation alone. From `k' > 0` in в„• we have `1 вүӨ k'`; NAT-addcompat's left order-compatibility (`p вүӨ n вҹ№ m + p вүӨ m + n`), instantiated at `m = #aвӮҒ`, `p = 1`, `n = k'`, lifts this to `#aвӮҒ + 1 вүӨ #aвӮҒ + k'`, and NAT-addcompat's strict successor inequality `n < n + 1` (for every `n вҲҲ в„•`), instantiated at `n = #aвӮҒ`, gives `#aвӮҒ < #aвӮҒ + 1`; chaining these sharpens TA5(d)'s equation to the strict length inequality `#aвӮҒ < #aвӮҒ + k' = #aвӮӮ`, so aвӮӮ has components beyond position #aвӮҒ. These two facts вҖ” agreement on all positions of the shorter tumbler and strict length excess in the longer вҖ” are precisely the conditions of the Prefix definition: `aвӮҒ вүј aвӮӮ` with `#aвӮҒ < #aвӮӮ`, so `aвӮҒ вүә aвӮӮ`. The relaxed rule therefore admits siblings aвӮҒ вүә aвӮӮ, falsifying T10a.2 (NonNestingSiblingPrefixes) вҖ” the within-allocator claim that distinct siblings of one allocator are prefix-incomparable (T10a-N). T10a.2 is an internal integrity property of T10a's sibling stream and is distinct from T10's non-nesting precondition `pвӮҒ вӢ  pвӮӮ вҲ§ pвӮӮ вӢ  pвӮҒ`, which is applied at the domain prefixes of two distinct allocators (as in Case 3 of GlobalUniqueness) and is supplied for non-ancestor-descendant pairs by T10a.5 (CrossAllocatorIncomparability); co-siblings of one allocator are not such a pair, so the failure of T10a.2 is a failure of T10a's own within-allocator guarantee rather than a failure at T10's inputs. The sibling restriction вҖ” `k = 0` only вҖ” is thus necessary for within-allocator prefix-incomparability (T10a.2). The remaining components of the axiom serve distinct purposes not addressed by this argument: the child-spawning bound `k' вҲҲ {1, 2}` is necessary for T4 preservation (Consequence 4, via TA5a's failure for `k вүҘ 3`), the at-most-once child-spawning constraint ensures uniqueness of child base addresses (Consequence 5), and the root T4 initialization seeds the inductive argument of Consequence 4. The discipline is sufficient for prefix-incomparability across all non-ancestor-descendant allocator output domains (T10a.1, T10a.3вҖ“T10a.5); the necessity proof establishes that its sibling restriction is indispensable to T10a.2, the within-allocator half of that guarantee. вҲҺ

- *Definitions:*
  - *Allocator tree* `рқ’Ҝ`: the set of allocators induced by T10a. Consists of a distinguished root together with every allocator reachable by finite iteration of the child-spawning rule. Each non-root allocator `A` carries a *spawning triple* `(parent(A), spawnPt(A), spawnParam(A))` with `parent(A) вҲҲ рқ’Ҝ`, `spawnPt(A) вҲҲ dom(parent(A))`, and `spawnParam(A) вҲҲ {1, 2}`; A's base address is `inc(spawnPt(A), spawnParam(A))` by TA5. The root has no spawning triple; its base address is the T4-valid address fixed by the initialization constraint.
  - *Identity of allocators:* `X = Y` iff both are the root, or both are non-root with identical spawning triples вҖ” `parent(X) = parent(Y)`, `spawnPt(X) = spawnPt(Y)`, `spawnParam(X) = spawnParam(Y)`. Equivalently, `X = Y` iff their root-path sequences of spawning triples coincide. Distinctness is `X вү  Y`. The at-most-once child-spawning constraint ensures each `(parent, t, k')` names at most one allocator.
  - *Derived tree relations:* `Y` is a *child* of `X` iff `parent(Y) = X`. The depth function satisfies `depth(root) = 0` and `depth(Y) = depth(parent(Y)) + 1`. `X` is an *ancestor* of `Y` iff `X = Y` or `X` is an ancestor of `parent(Y)`; `Y` is a *descendant* of `X` iff `X` is an ancestor of `Y`. `X` and `Y` are *in an ancestor-descendant relationship* iff one is an ancestor of the other. A *param-1 child* of `X` is a child with `spawnParam = 1`; a *param-2 child* is one with `spawnParam = 2`. The *param-k* prefix names the spawn parameter and is distinct from the tree-level `depth` function; a param-1 child `C` and a param-2 child `C'` of the same parent both satisfy `depth(C) = depth(C') = depth(X) + 1`.
  - *Domain:* `dom(A) = {tвӮҷ : n вүҘ 0}` where `tвӮҖ` is `A`'s base address and `tвӮҷвӮҠвӮҒ = inc(tвӮҷ, 0)` вҖ” the `inc(В·, 0)` chain extending the base. Child-spawning outputs `inc(t, k')` with `k' > 0` are excluded from the parent's domain and serve as the base of the child allocator `C` (with `parent(C) = parent-allocator`, `spawnPt(C) = t`, `spawnParam(C) = k'`), becoming the initial element of `dom(C)`.
  - *Same allocator:* `same_allocator(a, b) вүЎ вҲғA вҲҲ рқ’Ҝ : a вҲҲ dom(A) вҲ§ b вҲҲ dom(A)` вҖ” the predicate asserting that `a` and `b` both lie in some allocator's domain, with the existential ranging over the well-defined tree рқ’Ҝ.
- *Axiom:* The root allocator's base address satisfies T4. Allocators produce sibling outputs exclusively by `inc(В·, 0)`; child-spawning uses exactly one `inc(В·, k')` with `k' вҲҲ {1, 2}`, subject to the runtime precondition вҖ” imposed by the discipline and checked before each spawn вҖ” that `zeros(t) вүӨ 2` when `k' = 2`, where `zeros(В·)` is the zero-count function defined in T4. The chosen `inc(В·, k')` establishes the child's prefix, after which the parent resumes sibling production with `inc(В·, 0)`.
