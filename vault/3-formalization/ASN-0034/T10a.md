**T10a (AllocatorDiscipline).** The root allocator begins from a base address satisfying T4. Each allocator produces its sibling outputs exclusively by repeated application of `inc(·, 0)` — shallow increment at the last significant position. To spawn a child allocator, the parent performs one `inc(·, k')` with `k' ∈ {1, 2}`, subject to the runtime precondition — imposed by the discipline and checked before each spawn — that TA5a's zero-count bound holds on the chosen domain element `t`: `zeros(t) ≤ 3` when `k' = 1`, `zeros(t) ≤ 2` when `k' = 2`, where `zeros(·)` is the zero-count function defined in TA5a (itself inherited from T4). The axiom borrows `zeros(·)` from TA5a as a precondition symbol whose meaning the axiom itself relies on to be well-formed; a spawning event failing either bound is not admissible under the discipline. The `inc(·, k')` thus chosen establishes the child's prefix, then further allocation is delegated to the child. Each pair `(t, k')` — a domain element `t` of the parent together with a spawning parameter `k' ∈ {1, 2}` — produces at most one child-spawning event; the parent may not invoke `inc(t, k')` for the same `t` and `k'` in two distinct spawning operations. The parent's own sibling stream resumes with `inc(·, 0)`.

T10a constrains what would otherwise be an unregulated choice. Without it, an allocator could intermix shallow and deep increments, producing outputs of varying lengths whose prefix relationships would be uncontrolled. The child-spawning operation — a single deep increment with `k' ∈ {1, 2}` — establishes a new prefix at a deeper level, from which a new allocator continues with its own `inc(·, 0)` stream. The restriction to `k' ∈ {1, 2}` with the zero-count conditions of TA5a ensures that child-spawning preserves T4; unrestricted `k' ≥ 3` would introduce adjacent zeros, violating T4's field-segment constraint (TA5a, case `k ≥ 3`).

*Definitions.* The discipline induces a natural notion of allocator output, which we formalize here so that the consequences below may quantify over it. For each allocator A with base address `t₀`, its *domain* is the set

  `dom(A) = {tₙ : n ≥ 0}` where `tₙ₊₁ = inc(tₙ, 0)`,

the base address together with every iterated sibling output; the axiom's restriction of sibling production to `inc(·, 0)` makes this enumeration well-defined, and `inc` itself is supplied by TA5. Child-spawning outputs are excluded from the parent's domain by construction: when A performs `inc(t, k')` with `k' > 0`, the resulting address `c₀ = inc(t, k')` is not an element of `dom(A)` but serves as the base of the newly created child, becoming the initial element of `dom(child)`. We further define the predicate

  `same_allocator(a, b) ≡ ∃A : a ∈ dom(A) ∧ b ∈ dom(A)`,

asserting that two addresses both lie in the domain of a single allocator. Both definitions are well-founded relative to T10a's own dependencies: `inc` comes from TA5, and the base-address and sibling-production restrictions are supplied by the axiom itself.

*Justification.* T10a is a design axiom — it constrains allocator behavior rather than following from prior properties. The consequences below rest on six foundations: TA5, which defines `inc` and supplies the length postconditions `#inc(t, 0) = #t` (TA5(c)) and `#inc(t, k) = #t + k` for `k > 0` (TA5(d)), together with the strict monotonicity `inc(t, 0) > t` (TA5(a)); TA5a (IncrementPreservesT4), which establishes that `inc(t, k)` preserves T4 iff `k = 0`, or `k = 1 ∧ zeros(t) ≤ 3`, or `k = 2 ∧ zeros(t) ≤ 2`, and fails for `k ≥ 3`; T4 (HierarchicalParsing), whose field-segment constraint is the invariant that the discipline must preserve; T10 (PartitionIndependence), whose precondition — non-nesting prefixes — is the guarantee that T10a's discipline establishes for all non-ancestor-descendant allocator pairs (ancestor-descendant pairs inherently nest, as shown in Consequence 5); Prefix (PrefixRelation), from which it follows that tumblers of equal length are prefix-related only if identical, and whose definition underwrites the necessity argument (T10a-N); and T1 (LexicographicOrder), whose irreflexivity (T1 (a)) and transitivity (T1 (c)) lift the per-step monotonicity of `inc(·, 0)` to the strict increase of the entire enumeration chain (Consequence 7). We justify the constraint by establishing seven consequences on which the coordination-free uniqueness guarantees depend, then proving that the sibling restriction is necessary for prefix-incomparability.

**Consequence 1: Uniform sibling length.** Since T10a restricts sibling production to `inc(·, 0)`, and TA5(c) guarantees `#inc(t, 0) = #t`, every sibling output has the same length as the allocator's base address (T10a.1).

**Consequence 2: Non-nesting sibling prefixes.** By T10a.1, all siblings have equal length; tumblers of equal length are prefix-related only if identical (Prefix), so distinct siblings are prefix-incomparable (T10a.2).

**Consequence 3: Length separation between parent and child domains.** When the parent performs `inc(·, k')` with `k' ∈ {1, 2}`, TA5(d) gives `#inc(t, k') = #t + k'`, so the child's base address has length strictly exceeding any parent sibling. All child siblings then preserve this length by T10a.1, and across `d` nesting levels the separation is additive (T10a.3).

**Consequence 4: T4 preservation under the discipline.** The proof proceeds by induction on the allocator tree. *Base:* the root allocator's base address satisfies T4 by the initialization constraint. *Step:* given an allocator whose base address satisfies T4, TA5a guarantees that `inc(·, 0)` unconditionally preserves T4 (case `k = 0`), and the axiom restricts child-spawning to `k' ∈ {1, 2}` with the zero-count bounds of TA5a, so every child-spawning output `inc(t, k')` satisfies T4 — and this output serves as the child allocator's base, closing the induction. Every output of a conforming allocator therefore satisfies T4. Properties T4a–T4c and all downstream consumers of T4 are guaranteed their precondition by the discipline itself (T10a.4).

**Consequence 5: Cross-allocator prefix-incomparability.** Consequences 1–2 guarantee non-nesting among siblings of a single allocator; Consequence 3 separates parent from child by length. For ancestor-descendant allocator pairs, prefix-nesting holds by construction: when a parent spawns a child from output `tᵢ` via `inc(tᵢ, k')` with `k' > 0`, TA5(b) gives agreement on all positions `1..#tᵢ` and TA5(d) gives `#inc(tᵢ, k') > #tᵢ`, so `tᵢ ≼ inc(tᵢ, k')` — the spawning output is a proper prefix of the child's base address, and positional inheritance propagates this through the child's entire subtree. T10's non-nesting precondition therefore does not hold for such pairs, nor is it needed: their outputs have distinct lengths by T10a.3 and hence are distinct. For the remaining case — allocators not in an ancestor-descendant relationship — T10's precondition must be established. We prove that every such pair produces prefix-incomparable outputs (T10a.5).

Let X and Y be allocators not in an ancestor-descendant relationship. They share a lowest common ancestor P in the allocator tree; let Cₓ be the child of P on the path to X and Cᵧ the child on the path to Y. Were Cₓ = Cᵧ, that child would be a lower common ancestor, contradicting minimality, so Cₓ ≠ Cᵧ. Write m for the common length of P's domain elements (T10a.1). P spawns Cₓ from `(tₓ, k'ₓ)` and Cᵧ from `(tᵧ, k'ᵧ)` with `k'ₓ, k'ᵧ ∈ {1, 2}`, and the at-most-once constraint requires distinct spawning pairs: `(tₓ, k'ₓ) ≠ (tᵧ, k'ᵧ)`. Let `bₓ = inc(tₓ, k'ₓ)` and `bᵧ = inc(tᵧ, k'ᵧ)`.

*Base case: the branching point produces a divergence position.* Since `(tₓ, k'ₓ) ≠ (tᵧ, k'ᵧ)`, either `tₓ ≠ tᵧ` or `k'ₓ ≠ k'ᵧ`. If `tₓ ≠ tᵧ`: both are domain elements of P with `#tₓ = #tᵧ = m` (T10a.1), so by T3 they differ at some position `j ≤ m`; TA5(b) for `k > 0` preserves these positions, giving `bₓ[j] = tₓ[j] ≠ tᵧ[j] = bᵧ[j]`, with `j < #bₓ` and `j < #bᵧ`. If `tₓ = tᵧ` and `k'ₓ ≠ k'ᵧ`: one of `{k'ₓ, k'ᵧ}` is 1 and the other 2; take `k'ₓ = 1` and `k'ᵧ = 2` without loss of generality. TA5(d) places a 1 at position `m + 1` of `bₓ` (the single appended position) and a 0 at position `m + 1` of `bᵧ` (the zero-valued separator preceding the final 1), so `j = m + 1` with `bₓ[j] = 1 ≠ 0 = bᵧ[j]` and `j = min(#bₓ, #bᵧ)`. In both cases a position `j ≤ min(#bₓ, #bᵧ)` exists at which `bₓ[j] ≠ bᵧ[j]`.

*Inductive step: divergence propagates through the allocator tree.* We show by induction on depth that every output in the subtree rooted at Cₓ preserves the value `bₓ[j]` at position j — or, in the second case, preserves a strictly positive value there — and symmetrically for Cᵧ. Within any allocator A having base `b_A` satisfying T4, the sibling stream `inc(·, 0)` modifies only position `sig(b_A) = #b_A`: TA5(b) for `k = 0` preserves every position other than `sig`, and TA5(c) establishes actual modification at `sig` with `t'_{sig} = t_{sig} + 1` (TA5-SigValid and T10a.4 give `sig(b_A) = #b_A`). When A spawns a child from sibling s via `inc(s, k'')` with `k'' > 0`, TA5(b) copies positions `1..#s` into the child's base, whose length `#s + k''` exceeds `#s` (TA5(d)), so all inherited positions are thereafter interior and fixed.

When `j < #bₓ` — the case arising from `tₓ ≠ tᵧ` — position j differs from `sig(bₓ) = #bₓ`, so every sibling of Cₓ carries `bₓ[j]` at position j. Each child inherits it via TA5(b), and at every deeper level `#b_A > #bₓ > j`, so j is never the significant position of any descendant. The value `bₓ[j]` propagates unchanged through the entire subtree; the same argument carries `bᵧ[j]` through Cᵧ's subtree.

When `j = #bₓ` — the case arising from `k'ₓ = 1`, `k'ᵧ = 2` — siblings of Cₓ increment position j (since `sig(bₓ) = #bₓ = j`), producing values `1, 2, 3, …` starting from `bₓ[j] = 1` (TA5(c)). Every sibling therefore carries a strictly positive value at position j. When Cₓ spawns a grandchild from sibling s, TA5(b) copies `s[j] ≥ 1` into the grandchild's base, whose length exceeds j; from that depth onward, position j is interior and the positive value propagates unchanged. Every output in Cₓ's subtree has a strictly positive value at position j. On the Cᵧ side, `j = m + 1 < m + 2 = #bᵧ` (since `k'ᵧ = 2`), so position j is interior to Cᵧ's base; the sibling stream leaves it at `bᵧ[j] = 0`, and the same inheritance argument carries 0 through every descendant. The subtrees of Cₓ and Cᵧ carry distinct values at position j.

*Closure.* For any `x ∈ domain(X)` and `y ∈ domain(Y)`: X is Cₓ or a descendant of Cₓ, so T10a.3 (local monotonicity) gives `#x ≥ #bₓ` — with equality when X = Cₓ, by T10a.1; symmetrically `#y ≥ #bᵧ`. Since `j ≤ min(#bₓ, #bᵧ)` (established in the base case), both `#x ≥ j` and `#y ≥ j`, and `x[j] ≠ y[j]` by the inductive step. Were `x ≼ y`, Prefix would require `x[i] = y[i]` for all `i ≤ #x`, giving `x[j] = y[j]` — a contradiction. Symmetrically `y ⋠ x`. ∎

**Consequence 6: Domain disjointness.** Consequences 1, 3, and 5 together imply that distinct allocators have disjoint domains. For an ancestor–descendant pair, T10a.1 gives uniform length within each domain and T10a.3 gives a strict length inequality between ancestor and descendant streams, so no element can lie in both. For a non-ancestor–descendant pair, T10a.5 gives prefix-incomparability of all outputs, contradicted by the trivial reflexivity `t ≼ t` were `t` shared. The disjointness elevates `same_allocator(a, b)` from a bare existential to a witness-unique predicate: when `same_allocator(a, b)` holds, the witnessing allocator `A` with `a, b ∈ dom(A)` is uniquely determined by the pair `(a, b)` (T10a.6). Witness-uniqueness alone does not yet make the enumeration indices single-valued — a single allocator's chain `t₀, t₁, t₂, …` could in principle revisit the same tumbler under two indices — so the well-definedness of any predicate phrased over those indices, `allocated_before(a, b)` in T9 in particular, requires a complementary within-allocator injectivity, supplied next.

**Consequence 7: Enumeration injectivity.** Within any single allocator's chain `t₀, t₁, t₂, …`, the indexing map `n ↦ tₙ` is injective: `m ≠ n` implies `tₘ ≠ tₙ`. By TA5(a), each step produces a strictly greater tumbler, `tₙ₊₁ = inc(tₙ, 0) > tₙ`; induction on the gap using T1 (c) transitivity lifts this per-step increase to `tₘ < tₙ` for every `m < n`; T1 (a) irreflexivity then excludes `tₘ = tₙ`, yielding distinctness. Combined with Consequence 6, this secures single-valuedness of the enumeration indices `i, j` with `a = tᵢ, b = tⱼ` as functions of the pair `(a, b)` whenever `same_allocator(a, b)` holds: the witnessing allocator is unique (Consequence 6), and within it the index assigned to each element is unique (Consequence 7). Any predicate phrased over those indices — `allocated_before(a, b)` in T9 — therefore receives an unambiguous interpretation (T10a.7).

**Necessity.** Suppose the discipline is relaxed to permit any k ≥ 0 in the sibling stream. Let an allocator with base address b produce a₁ = inc(b, 0), then a₂ = inc(a₁, k') with k' > 0 — both legitimate sibling outputs under the relaxed rule. By TA5(b), since k' > 0, a₂ agrees with a₁ on every position 1 ≤ i ≤ #a₁. By TA5(d), #a₂ = #a₁ + k' > #a₁, so a₂ has components beyond position #a₁. These two facts — agreement on all positions of the shorter tumbler and strict length excess in the longer — are precisely the conditions of the Prefix definition: `a₁ ≼ a₂` with `#a₁ < #a₂`, so `a₁ ≺ a₂`. The relaxed rule therefore admits siblings a₁ ≺ a₂, falsifying T10a.2 (NonNestingSiblingPrefixes) — the within-allocator claim that distinct siblings of one allocator are prefix-incomparable, which is itself the vehicle by which T10a supplies T10's non-nesting precondition on co-sibling prefixes (T10a-N). The sibling restriction — `k = 0` only — is thus necessary for prefix-incomparability. The remaining components of the axiom serve distinct purposes not addressed by this argument: the child-spawning bound `k' ∈ {1, 2}` is necessary for T4 preservation (Consequence 4, via TA5a's failure for `k ≥ 3`), the at-most-once child-spawning constraint ensures uniqueness of child base addresses (Consequence 5), and the root T4 initialization seeds the inductive argument of Consequence 4. The discipline is sufficient for prefix-incomparability across all non-ancestor-descendant allocator output domains (T10a.1–T10a.5); the necessity proof establishes that its sibling restriction is indispensable to that guarantee. ∎

*Formal Contract:*
- *Definitions:*
  - *Domain:* `dom(A) = {tₙ : n ≥ 0}` where `t₀` is the allocator's base address and `tₙ₊₁ = inc(tₙ, 0)` — the `inc(·, 0)` chain extending the base. Child-spawning outputs `inc(t, k')` with `k' > 0` are excluded from the parent's domain and serve as the base of the child allocator, becoming the initial element of `dom(child)`.
  - *Same allocator:* `same_allocator(a, b) ≡ ∃A : a ∈ dom(A) ∧ b ∈ dom(A)` — the predicate asserting that `a` and `b` both lie in some allocator's domain.
- *Axiom:* The root allocator's base address satisfies T4. Allocators produce sibling outputs exclusively by `inc(·, 0)`; child-spawning uses exactly one `inc(·, k')` with `k' ∈ {1, 2}`, subject to the runtime precondition — imposed by the discipline and checked before each spawn — that `zeros(t) ≤ 3` when `k' = 1` and `zeros(t) ≤ 2` when `k' = 2`, where `zeros(·)` is the zero-count function defined in TA5a. The chosen `inc(·, k')` establishes the child's prefix, after which the parent resumes sibling production with `inc(·, 0)`. Each `(t, k')` pair — domain element and spawning parameter — yields at most one child-spawning event. Depends: T4 (HierarchicalParsing — the invariant the root must satisfy and the discipline must preserve; also the original site of the `zeros(·)` definition), TA5 (IncrementPostconditions — defines `inc` and its length/positional postconditions), TA5a (IncrementPreservesT4 — supplies both the `zeros(·)` symbol the axiom names in its precondition and the preservation bounds `k' = 1 ∧ zeros(t) ≤ 3`, `k' = 2 ∧ zeros(t) ≤ 2` that the discipline adopts as runtime constraints on child-spawning parameters).
- *Postconditions:*
  - T10a.1 (Uniform sibling length): For every allocator with base address b, all sibling outputs a satisfy #a = #b. Depends: TA5 (TA5(c): #inc(t, 0) = #t).
  - T10a.2 (Non-nesting sibling prefixes): For all siblings a, b from the same allocator, same_allocator(a, b) ∧ a ≠ b → a and b are prefix-incomparable. Depends: T10a.1 (#a = #b) and Prefix (equal-length tumblers are prefix-related only if identical).
  - T10a.3 (Length separation): For every child allocator spawned by `inc(·, k')` with k' ∈ {1, 2} from a parent with base length m, all child outputs c satisfy #c = m + k', and across d nesting levels the separation is exact: #output = m + k'₁ + k'₂ + … + k'_d. Local monotonicity: for any ancestor-descendant pair (A, B) on a lineage, #output(B) > #output(A). Depends: T10a.1 (uniform sibling length) and TA5 (TA5(d): #inc(t, k') = #t + k' for k' > 0).
  - T10a.4 (T4 preservation): The root allocator's base address satisfies T4 (initialization constraint); since `inc(·, 0)` unconditionally preserves T4 (TA5a) and child-spawning uses `k' ∈ {1, 2}` within TA5a bounds, every output of a conforming allocator satisfies T4 by induction on the allocator tree. Depends: T4 (HierarchicalParsing — the invariant preserved by induction), TA5a (inc(·, 0) preserves T4 unconditionally; inc(·, k') with k' ∈ {1, 2} preserves T4 within zero-count bounds).
  - T10a.5 (Cross-allocator prefix-incomparability): For any two allocators X and Y not in an ancestor-descendant relationship, for all x ∈ domain(X) and y ∈ domain(Y), x ⋠ y ∧ y ⋠ x. Depends: T10a (at-most-once constraint), T10a.1 (uniform length), T10a.3 (length separation), T10a.4 (T4 preservation), T4 (HierarchicalParsing — TA5-SigValid precondition), TA5 (increment postconditions), TA5-SigValid (sig = length for T4-valid addresses), T3 (divergence of distinct tumblers), Prefix (prefix definition). Together with T10a.2, this delivers T10's precondition for every non-ancestor-descendant allocator pair in the tree. Ancestor-descendant pairs inherently nest (TA5(b), TA5(d)); their output distinctness follows from length separation (T10a.3).
  - T10a.6 (Domain disjointness): For any two distinct allocators X and Y, dom(X) ∩ dom(Y) = ∅. Case 1 (ancestor–descendant): T10a.1 + T10a.3 — uniform per-domain length (T10a.1) combined with the strict length inequality between ancestor and descendant streams (T10a.3) excludes any common element. Case 2 (non-ancestor–descendant): T10a.5 + Prefix reflexivity — cross-allocator prefix-incomparability (T10a.5) is contradicted by `t ≼ t` were `t` shared. Witness-uniqueness corollary: when `same_allocator(a, b)` holds, the witnessing allocator A is uniquely determined by the pair (a, b). Single-valuedness of the enumeration indices i, j with a = tᵢ, b = tⱼ requires also the within-allocator injectivity of T10a.7; the two results together underwrite well-definedness of T9's allocated_before. Depends: T10a.1 (uniform sibling length), T10a.3 (length separation), T10a.5 (cross-allocator prefix-incomparability), Prefix (reflexivity of ≼).
  - T10a.7 (Enumeration injectivity): For every allocator A with domain `dom(A) = {tₙ : n ≥ 0}` where `tₙ₊₁ = inc(tₙ, 0)`, the indexing map `n ↦ tₙ` is injective: `m ≠ n → tₘ ≠ tₙ`. Proof: TA5(a) gives per-step strict increase `tₙ₊₁ > tₙ`; induction on the gap via T1 (c) transitivity yields `tₘ < tₙ` for `m < n`; T1 (a) irreflexivity converts strict inequality into distinctness. Equivalently, the enumeration is strictly increasing under the tumbler order. Together with T10a.6, this makes the enumeration indices `i, j` with `a = tᵢ, b = tⱼ` single-valued functions of the pair `(a, b)` whenever `same_allocator(a, b)` holds, supplying the second of the two pieces required for well-definedness of T9's `allocated_before`. Depends: T10a (enumeration definition), TA5 (TA5(a): `inc(t, 0) > t`), T1 (T1 (a): irreflexivity; T1 (c): transitivity).
  - T10a-N (Necessity of sibling restriction): Under the relaxed rule (any k ≥ 0 in the sibling stream), the pair a₁ = inc(b, 0) and a₂ = inc(a₁, k') with k' > 0 are sibling outputs satisfying a₁ ≺ a₂ — by TA5(b) (agreement on all positions of a₁) and TA5(d) (#a₂ > #a₁), invoking the Prefix definition. This falsifies T10a.2 (NonNestingSiblingPrefixes) — T10a's own within-allocator consequence that distinct sibling outputs of one allocator are prefix-incomparable — and T10a.2 is the vehicle by which T10a supplies T10's non-nesting precondition on co-sibling prefixes, so the downstream effect on T10 flows through T10a.2 rather than through a direct application of T10 to the pair. The sibling restriction (`k = 0` only) is therefore necessary for prefix-incomparability. The remaining axiom components — the `k' ∈ {1, 2}` child-spawning bound, the at-most-once constraint, and the root T4 initialization — serve T4 preservation (TA5a) and child-prefix uniqueness respectively; their necessity for those distinct purposes is not addressed by this argument. Depends: TA5 (TA5(b): agreement on positions 1..#t; TA5(d): #inc(t, k') = #t + k'), Prefix (prefix definition), T10a.2 (NonNestingSiblingPrefixes — the within-allocator consequence directly falsified, matching the per-step citation convention without needing to promote the pair to T10 as candidate independent-allocator prefixes), and T10 (non-nesting precondition supplied downstream through T10a.2).
