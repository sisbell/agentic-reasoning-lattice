**TS5 (ShiftAmountMonotonicity).**

`(A v, n₁, n₂ : n₁ ∈ ℕ ∧ n₂ ∈ ℕ ∧ n₁ ≥ 1 ∧ n₂ > n₁ ∧ #v = m : shift(v, n₁) < shift(v, n₂))`

*Proof.* We show that shifting a tumbler by a larger amount produces a strictly greater result: if n₂ exceeds n₁, then the shift by n₂ overshoots the shift by n₁.

Fix v ∈ T with #v = m, and fix n₁, n₂ ∈ ℕ with n₁ ≥ 1 and n₂ > n₁. The carrier-typings `n₁ ∈ ℕ` and `n₂ ∈ ℕ` are named separately in the preconditions because each is consumed downstream as the named source for NAT-sub's three ℕ-typing clauses, for TS3's `a ∈ ℕ` precondition under the substitution `a = n₁`, and (transitively, via NAT-sub's conditional closure on the difference `d = n₂ − n₁`) for TS3's `b ∈ ℕ` precondition under `b = d` and TS4's `n ∈ ℕ` precondition under `n = d` — the order facts `n₁ ≥ 1` and `n₂ > n₁` presuppose rather than establish carrier membership (the convention OrdinalDisplacement's own proof articulates at its m-th-position-typing site, and TS3 re-cites at each of its three OrdinalShift invocation sites). We must prove shift(v, n₁) < shift(v, n₂).

Define d = n₂ − n₁. Three NAT-sub clauses participate at this site, each discharged to named sources. The ℕ-typing `d ∈ ℕ` follows from NAT-sub's conditional closure axiom `(A m, n ∈ ℕ : m ≥ n : m − n ∈ ℕ)` instantiated at `m = n₂, n = n₁`: NAT-sub's two ℕ-typing preconditions transfer directly from TS5's own preconditions `n₂ ∈ ℕ` and `n₁ ∈ ℕ`, and the order precondition `n₂ ≥ n₁` follows from TS5's hypothesis `n₂ > n₁` via NAT-order's defining clause `m ≤ n ⟺ m < n ∨ m = n` read right-to-left at the strict disjunct. The order fact `d ≥ 1` follows from NAT-sub's strict positivity axiom `(A m, n ∈ ℕ : m > n : m − n ≥ 1)` instantiated at `m = n₂, n = n₁`: the same two ℕ-typing preconditions transfer from TS5's preconditions, and the strict order precondition `n₂ > n₁` is exactly TS5's hypothesis. The rewrite `n₂ = n₁ + d` follows from NAT-sub's left-inverse characterisation `(A m, n ∈ ℕ : m ≥ n : n + (m − n) = m)` instantiated at `m = n₂, n = n₁`: again the two ℕ-typing preconditions transfer from TS5's preconditions, the order precondition `n₂ ≥ n₁` is supplied by the same NAT-order conversion, and the resulting equation `n₁ + (n₂ − n₁) = n₂`, with `n₂ − n₁` abbreviated as `d`, becomes `n₁ + d = n₂`. So we have n₂ = n₁ + d with both n₁ ≥ 1 (TS5's precondition) and d ≥ 1 (just established).

We invoke TS3 (ShiftComposition), which states that for any tumbler u with #u = m and any pair of shift amounts a, b ∈ ℕ with a ≥ 1, b ≥ 1: shift(shift(u, a), b) = shift(u, a + b). Here u = v, a = n₁, b = d. TS3's six preconditions are discharged to named sources: `v ∈ T` (TS5's precondition, discharging TS3's first-argument carrier-typing); `n₁ ∈ ℕ` (TS5's precondition, discharging TS3's `a ∈ ℕ` under `a = n₁`); `d ∈ ℕ` (just established via NAT-sub's conditional closure, discharging TS3's `b ∈ ℕ` under `b = d`); `n₁ ≥ 1` (TS5's precondition); `d ≥ 1` (just established via NAT-sub's strict positivity); and `#v = m` (TS5's precondition). Therefore shift(shift(v, n₁), d) = shift(v, n₁ + d) = shift(v, n₂), the last equality by the NAT-sub left-inverse rewrite above. This expresses the larger shift as a composition: first shift by n₁, then shift the result by d.

Let u = shift(v, n₁). By OrdinalShift, u is a tumbler with #u = m (shift preserves length). Then shift(v, n₂) = shift(u, d). We invoke TS4 (ShiftStrictIncrease), which states that for any tumbler u with #u = m and any n ∈ ℕ with n ≥ 1: shift(u, n) > u. Here u = shift(v, n₁) and n = d. TS4's four preconditions are discharged to named sources: `u ∈ T` (from OrdinalShift's exported carrier-typing of `shift(v, n₁)` just cited); `d ∈ ℕ` (just established via NAT-sub's conditional closure, discharging TS4's `n ∈ ℕ` under `n = d`); `d ≥ 1` (just established via NAT-sub's strict positivity); and `#u = m` (just noted). Therefore shift(u, d) > u.

Substituting back: shift(v, n₂) = shift(u, d) > u = shift(v, n₁), that is, shift(v, n₁) < shift(v, n₂). ∎

*Worked example.* Let v = [2, 3, 7] (m = 3) and n = 4. Then δ(4, 3) = [0, 0, 4] with action point 3. TA0: k = 3 ≤ 3 = #v. By TumblerAdd: shift(v, 4) = [2, 3, 7 + 4] = [2, 3, 11].

For TS1: take v₁ = [2, 3, 5] < v₂ = [2, 3, 9] with n = 4. Then shift(v₁, 4) = [2, 3, 9] < [2, 3, 13] = shift(v₂, 4). ✓

For TS3: shift(shift([2, 3, 7], 4), 3) = shift([2, 3, 11], 3) = [2, 3, 14] = shift([2, 3, 7], 7). ✓

*Formal Contract:*
- *Preconditions:* v ∈ T, n₁ ∈ ℕ, n₂ ∈ ℕ, n₁ ≥ 1, n₂ > n₁, #v = m
- *Depends:* TS3 (ShiftComposition) — invoked at the proof's first reduction to express the larger shift as a composition ("We invoke TS3 (ShiftComposition), which states that for any tumbler u with #u = m and any pair of shift amounts a, b ∈ ℕ with a ≥ 1, b ≥ 1: shift(shift(u, a), b) = shift(u, a + b). [...] Therefore shift(shift(v, n₁), d) = shift(v, n₁ + d) = shift(v, n₂)"); without TS3 the rewrite of `shift(v, n₂)` as `shift(shift(v, n₁), d)` could not be performed. TS3's six preconditions at the instantiation `u = v, a = n₁, b = d` are discharged to named sources: `v ∈ T` from TS5's own precondition (discharging TS3's first-argument carrier-typing); `n₁ ∈ ℕ` from TS5's own precondition under the substitution `a = n₁` (discharging TS3's `a ∈ ℕ` precondition); `d ∈ ℕ` from NAT-sub's conditional closure axiom applied to `n₁, n₂ ∈ ℕ` and `n₂ ≥ n₁` (cited separately below) under the substitution `b = d` (discharging TS3's `b ∈ ℕ` precondition); `n₁ ≥ 1` from TS5's own precondition; `d ≥ 1` from NAT-sub's strict positivity axiom applied to `n₁, n₂ ∈ ℕ` and `n₂ > n₁` (cited separately below); and `#v = m` from TS5's own precondition. OrdinalShift (OrdinalShift) — invoked between the TS3 and TS4 calls to license both `u ∈ T` and `#u = m` for `u = shift(v, n₁)` ("By OrdinalShift, u is a tumbler with #u = m (shift preserves length)"); TS4's `u ∈ T` precondition (under the substitution `v = u`) and `#u = m` precondition both depend on this length-and-carrier-preservation fact. TS4 (ShiftStrictIncrease) — invoked at the second reduction to convert the d-shift of `u` into a strict increase ("We invoke TS4 (ShiftStrictIncrease), which states that for any tumbler u with #u = m and any n ∈ ℕ with n ≥ 1: shift(u, n) > u. [...] Therefore shift(u, d) > u"); the substitution `shift(v, n₂) = shift(u, d) > u = shift(v, n₁)` collapses the two-step reduction into the desired strict ordering. TS4's four preconditions at the instantiation `u = shift(v, n₁), n = d` are discharged to named sources: `u ∈ T` from OrdinalShift's exported carrier-typing of `shift(v, n₁)` (cited above); `d ∈ ℕ` from NAT-sub's conditional closure axiom (discharging TS4's `n ∈ ℕ` precondition under the substitution `n = d`); `d ≥ 1` from NAT-sub's strict positivity axiom; and `#u = m` from OrdinalShift's exported length-preservation. NAT-sub (NatPartialSubtraction) — invoked at the opening sentences "Define d = n₂ − n₁. […] The ℕ-typing `d ∈ ℕ` follows from NAT-sub's conditional closure […] The order fact `d ≥ 1` follows from NAT-sub's strict positivity […] The rewrite `n₂ = n₁ + d` follows from NAT-sub's left-inverse characterisation" at three sites: conditional closure (`m ≥ n ⟹ m − n ∈ ℕ`) instantiated at `m = n₂, n = n₁` discharges `d ∈ ℕ`, with the two ℕ-typing preconditions `n₁ ∈ ℕ` and `n₂ ∈ ℕ` transferring directly from TS5's own preconditions and the order precondition `n₂ ≥ n₁` supplied from TS5's hypothesis `n₂ > n₁` via NAT-order's defining clause; strict positivity (`m > n ⟹ m − n ≥ 1`) instantiated at `m = n₂, n = n₁` discharges `d ≥ 1` from TS5's hypothesis `n₂ > n₁`, with the same two ℕ-typing preconditions transferring from TS5's preconditions; and left-inverse characterisation (`n + (m − n) = m`) instantiated at `m = n₂, n = n₁` discharges the rewrite `n₂ = n₁ + d` that TS3's instantiation `b = d` and the closing equality `shift(v, n₁ + d) = shift(v, n₂)` both require, with the same ℕ-typing transfer and the same NAT-order conversion of `n₂ > n₁` into `n₂ ≥ n₁`. Without NAT-sub these three steps would appeal to background ℕ arithmetic, contrary to T0's convention that proofs cite only the ℕ facts they use; without TS5's named `n₁ ∈ ℕ, n₂ ∈ ℕ` preconditions the three ℕ-typing preconditions of NAT-sub's three consumed clauses would have no named source, since the order facts `n₁ ≥ 1` and `n₂ > n₁` presuppose rather than establish carrier membership (the convention OrdinalDisplacement's own proof articulates at its m-th-position-typing site). NAT-order (NatStrictTotalOrder) — invoked at the same opening sentences to convert the strict inequality `n₂ > n₁` (TS5's precondition) into the weak `n₂ ≥ n₁` required by NAT-sub's conditional-closure and left-inverse-characterisation clauses (via the defining clause `m ≤ n ⟺ m < n ∨ m = n` read right-to-left at the strict disjunct); without NAT-order the precondition for NAT-sub's conditional closure and left-inverse characterisation would not be discharged from the hypothesis. NAT-sub's strict positivity clause does not consume NAT-order: its strict-order precondition `n₂ > n₁` transfers directly from TS5's hypothesis without conversion.
- *Postconditions:* shift(v, n₁) < shift(v, n₂)
