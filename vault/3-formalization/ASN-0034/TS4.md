**TS4 (ShiftStrictIncrease).**

`(A v, n, m : v ∈ T ∧ n ∈ ℕ ∧ n ≥ 1 ∧ #v = m : shift(v, n) > v)`

The dummy `m` in the binder is a local abbreviation for `#v`: for any fixed `v ∈ T`, the range predicate `#v = m` determines `m` uniquely as the length of `v` (with `m ∈ ℕ` following by T0's length operator typing `#·: T → ℕ` applied to `v ∈ T`, and `m ≥ 1` following by T0's length axiom `#a ≥ 1 for all a ∈ T`), so the universal over `m` collapses to the single witness `m = #v` — an abbreviation pattern matching the one OrdinalShift's own Definition clause `shift(v, n) = v ⊕ δ(n, m) where m = #v` uses at its point of introduction, and matching TS3's binder preface at its analogous dummy-`m` site.

*Proof.* We show that every ordinal shift by a positive amount produces a result strictly greater than the original tumbler.

Fix v ∈ T, and let m abbreviate #v so that the range predicate `#v = m` binds m as the length of v. Fix n ∈ ℕ with n ≥ 1. Before invoking OrdinalShift at `(v, n)` we discharge its `n ∈ ℕ` precondition from TS4's own precondition `n ∈ ℕ` — an order statement like `n ≥ 1` presupposes rather than establishes carrier membership (the convention OrdinalDisplacement's own proof articulates at its m-th-position-typing site, and TS3 re-cites at each of its three OrdinalShift invocation sites), so the carrier-typing must be routed from the separately named `n ∈ ℕ` precondition rather than from the order precondition `n ≥ 1` alone. By OrdinalShift, shift(v, n) = v ⊕ δ(n, m), so we must show v ⊕ δ(n, m) > v.

We apply TA-strict (Strict increase) with start position a = v and displacement w = δ(n, m). TA-strict requires two preconditions: Pos(w), and actionPoint(w) ≤ #a. We verify each.

*First precondition: Pos(δ(n, m)).* By OrdinalDisplacement's exported postcondition `Pos(δ(n, m))` — discharged inside OrdinalDisplacement's own proof through the `n ≥ 1 ⟹ n ≠ 0` promotion (NAT-addcompat anchor `0 < 1`, NAT-order's defining clause + transitivity of `<` + irreflexivity) witnessing TA-Pos's existential at `i = m`, and re-exported as a named postcondition so the component-nonzero promotion need not be re-discharged here. This matches the route TS1 applies at its analogous `Pos(δ(n, m))` discharge (precondition check (v)) and that OrdinalShift applies at its TA0 precondition-three consumption site, rather than re-derived from δ's structural form and the positivity of its m-th component inline.

*Second precondition: actionPoint(δ(n, m)) ≤ #v.* By OrdinalDisplacement, the action point of δ(n, m) is m (position m is the first nonzero component, since positions 1 through m − 1 are zero and position m is n ≥ 1). Since #v = m, the precondition m ≤ m holds.

Both preconditions are satisfied. By TA-strict: v ⊕ δ(n, m) > v, that is, shift(v, n) > v. ∎
