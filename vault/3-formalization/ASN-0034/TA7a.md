## Subspace closure

When arithmetic advances a position within one element subspace, the result must remain in that subspace. Text positions must not cross into link space, and vice versa.

An element-local position within subspace `S` has two components: the subspace identifier `N` and the ordinal `x`. A natural first attempt at an element-local displacement is `w = [0, n]` ‚Äî action point `k = 2`, preserving the subspace identifier and advancing the ordinal. Addition works: `[N, x] ‚äï [0, n] = [N, x + n]`, preserving the subspace. But subtraction exposes a subtlety: `[N, x] ‚äñ [0, n]` finds the first divergence at position 1 (where `N ‚âÝ 0`), not at position 2 where the intended action lies. The subtraction produces `[N - 0, x] = [N, x]` ‚Äî a no-op. The abstract `‚äñ` cannot shift a position backward by a displacement that disagrees with the position at the subspace identifier.

Gregory's implementation reveals the resolution. The operands passed to the arithmetic during shifts are not full element-local positions; they are *within-subspace ordinals* ‚Äî the second component alone. The subspace identifier is not an operand to the shift; it is structural context that determines *which* positions are subject to the shift. The arithmetic receives ordinals, not full positions.

**TA7a (SubspaceClosure).** The canonical representation for shift arithmetic is the *ordinal-only* formulation: a position in a subspace with identifier `N` and ordinal `o = [o‚ÇÅ, ..., o‚Çò]` (where `m ‚â• 1`) is represented as the tumbler `o` for arithmetic purposes, with `N` held as structural context. Define **S** = {o ‚àà T : #o ‚â• 1 ‚àß (A i : 1 ‚â§ i ‚â§ #o : o·µ¢ > 0)} ‚Äî ordinals with all positive components, matching the shape of an element field under T4 (a non-zero run delimited by separators; positivity of non-zero entries is supplied by T0's carrier ‚Ñï). An element-local displacement is a positive tumbler `w` with action point `k` satisfying `1 ‚â§ k ‚â§ m`. In this formulation:

  `(A o ‚àà S, Pos(w) : k ‚â§ #o ‚üπ o ‚äï w ‚àà T)`

  `(A o ‚àà S, Pos(w) : o ‚â• w ‚üπ o ‚äñ w ‚àà T)`

Both claims assert closure in T: arithmetic on ordinals, with the subspace identifier held as structural context, produces results that remain in T. The subspace identifier is not an operand ‚Äî it determines *which* positions are subject to the shift, but never enters the arithmetic. This design ensures that no shift can escape the subspace.

The ordinal-only formulation is not arbitrary. The natural 2-component formulation `[N, x]` fails for subtraction: `[N, x] ‚äñ [0, n]` finds the divergence at position 1 (where `N > 0 = 0`), producing `[N, x]` ‚Äî a no-op rather than a genuine shift. Stripping the subspace identifier from the operands avoids this degeneracy.

*Proof.* We prove each conjunct of TA7a, then analyze the finer question of S-membership.

Let `o = [o‚ÇÅ, ..., o‚Çò]` with `o ‚àà S`, so `m ‚â• 1` and every `o·µ¢ > 0`. Let `w` be a positive displacement with action point `k = min({i : 1 ‚â§ i ‚â§ #w ‚àß w·µ¢ ‚âÝ 0})`.

**Conjunct 1** (`‚äï`-closure in T). The precondition gives `o ‚àà T`, `w ‚àà T`, `Pos(w)`, and `k ‚â§ #o = m`. These are exactly the preconditions of TA0 (well-defined addition). By TA0, `o ‚äï w ‚àà T`, with `#(o ‚äï w) = #w`. The subspace identifier, held as structural context outside the operands, is untouched.

A stronger result holds for S-membership. By TumblerAdd's constructive definition, the result `r = o ‚äï w` has components: `r·µ¢ = o·µ¢ > 0` for `1 ‚â§ i < k` (prefix copied from `o ‚àà S`); `r‚Çñ = o‚Çñ + w‚Çñ` by the construction's action-point clause, with the strict positivity `r‚Çñ > 0` established via a four-step chain consuming NAT-addcompat and NAT-order together ‚Äî NAT-addcompat's left order-compatibility (`n ‚â• p ‚üπ m + n ‚â• m + p`, instantiated at `m = o‚Çñ, n = w‚Çñ, p = 1`) lifts ActionPoint's `w‚Çñ ‚â• 1` into the non-strict `o‚Çñ + w‚Çñ ‚â• o‚Çñ + 1`; NAT-addcompat's strict successor inequality (`n < n + 1`, instantiated at `n = o‚Çñ`) supplies the strict `o‚Çñ + 1 > o‚Çñ`; NAT-order's defining clause `m ‚â§ n ‚ü∫ m < n ‚à® m = n` instantiated at `m = o‚Çñ + 1, n = o‚Çñ + w‚Çñ` unfolds the `‚â•` into `o‚Çñ + w‚Çñ > o‚Çñ + 1 ‚à® o‚Çñ + w‚Çñ = o‚Çñ + 1`, the strict disjunct chains with `o‚Çñ + 1 > o‚Çñ` through NAT-order's transitivity of `<` to yield `o‚Çñ + w‚Çñ > o‚Çñ`, and the equality disjunct substitutes `o‚Çñ + w‚Çñ = o‚Çñ + 1` into `o‚Çñ + 1 > o‚Çñ` to yield the same conclusion (the same strict-advancement chain TumblerAdd's *ordering guarantee* paragraph derives at this site); and NAT-order's transitivity of `<` combines `o‚Çñ + w‚Çñ > o‚Çñ` with `o‚Çñ > 0` (the latter supplied because `o ‚àà S`) to deliver the final `o‚Çñ + w‚Çñ > 0`; and `r·µ¢ = w·µ¢` for `k < i ‚â§ #w` (tail copied from the displacement). Components before and at the action point are positive. The result is in S precisely when every tail component `w·µ¢` (for `i > k`) is also positive. For single-component ordinals ‚Äî the common case ‚Äî `[x] ‚äï [n] = [x + n]`, which is unconditionally in S since both `x > 0` and `n > 0`.

For example, spanning from ordinal `[1, 3, 2]` to `[1, 5, 7]` requires displacement `[0, 2, 7]` (action point `k = 2`). TumblerAdd produces `[1, 3 + 2, 7] = [1, 5, 7]` ‚Äî position 1 of the ordinal is copied from the start, preserving the ordinal prefix.

**Conjunct 2** (`‚äñ`-closure in T). The precondition gives `o ‚àà T`, `w ‚àà T`, `Pos(w)`, and `o ‚â• w`. The triple `o ‚àà T`, `w ‚àà T`, `o ‚â• w` matches the preconditions of TA2 (well-defined subtraction); the additional `Pos(w)` is *not* consumed by TA2 ‚Äî TA2's contract delivers T-closure for any `w ‚àà T` satisfying `o ‚â• w`, irrespective of positivity ‚Äî but is held in reserve for the action-point case analysis below, where the action point `k = actionPoint(w)` is well-defined only under `Pos(w)` (per ActionPoint's contract, whose own precondition list pairs `w ‚àà T` with `Pos(w)`). By TA2 applied at the triple, `o ‚äñ w ‚àà T`. The subspace identifier is again untouched.

The S-membership question for `‚äñ` is more delicate. We analyze by action point and divergence position, using TumblerSub's constructive definition: zero-pad to length `L` ‚Äî the longer of `#o` and `#w`, named by NAT-order's trichotomy on `(#o, #w)` per TumblerSub's Definition (sub-case (Œ±) `#o = #w` with `L = #o`; sub-case (Œ≤) `#o < #w` with `L = #w`; sub-case (Œ≥) `#w < #o` with `L = #o`) rather than by a primitive binary-maximum operator on ‚Ñï, matching the per-step convention TumblerSub and TA2 apply at the structurally identical length-pair dispatch ‚Äî find the divergence position `d` (the first position where the padded sequences differ), then set `r·µ¢ = 0` for `i < d`, `r_d = o_d - w_d`, and `r·µ¢ = o·µ¢` (zero-padded) for `i > d`.

*Preliminary: when `#w > m`.* The hypothesis `#w > m` is the same as `#o < #w` at `#o = m`, placing the length pair `(#o, #w) = (m, #w)` in sub-case (Œ≤) of TumblerSub's NAT-order trichotomy dispatch ‚Äî where `#o < #w` and `L = #w` ‚Äî so TumblerSub produces a result of length `L = #w > m` rather than naming the length by a primitive binary-maximum operator on ‚Ñï. The zero-padded minuend has zeros at positions `m + 1` through `#w`, so the result inherits trailing zeros at those positions and lies in T \ S. The cases below assume `#w ‚â§ m`.

*Case `k ‚â• 2`:* The displacement has `w·µ¢ = 0` for all `i < k`, so in particular `w‚ÇÅ = 0`. Since `o ‚àà S`, `o‚ÇÅ > 0`. Therefore `o‚ÇÅ ‚âÝ w‚ÇÅ` and the divergence falls at `d = 1`. TumblerSub produces: `r‚ÇÅ = o‚ÇÅ ‚àí 0 = o‚ÇÅ > 0` ‚Äî the identity `o‚ÇÅ ‚àí 0 = o‚ÇÅ` is obtained by instantiating NAT-sub's left-inverse characterisation `n + (m ‚àí n) = m` at `m = o‚ÇÅ, n = 0`, whose precondition `o‚ÇÅ ‚â• 0` is supplied by NAT-zero's lower-bound axiom, yielding `0 + (o‚ÇÅ ‚àí 0) = o‚ÇÅ`; NAT-closure's additive identity `0 + n = n` at `n = o‚ÇÅ ‚àí 0` then rewrites the left-hand side to `o‚ÇÅ ‚àí 0`, closing the identity `o‚ÇÅ ‚àí 0 = o‚ÇÅ`. Routing through the left-inverse form rather than NAT-sub's right-telescoping clause `(o‚ÇÅ + 0) ‚àí 0 = o‚ÇÅ` is forced by the NAT-* citation discipline: right-telescoping would require `o‚ÇÅ + 0 = o‚ÇÅ` (the right additive identity) to collapse, and NAT-closure supplies only the left identity `0 + o‚ÇÅ = o‚ÇÅ`, so a right-telescoping route would have to appeal to commutativity of addition on ‚Ñï ‚Äî an axiom that T0's NAT-* enumeration excludes, and that NAT-sub's own contract avoids precisely by stating both inverse forms explicitly. For `1 < i ‚â§ m`, `r·µ¢ = o·µ¢ > 0` (copied from the minuend since `i > d = 1`). When `#w ‚â§ m`, the result has length `m` and equals `o` itself ‚Äî a no-op. The result is trivially in S. This is the vacuous closure: TumblerSub finds the mismatch at the ordinal's first positive component rather than at the displacement's intended action point.

*Case `k = 1`, divergence `d = 1`:* The displacement has `w‚ÇÅ > 0`, and `o‚ÇÅ ‚âÝ w‚ÇÅ`. Since `o ‚â• w`, the ordering at the first divergence position requires `o‚ÇÅ > w‚ÇÅ` (T1). TumblerSub produces: `r‚ÇÅ = o‚ÇÅ - w‚ÇÅ > 0` by NAT-sub (strict positivity), whose precondition `o‚ÇÅ > w‚ÇÅ` is the strict inequality just derived; `r‚ÇÅ ‚â• 1` since `o‚ÇÅ > w‚ÇÅ ‚â• 1`. For `1 < i ‚â§ m`, `r·µ¢ = o·µ¢ > 0` (copied from the minuend). When `#w ‚â§ m`, all components are positive and the result is in S.

*Case `k = 1`, divergence `d > 1`:* The displacement has `w‚ÇÅ > 0`, and `o‚ÇÅ = w‚ÇÅ` with `zpd(o, w)` defined at some position `d > 1` (the operands agree at position 1 but disagree at a later position). TumblerSub zeros all positions before `d`: `r·µ¢ = 0` for `1 ‚â§ i < d`. In particular `r‚ÇÅ = 0`, so the result has a zero first component and is not in S. Counterexample: `o = [5, 3]`, `w = [5, 1]` (action point `k = 1`, divergence `d = 2`). TumblerSub yields `r = [0, 3 - 1] = [0, 2]` ‚Äî here `3 - 1 = 2` is NAT-sub's conditional-closure and right-inverse clauses on the naturals `3, 1 ‚àà ‚Ñï` with `3 ‚â• 1`. We have `[0, 2] ‚àà T` (confirming the T-closure claim) but `[0, 2] ‚àâ S ‚à™ Z`. This sub-case arises when `o` and `w` share a leading prefix and disagree somewhere beyond it ‚Äî the subtraction produces a displacement-like tumbler with leading zeros rather than a valid ordinal position.

*Case `k = 1`, no divergence (`o` and `w` zero-padded equal):* The displacement has `w‚ÇÅ > 0`, and the zero-padded sequences agree at every position ‚Äî TumblerSub's Definition places this in its no-divergence branch, under which no divergence position `d` exists and the result is the zero tumbler of length `L`. Under `#w ‚â§ m`, NAT-order's trichotomy on the length pair `(#o, #w) = (m, #w)` leaves sub-cases (Œ±) `#o = #w = m` with `L = m` or (Œ≥) `#w < m` with `L = m`; sub-case (Œ≥) is excluded because `o ‚àà S` gives `o·µ¢ > 0` for all `1 ‚â§ i ‚â§ m` while the zero-padded extension of `w` sets `w·µ¢ = 0` for `i > #w` (with `0 ‚àà ‚Ñï` by NAT-zero), forcing a divergence at position `#w + 1 ‚â§ m` in contradiction of the no-divergence hypothesis. The pair therefore lies in sub-case (Œ±), giving `#o = #w = m` and `L = m`, and zero-padded agreement reduces to componentwise equality `o·µ¢ = w·µ¢` for `1 ‚â§ i ‚â§ m`, i.e., `o = w`. TumblerSub then produces `o ‚äñ w = [0, ..., 0]` of length `L = m`; every component is `0`, so `Zero(o ‚äñ w)` holds by TA-Pos's universal defining clause for `Zero`, whence `o ‚äñ w ‚àà Z`. For example, `o = w = [1, 2]` gives `o ‚äñ w = [0, 0] ‚àà Z` ‚Äî a sentinel result recording that the two positions coincide, without any intervening shift.

For single-component ordinals, the `d > 1` sub-case cannot arise (there is only one position), and `‚äñ` gives closure in S ‚à™ Z: `[x] ‚äñ [n]` is `[x - n] ‚àà S` when `x > n`, or `[0] ‚àà Z` when `x = n` (a sentinel, TA6).

In every case, the result lies in T. The subspace identifier, held as structural context outside the operands, is never modified by either operation. TA7a holds. ‚àé

The restriction to element-local displacements is necessary. An unrestricted displacement whose action point falls at the subspace-identifier position could produce an address in a different subspace ‚Äî TA7a cannot hold for arbitrary `w`.
