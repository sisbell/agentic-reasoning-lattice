## Spans

By **Definition (Span)**, a span denotes a contiguous range of tumblers from a start address up to but not including the displaced endpoint. The form of `ℓ` depends on the hierarchical level at which the span operates, because the action point of `ℓ` must match the level of the start address `s`.

Nelson makes spans self-describing at every hierarchical level: "A digit of 'one' may be used to designate all of a given version, all versions of a given document, all works of a given author, all documents in a given project, all documents on a given server — or the entire docuverse." The "1-position convention" exploits T5: because subtrees are contiguous, a span whose start is a high-level prefix and whose length reaches to the next sibling captures exactly that subtree's content.

And a span's *content* may be empty — the range populated by nothing at present — yet the span itself remains a valid address-set: "A span that contains nothing today may at a later time contain a million documents." Throughout this document, *span* denotes the address-set `span(s, ℓ) = {t ∈ T : s ≤ t < s ⊕ ℓ}` fixed by Definition (Span) — the range determined by the endpoints — and *a span's content* denotes the documents populating that range at a given moment. The two are distinct: T12(b) below will show that the address-set `span(s, ℓ)` is always non-empty (it contains at least `s`), whereas the content populating that range is a question about the current state of the system, not about the tumbler algebra, and may indeed be empty.

**T12 (SpanWellDefinedness).** For any `(s, ℓ)` satisfying the preconditions of Definition (Span), the set `span(s, ℓ)` has three properties: its upper bound `s ⊕ ℓ` exists in `T`, the set is non-empty (with `s` always a member), and it is order-convex under T1 — there is no tumbler between two members that is not itself a member.

*Proof.* Let `(s, ℓ)` satisfy the preconditions of Definition (Span): `s ∈ T`, `ℓ ∈ T`, `Pos(ℓ)`, and the action point `k` of `ℓ` satisfies `k ≤ #s`. Write `S = span(s, ℓ) = {t ∈ T : s ≤ t < s ⊕ ℓ}` — the set fixed by Definition (Span).

*(a) Endpoint existence.* The preconditions of Definition (Span) supply `s ∈ T`, `ℓ ∈ T`, `Pos(ℓ)`, and `k ≤ #s`, where `k` is the action point of `ℓ`. These are precisely the four preconditions of TA0 under the instantiation `(a, w) := (s, ℓ)` — the two membership conditions `s ∈ T` and `ℓ ∈ T`, and the two non-membership conditions (the displacement `ℓ` is positive and its action point does not exceed the length of the base `s`) — so TA0 guarantees `s ⊕ ℓ ∈ T`. The set `S` is therefore well-defined — its upper bound exists in `T`.

*(b) Non-emptiness.* We show `s ∈ S` by verifying both defining conditions. First, `s ≤ s` holds by reflexivity of `≤` (from T1: `a ≤ b` iff `a < b ∨ a = b`, and `s = s`). Second, the preconditions of Definition (Span) supply `s ∈ T`, `ℓ ∈ T`, `Pos(ℓ)`, and `k ≤ #s` — precisely TA-strict's four preconditions under the instantiation `(a, w) := (s, ℓ)` — so TA-strict gives `s ⊕ ℓ > s`, i.e., `s < s ⊕ ℓ`. Both conditions hold, so `s ∈ S` and the set contains at least one element.

*(c) Contiguity.* We show that `S` is order-convex: for any `a, c ∈ S` and `b ∈ T` with `a ≤ b ≤ c`, we have `b ∈ S`. By T1, `<` is a strict total order on `T`, and transitivity of `<` is established by T1(c). Transitivity of `≤` follows: if `x ≤ y` and `y ≤ z`, then by case analysis on the disjunctions (`x < y ∨ x = y` and `y < z ∨ y = z`), the four combinations each yield `x ≤ z` — the (<,<) case composes `x < y` and `y < z` via T1(c) to give `x < z`; the (<,=) case substitutes `y = z` into `x < y` to give `x < z`; the (=,<) case substitutes `x = y` into `y < z` to give `x < z`; and the (=,=) case chains `x = y` and `y = z` to give `x = z`.

From `a ∈ S` we have `s ≤ a`. Combined with `a ≤ b`, transitivity of `≤` gives `s ≤ b` — the first defining condition of membership in `S`.

From `c ∈ S` we have `c < s ⊕ ℓ`. Since `b ≤ c`, either `b = c` — in which case `b < s ⊕ ℓ` immediately — or `b < c`, in which case transitivity of `<` (T1(c)) with `c < s ⊕ ℓ` gives `b < s ⊕ ℓ`. In either case, `b < s ⊕ ℓ` — the second defining condition of membership in `S`.

Together, `s ≤ b` and `b < s ⊕ ℓ`, so `b ∈ S`. No tumbler lying between two members of `S` falls outside `S`. ∎

We reserve T5 for the distinct claim that *prefix-defined* sets are contiguous — a non-trivial property of the lexicographic order.

*Formal Contract:*
- *Premise:* `(s, ℓ)` satisfies the preconditions of Definition (Span) — equivalently, `s ∈ T`, `ℓ ∈ T`, `Pos(ℓ)`, and `actionPoint(ℓ) ≤ #s`.
- *Depends:* Definition (Span) — fixes the symbol `span(s, ℓ) = {t ∈ T : s ≤ t < s ⊕ ℓ}` and the preconditions on `(s, ℓ)`, which this corollary takes as its premise and whose theorem-level consequences it establishes; the proof opens by writing `S = span(s, ℓ) = {t ∈ T : s ≤ t < s ⊕ ℓ}` as "the set fixed by Definition (Span)", so every later reference to `S` is a reference to that symbol. T0 (CarrierSetDefinition) — supplies the carrier `T` and the length operator `#`. `∈ T` appears in T12's premise at `s ∈ T` and `ℓ ∈ T`, in postcondition (a) at `s ⊕ ℓ ∈ T`, in postcondition (c)'s quantifier range at `b ∈ T` ("for all `a, c ∈ span(s, ℓ)` and `b ∈ T`"), and in the proof's closing phrase of part (a) "its upper bound exists in `T`" which restates postcondition (a); `#` appears in the premise at `actionPoint(ℓ) ≤ #s` and in the proof's restatement of that premise as "the action point `k` of `ℓ` satisfies `k ≤ #s`" and again in parts (a) and (b) where `k ≤ #s` is invoked to discharge the preconditions of TA0 and TA-strict — each a direct length lookup. T12 writes T0's vocabulary directly in its own premise, postconditions (a) and (c), and proof, so it cites T0 directly in parallel with T1 (which cites T0 for `#a` and component projection `aₖ` on `a ∈ T`), Span (which cites T0 for `T` and `#` in its pair-components, precondition, and defining set), TA0 (which cites T0 for `∈ T` and `#` in its precondition and postcondition lists), and TA-strict (which cites T0 for `∈ T` in its quantifier range and `#` in its precondition) rather than inheriting through any of them. The narrow coverage of each alternative handle justifies the direct citation: Span's postcondition fixes the set symbol `span(s, ℓ) = {t ∈ T : s ≤ t < s ⊕ ℓ}` but does not re-export `T` as a handle for a fresh ambient element `b` ranging over the carrier outside that set, so postcondition (c)'s `b ∈ T` is not sourced through Span; TA0's re-export handle covers only the two postconditions `a ⊕ w ∈ T` and `#(a ⊕ w) = #w` specialised at the forward-add pair, which licenses postcondition (a)'s `s ⊕ ℓ ∈ T` as a handle lookup but neither the premise's `s ∈ T`, `ℓ ∈ T`, and `#s` (which TA0 writes in its own precondition list but does not re-export) nor postcondition (c)'s `b ∈ T` (which ranges over an ambient element disjoint from TA0's forward-add pair); and TA-strict's handle re-exports only the strict inequality `a ⊕ w > a`, covering none of T12's `∈ T` or `#` usages. This matches the per-step citation discipline used in Span, TA0, and TA-strict: a property that writes a foundation's vocabulary directly in its own statement cites that foundation directly, whether or not some intermediate handle also cites it. T1 (LexicographicOrder) — invoked at three steps in the proof. At the start of (b) Non-emptiness, the reflexivity of `≤` ("`s ≤ s` holds by reflexivity of `≤` (from T1: `a ≤ b` iff `a < b ∨ a = b`, and `s = s`)") rests on T1's definition of `≤` as `< ∨ =`. At the start of (c) Contiguity, transitivity of `≤` is constructed from T1's strict total order ("By T1, `<` is a strict total order on `T`, and transitivity of `<` is established by T1(c)") and the enumerated case analysis "the four combinations each yield `x ≤ z` — the (<,<) case composes `x < y` and `y < z` via T1(c) to give `x < z`; the (<,=) case substitutes `y = z` into `x < y` to give `x < z`; the (=,<) case substitutes `x = y` into `y < z` to give `x < z`; and the (=,=) case chains `x = y` and `y = z` to give `x = z`" invokes T1(c) once for the (<,<) combination and discharges the remaining three combinations by substitution of equality, together licensing the chain `s ≤ a ≤ b ⟹ s ≤ b`. At the close of (c), T1(c) is invoked once more ("`b < c`, in which case transitivity of `<` (T1(c)) with `c < s ⊕ ℓ` gives `b < s ⊕ ℓ`") to compose the strict-inequality chain that yields the second defining condition `b < s ⊕ ℓ`. TA0 (WellDefinedAddition) — invoked at (a) Endpoint existence: "These are precisely the four preconditions of TA0 under the instantiation `(a, w) := (s, ℓ)` — the two membership conditions `s ∈ T` and `ℓ ∈ T`, and the two non-membership conditions (the displacement `ℓ` is positive and its action point does not exceed the length of the base `s`) — so TA0 guarantees `s ⊕ ℓ ∈ T`" — TA0 supplies the postcondition `s ⊕ ℓ ∈ T` from the preconditions `s ∈ T`, `ℓ ∈ T`, `Pos(ℓ)`, and `k ≤ #s` inherited from Definition (Span), and so licenses the upper-bound expression `s ⊕ ℓ` appearing in the definition of `S`. TA-strict (StrictIncrease) — invoked at (b) Non-emptiness: "the preconditions of Definition (Span) supply `s ∈ T`, `ℓ ∈ T`, `Pos(ℓ)`, and `k ≤ #s` — precisely TA-strict's four preconditions under the instantiation `(a, w) := (s, ℓ)` — so TA-strict gives `s ⊕ ℓ > s`, i.e., `s < s ⊕ ℓ`" — TA-strict supplies the strict inequality `s < s ⊕ ℓ` that establishes the second defining condition `s < s ⊕ ℓ` of membership of `s` in `S`.
- *Postconditions:* (a) `s ⊕ ℓ ∈ T` (endpoint exists, by TA0). (b) `s ∈ span(s, ℓ)` (non-empty, by T1's `≤`-reflexivity for `s ≤ s` and TA-strict for `s < s ⊕ ℓ`). (c) `span(s, ℓ)` is order-convex under T1 (for all `a, c ∈ span(s, ℓ)` and `b ∈ T`, `a ≤ b ≤ c` implies `b ∈ span(s, ℓ)`).
