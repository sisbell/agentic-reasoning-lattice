## Spans

By **Definition (Span)**, a span denotes a contiguous range of tumblers from a start address up to but not including the displaced endpoint. The form of `‚Ąď` depends on the hierarchical level at which the span operates, because the action point of `‚Ąď` must match the level of the start address `s`.

Nelson makes spans self-describing at every hierarchical level: "A digit of 'one' may be used to designate all of a given version, all versions of a given document, all works of a given author, all documents in a given project, all documents on a given server ‚ÄĒ or the entire docuverse." The "1-position convention" exploits T5: because subtrees are contiguous, a span whose start is a high-level prefix and whose length reaches to the next sibling captures exactly that subtree's content.

And a span may be empty ‚ÄĒ populated by nothing at present ‚ÄĒ yet valid: "A span that contains nothing today may at a later time contain a million documents." The range is determined by the endpoints; what is actually stored within that range is a question about the current state of the system, not about the tumbler algebra.

**T12 (SpanWellDefinedness).** For any `(s, ‚Ąď)` satisfying the preconditions of Definition (Span), the set `span(s, ‚Ąď)` has three properties: its upper bound `s ‚äē ‚Ąď` exists in `T`, the set is non-empty (with `s` always a member), and it is order-convex under T1 ‚ÄĒ there is no tumbler between two members that is not itself a member.

*Proof.* Let `(s, ‚Ąď)` satisfy the preconditions of Definition (Span): `s ‚ąą T`, `‚Ąď ‚ąą T`, `Pos(‚Ąď)`, and the action point `k` of `‚Ąď` satisfies `k ‚Č§ #s`. Write `S = span(s, ‚Ąď) = {t ‚ąą T : s ‚Č§ t < s ‚äē ‚Ąď}` ‚ÄĒ the set fixed by Definition (Span). We note that `k ‚Č§ #s` is equivalent to requiring that the number of leading zeros in `‚Ąď` ‚ÄĒ which is `k ‚ąí 1`, since the action point is the first nonzero position ‚ÄĒ be strictly less than `#s`: the two are restatements of the same arithmetic condition.

*(a) Endpoint existence.* The preconditions of Definition (Span) supply `Pos(‚Ąď)` and `k ‚Č§ #s`, where `k` is the action point of `‚Ąď`. These are precisely the preconditions of TA0 (the displacement `‚Ąď` is positive and its action point does not exceed the length of the base `s`), so TA0 guarantees `s ‚äē ‚Ąď ‚ąą T`. The set `S` is therefore well-defined ‚ÄĒ its upper bound exists in `T`.

*(b) Non-emptiness.* We show `s ‚ąą S` by verifying both defining conditions. First, `s ‚Č§ s` holds by reflexivity of `‚Č§` (from T1: `a ‚Č§ b` iff `a < b ‚ą® a = b`, and `s = s`). Second, since `Pos(‚Ąď)` and `k ‚Č§ #s`, TA-strict gives `s ‚äē ‚Ąď > s`, i.e., `s < s ‚äē ‚Ąď`. Both conditions hold, so `s ‚ąą S` and the set contains at least one element.

*(c) Contiguity.* We show that `S` is order-convex: for any `a, c ‚ąą S` and `b ‚ąą T` with `a ‚Č§ b ‚Č§ c`, we have `b ‚ąą S`. By T1, `<` is a strict total order on `T`, and transitivity of `<` is established by T1(c). Transitivity of `‚Č§` follows: if `x ‚Č§ y` and `y ‚Č§ z`, then by case analysis on the disjunctions (`x < y ‚ą® x = y` and `y < z ‚ą® y = z`), each combination yields `x ‚Č§ z` ‚ÄĒ the two strict cases compose via T1(c), and the equality cases are immediate by substitution.

From `a ‚ąą S` we have `s ‚Č§ a`. Combined with `a ‚Č§ b`, transitivity of `‚Č§` gives `s ‚Č§ b` ‚ÄĒ the first defining condition of membership in `S`.

From `c ‚ąą S` we have `c < s ‚äē ‚Ąď`. Since `b ‚Č§ c`, either `b = c` ‚ÄĒ in which case `b < s ‚äē ‚Ąď` immediately ‚ÄĒ or `b < c`, in which case transitivity of `<` (T1(c)) with `c < s ‚äē ‚Ąď` gives `b < s ‚äē ‚Ąď`. In either case, `b < s ‚äē ‚Ąď` ‚ÄĒ the second defining condition of membership in `S`.

Together, `s ‚Č§ b` and `b < s ‚äē ‚Ąď`, so `b ‚ąą S`. No tumbler lying between two members of `S` falls outside `S`. ‚ąé

We reserve T5 for the distinct claim that *prefix-defined* sets are contiguous ‚ÄĒ a non-trivial property of the lexicographic order.

*Formal Contract:*
- *Premise:* `(s, ‚Ąď)` satisfies the preconditions of Definition (Span) ‚ÄĒ equivalently, `s ‚ąą T`, `‚Ąď ‚ąą T`, `Pos(‚Ąď)`, and `actionPoint(‚Ąď) ‚Č§ #s`.
- *Depends:* Definition (Span) ‚ÄĒ fixes the symbol `span(s, ‚Ąď) = {t ‚ąą T : s ‚Č§ t < s ‚äē ‚Ąď}` and the preconditions on `(s, ‚Ąď)`, which this corollary takes as its premise and whose theorem-level consequences it establishes; the proof opens by writing `S = span(s, ‚Ąď) = {t ‚ąą T : s ‚Č§ t < s ‚äē ‚Ąď}` as "the set fixed by Definition (Span)", so every later reference to `S` is a reference to that symbol. T1 (LexicographicOrder) ‚ÄĒ invoked at three steps in the proof. At the start of (b) Non-emptiness, the reflexivity of `‚Č§` ("`s ‚Č§ s` holds by reflexivity of `‚Č§` (from T1: `a ‚Č§ b` iff `a < b ‚ą® a = b`, and `s = s`)") rests on T1's definition of `‚Č§` as `< ‚ą® =`. At the start of (c) Contiguity, transitivity of `‚Č§` is constructed from T1's strict total order ("By T1, `<` is a strict total order on `T`, and transitivity of `<` is established by T1(c)") and the case analysis "`x ‚Č§ y` and `y ‚Č§ z`, then by case analysis on the disjunctions ‚Ä¶ each combination yields `x ‚Č§ z` ‚ÄĒ the two strict cases compose via T1(c)" composes T1(c) with the equality cases to license the chain `s ‚Č§ a ‚Č§ b ‚üĻ s ‚Č§ b`. At the close of (c), T1(c) is invoked once more ("`b < c`, in which case transitivity of `<` (T1(c)) with `c < s ‚äē ‚Ąď` gives `b < s ‚äē ‚Ąď`") to compose the strict-inequality chain that yields the second defining condition `b < s ‚äē ‚Ąď`. TA0 (WellDefinedAddition) ‚ÄĒ invoked at (a) Endpoint existence: "These are precisely the preconditions of TA0 (the displacement `‚Ąď` is positive and its action point does not exceed the length of the base `s`), so TA0 guarantees `s ‚äē ‚Ąď ‚ąą T`" ‚ÄĒ TA0 supplies the postcondition `s ‚äē ‚Ąď ‚ąą T` from the preconditions `Pos(‚Ąď)` and `k ‚Č§ #s` inherited from Definition (Span), and so licenses the upper-bound expression `s ‚äē ‚Ąď` appearing in the definition of `S`. TA-strict (StrictIncrease) ‚ÄĒ invoked at (b) Non-emptiness: "since `Pos(‚Ąď)` and `k ‚Č§ #s`, TA-strict gives `s ‚äē ‚Ąď > s`, i.e., `s < s ‚äē ‚Ąď`" ‚ÄĒ TA-strict supplies the strict inequality `s < s ‚äē ‚Ąď` that establishes the second defining condition `s < s ‚äē ‚Ąď` of membership of `s` in `S`.
- *Postconditions:* (a) `s ‚äē ‚Ąď ‚ąą T` (endpoint exists, by TA0). (b) `s ‚ąą span(s, ‚Ąď)` (non-empty, by TA-strict). (c) `span(s, ‚Ąď)` is order-convex under T1 (for all `a, c ‚ąą span(s, ‚Ąď)` and `b ‚ąą T`, `a ‚Č§ b ‚Č§ c` implies `b ‚ąą span(s, ‚Ąď)`).
