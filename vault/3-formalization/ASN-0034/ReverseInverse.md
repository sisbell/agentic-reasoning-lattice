**ReverseInverse (ReverseInverse).** `(A a, w : a ‚â• w ‚àß Pos(w) ‚àß k = #a ‚àß #w = k ‚àß (A i : 1 ‚â§ i < k : a·µ¢ = 0) : (a ‚äñ w) ‚äï w = a)`, where `k` is the action point of `w`.

*Proof.* We show that subtracting `w` from `a` and then adding `w` back recovers `a` exactly, under conditions that make the two operations mutually inverse. Throughout, `k` denotes the action point of `w` ‚Äî the least position with `w‚Çñ > 0` ‚Äî so by definition `w·µ¢ = 0` for all `i < k`.

**Step 1: the structure of `y = a ‚äñ w`.** By TumblerSub, subtraction scans `a` and `w` for the first position where they differ, zero-padding the shorter to a common length `L` furnished by NAT-order's trichotomy on the length pair `(#a, #w)` ‚Äî (Œ±) `#a = #w` with `L = #a`, (Œ≤) `#a < #w` with `L = #w`, or (Œ≥) `#w < #a` with `L = #a` ‚Äî rather than by a primitive binary-maximum operator on ‚Ñï, reproducing TumblerSub's own length-pair dispatch at the same operand pair. The preconditions `k = #a` and `#w = k` give `#a = k = #w`, placing `(#a, #w)` in sub-case (Œ±), so `L = #a = k` and the padding region is empty ‚Äî no padding is needed. At each position `i < k`, both `a·µ¢ = 0` (by the zero-prefix precondition) and `w·µ¢ = 0` (by definition of action point), so the operands agree before position `k`.

NAT-order's trichotomy at the component pair `(a‚Çñ, w‚Çñ)` enumerates three outcomes ‚Äî `a‚Çñ < w‚Çñ`, `a‚Çñ = w‚Çñ`, or `a‚Çñ > w‚Çñ` ‚Äî of which we rule out the first. Under the hypothetical `a‚Çñ < w‚Çñ`, the pre-`k` agreement `a·µ¢ = w·µ¢ = 0` for `1 ‚â§ i < k` (just established from the zero-prefix precondition and the action-point definition) together with the lengths `k ‚â§ #a` and `k ‚â§ #w` (read off from `k = #a` and `#w = k`) witness `a < w` via T1 case (i) at divergence position `k`; this contradicts the precondition `a ‚â• w`. Two cases remain. If `a‚Çñ = w‚Çñ`, then `a` and `w` agree at every position ‚Äî there are no positions beyond `k` since both have length `k` ‚Äî and TumblerSub produces the zero tumbler of length `k`. If `a‚Çñ > w‚Çñ`, then `k` is the first divergence, and TumblerSub produces `y·µ¢ = 0` for `i < k`, `y‚Çñ = a‚Çñ - w‚Çñ > 0` (the membership `a‚Çñ - w‚Çñ ‚àà ‚Ñï` by NAT-sub's conditional closure under `a‚Çñ ‚â• w‚Çñ` from NAT-order; the strict positivity `a‚Çñ - w‚Çñ > 0` by NAT-sub's strict-positivity clause under `a‚Çñ > w‚Çñ`), and no components beyond `k` (since `L = k` by the sub-case (Œ±) dispatch above). In either case, `y` has three properties we record for later use:

- (Y1) `#y = k`
- (Y2) `y·µ¢ = 0` for all `1 ‚â§ i < k`
- (Y3) `y‚Çñ = a‚Çñ - w‚Çñ`

**Step 2: TA4 applies to `y` and `w`.** TA4 (Partial inverse) requires six preconditions at the invocation `TA4's a := y`: `y ‚àà T` (by TumblerSub's carrier-membership postcondition on `a ‚äñ w` ‚Äî the only precondition discharged through a cross-Step 1 postcondition rather than directly from the outer preconditions or Y1/Y2, applicable since the outer preconditions `a ‚àà T`, `w ‚àà T`, and `a ‚â• w` discharge TumblerSub's three preconditions at the same operand pair, yielding `y = a ‚äñ w ‚àà T`), `w ‚àà T` (from the outer precondition of ReverseInverse), `Pos(w)` (given), `k = #y` (by Y1), `#w = k` (given), and `(A i : 1 ‚â§ i < k : y·µ¢ = 0)` (by Y2). **Step 3: `y ‚äï w = a` by contradiction via TA3-strict.** Assume for contradiction that `y ‚äï w ‚âÝ a`. We verify the preconditions of TA3-strict (Order preservation under subtraction, strict), which requires strict ordering between two tumblers, both `‚â• w`, and equal length.

*Equal length.* By the result-length identity (TumblerAdd), `#(y ‚äï w) = #w`. The preconditions give `#w = k` and `k = #a`, so `#(y ‚äï w) = #a`.

*`a ‚â• w`.* Given as a precondition of ReverseInverse.

*`y ‚äï w > w`.* By TumblerAdd, for `i < k`: `(y ‚äï w)·µ¢ = y·µ¢ = 0 = w·µ¢` (using Y2 and the definition of action point). At position `k`: `(y ‚äï w)‚Çñ = y‚Çñ + w‚Çñ`. *Strict ordering between `y ‚äï w` and `a`.* By T1 (trichotomy), since `y ‚äï w ‚âÝ a`, exactly one of `y ‚äï w < a` or `y ‚äï w > a` holds. We derive a contradiction from each.

*Case `y ‚äï w > a`:* We have `a < y ‚äï w`, `a ‚â• w`, `y ‚äï w ‚â• w` (established above, in fact strict), and `#a = #(y ‚äï w)`. TA3-strict gives `a ‚äñ w < (y ‚äï w) ‚äñ w`. Both cases are impossible, so the assumption `y ‚äï w ‚âÝ a` is false. Therefore `(a ‚äñ w) ‚äï w = a`. ‚àé
