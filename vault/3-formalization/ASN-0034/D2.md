**D2 (DisplacementUnique).** Under D1's preconditions (a < b, divergence(a, b) ≤ #a, #a ≤ #b), for any w ∈ T with Pos(w) and actionPoint(w) ≤ #a such that a ⊕ w = b, we have w = b ⊖ a.

*Proof.* We show that any displacement w carrying a to b must equal the canonical displacement b ⊖ a. The argument has two steps: we produce a second witness for the same equation, then apply left cancellation to conclude the two witnesses are identical.

**Step 1: a second witness.** By D1 (DisplacementRoundTrip), the preconditions a < b, divergence(a, b) ≤ #a, and #a ≤ #b guarantee that a ⊕ (b ⊖ a) = b. So both w and b ⊖ a, when added to a, yield b.

**Step 2: establishing TA-LC's preconditions.** To apply left cancellation (TA-LC), we must verify that both additions satisfy TA0 — that is, both displacements are positive and their action points fall within #a.

For w: the hypotheses Pos(w) and actionPoint(w) ≤ #a are furnished directly by the statement of D2, so TA0's preconditions are satisfied without appeal to the equation a ⊕ w = b. The addition a ⊕ w is therefore well-defined and equals b by assumption. TumblerAdd's result-length identity — the postcondition `#(a ⊕ w) = #w` stating that the length of the sum is determined entirely by the displacement — applied to the now-well-defined `a ⊕ w` yields `#w = #(a ⊕ w) = #b`, the second equality substituting the hypothesis `a ⊕ w = b`. This pins `#w = #b` through the equation rather than as an unstated precondition — a length routing the conclusion `w = b ⊖ a` of Step 3 relies on implicitly but which no precondition of D2 otherwise furnishes.

For b ⊖ a: let k = divergence(a, b). We eliminate Divergence case (ii) by addressing each of its sub-cases as Divergence states them. Sub-case (ii-a) — `#a < #b` with `divergence(a, b) = #a + 1`, whence `k = #a + 1` — is foreclosed by combining the sub-case value with the precondition `k ≤ #a`: substitution yields `#a + 1 ≤ #a`, which against NAT-addcompat's strict successor inequality `#a < #a + 1` is refuted by NAT-order's trichotomy at the pair `(#a, #a + 1)` — its exactly-one clause excluding both `#a + 1 < #a` and `#a + 1 = #a` once `#a < #a + 1` is fixed. Sub-case (ii-b) — `#b < #a` with `divergence(a, b) = #b + 1` — is foreclosed directly by the precondition `#a ≤ #b`: NAT-order's trichotomy at the length pair `(#a, #b)` rules out `#b < #a` co-holding with either disjunct of `#a ≤ #b` (the strict `#a < #b` or the equality `#a = #b`). The remaining situation `#a = #b` (the equality disjunct of the precondition) is excluded from case (ii) at the case-hypothesis level, since Divergence case (ii) requires `#a ≠ #b`. Case (ii) is therefore eliminated on each of its sub-cases and at its case hypothesis, placing us in case (i): `k ≤ #a ∧ k ≤ #b` (the shared-bound conjunction Divergence case (i) states in place of any primitive binary-minimum on ℕ), with aᵢ = bᵢ for all i < k, and aₖ < bₖ at the divergence point (the strict inequality from a < b at the divergence point, by T1). TumblerSub's component formulas and action-point identification are parameterised by `zpd(b, a)`, not by `divergence(a, b)`, so to apply them at `k` we must identify the two indices. The ZPD–Divergence relationship (ZPD), instantiated at the single operand pair `(b, a)` to match `zpd(b, a)`'s operand order, gives `zpd(b, a) = divergence(b, a)`; Divergence's symmetry postcondition `divergence(a, b) = divergence(b, a)` then bridges this to `k = divergence(a, b)`, yielding `zpd(b, a) = divergence(b, a) = divergence(a, b) = k` without instantiating the ZPD–Divergence relationship at two different operand pairs. Before invoking TumblerSub's component formulas at this `k`, we discharge TumblerSub's first precondition `a ≥ w` — which, at the invocation `b ⊖ a` with minuend `b` and subtrahend `a`, reads `b ≥ a` — from the precondition `a < b` via T1's strict-to-weak weakening: T1's abbreviation block unfolds `b ≥ a` as `a ≤ b`, which further unfolds as `a < b ∨ a = b`, and the precondition `a < b` fixes the left disjunct, whence `b ≥ a`. With `b ≥ a` discharged and `a, b ∈ T` by hypothesis, TumblerSub's preconditions at minuend `b` and subtrahend `a` are satisfied, and its postcondition supplies `b ⊖ a ∈ T` — the carrier-membership conclusion TA-LC's preconditions require at `y = b ⊖ a` (and TA0's preconditions at the end of Step 2 likewise require as `w ∈ T` with `w = b ⊖ a`), matching the first of D1's parallel three-property check on `w`. TumblerSub at `k = zpd(b, a)` yields `(b ⊖ a)ᵢ = 0` for i < k (the agreement prefix copied as zeros) and `(b ⊖ a)ₖ = bₖ − aₖ`, which lies in ℕ by NAT-sub (conditional closure, with precondition `bₖ ≥ aₖ` from `bₖ > aₖ` via NAT-order) and satisfies `(b ⊖ a)ₖ ≥ 1` by NAT-sub (strict positivity, with precondition `bₖ > aₖ`). Hence Pos(b ⊖ a). TumblerSub's conditional postcondition gives actionPoint(b ⊖ a) = zpd(b, a), applicable because zpd(b, a) is defined (established above as zpd(b, a) = k); composed with that established equality, this yields actionPoint(b ⊖ a) = k. The conditional postcondition itself rests on ActionPoint's defining minimum-formula actionPoint(b ⊖ a) = min({i : 1 ≤ i ≤ #(b ⊖ a) ∧ (b ⊖ a)ᵢ ≠ 0}) applied to b ⊖ a — every component before position k is zero by TumblerSub's component formulas, and (b ⊖ a)ₖ > 0 by Pos(b ⊖ a) (established above), so the minimum is k; this matches D0's and D1's routing of the analogous action-point identification through ActionPoint's definition. Since k ≤ #a, TA0's preconditions are satisfied.

**Step 3: cancellation.** From the hypothesis a ⊕ w = b and Step 1's a ⊕ (b ⊖ a) = b, we obtain:

  a ⊕ w = a ⊕ (b ⊖ a)

Both sides are well-defined (Step 2). By TA-LC (LeftCancellation) — which states that a ⊕ x = a ⊕ y implies x = y when both additions satisfy TA0 — we conclude w = b ⊖ a.  ∎

D1 and D2 together characterize the displacement completely: D1 says b ⊖ a recovers b, D2 says nothing else does.

When a = b, no displacement is needed; the degenerate case is handled separately since b ⊖ a produces the zero tumbler and a ⊕ (b ⊖ a) is not well-formed (TA0 requires Pos(w)). D0 ensures the displacement is well-defined; D1 ensures the round-trip is faithful when additionally #a ≤ #b.

*Worked example.* Consider a = [1, 2, 3] and b = [1, 5, 1]. We have #a = #b = 3.

*D0 check.* divergence(a, b) = 2, since a₁ = b₁ = 1 and a₂ = 2 ≠ 5 = b₂. The condition k = 2 ≤ #a = 3 is satisfied.

*Displacement.* By TumblerSub, w = b ⊖ a: w₁ = 0 (i < k), w₂ = 5 − 2 = 3 (i = k, via NAT-sub conditional closure and right-inverse on the naturals 5, 2 with 5 ≥ 2), w₃ = 1 (i > k, from b). So w = [0, 3, 1].

*Round-trip.* The action point of w is 2. By TumblerAdd, a ⊕ [0, 3, 1]: position 1 copies a₁ = 1, position 2 computes 2 + 3 = 5, position 3 copies w₃ = 1. Result: [1, 5, 1] = b.  ✓

*Uniqueness check.* Suppose some other w' also satisfies a ⊕ w' = b = [1, 5, 1]. By D2, w' = b ⊖ a = [0, 3, 1] = w. There is no alternative displacement.

The generalization to #a < #b can be seen with a' = [1, 2] and the same b = [1, 5, 1]. Here #a' = 2 < 3 = #b, the divergence is still 2 (a'₂ = 2 ≠ 5 = b₂), and k = 2 ≤ #a' = 2 satisfies D0. TumblerSub (zero-padding a' to length 3) gives the same w = [0, 3, 1] of length 3. The round-trip a' ⊕ [0, 3, 1] produces [1, 5, 1] = b — the result has length #w = 3 = #b, matching the target.

*Formal Contract:*
- *Preconditions:* a ∈ T, b ∈ T, w ∈ T, a < b, divergence(a, b) ≤ #a, #a ≤ #b, Pos(w), actionPoint(w) ≤ #a, a ⊕ w = b
- *Depends:* D1 (DisplacementRoundTrip) — Step 1 invokes D1 to supply the second witness `a ⊕ (b ⊖ a) = b`, which is the equation that left cancellation will be applied to in Step 3. T1 (LexicographicOrder) — invoked in Step 2 at two distinct sites for two distinct roles. (i) *Divergence-point strict inequality*: derives `bₖ > aₖ` at the divergence position from `a < b`, which establishes `(b ⊖ a)ₖ ≥ 1` and hence the positivity of `b ⊖ a`. (ii) *Strict-to-weak weakening for TumblerSub's first precondition*: T1's abbreviation block unfolds `b ≥ a` as `a ≤ b`, which further unfolds as `a < b ∨ a = b`; the precondition `a < b` fixes the left disjunct, whence `b ≥ a` — the strict-to-weak weakening supplying the precondition TumblerSub requires at the invocation `b ⊖ a` (minuend `b`, subtrahend `a`). This discharge is consumed by the TumblerSub citation below before its component formulas are consumed in Step 2, matching D1's analogous `a < b implies b ≥ a` discharge at its parallel TumblerSub invocation. Divergence (Divergence) — Step 2 case-analyses Divergence's cases for the pair `(a, b)`: case (ii) is eliminated by addressing sub-cases (ii-a) and (ii-b) separately and excluding the `#a = #b` situation at the case-hypothesis level (since Divergence case (ii) requires `#a ≠ #b`). Sub-case (ii-a) — `#a < #b` with `divergence(a, b) = #a + 1`, whence `k = #a + 1` — is refuted against `k ≤ #a` via NAT-addcompat's `#a < #a + 1` and NAT-order's trichotomy at `(#a, #a + 1)`. Sub-case (ii-b) — `#b < #a` with `divergence(a, b) = #b + 1` — is refuted directly by the precondition `#a ≤ #b` via NAT-order's trichotomy at the length pair `(#a, #b)`. The elimination places the argument in case (i): `k ≤ #a ∧ k ≤ #b` (the shared-bound conjunction Divergence case (i) states in place of any primitive binary-minimum on ℕ), with the component-agreement predicate `aᵢ = bᵢ` for `i < k` and the strict inequality `aₖ < bₖ` (via T1 at the divergence point) available. Divergence's symmetry postcondition `divergence(a, b) = divergence(b, a)` bridges the operand-pair mismatch between the precondition's `k = divergence(a, b)` and the ZPD–Divergence relationship's natural instantiation at `(b, a)` (matching `zpd(b, a)`'s operand order), yielding `zpd(b, a) = divergence(b, a) = divergence(a, b) = k` without any operand swap on the ZPD side. ZPD (ZPD) — Step 2 invokes the ZPD–Divergence relationship, which in case (i) identifies `zpd(b, a) = divergence(b, a)` when instantiated at the operand pair `(b, a)` to match `zpd(b, a)`'s operand order; the onward identification with `k = divergence(a, b)` is supplied by Divergence's symmetry postcondition (cited above), not by re-instantiating the ZPD–Divergence relationship at a different operand pair. This chain — `zpd(b, a) = divergence(b, a) = divergence(a, b) = k` — is what licenses TumblerSub's component formulas and action-point identification — both parameterised by `zpd(b, a)` — at the index `k = divergence(a, b)` used throughout Step 2 (`(b ⊖ a)ₖ = bₖ − aₖ`, the agreement prefix zeros, and `actionPoint(b ⊖ a) = k`). TumblerSub (TumblerSub) — invoked in Step 2 at three sites: (i) its postcondition `a ⊖ w ∈ T`, instantiated at the operand pair `(b, a)`, supplies the carrier-membership conclusion `b ⊖ a ∈ T` — the membership premise TA-LC's preconditions require at `y = b ⊖ a` (and that TA0's preconditions at the end of Step 2 require as `w ∈ T` with `w = b ⊖ a`), matching the first of D1's parallel three-property check on `w`; (ii) its component formulas supply `(b ⊖ a)ᵢ = 0` for `i < k` and `(b ⊖ a)ₖ = bₖ − aₖ` at `k = zpd(b, a)`; (iii) its conditional postcondition supplies the action-point identification `actionPoint(b ⊖ a) = zpd(b, a)`. Its first precondition `a ≥ w` — at the invocation `b ⊖ a` (minuend `b`, subtrahend `a`), instantiating as `b ≥ a` — is discharged before consumption via T1's strict-to-weak weakening on the precondition `a < b` (enumerated at site (ii) of T1's Depends entry above): without this discharge, TumblerSub's carrier-membership, component formulas, and action-point identification would be consumed with an undischarged entry precondition, matching D1's analogous `a < b implies b ≥ a` discharge at its parallel TumblerSub invocation. Also invoked in the Worked example to compute `w = [0, 3, 1]`. TumblerAdd (TumblerAdd) — Step 2 invokes TumblerAdd's result-length identity (the postcondition `#(a ⊕ w) = #w`) on the well-defined addition `a ⊕ w` to derive `#w = #(a ⊕ w) = #b` — the second equality substituting the hypothesis `a ⊕ w = b`. This pins `#w = #b` through the equation rather than as an unstated precondition, supplying the length routing the conclusion `w = b ⊖ a` of Step 3 relies on implicitly: without the result-length identity, the conclusion's length equality `#w = #(b ⊖ a) = #b` (the length TumblerSub's dispatch assigns to `b ⊖ a` under `#a ≤ #b`) would rest on a length premise that D2's preconditions do not furnish. Also invoked in the Worked example to verify the round-trip `a ⊕ [0, 3, 1] = [1, 5, 1]`. ActionPoint (ActionPoint) — supplies the action-point function used in the precondition `actionPoint(w) ≤ #a` and in Step 2's claim that `b ⊖ a` has action point `k`. The proof's identification `actionPoint(b ⊖ a) = k` is the value TumblerSub's conditional postcondition `actionPoint(b ⊖ a) = zpd(b, a)` supplies, after composition with the established `zpd(b, a) = k`. The conditional postcondition rests on ActionPoint's defining minimum-formula `actionPoint(b ⊖ a) = min({i : 1 ≤ i ≤ #(b ⊖ a) ∧ (b ⊖ a)ᵢ ≠ 0})` applied to `b ⊖ a` — every component before position k is zero by TumblerSub's component formulas, and `(b ⊖ a)ₖ > 0` by Pos(b ⊖ a) (established above), so the minimum is k; this matches D0's and D1's routing of the analogous action-point identification through ActionPoint's definition. TA-Pos (PositiveTumbler) — supplies the positivity predicate `Pos(·)` invoked in the precondition `Pos(w)` and in Step 2's verification that `Pos(b ⊖ a)` follows from `(b ⊖ a)ₖ ≥ 1`. TA0 (WellDefinedAddition) — Step 2 verifies "TA0's preconditions" twice (once for `w` and once for `b ⊖ a`) so that both additions are well-defined; without TA0 the equation `a ⊕ w = a ⊕ (b ⊖ a)` of Step 3 would have no licensed source for "both sides are well-defined". TA-LC (LeftCancellation) — Step 3's concluding step: "By TA-LC (LeftCancellation) … we conclude w = b ⊖ a" — TA-LC supplies the cancellation rule that converts the equation `a ⊕ w = a ⊕ (b ⊖ a)` into `w = b ⊖ a`, which is the theorem's postcondition. NAT-sub (NatPartialSubtraction) — conditional closure and strict positivity discharge `(b ⊖ a)ₖ = bₖ − aₖ ∈ ℕ` and `(b ⊖ a)ₖ ≥ 1` in Step 2 (from `bₖ > aₖ`), supplying the positivity witness that feeds into TA-Pos; conditional closure and right-inverse discharge the Worked example's arithmetic step `w₂ = 5 − 2 = 3`. NAT-addcompat (NatAdditionOrderAndSuccessor) — supplies the strict successor inequality `#a < #a + 1` used in the sub-case (ii-a) elimination: combined with `#a + 1 ≤ #a` (from substituting the sub-case value `k = #a + 1` into the precondition `k ≤ #a`) it produces the conjunction that NAT-order's trichotomy at `(#a, #a + 1)` forecloses, refuting sub-case (ii-a). NAT-order (NatStrictTotalOrder) — invoked at three distinct ℕ-pairs, each an instance of the same trichotomy/order-definition axiom at distinct points. *Length pair `(#a, #a + 1)`*: trichotomy forecloses sub-case (ii-a) — once NAT-addcompat supplies `#a < #a + 1`, the exactly-one clause excludes both `#a + 1 < #a` and `#a + 1 = #a`, refuting `#a + 1 ≤ #a` obtained by substituting the sub-case value `k = #a + 1` into the precondition `k ≤ #a`. *Length pair `(#a, #b)`*: trichotomy forecloses sub-case (ii-b) — `#b < #a` cannot co-hold with either disjunct of the precondition `#a ≤ #b` (the strict `#a < #b` or the equality `#a = #b`). *Component pair `(aₖ, bₖ)`*: converts the strict inequality `bₖ > aₖ` into the weak `bₖ ≥ aₖ` required by NAT-sub's conditional-closure precondition, via the definition of `≤` from `<`.
- *Postconditions:* w = b ⊖ a
