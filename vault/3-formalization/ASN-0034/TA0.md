**TA0 (WellDefinedAddition).** `(A a, w ∈ T : Pos(w) ∧ actionPoint(w) ≤ #a : a ⊕ w ∈ T ∧ #(a ⊕ w) = #w)`.

TumblerAdd's first two postconditions are precisely `a ⊕ w ∈ T` and `#(a ⊕ w) = #w`: the per-component-in-ℕ paragraph inside TumblerAdd discharges membership via T0's characterisation of T as finite sequences over ℕ with length ≥ 1 (reading the prefix-copy rule off `a ∈ T`, NAT-closure's closure of ℕ under addition at the action point, and the tail-copy rule off `w ∈ T`, together with T0's length axiom applied to `w` to supply `#w ≥ 1` extended through the result-length identity to `#(a ⊕ w) ≥ 1`), and the result-length identity paragraph discharges `#(a ⊕ w) = #w` via NAT-sub's conditional closure (for `k - 1 ∈ ℕ` and `n - k ∈ ℕ`) together with NAT-sub's right-inverse and left-inverse characterisations (for the collapses `(k - 1) + 1 = k` and `k + (n - k) = n`). TA0 does not re-prove these — it exports TumblerAdd's first two postconditions as a single labelled well-definedness fact so that downstream users (TA1, TA1-strict, TA-LC, TA-RC, TA-MTO, TA-assoc, TA4, TA7a, OrdinalShift, D0, D1, D2, Span, T12, TS3, and every property consuming "a ⊕ w is a tumbler of length #w" without needing the ordering or dominance guarantees) can reference one corollary rather than cherry-pick from TumblerAdd's four-postcondition list. The precondition `actionPoint(w) ≤ #a` is the entry condition for TumblerAdd's three-region construction — its motivation (ensuring that the prefix-copy region indexes valid positions within `a` and that the action-point component `aₖ` exists within the start position) is argued inside TumblerAdd and inherited here unchanged; TA0's own content is the labelled handle, not the construction-side argument.

*Proof.* Immediate from TumblerAdd's first two postconditions `a ⊕ w ∈ T` and `#(a ⊕ w) = #w` under the preconditions `a, w ∈ T`, `Pos(w)`, `actionPoint(w) ≤ #a`, which are exactly TA0's hypotheses. ∎
