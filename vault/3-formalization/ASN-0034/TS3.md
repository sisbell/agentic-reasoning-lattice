**TS3 (ShiftComposition).**

`(A v, n₁, n₂ : n₁ ∈ ℕ ∧ n₂ ∈ ℕ ∧ n₁ ≥ 1 ∧ n₂ ≥ 1 ∧ #v = m : shift(shift(v, n₁), n₂) = shift(v, n₁ + n₂))`

*Proof.* We show that shifting by n₁ then n₂ is the same as shifting by their sum: composing two ordinal shifts reduces to a single shift whose amount is the sum of the individual amounts.

Fix v ∈ T with #v = m, and fix n₁, n₂ ∈ ℕ with n₁ ≥ 1, n₂ ≥ 1. We must prove shift(shift(v, n₁), n₂) = shift(v, n₁ + n₂). We compute each side by expanding OrdinalShift and applying TumblerAdd's constructive definition (Definition TumblerAdd), then show the results agree at every component.

**Left side.** Before invoking OrdinalShift at `(v, n₁)` we discharge its `n ∈ ℕ` precondition from TS3's own precondition `n₁ ∈ ℕ` under the substitution `n = n₁` — an order statement like `n₁ ≥ 1` presupposes rather than establishes carrier membership (the convention OrdinalDisplacement's own proof articulates at its m-th-position-typing site), so the carrier-typing must be routed from the separately named `n₁ ∈ ℕ` precondition rather than from the order precondition `n₁ ≥ 1` alone. By OrdinalShift, shift(v, n₁) = v ⊕ δ(n₁, m), where δ(n₁, m) = [0, ..., 0, n₁] has action point m. Since m = #v, the precondition of TA0 is satisfied (k = m ≤ m = #v). Let u = v ⊕ δ(n₁, m). By TumblerAdd's constructive definition with action point k = m:

- For 1 ≤ i < m: uᵢ = vᵢ (prefix copy — all m − 1 higher-level components are preserved).
- At i = m: uₘ = vₘ + n₁ (single-component advance).
- No positions i > m exist, since #u = #δ(n₁, m) = m by the result-length identity.

Now we apply the second shift. Before invoking OrdinalShift at `(u, n₂)` we discharge its `n ∈ ℕ` precondition from TS3's own precondition `n₂ ∈ ℕ` under the substitution `n = n₂`, again routing the carrier-typing through the separately named `n₂ ∈ ℕ` precondition rather than through the order precondition `n₂ ≥ 1`. By OrdinalShift, shift(u, n₂) = u ⊕ δ(n₂, m), where δ(n₂, m) has action point m. The precondition of TA0 is satisfied: k = m ≤ m = #u. Let L = u ⊕ δ(n₂, m) denote the left side. By TumblerAdd with action point k = m:

- For 1 ≤ i < m: Lᵢ = uᵢ = vᵢ (prefix copy, substituting the values of u established above).
- At i = m: Lₘ = uₘ + n₂ = (vₘ + n₁) + n₂ (advance, substituting uₘ = vₘ + n₁).
- Length: #L = #δ(n₂, m) = m by the result-length identity.

**Right side.** We first source two sub-facts about `n₁ + n₂` required by OrdinalDisplacement's preconditions at the argument `n = n₁ + n₂`: the ℕ-typing `n₁ + n₂ ∈ ℕ` and the order fact `n₁ + n₂ ≥ 1`. Each sub-fact is routed through named NAT-* axioms under the per-step citation discipline this ASN enforces at sister strict-from-`≥` sites (TumblerAdd's strict-advancement chain, OrdinalDisplacement's `n ≥ 1 ⟹ n ≠ 0` promotion, TA-Pos's Case `#z ≥ k` step).

The ℕ-typing `n₁ + n₂ ∈ ℕ` is supplied by NAT-closure's addition-closure axiom `(A m, n ∈ ℕ :: m + n ∈ ℕ)` instantiated at `m = n₁, n = n₂` — the axiom's two preconditions transfer directly from TS3's own preconditions `n₁ ∈ ℕ, n₂ ∈ ℕ`.

The order fact `n₁ + n₂ ≥ 1` is routed through the chain `n₁ + n₂ ≥ 1 + n₂ ≥ 1 + 1 ≥ 1`, each link sourced from NAT-addcompat and each composition from NAT-order. NAT-addcompat's right order compatibility `(A m, n, p ∈ ℕ : n ≥ p : n + m ≥ p + m)` instantiated at `m = n₂, n = n₁, p = 1`, with premise `n₁ ≥ 1` supplied by TS3's precondition, yields `n₁ + n₂ ≥ 1 + n₂`. NAT-addcompat's left order compatibility `(A m, n, p ∈ ℕ : n ≥ p : m + n ≥ m + p)` instantiated at `m = 1, n = n₂, p = 1`, with premise `n₂ ≥ 1` supplied by TS3's precondition, yields `1 + n₂ ≥ 1 + 1`, i.e., `1 + n₂ ≥ 2`. NAT-addcompat's strict successor inequality `(A n ∈ ℕ :: n < n + 1)` instantiated at `n = 1` supplies `1 < 1 + 1`, i.e., `1 < 2`, from which NAT-order's defining clause `m ≤ n ⟺ m < n ∨ m = n` — reading the disjunction right-to-left at the `1 < 2` disjunct — returns `1 ≤ 2`, i.e., `2 ≥ 1`.

Composing the three non-strict links `n₁ + n₂ ≥ 1 + n₂`, `1 + n₂ ≥ 2`, and `2 ≥ 1` into the single fact `n₁ + n₂ ≥ 1` consumes NAT-order's defining clause paired with NAT-order's transitivity of `<`: unfolding each non-strict inequality `a ≥ b` into the disjunction `a > b ∨ a = b` via the defining clause, each pairwise composition closes by NAT-order's transitivity of `<` in the strict-strict case and by substitution in the three mixed/equal cases, refolding via the defining clause to deliver the composed non-strict inequality in every case. Two such pairwise compositions — first of `n₁ + n₂ ≥ 1 + n₂` with `1 + n₂ ≥ 2` to yield `n₁ + n₂ ≥ 2`, then of `n₁ + n₂ ≥ 2` with `2 ≥ 1` to yield `n₁ + n₂ ≥ 1` — discharge the chain.

So δ(n₁ + n₂, m) is a well-formed ordinal displacement with action point m. Before invoking OrdinalShift at `(v, n₁ + n₂)` we discharge its `n ∈ ℕ` precondition at the argument `n = n₁ + n₂` from the just-established `n₁ + n₂ ∈ ℕ`: the routing here is NAT-closure applied to TS3's two `n_i ∈ ℕ` preconditions rather than a direct transfer, since TS3 names `n₁ ∈ ℕ` and `n₂ ∈ ℕ` separately but not their sum's ℕ-typing, and the convention this ASN applies forecloses routing the typing through the order chain `n₁ + n₂ ≥ 1 + n₂ ≥ 1 + 1 ≥ 1` alone. By OrdinalShift, shift(v, n₁ + n₂) = v ⊕ δ(n₁ + n₂, m). The precondition of TA0 is satisfied: k = m ≤ m = #v. Let R = v ⊕ δ(n₁ + n₂, m) denote the right side. By TumblerAdd with action point k = m:

- For 1 ≤ i < m: Rᵢ = vᵢ (prefix copy).
- At i = m: Rₘ = vₘ + (n₁ + n₂) (single-component advance).
- Length: #R = #δ(n₁ + n₂, m) = m by the result-length identity.

**Comparison.** Both sides have length m: #L = m = #R. We verify component-wise equality:

- For 1 ≤ i < m: Lᵢ = vᵢ = Rᵢ — both sides copied the prefix from v.
- At i = m: Lₘ = (vₘ + n₁) + n₂ and Rₘ = vₘ + (n₁ + n₂). These are equal by NAT-addassoc (NatAdditionAssociative), which states `(m + n) + p = m + (n + p)` for every `m, n, p ∈ ℕ`: instantiated with `vₘ, n₁, n₂`, it yields `(vₘ + n₁) + n₂ = vₘ + (n₁ + n₂)`.

Every component agrees and both tumblers have the same length. By T3 (CanonicalRepresentation): L = R, that is, shift(shift(v, n₁), n₂) = shift(v, n₁ + n₂). ∎

*Formal Contract:*
- *Preconditions:* v ∈ T, n₁ ∈ ℕ, n₂ ∈ ℕ, n₁ ≥ 1, n₂ ≥ 1, #v = m
- *Depends:* OrdinalShift (OrdinalShift) — invoked three times (left side, second shift, right side) to unfold `shift(·, n) = · ⊕ δ(n, m)` ("By OrdinalShift, shift(v, n₁) = v ⊕ δ(n₁, m)" / "By OrdinalShift, shift(u, n₂) = u ⊕ δ(n₂, m)" / "By OrdinalShift, shift(v, n₁ + n₂) = v ⊕ δ(n₁ + n₂, m)"); without OrdinalShift the entire reduction to `⊕` arithmetic could not begin. OrdinalShift's `n ∈ ℕ` precondition is discharged at each of the three invocation sites to a named source: at `(v, n₁)` from TS3's own precondition `n₁ ∈ ℕ` under the substitution `n = n₁`; at `(u, n₂)` from TS3's own precondition `n₂ ∈ ℕ` under `n = n₂`; and at `(v, n₁ + n₂)` from the right-side preface's `n₁ + n₂ ∈ ℕ`, itself supplied by NAT-closure's addition-closure axiom applied to `n₁, n₂ ∈ ℕ` (cited separately below). Without these three `n ∈ ℕ` discharges the chain from TS3 to OrdinalShift — and transitively to OrdinalDisplacement — would have a broken link at each unfolding step, since an order statement like `n ≥ 1` presupposes rather than establishes carrier membership (the convention OrdinalDisplacement's own proof articulates at its m-th-position-typing site) and cannot supply OrdinalShift's carrier-typing precondition on `n` from `n₁ ≥ 1` or `n₂ ≥ 1` alone. OrdinalDisplacement (OrdinalDisplacement) — invoked at every shift unfolding to fix the displacement form `δ(n, m) = [0, ..., 0, n]` with action point `m` ("δ(n₁, m) = [0, ..., 0, n₁] has action point m" and analogous instances for `δ(n₂, m)` and `δ(n₁ + n₂, m)") and at the right-side preface to source the well-formedness of `δ(n₁ + n₂, m)` from the chain `n₁ + n₂ ≥ 1 + n₂ ≥ 1 + 1 ≥ 1` discharged above via NAT-addcompat (the three order-compatibility and successor-inequality links) and NAT-order (the defining-clause unfoldings and transitivity-of-`<` compositions), with the ℕ-typing `n₁ + n₂ ∈ ℕ` supplied separately by NAT-closure (cited below). NAT-closure (NatArithmeticClosureAndIdentity) — invoked at the right-side preface to supply the ℕ-typing `n₁ + n₂ ∈ ℕ` required as the `n ∈ ℕ` source for OrdinalShift's third invocation at `(v, n₁ + n₂)`: NAT-closure's addition-closure axiom `(A m, n ∈ ℕ :: m + n ∈ ℕ)` instantiated at `m = n₁, n = n₂` yields `n₁ + n₂ ∈ ℕ`, the axiom's two preconditions transferring directly from TS3's own preconditions `n₁ ∈ ℕ, n₂ ∈ ℕ`. Without this citation OrdinalShift's third invocation has no named source for its `n ∈ ℕ` precondition at the sum — TS3's preconditions name only `n₁ ∈ ℕ` and `n₂ ∈ ℕ` separately, not their sum's ℕ-typing — and the convention this ASN applies at the OrdinalDisplacement m-th-position-typing site ("an order statement like `n ≥ 1` presupposes rather than establishes carrier membership") forecloses routing the typing through the order chain `n₁ + n₂ ≥ 1 + n₂ ≥ 1 + 1 ≥ 1` alone. The first two OrdinalShift invocations at `(v, n₁)` and `(u, n₂)` do not consume NAT-closure: their `n ∈ ℕ` preconditions transfer directly from TS3's preconditions `n₁ ∈ ℕ` and `n₂ ∈ ℕ` respectively, without any closure operation applied. NAT-closure's successor-closure and additive-identity clauses are not consumed here — only the addition-closure clause `(A m, n ∈ ℕ :: m + n ∈ ℕ)` is — matching the per-clause citation discipline established at OrdinalShift's own citations of NAT-closure (additive identity only) and at OrdinalDisplacement's citations of NAT-zero (lower-bound clause only). NAT-addcompat (NatAdditionOrderAndSuccessor) — invoked at the right-side preface to supply the order fact `n₁ + n₂ ≥ 1` required as the `n ≥ 1` source for OrdinalDisplacement's precondition at the argument `n = n₁ + n₂`, and thereby for OrdinalShift's third invocation at `(v, n₁ + n₂)` whose `n ≥ 1` is routed through OrdinalDisplacement. Three NAT-addcompat clauses participate in the chain `n₁ + n₂ ≥ 1 + n₂ ≥ 1 + 1 ≥ 1`: (i) right order compatibility `(A m, n, p ∈ ℕ : n ≥ p : n + m ≥ p + m)` instantiated at `m = n₂, n = n₁, p = 1` with premise `n₁ ≥ 1` (TS3's precondition) supplies the first link `n₁ + n₂ ≥ 1 + n₂`; (ii) left order compatibility `(A m, n, p ∈ ℕ : n ≥ p : m + n ≥ m + p)` instantiated at `m = 1, n = n₂, p = 1` with premise `n₂ ≥ 1` (TS3's precondition) supplies the second link `1 + n₂ ≥ 1 + 1`; (iii) strict successor inequality `(A n ∈ ℕ :: n < n + 1)` instantiated at `n = 1` supplies the anchor `1 < 1 + 1`, from which NAT-order's defining clause unfolds to the third link `2 ≥ 1`. Both order-compatibility clauses (left and right) are consumed because the two supplied premises `n₁ ≥ 1` and `n₂ ≥ 1` appear on opposite sides of the sum `n₁ + n₂`: the right-compatibility clause adds `n₂` on the right of `n₁ ≥ 1`, and the left-compatibility clause adds `1` on the left of `n₂ ≥ 1`, each intermediate form supplying one arm of the chain. Without these NAT-addcompat citations the right-side preface's order chain rests on background arithmetic rather than the enumerated ℕ axioms, breaking the per-step citation discipline sister proofs enforce at their strict-from-`≥` promotion sites (TumblerAdd's `rₖ = aₖ + wₖ ≥ aₖ + 1 > aₖ`, OrdinalDisplacement's `n ≥ 1 ⟹ n ≠ 0`, TA-Pos's Case `#z ≥ k` step). NAT-order (NatStrictTotalOrder) — invoked at the right-side preface in two roles: (a) composing the three non-strict links `n₁ + n₂ ≥ 1 + n₂`, `1 + n₂ ≥ 2`, and `2 ≥ 1` into the single fact `n₁ + n₂ ≥ 1` via two pairwise applications, and (b) converting the strict `1 < 2` (supplied by NAT-addcompat's successor inequality) into the non-strict `2 ≥ 1`. Two NAT-order clauses participate: the defining clause `m ≤ n ⟺ m < n ∨ m = n` unfolds each non-strict inequality into a strict-or-equal disjunction for case analysis during composition and, read right-to-left at the `1 < 2` disjunct, refolds the strict `1 < 2` into the non-strict `1 ≤ 2`; NAT-order's transitivity of `<` closes the strict-strict case of each pairwise composition (the three mixed/equal cases close by substitution, which is not a NAT-order clause). Without NAT-order's defining clause and transitivity the three links of the chain `n₁ + n₂ ≥ 1 + n₂ ≥ 1 + 1 ≥ 1` cannot be composed into the single fact `n₁ + n₂ ≥ 1`, and the promotion of `1 < 2` to `2 ≥ 1` has no named source. NAT-order's irreflexivity and trichotomy clauses are not consumed here — only the defining clause and transitivity of `<` — matching the per-clause citation discipline OrdinalDisplacement's `n ≥ 1 ⟹ n ≠ 0` promotion applies at its analogous site (where all three NAT-order clauses are consumed), where the additional irreflexivity clause is required to rule out the `n = 0` specialisation that TS3's chain does not need. TA0 (WellDefinedAddition) — invoked three times to discharge the precondition `k ≤ #v` for each `⊕` ("the precondition of TA0 is satisfied (k = m ≤ m = #v)" / "The precondition of TA0 is satisfied: k = m ≤ m = #u" / "The precondition of TA0 is satisfied: k = m ≤ m = #v") and to source the result-length identity `#u = #δ(n₁, m) = m` (and analogous identities for `#L` and `#R`); without TA0 the lengths would not be derivable and the comparison step could not establish `#L = m = #R`. TumblerAdd (TumblerAdd) — invoked at every component computation to apply the three-region rule (prefix copy for `i < k`, single-component advance at `i = k`, tail copy for `i > k`) ("By TumblerAdd's constructive definition with action point k = m") that produces the explicit values of `uᵢ`, `Lᵢ`, and `Rᵢ`. NAT-addassoc (NatAdditionAssociative) — invoked at the comparison step to convert `(vₘ + n₁) + n₂ = vₘ + (n₁ + n₂)` ("These are equal by NAT-addassoc (NatAdditionAssociative), which states `(m + n) + p = m + (n + p)` for every `m, n, p ∈ ℕ`"); without NAT-addassoc the load-bearing equality at position `m` is unsourced. T3 (CanonicalRepresentation) — invoked at the proof closing to convert component-wise plus length agreement into tumbler equality ("Every component agrees and both tumblers have the same length. By T3 (CanonicalRepresentation): L = R").
- *Postconditions:* shift(shift(v, n₁), n₂) = shift(v, n₁ + n₂)
- *Frame:* #shift(shift(v, n₁), n₂) = #v = m (shift preserves tumbler length)
