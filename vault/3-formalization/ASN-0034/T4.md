## Hierarchical structure

Tumblers encode a containment hierarchy. Nelson uses zero-valued components as structural delimiters:

> "There are four major fields, each expandable indefinitely, with three major dividers between them. These fields are Server, User, Document and Contents."

> "A tumbler address may have at most three zero digits... These are interpreted as the major dividers, and have lexical significance as punctuation."

We formalize this. Define a *field separator* as a component with value zero. An address tumbler has the form:

`t = NвӮҒ. ... .NвӮҗ . 0 . UвӮҒ. ... .UбөҰ . 0 . DвӮҒ. ... .Dбө§ . 0 . EвӮҒ. ... .EОҙ`

where each `Nбөў, Uвұј, DвӮ–, EвӮ— > 0`. The four fields are:

- **Node field** `NвӮҒ. ... .NвӮҗ`: identifies the server. "The server address always begins with the digit 1, since all other servers are descended from it."
- **User field** `UвӮҒ. ... .UбөҰ`: identifies the account. "Typically, the user will have no control over the node address he, she or it is assigned; but once assigned a User account, the user will have full control over its subdivision forevermore."
- **Document field** `DвӮҒ. ... .Dбө§`: identifies the document and version. Nelson notes the boundary between base document and version is not syntactically marked вҖ” "the version, or subdocument number is only an accidental extension of the document number, and strictly implies no specific relationship of derivation."
- **Element field** `EвӮҒ. ... .EОҙ`: identifies the content element. The first component distinguishes the *subspace*: 1 for text content, 2 for links.

Not every tumbler need have all four fields. A tumbler with zero zeros addresses a node. One zero: a user account. Two zeros: a document. Three zeros: an element. The count of zero-valued components determines the specificity level.

**T4 (HierarchicalParsing).** Define `zeros(t) = #{i : 1 вүӨ i вүӨ #t вҲ§ tбөў = 0}`, the count of zero-valued components in `t`. Every tumbler `t вҲҲ T` used as an address satisfies four positional conditions, stated entirely in terms of the indices at which zero occurs: at most three of its components are zero, no two zeros are adjacent, `tвӮҒ` is not zero, and `t_{#t}` is not zero. Formally: `zeros(t) вүӨ 3`; `(A i : 1 вүӨ i < #t : В¬(tбөў = 0 вҲ§ tбөўвӮҠвӮҒ = 0))`; `tвӮҒ вү  0`; `t_{#t} вү  0`. We call the last three conditions (no adjacent zeros, `tвӮҒ вү  0`, `t_{#t} вү  0`) collectively the *field-segment constraint*. T0 already fixes the carrier as в„•, so a component is either zero or strictly positive вҖ” no further clause is needed to force non-zero components to be positive, which is why positivity is not listed as a separate axiom here. Each condition refers only to which indices of `t` contain zero; none appeals to any prior notion of which fields are "present" in the address. Checking them requires only a single scan of the raw component sequence. The *field segments* of `t` are the maximal contiguous sub-sequences of non-zero positions вҖ” `k+1` of them when `zeros(t) = k`, separated by the zeros вҖ” and the field-segment constraint is exactly the requirement that all of them are non-empty. Without the field-segment constraint, a tumbler like `[1, 0, 0, 3]` would have `zeros(t) = 2` yet position `2` would be adjacent to position `3`, leaving an empty interior field segment between them; the no-adjacent-zeros clause rules exactly this out, and the two boundary clauses rule out the analogous degeneracies at the ends. The correspondence between the canonical written form and the positional conditions rests on a definitional role-assignment: T4 stipulates that zero components are field separators, while the non-zero components (strictly positive by T0's carrier в„•) are field components. The two roles are therefore positionally decidable by inspecting `tбөў` вҖ” zero or non-zero вҖ” with no further axiom required, and the number of zero-valued components determines the number of separators, which determines the number of fields.

Three consequences of T4's axiom вҖ” each stated and proved as its own first-class numbered theorem in this ASN, with its own statement file (`T4a.md`, `T4b.md`, `T4c.md`), proof object, and formal contract вҖ” round out the parsing picture:

  - **T4a (SyntacticEquivalence)** proves that the field-segment constraint (no adjacent zeros, `tвӮҒ вү  0`, `t_{#t} вү  0`) is logically equivalent to the condition that every field segment of `t` is non-empty. The equivalence is the bridge between T4's positional form and the semantic reading that every present field has at least one component; the proof proceeds by case analysis on `k = zeros(t) вҲҲ {0, 1, 2, 3}` in both the forward and reverse directions and is discharged in T4a.md.
  - **T4b (UniqueParse)** proves that the partial function `fields(t)` decomposing a T4-valid tumbler into its node, user, document, and element sub-sequences is well-defined and uniquely determined by `t` alone. T3 (CanonicalRepresentation) supplies the rigidity of component values that licenses uniqueness, and T4a supplies the segment-non-emptiness reading that the case analysis consumes; the proof is discharged in T4b.md.
  - **T4c (LevelDetermination)** proves that `zeros(t)` bijects onto the four hierarchical levels: `zeros(t) = 0` вҶ” node address (node field only); `= 1` вҶ” user address (node and user fields); `= 2` вҶ” document address (node, user, and document fields); `= 3` вҶ” element address (all four fields). T4b's identification of zeros with separators supplies the equality between `zeros(t)` and the separator count, so the number of fields present is `zeros(t) + 1`; the proof is discharged in T4c.md.

Because each of T4a, T4b, T4c is a first-class theorem with its own numbered statement and proof rather than an inline consequence of T4's body prose, downstream citations to these labels вҖ” for instance in T4b's Depends list for T4a, in T7's Depends list for T4a and T4b, and in T4a's framing reference to T4c вҖ” point at those separate proof objects and terminate an unbroken chain; no citation here relies on a named-but-unstated corollary.

A subtlety deserves emphasis: the hierarchy is *convention layered over flat arithmetic*, not enforcement by the algebra. Gregory's analysis reveals that the comparison, addition, subtraction, and increment operations treat every mantissa slot identically. There is no `isparent`, `isancestor`, or `ischild` primitive in the arithmetic layer. The algebra operates on flat sequences of non-negative integers; the hierarchical interpretation is projected onto those sequences by the allocation machinery and the field-parsing function `fields(t)`. This is a deliberate design choice. The arithmetic layer is kept flat so that comparison and span computation are simple, uniform operations with no parsing of semantic structure.

Hierarchy is constructed by the allocation machinery, not by the algebra. The `.0.` separator is produced when the allocation `depth` parameter equals 2 вҖ” creating a child at a *different hierarchical type* than its parent (e.g., an ACCOUNT creating a DOCUMENT). When creating a same-type child (DOCUMENT creating DOCUMENT = versioning), `depth = 1`, and no zero separator is introduced. Gregory confirms: there was even a bug where the first document under an account failed to receive its `.0.` separator вҖ” the convention had to be explicitly constructed by the allocator, not enforced by any algebraic invariant.

T4 is an axiom: it constrains which tumblers the system admits as valid addresses. Its three consequences вҖ” T4a (SyntacticEquivalence), T4b (UniqueParse), T4c (LevelDetermination) вҖ” are each stated and proved as separate first-class numbered theorems in this ASN, with their own statement files (`T4a.md`, `T4b.md`, `T4c.md`) and formal contracts; together they verify the parsing and level-determination properties that follow from the T4 constraints, with T3 (CanonicalRepresentation) supplied as an additional precondition for T4b's uniqueness argument. T3 is essential for T4b: canonical representation guarantees that the component sequence of `t` is fixed, so the separator positions computed by scanning for zeros are uniquely determined. Without T3, two representations of the same tumbler might yield different separator sets, and uniqueness of the parse would not follow. No further properties are required, and citations to T4a, T4b, T4c elsewhere in the ASN resolve to those separate proof objects rather than to prose summaries here. вҲҺ

*Formal Contract:*
- *Axiom:* Valid address tumblers satisfy the following positional conditions on the component sequence, none of which appeals to any prior notion of field presence: `zeros(t) вүӨ 3`; `(A i : 1 вүӨ i < #t : В¬(tбөў = 0 вҲ§ tбөўвӮҠвӮҒ = 0))`; `tвӮҒ вү  0`; `t_{#t} вү  0` (the last three together form the field-segment constraint, guaranteeing that the maximal non-zero contiguous sub-sequences of `t` вҖ” the field segments вҖ” are all non-empty). Positivity of non-zero components is not a separate axiom clause: T0's carrier в„• already makes `tбөў вү  0 вҮ” tбөў > 0`. **Definitional role-assignment:** on top of these positional conditions, T4 further stipulates that a position `i` of `t` is a *field separator* iff `tбөў = 0`, fixing as part of the axiom the biconditional identification of the set of separator positions with the set of zero-valued positions вҖ” the two directions `tбөў = 0 вҮ’ i` is a separator and `i` is a separator `вҮ’ tбөў = 0` are both licensed here rather than in body prose alone. Given this role-assignment together with the positional conditions above, a T4-valid address tumbler has the canonical written form `t = NвӮҒ. ... .NвӮҗ . 0 . UвӮҒ. ... .UбөҰ . 0 .
