**TA-strict (StrictIncrease).** `(A a, w ∈ T : Pos(w) ∧ actionPoint(w) ≤ #a : a ⊕ w > a)`.

TumblerAdd's third postcondition is precisely `a ⊕ w > a (T1)`: the *ordering guarantee* paragraph inside TumblerAdd derives it from the three-region construction — the prefix-copy rule supplies `rᵢ = aᵢ` for `1 ≤ i < k`, the advance clause supplies `rₖ = aₖ + wₖ > aₖ` via NAT-addcompat's left order-compatibility (lifting `wₖ ≥ 1` into `aₖ + wₖ ≥ aₖ + 1`), NAT-addcompat's strict successor inequality (`aₖ + 1 > aₖ`), and NAT-order's composition of the `≥`/`>` pair into a strict inequality, and T1 case (i) with divergence position `k` lifts the component inequality `rₖ > aₖ` to `a < a ⊕ w`. TA-strict does not re-prove this — it exports the ordering guarantee as a single labelled fact so that downstream users (chiefly T12 span well-definedness, whose *Non-emptiness* branch cites TA-strict to establish `s ∈ span(s, ℓ)`) can reference one corollary rather than quote TumblerAdd's four-postcondition list. The non-degeneracy of tumbler addition — the observation that no start/displacement pair collapses onto `a ⊕ w = a` — is carried by TumblerAdd's definition itself: ActionPoint's `wₖ ≥ 1` and the advance clause `rₖ = aₖ + wₖ` force strict motion at position `k` by construction, so TA-strict is a theorem derived from TumblerAdd, not an independent axiom ruling out a no-op model.

*Proof.* Immediate from TumblerAdd's *ordering guarantee* postcondition `a ⊕ w > a (T1)` under the preconditions `a, w ∈ T`, `Pos(w)`, `actionPoint(w) ≤ #a`, which are exactly TA-strict's hypotheses. ∎

*Formal Contract:*
- *Preconditions:* `a ∈ T`, `w ∈ T`, `Pos(w)`, `k ≤ #a` where `k` is the action point of `w`
- *Depends:* TumblerAdd (TumblerAdd) — the sole arithmetic source; TumblerAdd's *ordering guarantee* paragraph derives `a ⊕ w > a (T1)` as one of its four postconditions, and TA-strict invokes this postcondition unchanged, so the per-step NAT-* axioms that TumblerAdd cites (NAT-addcompat for the left order-compatibility lift `wₖ ≥ 1 ⟹ aₖ + wₖ ≥ aₖ + 1` and the strict successor inequality `aₖ + 1 > aₖ`, NAT-order for the `≥`/`>` composition to strict, NAT-zero for the ℕ lower bounds consumed in the ordering-guarantee sub-proof, NAT-closure for the closure of ℕ under addition at the action point) are transitively sourced through TumblerAdd rather than re-listed here — TA-strict introduces no fresh arithmetic step beyond the postcondition lookup. TA-Pos (PositiveTumbler) — invoked through the precondition `Pos(w)`, which TumblerAdd's precondition list requires and which is the entry condition for the action-point construction; without `Pos(w)`, the action point does not exist and TumblerAdd's ordering-guarantee postcondition does not engage. ActionPoint (ActionPoint) — invoked through the precondition `actionPoint(w) ≤ #a`; ActionPoint's defining minimum-position formula is the construct used to express this bound, and its lower bound `1 ≤ k` is a silent premise of TumblerAdd's three-region split that underwrites the postcondition TA-strict exports.
