## Definition (Span)

**Definition (Span).** A *span* is a pair `(s, ℓ)` where `s ∈ T` is a start address and `ℓ ∈ T` is a length — a positive tumbler used as a displacement whose action point satisfies `actionPoint(ℓ) ≤ #s` — denoting the contiguous range from `s` up to but not including `s ⊕ ℓ`. The two conditions `Pos(ℓ)` and `actionPoint(ℓ) ≤ #s` are precisely TA0's preconditions for `s ⊕ ℓ ∈ T`, so under them the upper bound denotes a tumbler in `T` and the set `{t ∈ T : s ≤ t < s ⊕ ℓ}` — the span — is well-defined; pairs `(s, ℓ)` violating either condition are not spans.

*Formal Contract:*
- *Preconditions:* `s ∈ T`, `ℓ ∈ T`, `Pos(ℓ)`, `actionPoint(ℓ) ≤ #s`
- *Definition:* `span(s, ℓ) = {t ∈ T : s ≤ t < s ⊕ ℓ}`
- *Depends:* T0 (CarrierSetDefinition) — supplies the carrier `T` and the length operator `#`: `∈ T` appears at `s ∈ T` and `ℓ ∈ T` in the pair components and at `{t ∈ T : s ≤ t < s ⊕ ℓ}` in the defining set, and `#s` appears in the precondition `actionPoint(ℓ) ≤ #s`. This definition writes `T` and `#` directly in its own body, so it cites T0 directly in parallel with T1 (which cites T0 for `#a` and component projection `aₖ` on `a ∈ T`) and TA0 (which cites T0 for `∈ T` in the postcondition `a ⊕ w ∈ T`) rather than inheriting through either of them — both consume T0 themselves and neither re-exports T0's vocabulary as a handle the way TA0 re-exports TumblerAdd's first two postconditions; without T0 the four usages have no source. TA-Pos (PositiveTumbler) — supplies the positivity predicate `Pos(·)` used in the precondition `Pos(ℓ)`; without TA-Pos the symbol `Pos` has no source and the precondition `Pos(ℓ)` cannot be discharged. ActionPoint (ActionPoint) — supplies the action-point function `actionPoint(·)` referenced in the precondition `actionPoint(ℓ) ≤ #s`; ActionPoint licenses `actionPoint(ℓ)` as a legal index into `ℓ` (under `Pos(ℓ)`), which the bound `≤ #s` then compares against the length of the base. TumblerAdd (TumblerAdd) — supplies the operator `⊕` appearing in the upper bound `s ⊕ ℓ` of the defining set `{t ∈ T : s ≤ t < s ⊕ ℓ}`; the piecewise construction of `⊕` is what gives `s ⊕ ℓ` any meaning as a tumbler expression. TA0 (WellDefinedAddition) — supplies the postcondition `s ⊕ ℓ ∈ T` under the preconditions `Pos(ℓ)` and `actionPoint(ℓ) ≤ #s`, which are exactly TA0's preconditions; so TA0 licenses the span's upper bound to denote a legal tumbler, and the narrative's sentence "the two conditions `Pos(ℓ)` and `actionPoint(ℓ) ≤ #s` are precisely TA0's preconditions for `s ⊕ ℓ ∈ T`, so under them the upper bound denotes a tumbler in `T`" rests on this citation. T1 (LexicographicOrder) — supplies the strict total order `<` used in `t < s ⊕ ℓ` and, via the non-strict companion `a ≤ b ⟺ a < b ∨ a = b` introduced in T1's Abbreviations block, the relation `s ≤ t` that fixes the span's lower bound; without T1 the two inequalities bracketing the defining set are undefined symbols.
