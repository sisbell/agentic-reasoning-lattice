# Cone Review — ASN-0036/S5 (cycle 4)

*2026-04-14 14:53*

### AX-2 is invoked under three incompatible characterizations across the ASN
**Foundation**: GlobalUniqueness, T10a (ASN-0034)
**ASN**: S4 proof: "By AX-2 (allocation-mediated entry), `dom(Σ.C) ⊆ allocated(s)`"; S1 proof: "a containment formally stated as AX-2 (allocation-mediated entry)"; S5 proof: "by AX-2 (allocation-mediated entry), this is the exclusive mechanism by which addresses enter `dom(C)`"; S5 formal contract: "AX-2 (allocation-mediated entry, ensuring content-creation transitions are T10a-conforming allocation events)"
**Issue**: Four citations of AX-2 describe three properties of ascending logical strength. (1) S4's proof and S1's proof use AX-2 as a **set containment**: `dom(Σ.C) ⊆ allocated(s)` — every address in the content store was allocated at some point. (2) S5's proof uses AX-2 as an **exclusive entry mechanism**: allocation is "the exclusive mechanism by which addresses enter `dom(C)`" — every transition that adds an address to `dom(C)` does so via an allocation event. (3) S5's formal contract uses AX-2 as an **operational identity**: "content-creation transitions are T10a-conforming allocation events" — content creation and allocation are the same transition type. Characterization (3) implies (2), which implies (1), but not conversely. Under (1) alone, an address could be allocated in one transition and added to `dom(C)` in a later non-allocation transition — a scenario excluded by (2) and (3). Under (2) but not (3), a content-creation transition could involve a non-T10a allocation — a scenario excluded by (3). All three characterizations happen to be sufficient for the proofs that cite them (S4 needs only (1); S5's constructions satisfy all three), so no current proof is invalid. But the three descriptions are logically distinct, and when AX-2 is formally stated, a single definition must be chosen.
**What needs resolving**: The formal statement of AX-2 must adopt a single characterization strong enough to support all citation sites. Since S5's contract requires the strongest form (content-creation transitions are T10a-conforming allocation events), that is the minimum. Alternatively, the weaker sites (S4, S1) could be re-justified under the strongest form, and the AX-2 statement could explicitly derive the weaker containment as a corollary, so that each proof cites the strength it actually needs.
