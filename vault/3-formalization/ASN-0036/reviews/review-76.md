### D-CTG's inner quantifier domain is implicit, creating an unacknowledged inconsistency with D-CTG-depth's T-membership guard
**Foundation**: T0(a) (UnboundedComponentValues): guarantees tumblers in T exceeding any bound at a given component, not tumblers at every intermediate value
**ASN**: D-CTG invariant: `(A v : subspace(v) = S ∧ #v = #u ∧ u < v < q : v ∈ V_S(d))`; D-CTG body text: "contiguity at depth 2 says V_S(d) occupies a single unbroken block of ordinals"; D-CTG-depth Reduction: `(A n : (v₁)ₘ < n < (v₂)ₘ ∧ π.n ∈ T : π.n ∈ V_S(d))` with the explicit note: "The T-membership guard is necessary: T0(a) guarantees tumblers exceeding any bound but not at every intermediate value, so π.n may fail to belong to T for some n"
**Issue**: D-CTG's inner universal `(A v : ...)` has no explicit domain. The natural reading is `v ∈ T`, which means D-CTG only forces T-resident intermediates into V_S(d) — if some tumbler value π.n ∉ T, D-CTG does not require it, and V_S(d) could have gaps at non-T positions. D-CTG-depth recognizes this explicitly: its Reduction adds a `π.n ∈ T` guard and documents why. But D-CTG itself does not — its prose describes the result as "a single unbroken block of ordinals" and "every intermediate position," claims that hold only if T contains every relevant intermediate (a density property that T0(a) does not guarantee and that D-CTG-depth explicitly notes is not guaranteed). The two properties in the same ASN have incompatible stances on whether T-density is assumed: D-CTG's prose assumes it; D-CTG-depth's formal contract denies it. An operation author reading D-CTG's prose would implement to the "unbroken block" invariant; an author reading D-CTG-depth's reduction would implement to the weaker T-guarded version.
**What needs resolving**: Either establish T-density (all finite positive-integer sequences are in T, making the guard trivial) — in which case D-CTG-depth's guard becomes unnecessary — or add an analogous note to D-CTG's body text acknowledging that the formal invariant only constrains T-resident intermediates, aligning D-CTG's prose with D-CTG-depth's already-correct treatment. The formal contracts should present a uniform stance on T-density.

### D-CTG-depth's Reduction result is locked behind preconditions it does not directly require
**Foundation**: T1 (LexicographicOrder, ASN-0034): total order by component comparison
**ASN**: D-CTG-depth Proof section: uses D-CTG + S8-fin + T0(a) + T1 + T3 to establish postcondition (1) (shared prefix). D-CTG-depth Reduction section: uses postcondition (1) + T1 only to establish postcondition (2) (intermediates share prefix) and the contiguity reduction.
**Issue**: The Reduction's argument is self-contained: given that all V_S(d) members share a prefix π (postcondition 1), any intermediate w between two members must also share π — otherwise T1 forces w outside the (v₁, v₂) interval. This reasoning requires only postcondition (1) and T1. It does not use D-CTG, S8-fin, or T0(a) directly. Yet D-CTG-depth's formal contract lists D-CTG and S8-fin as preconditions for the entire property, including postcondition (2). A downstream consumer who independently establishes the shared-prefix property — for example, an operation that constructs V_S(d) with a known prefix — cannot cite D-CTG-depth's postcondition (2) without also satisfying D-CTG and S8-fin, even though the Reduction's logic doesn't need them. This is the same bundling pattern as finding #4 (S8-depth bundles results with different dependency profiles under one contract), now appearing at D-CTG-depth: the Proof and the Reduction have distinct dependency profiles but share a single precondition list.
**What needs resolving**: Either separate the Reduction into its own property (preconditions: shared-prefix result for V_S(d) + T1; postcondition: intermediates share prefix, contiguity reduces to last component) or annotate within D-CTG-depth's formal contract which preconditions serve which postconditions — so that a consumer needing only the Reduction can trace its actual dependencies.

### D-CTG-depth's precondition attributes the m ≥ 3 lower bound to S8-depth, which provides only depth uniformity
**Foundation**: S8-depth (Fixed-depth V-positions): `(A d ∈ D, p, q : p ∈ dom(Σ.M(d)) ∧ q ∈ dom(Σ.M(d)) ∧ p₁ = q₁ : #p = #q)` — all positions in a subspace share the same depth
**ASN**: D-CTG-depth preconditions: "common depth m ≥ 3 (S8-depth)"
**Issue**: The precondition phrase "common depth m ≥ 3 (S8-depth)" cites S8-depth as the source of both the depth uniformity (common m) and the lower bound (m ≥ 3). S8-depth provides only uniformity: if two positions share a subspace, they share a depth. It says nothing about what that depth is — the uniform depth could be 1, 2, 3, or any value. The m ≥ 3 constraint is a separate condition that the consumer must independently verify (e.g., from knowledge of which subspace is being addressed, or from the hierarchical structure of T4). By attributing both to S8-depth, the precondition creates a false dependency chain: a reader tracing D-CTG-depth's requirements back to S8-depth would find no m ≥ 3 guarantee and would have no citation to follow for the lower bound. D-CTG-depth's proof requires m ≥ 3 for the range [2, m − 1] to be non-empty — without it, the proof's contradiction assumption is vacuously false and the postconditions hold trivially but say nothing interesting.
**What needs resolving**: Split the precondition into two distinct requirements with separate citations: "common depth m (S8-depth)" for the uniformity, and "m ≥ 3" either uncited (as a consumer obligation) or citing the source that constrains the subspace's depth (e.g., T4 hierarchical level or a subspace-depth property).
