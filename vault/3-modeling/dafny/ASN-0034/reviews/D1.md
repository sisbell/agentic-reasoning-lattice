ensures contains two extra guarantees not in formal postconditions

The formal contract specifies:
  *Postconditions:* a ⊕ (b ⊖ a) = b

The Dafny code has:
ensures var d := TS.TumblerSub(b, a);
        IsPositive(d) && TA.ActionPoint(d) < |a.components| && TA.TumblerAdd(a, d) == b
Missing/extra/wrong:
  The formal postcondition is a single equality: `a ⊕ (b ⊖ a) = b`. The Dafny ensures adds two conjuncts that are proof-internal intermediate facts, not contract postconditions:
  - `IsPositive(d)` — established mid-proof ("w > 0") as a precondition of TumblerAdd, not a stated postcondition
  - `TA.ActionPoint(d) < |a.components|` — established mid-proof ("the action point of w is k … k ≤ #a") as a precondition of TumblerAdd (TA0), not a stated postcondition

  These are helper facts the verifier needs to discharge the main goal, but exposing them in `ensures` strengthens the lemma's contract beyond the formal specification. They should be established as `assert` statements inside the proof body, not in the `ensures` clause.
