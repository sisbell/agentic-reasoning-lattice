# Review of ASN-0084

## REVISE

(none)

## OUT_OF_SCOPE

### Topic 1: Link subspace rearrangement
The postconditions apply to any subspace S, but links (subspace 2) carry endset structure that may require additional guarantees when rearranged. Whether rearranging V-positions in the link subspace preserves link well-formedness is a separate concern.
**Why out of scope**: Link structure is defined in a future ASN; this ASN correctly treats all subspaces uniformly at the arrangement level.

### Topic 2: Composition of rearrangements
Whether the composition of two cut-point rearrangements is expressible as a single cut-point rearrangement, and what the resulting cut sequence would be. The ASN raises this as an open question.
**Why out of scope**: This is new territory requiring analysis of the permutation group generated by cut-point transpositions.

### Topic 3: Relationship to editing operations
How cut-point rearrangement relates to the standard operation vocabulary (INSERT, DELETE, MOVE). The rearrangement is a pure permutation of existing content; editing operations may combine rearrangement with allocation or domain changes.
**Why out of scope**: The operation ASNs that consume this machinery are separate specifications.

VERDICT: CONVERGED
