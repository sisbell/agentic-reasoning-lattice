# Review of ASN-0065

## REVISE

### Issue 1: CutSequence definition prevents rearranging the last document position

**ASN-0065, Cut Points and the Region Partition**: "A *cut sequence* for document d in subspace S is a tuple C = (c₀, c₁, ..., c_{n−1}) of V-positions satisfying..."

**Problem**: The term "V-positions" in the foundation (ASN-0036, S8a) means elements of dom(M(d)). Under this reading, c_{n−1} must be in V_S(d). Since the affected range is the half-open interval [c₀, c_{n−1}), the last position of V_S(d) can never appear inside the affected range — it is always exterior.

Concretely: for V_S(d) = {[S, 1], ..., [S, n]}, any c_{n−1} ∈ V_S(d) satisfies c_{n−1} ≤ [S, n], so the affected range reaches at most [S, n−1]. To swap positions [S, n−1] and [S, n], you need c_{n−1} = [S, n+1] ∉ V_S(d). For a 2-position document V_S(d) = {[S, 1], [S, 2]}, no valid 3-cut sequence exists at all (CS2 requires three strictly ordered positions from V_S(d), but only two exist).

None of the postcondition formulas evaluate M(d) at c_{n−1} — it is used purely as an exclusive upper bound in comparisons (R-EXT: "v ≥ c₂"). R-PRE(iv) quantifies over positions *below* c_{n−1} and correctly validates the affected range even when c_{n−1} ∉ V_S(d). The formal machinery is ready; only the definition's terminology creates the restriction.

**Required**: Replace "of V-positions" with "of tumblers" in the CutSequence definition, or add a note: "Cut positions are tumblers satisfying CS1–CS4; c_{n−1} serves as an exclusive upper bound and need not be in V_S(d)." The formal constraints CS1–CS4 (subspace, depth, strict ordering) already capture the intended form without requiring dom(M(d)) membership.

## OUT_OF_SCOPE

### Topic 1: Composability of rearrangements
**Why out of scope**: Whether two rearrangements compose into a single rearrangement, or whether sequences of rearrangements can reach arrangements unreachable by any single operation, is a combinatorial question about the permutation group generated by cut-based transpositions — new territory requiring its own analysis.

### Topic 2: k-cut generalization for k > 4
**Why out of scope**: Nelson defines 3-cut and 4-cut forms; generalizing to arbitrary k requires defining the region partition and displacement formulas for arbitrary numbers of transposed regions, which is a new operation class.

### Topic 3: Depth > 2 V-position generalization
**Why out of scope**: The ASN correctly argues (citing D-CTG-depth and D-SEQ) that the generalization is structurally identical. Formalizing the deeper case requires spelling out the ordinal shift arithmetic at depth m, which is routine but belongs in a separate note or revision if needed.

VERDICT: REVISE
