# Review of ASN-0065

## REVISE

### Issue 1: No worked example for the 4-cut swap

**ASN-0065, "Worked Example: 3-Cut Pivot on a 5-Position Document"**: The ASN traces a 3-cut pivot in full detail — R-EXT, R-P1, R-P2, R-CP, R-PPERM, and the resulting block decomposition — but provides no worked example for the 4-cut swap.

**Problem**: R-S1, R-S2, R-S3 are distinct postconditions from R-P1, R-P2. The 3-cut example does not exercise them. The middle-region displacement `w_β − w_α` can be positive, negative, or zero depending on relative widths — a qualitatively different structure from the 3-cut case, where every region shifts by a single constant. Without a traced example (particularly one where `w_α ≠ w_β`), there is no mechanical verification that the three swap clauses tile the affected range correctly, that R-SPERM's four-case bijection produces the claimed arrangement, or that the block decomposition transforms as R-BLK claims when three regions (not two) are in play.

**Required**: Add a worked example for the 4-cut swap with `w_α ≠ w_β`, tracing R-S1, R-S2, R-S3, R-SPERM, R-CP, and the resulting block decomposition position by position. The example should demonstrate the middle-region displacement being nonzero.

### Issue 2: Arrangement invariants not explicitly verified

**ASN-0065, "Rearrangement as Arrangement Reordering" and "Frame Conditions"**: The ASN identifies REARRANGE as K.μ~ and cites J3 for `R' = R`, but never verifies the K.μ~ preconditions from ASN-0047 (that π produces V-positions satisfying S8a; that `M'(d)` satisfies S8-depth), and never states that the reachable-state invariants S2, S3, S8a, S8-depth, S8-fin are preserved. None of these appear in the Properties Introduced table.

**Problem**: ASN-0047's ReachableStateInvariants theorem requires every valid composite transition to preserve `S2 ∧ S3 ∧ S8a ∧ S8-depth ∧ S8-fin`. The preservation is trivial here — `dom(M'(d)) = dom(M(d))` inherits S8a, S8-depth, S8-fin; R-PIV/R-SWP establish S2; R-CP with pre-state S3 and R-CF(a) establish S3 — but the conclusions must be stated. The K.μ~ preconditions are equally trivial (π maps `dom(M(d))` to itself) but unstated. Without these, the ASN asserts REARRANGE is K.μ~ without completing the verification that makes the assertion meaningful.

**Required**: (a) Verify K.μ~ preconditions: π maps `dom(M(d))` to itself, so S8a and S8-depth are inherited from the pre-state. (b) State S2 preservation (each V-position assigned exactly one I-address, by R-PIV/R-SWP). (c) State S3 preservation (`ran(M'(d)) = ran(M(d)) ⊆ dom(C) = dom(C')`). (d) State S8a, S8-depth, S8-fin preservation (`dom(M'(d)) = dom(M(d))`). (e) Add these to the Properties Introduced table.

## OUT_OF_SCOPE

### Topic 1: Composition closure
Whether the composition of two rearrangements is expressible as a single rearrangement. The ASN lists this as an open question; answering it requires characterizing the group generated by cut-based transpositions on contiguous ordinal ranges.
**Why out of scope**: New algebraic territory. The current ASN correctly specifies a single rearrangement.

### Topic 2: Depth generalization beyond depth-2 V-positions
The commutativity of ordinal increment with the rearrangement permutation (`π(vₖ + k) = π(vₖ) + k`) relies on depth-2 structure where ordinal increment is natural-number addition on a single component. At deeper depths, D-CTG-depth (ASN-0036) reduces contiguity to the last component, suggesting generalization is feasible but requiring separate analysis of multi-component displacement interaction.
**Why out of scope**: The ASN explicitly restricts to depth 2, and D-SEQ ensures the treatment is rigorous within that scope. Generalization is a future ASN.

VERDICT: REVISE
