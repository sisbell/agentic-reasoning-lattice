# Sub-Questions — Rearrange Operation

**Inquiry:** What must REARRANGE (reordering content within a document without insertion or deletion) preserve and establish? How does moving content differ from delete-then-insert, and what address, link, and provenance guarantees must hold across rearrangement?

1. [nelson] When content is rearranged within a document, must the identity of each content span remain the same as before the operation, or may the system assign new identities to the moved material?
2. [nelson] Must links that point to rearranged content continue to resolve to the same content after the move, regardless of where that content now sits within the document?
3. [nelson] How must rearrangement differ from a delete-then-insert sequence in terms of content continuity — must the system treat moved content as having never left the document?
4. [nelson] After rearrangement, must the provenance record show that the content originated at its original moment of creation, or may it reflect the moment of the move?
5. [nelson] If rearranged content is shared by transclusion into other documents, must those other documents remain completely unaffected by the reordering in the source document?
6. [nelson] Must the document's version history record a rearrangement as a single atomic operation, distinct from any combination of deletions and insertions?
7. [nelson] Must the addresses of content that was NOT moved remain stable after a rearrangement operation changes the ordering around them?
8. [nelson] When content is rearranged, must the system preserve the original author attribution of each moved span, even if a different user performs the rearrangement?
9. [nelson] Must a comparison between the version before rearrangement and the version after reveal exactly which spans changed position, rather than showing apparent deletions and insertions?
10. [nelson] If two spans of content within a document swap positions, must the system guarantee that no moment exists in which either span is absent from the document?

12. [gregory] After a PIVOT(a, b, c), do the I-addresses in the swapped regions remain in exactly the same POOM bottom crums (just with modified V-displacements), or can the operation split a single bottom crum into multiple crums if a cut point falls in the interior of an existing span?
