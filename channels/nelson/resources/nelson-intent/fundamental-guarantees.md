# Fundamental Guarantees

Source: Literary Machines (synthesized from multiple sections)

## Overview

Nelson describes Xanadu as having three interlocking structures: conceptual, technical, and **contractual**. The contractual structure "makes it possible for people to use it confidently" (4/4). This document collects the explicit guarantees Nelson promises to users.

---

## 1. Permanence Guarantee

### What Nelson Promises

**Any address, once assigned, remains valid forever.** Content committed to the system becomes part of a permanent archive.

> "New items may be continually inserted in tumbler-space while the other addresses remain valid. This means that any address of any document in an ever-growing network may be specified by a permanent tumbler address." (4/19)

> "It is in the common interest that a thing once published stay published, as in the world of paper. Other readers and users will come to depend on its accessibility. Consequently its author may not withdraw it except by lengthy due process." (2/43)

> "This attrition of the heritage can in principle be stopped... Only digital archives give us long-term hope for preserving our heritage." (3/25)

**What Users Can Rely On:**
- References you make today will work indefinitely
- Published content cannot be easily withdrawn
- Old versions remain accessible even when superseded
- The system is designed to "be put in deep rock and deep space"

---

## 2. Attribution Guarantee

### What Nelson Promises

**You can always ascertain the origin of any content.** Attribution is structural, not metadata that can be stripped.

> "You always know where you are, and can at once ascertain the home document of any specific word or character." (2/40)

> "A link need not point anywhere in its home document. Its home document indicates who owns it, and not what it points to." (4/12)

> "The connection to the source is always visible and traceable."

**What Users Can Rely On:**
- Every byte traces back to its original document
- Windowed (transcluded) content maintains connection to source
- Link ownership is determined by home document
- Attribution cannot be severed by copying within the system

---

## 3. Version History Guarantee (Historical Backtrack)

### What Nelson Promises

**Every version you ever created still exists.** You can navigate backward through time to any previous state.

> "The file management system we are talking about automatically keeps track of the changes and the pieces, so that when you ask for a given part of a given version at a given time, it comes to your screen." (2/14-2/15)

> "There is thus no 'basic' version of a document set apart from other versions... any arrangement of the same materials is a priori better than other arrangements." (2/19)

> "Being able to go back through changes, and perhaps restore an earlier state, is called the problem of historical backtrack." (3/13)

**What Users Can Rely On:**
- Edit freely knowing nothing is destroyed
- Ask for "a certain part of a certain version at a certain point in time"
- Compare any two versions side by side
- See what changed between versions automatically

---

## 4. Automatic Royalty Guarantee

### What Nelson Promises

**Authors receive automatic payment proportional to use.** No negotiation required; compensation flows with content delivery.

> "In our planned service, there is a royalty on every byte transmitted. This is paid automatically by the user to the owner every time a fragment is summoned, as part of the proportional use of byte delivery." (2/43)

> "If a modified document is read, the original owner and the modifier split the royalty in proportion to who wrote what, as determined automatically." (2/45)

> "Royalty is measured in nibs. A nib is the royalty or author's reserve charged per byte." (5/12)

**What Users Can Rely On:**
- Publish once, receive royalties forever (as long as content is accessed)
- When quoted, you get your share automatically
- No need to track licenses or chase payments
- Income proportional to actual use, not negotiating skill

---

## 5. Quotation Without Permission Guarantee

### What Nelson Promises

**Anything published may be quoted without asking permission.** Permission is pre-granted through the publication contract.

> "Since the copyright holder gets an automatic royalty, anything may be quoted without further permission. That is, permission has already been granted: for part of the publication contract is the provision, 'I agree that anyone may link and window to my document.'" (2/45)

> "This means a whole new pluralistic publishing form. If anything which is already published can be included in anything newly published, any new viewpoint can be freely presented by an easily-made annotated collage." (2/45)

**What Users Can Rely On:**
- Quote any published content freely
- Build compound documents from existing materials
- No risk of infringement (within the system)
- Original viewpoint remains accessible: "Show me what this was originally"

---

## 6. Link Freedom Guarantee

### What Nelson Promises

**Each user is free to link to anything.** Authors who publish relinquish control over incoming links.

> "Accessibility and free linking make a two-sided coin. On the one hand, each user is free to link to anything privately or publicly. By the same token, each author of a published work is relinquishing the right to control links into that work. This relinquishment must also be part of the publishing contract." (2/43)

**What Users Can Rely On:**
- Link to any published document without asking
- No one can prevent you from linking to their work
- Both in-links and out-links are discoverable
- Two-way reading: see what links to a document, not just from it

---

## 7. Privacy Guarantee

### What Nelson Promises

**The network will not monitor what you read or write.** This is a constraint on system design, not just policy.

> "The network will not, may not monitor what is read or what is written in private documents. But these matters are not easy to guarantee." (2/59)

> "This is a libertarian system: restrict it, and all will lose."

**What Users Can Rely On:**
- Reading history is not tracked by the system
- Private documents remain truly private
- Anonymous publication is possible ("John Doe publication")
- System inherits printing press freedoms

---

## 8. Ownership Guarantee

### What Nelson Promises

**Only the owner may change or withdraw a document.** Ownership is absolute and enforceable.

> "Every document has an owner, the person who created and stored it (or someone who arranged it to be created and stored, such as a publishing company)... Only the owner has a right to withdraw a document or change it." (2/29)

**What Users Can Rely On:**
- Your documents belong to you completely
- No one else can modify your work
- Link ownership determined by home document
- Compound documents: each part owned by its creator

---

## 9. Non-Destruction Guarantee

### What Nelson Promises

**Modifications never damage originals.** New versions reference old content without copying or overwriting.

> "Thus users may create new published documents out of old ones indefinitely, making whatever changes seem appropriate--without damaging the originals. This is done by inclusion links." (2/45)

> "The integrity of each document is maintained by keeping the two aspects separate: derivative documents are permanently defined (and stored) in terms of the originals and the changes." (2/37)

**What Users Can Rely On:**
- Others can quote and modify your work into new versions
- Your original remains exactly as you wrote it
- Readers can always see "what this was originally"
- Derivative works don't affect the source

---

## The Contractual Foundation

Nelson envisions these guarantees as **contractual obligations**, not just features:

> "Because the conceptual structure expects participants to behave in certain ways, these are embraced in the contract offered to users. These provisions are necessary for the orderly and confident use of published material by many people." (4/4)

The "Internal Copyright Convention" creates a parallel legal framework where:
1. Everyone agrees to the same rules when joining
2. Economic rights (royalties) replace control rights (permissions)
3. The system enforces the bargain technically

---

## What Nelson Does NOT Guarantee

Some things are explicitly left unspecified or acknowledged as difficult:

- **Privacy is "not easy to guarantee"** - Nelson states the principle firmly but admits technical difficulty
- **Time is not encoded in addresses** - "Time is kept track of separately" (4/18)
- **Withdrawal requires "lengthy due process"** - Not impossible, just difficult
- **What happens outside the system** - "Normal copyright law applies" once content leaves
