# Mail Link

Source: Literary Machines 4/55 (page 222)

## Semantic Intent

### What It Means

A **mail link** is a specialized link type that delivers material to a user without moving it. Unlike conventional email where messages are copied and sent, Xanadu "mail" stays exactly where the sender created it - the mail link simply connects that material to the recipient's space.

This inverts the familiar model of messaging. Nothing travels. The sender creates content at their address and creates a mail link connecting it to the recipient. The recipient "opens their mailbox" by querying for mail links pointing to their space that are newer than what they've already seen.

### User Guarantee

- **Mail is not copied or moved.** The content lives permanently at the sender's address.
- **Delivery timing is recorded.** Since links are dated, the time of "mailing" is captured automatically.
- **Recipients control what they see.** Opening the mailbox is a front-end request - users query for recent mail links rather than having content pushed at them.
- **Orderly connection to user space.** Mail links connect to the user's address space "in an orderly fashion" - they don't just appear randomly but are structured.

### Principle Served

**Reference, not duplication.** Mail embodies the core Xanadu principle that content should be referenced, not copied. A message exists once, at one address, and is connected to recipients - not duplicated into their inboxes.

**Permanent, addressable communication.** Because mail content stays where it was created (at the sender's tumbler address), it becomes part of the permanent docuverse. It can be linked to, quoted from, and referenced - unlike ephemeral email that exists only in mailboxes.

**Sender retains control.** The sender owns the content (it's at their address). They could potentially link corrections or superseding versions to it. The mail link is the *notification* of content's existence, not the content itself.

### How Users Experience It

- Compose a message that lives at your address in the docuverse
- Create a mail link connecting it to the recipient's space
- The recipient later opens their "mailbox" by requesting recent mail links
- They see new items since their last check, with timestamps from the link dates
- Reading mail means following links to content that exists elsewhere
- Old mail remains accessible because the content never moved - it's still at the sender's address

### Nelson's Words

> "'Mail' is material addressed to a user through a mail link. It is not sent, but merely lives where it is first put. The mail link connects any document to the user's space in an orderly fashion. Since links will be dated, the time of mailing is shown. The user 'opens the mailbox' with a front-end request for items with a mail link more recent than the material already seen." (4/55)
