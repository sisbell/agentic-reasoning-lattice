# Suggested-Threading Links

Source: Literary Machines 4/54 (page 221)

## Semantic Intent

### What It Means

A **suggested-threading link** is a link type that proposes a reading path through a body of material. Unlike a simple jump-link that says "here's something related," a threading link says "if you're following this thread, here's the next step."

The key word is *chainable*: these links form sequences. One threading link leads to the next, creating a proposed pathway through content that may be scattered across the docuverse. The author of the thread assembles a curated journey without copying or rearranging the underlying material.

Threading links are *suggestions*, not commands. The user remains free to ignore them, take detours, or follow a different thread entirely. They represent the author's recommendation for how to traverse material, not a constraint on navigation.

### User Guarantee

- **Pathways through scattered material.** Threading links create coherent reading sequences through content that may live in many different documents across the docuverse.
- **Non-destructive curation.** Creating a thread doesn't copy or modify the threaded material - it's a layer of suggested navigation over existing content.
- **Chainability.** Threading links can be followed sequentially, forming a continuous path through material.
- **Optional adherence.** Users may follow the suggested thread or ignore it - the thread is a recommendation, not a mandate.

### Principle Served

**Literature is non-sequential.** Nelson's core insight is that writing and reading are not inherently linear. A corpus of material can be traversed in many ways. Threading links let authors propose orderings without imposing them.

**Multiple views of the same content.** Different threading links can propose different paths through the same underlying material. A teacher might create an introductory thread, an expert might create an advanced thread, and a critic might create a thematic thread - all connecting the same documents in different sequences.

**Separation of content and structure.** The material exists independently of how it's traversed. Threading links add structure *on top of* content, preserving the author's original organization while enabling alternative pathways.

### How Users Experience It

- Encounter a threading link and see that it's part of a proposed path
- Follow the thread to move through curated material in the suggested sequence
- Recognize they're in a thread and choose to continue or branch off
- Create their own threads through material they find valuable
- Discover multiple competing threads through the same corpus and choose which to follow
- Share threads as a way of guiding others through complex material

### Nelson's Words

> "Suggested-Threading Links: These are chainable links which propose a pathway through a corpus of material." (4/54)
