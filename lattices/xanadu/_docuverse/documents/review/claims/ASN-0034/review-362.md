# Regional Review — ASN-0034/T4a (cycle 5)

*2026-04-22 02:02*

### T4 Depends omits NAT-order
**Foundation**: NAT-order supplies `<` on ℕ, with `≤` derived from `<`
**ASN**: T4's Formal Contract *Depends:* lists only T0, NAT-zero, NAT-discrete. T4's Axiom uses `≤` ("`zeros(t) ≤ 3`") and `<` ("`1 ≤ i < #t`"), and the Postconditions clause for T4c uses `↔` chained over comparisons `zeros(t) = k`.
**Issue**: Both `<` and `≤` are introduced only in NAT-order (strict total order with `≤` defined as `< ∨ =`). T4 cannot express its own axiom without NAT-order in scope, but NAT-order is not declared a dependency. T4a's Depends and T4b's Depends both correctly cite NAT-order; T4's does not.
**What needs resolving**: Add NAT-order to T4's Depends (with an explicit role, e.g., supplying `<`, `≤` for the zero-count bound and the adjacency clause), or rewrite T4's Axiom without `<`/`≤`.

### T4c has no standalone Formal Contract
**Foundation**: structural — T4a and T4b each appear as numbered theorems with their own *Axiom/Preconditions/Definition/Depends/Postconditions* blocks
**ASN**: T4's prose: "Two direct consequences of T4 round out the parsing picture, each a first-class numbered theorem in this ASN: **T4a (SyntacticEquivalence)** … **T4c (LevelDetermination)** …". T4's own Postconditions: "T4c (LevelDetermination): on the T4-valid subset, the four biconditionals define *node address*, *user address*, *document address*, *element address* by zero count; the four labels are pairwise distinct, and each is realised by some T4-valid tumbler."
**Issue**: T4c is promoted verbally to "first-class numbered theorem" status alongside T4a and T4b, and is assigned non-trivial content — four biconditionals, pairwise distinctness of labels, realisation by some tumbler. T4a and T4b each carry full Formal Contract blocks with explicit Depends, Preconditions, Postconditions, and proofs (T4a has a Forward/Reverse derivation; T4b a case analysis). T4c carries none — no Preconditions, no Depends, no proof. The realisation clause ("each is realised by some T4-valid tumbler") in particular asserts existence without exhibiting witnesses, and the pairwise-distinctness clause needs an argument that the four level labels denote distinct concepts.
**What needs resolving**: Either add a standalone T4c section with its own Formal Contract and proof, or demote T4c's status in T4's prose to a direct postcondition (no proof obligation beyond the stated biconditionals), dropping the "first-class numbered theorem" framing.

### Zero-position notation inconsistent between T4a and T4b
**Foundation**: structural — a single ASN should fix one notation for a single concept
**ASN**: T4a proof: "Set `k = zeros(t) ∈ {0, 1, 2, 3}` with zeros at positions `p₁ < p₂ < … < pₖ`". T4b proof: "Let the zero positions in increasing order be `s₁ < s₂ < ... < s_k`".
**Issue**: The same construct — the strictly increasing enumeration of zero positions of a T4-valid `t` — is denoted `p_i` in T4a and `s_i` in T4b. A reader who derives an inequality in T4a (e.g., `p_{i+1} ≥ p_i + 2`) and then tries to cite it in T4b finds the same inequality restated as `s_{j+1} ≥ s_j + 2`. The T4b proof in fact invokes "T4a's reverse direction applied to the field-segment constraint" to obtain these inequalities — but the inequalities as derived by T4a are about `p_i`, while T4b discharges non-emptiness using `s_i`. No identification `p_i = s_i` is made.
**What needs resolving**: Pick one name for the zero-position enumeration and use it throughout T4a and T4b (or explicitly identify the two enumerations when T4b cites T4a).

### T4b's "well-definedness" forward/reverse step restates the definition
**Foundation**: T4's stipulation that position `i` is a field separator iff `tᵢ = 0`
**ASN**: T4b proof: "The well-definedness argument: under T4's role-assignment, separator positions are exactly recoverable from the raw tumbler. Forward: every separator has value 0. Reverse: if `tᵢ = 0` then `i` is a separator; non-zero positions carry field components. So `{i : 1 ≤ i ≤ #t ∧ tᵢ = 0}` is exactly the separator set."
**Issue**: T4's Axiom already stipulates the biconditional `i is a separator ⇔ tᵢ = 0` as a *definition*. The two clauses here labeled "Forward" and "Reverse" are the two directions of the very biconditional that defines "separator" — they cannot be proved or derived; they are the definition restated. The step contributes no content to the uniqueness argument; the actual work is done later by the case analysis citing T4a's inequalities. As presented, a precise reader cannot tell whether this paragraph is meant as a derivation (which would be circular) or as a reminder of the definition (in which case its "Forward/Reverse" framing is misleading).
**What needs resolving**: Either drop the Forward/Reverse framing and write a single sentence recalling the definition, or re-anchor the paragraph on the non-trivial step — that the separator set's being determined by `t` implies the field boundaries are determined by `t` — without the proof-shaped restatement of the definition.

### T4b Formal Contract Definition uses sentinels the proof does not use
**Foundation**: structural — the Definition and Proof sections should describe the same construction
**ASN**: T4b Formal Contract *Definition*: "Let `s₁ < s₂ < ... < s_k` enumerate the zero positions, `k = zeros(t) ∈ {0, 1, 2, 3}`, with sentinels `s₀ = 0` and `s_{k+1} = #t + 1`; the `(i+1)`-th field segment (for `0 ≤ i ≤ k`) is `(t_{s_i + 1}, ..., t_{s_{i+1} - 1})`." T4b proof: case analysis on `k ∈ {0, 1, 2, 3}`, each case writing out the field sub-sequences explicitly in terms of `s₁, …, s_k` and `#t`, without invoking `s₀` or `s_{k+1}`.
**Issue**: The Formal Contract's uniform sentinel-based definition and the proof's ad-hoc case-by-case construction describe the same partial function differently; neither references the other. The sentinel values `s₀ = 0` and `s_{k+1} = #t + 1` lie outside T0's index domain `{1, …, #t}`, and the arithmetic `s_i + 1` / `s_{i+1} - 1` when `s_i = 0` or `s_{i+1} = #t + 1` relies on `+`/`−` not axiomatized in the foundations in scope. A reader reconciling the two representations must do the bridging work themselves, and the definition's use of out-of-domain indices is not justified.
**What needs resolving**: Either fix the Definition and the proof to use a single representation — e.g., the case-by-case form without sentinels, or the uniform sentinel form justified by a statement about arithmetic on ℕ — or add a bridge paragraph showing the two representations agree.

### T4c's realisation clause asserts existence without witnesses or proof
**Foundation**: T4c's own stated content (in T4's Postconditions)
**ASN**: T4's Postconditions: "T4c (LevelDetermination): on the T4-valid subset, the four biconditionals define *node address*, *user address*, *document address*, *element address* by zero count; the four labels are pairwise distinct, and **each is realised by some T4-valid tumbler**."
**Issue**: "Each is realised by some T4-valid tumbler" is an existence claim: for each `k ∈ {0, 1, 2, 3}` there is a T4-valid `t` with `zeros(t) = k`. No witnesses are exhibited and no existence proof appears. Exhibiting a witness at `k = 3` requires a tumbler with at least three zero positions, which in turn requires at least four non-zero positions interleaved — hence components `1` and distinct position indices. Under the foundations in scope (T0, NAT-zero, NAT-discrete, NAT-order), the existence of a numeral `1 ∈ ℕ` and of tumblers of length ≥ 7 is not a derivable fact from stated axioms. The realisation clause therefore hangs without support.
**What needs resolving**: Either drop the realisation clause (leaving the four biconditionals as a pure definition), or exhibit witnesses and justify their T-membership / ℕ-membership from axioms in scope (which may require an additional axiom about the existence of positive numerals or non-trivial tumblers).
