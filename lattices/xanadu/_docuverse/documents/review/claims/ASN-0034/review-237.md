### T10a-N omits NAT-zero and NAT-discrete for the `k' > 0 ⟹ 1 ≤ k'` sharpening
**Foundation**: TA5's Depends establishes the per-step convention that the inference "non-zero ⇒ strictly positive on ℕ" — in particular, sharpening `k > 0` in ℕ to `k ≥ 1` — is sourced jointly from **NAT-zero** (`0 ≤ n` on ℕ, supplying the premise for NAT-discrete's instantiation) and **NAT-discrete** (instantiated at `m = 0`, ruling out the interval `0 ≤ k < 1` under `k ≠ 0`), and TA5's own proof for Case `k > 0` cites this trio explicitly: "NAT-zero supplies the premise `0 ≤ k` required to instantiate NAT-discrete at `m = 0`... together NAT-zero and NAT-discrete sharpen `k > 0` to `k ≥ 1`." The convention is "rather than collapsing the lower-bound step into an implicit appeal to NAT-discrete alone."
**ASN**: T10a-N (Necessity of sibling restriction) proof: "From `k' > 0` in ℕ we have `1 ≤ k'`; NAT-addcompat's left order-compatibility ..., instantiated at `m = #a₁`, `p = 1`, `n = k'`, lifts this to `#a₁ + 1 ≤ #a₁ + k'` ..." T10a-N's Depends lists TA5, NAT-addcompat, Prefix, T10a.2, T10a.5, T10 — neither NAT-zero nor NAT-discrete.
**Issue**: The step "From `k' > 0` in ℕ we have `1 ≤ k'`" is precisely the ℕ sharpening TA5's Case `k > 0` discharges from NAT-zero + NAT-discrete jointly. It is a distinct inference from the NAT-addcompat step that follows: NAT-addcompat's left order-compatibility consumes the already-sharpened hypothesis `1 ≤ k'` as input (the `p ≤ n` premise), and cannot itself deliver `1 ≤ k'` from `k' > 0` alone — that requires ruling out the interval `0 < k' < 1` on ℕ, which is NAT-discrete's content (with NAT-zero furnishing the lower-bound premise). T10a-N performs the sharpening in its proof body but cites no NAT-level authority for it; the handwave "`k' > 0` in ℕ we have `1 ≤ k'`" is the precise pattern the document has committed to replacing with explicit per-step citations in T4, T4a, T4c, TA5-SigValid, TA5, TA5a, and T10a.8 (the latter now fully cited after cycles 1/2). T10a-N is the only remaining site in the ASN where this `> 0 ⟹ ≥ 1` ℕ-sharpening is performed without the trio citation; NAT-addcompat alone, which T10a-N's Depends does list, does not supply the step.
**What needs resolving**: T10a-N's proof and Depends must cite NAT-zero and NAT-discrete at the step sharpening `k' > 0` to `1 ≤ k'` on ℕ — prior to the NAT-addcompat lift — matching the per-step trio-citation convention TA5's Case `k > 0` establishes and the resolved T10a.8 now follows.
