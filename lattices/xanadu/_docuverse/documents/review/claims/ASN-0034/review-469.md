# Regional Review — ASN-0034/NAT-card (cycle 4)

*2026-04-24 02:04*

### NAT-card axiom clause does not syntactically posit uniqueness of `k`
**Class**: REVISE
**Foundation**: —
**ASN**: NAT-card Axiom (enumeration-characterisation clause): "`(A n ∈ ℕ, S : S ⊆ {1, 2, …, n} :: |S|` is a `k ∈ ℕ` such that `S` admits a strictly increasing enumeration `s₁ < s₂ < … < s_k` under `<` on ℕ with `S = {s₁, s₂, …, s_k})` … — existence of the enumeration and the selection of a single `k` per `S` are both clauses of the postulate …".
**Issue**: The quantified clause says "`|S|` is a `k ∈ ℕ` such that …". The indefinite article "a" asserts only *existence* of such a `k`; it does not, by syntax, pin `|S|` to a unique value. For `|S|` to be a well-defined function of `S`, uniqueness of `k` must be part of the predicate the quantifier asserts, not a meta-comment outside the quantifier body. The trailing gloss "the selection of a single `k` per `S` are both clauses of the postulate" is prose *about* what the clause is supposed to say rather than a quantified assertion the reader can cite. A downstream consumer reading only the axiom line cannot conclude `|S|` is uniquely determined without appealing to the surrounding English.
**What needs resolving**: Put uniqueness inside the clause: e.g. "`|S|` is the unique `k ∈ ℕ` such that …" or an explicit conjunct asserting that any two such `k` coincide. After the clause expresses uniqueness, the gloss can be dropped.

### NAT-card body paragraph is a use-site inventory duplicating the Depends slot
**Class**: REVISE
**Foundation**: —
**ASN**: NAT-card body: "The axiom body invokes symbols beyond ℕ's primitive membership. The strict order `<` appears in the strictly increasing enumeration … and the non-strict companion `≤` in the upper bound `|S| ≤ n` — both defined in NAT-order — and NAT-order's strict-total-order discipline … is what makes 'strictly increasing enumeration' a well-formed predicate … . The initial segment `{1, 2, …, n}` unfolds to `{j ∈ ℕ : 1 ≤ j ∧ j ≤ n}`, a further use of NAT-order's `≤` with lower bound `1 ∈ ℕ` posited by NAT-closure … . The separation of the empty case … uses NAT-closure's `0 < 1`. The literal `0` appearing in the empty-enumeration case `k = 0` and the consequent `|∅| = 0` is the `0 ∈ ℕ` posited by NAT-zero. The disambiguation remark referencing T0's tumbler-length `#· : T → ℕ` pins `|·|` as a distinct operator. All four foundations are declared in the Depends slot so that the axiom body can be read without silently importing them."
**Issue**: This paragraph is a catalogue of which symbol comes from which foundation — the exact content the Depends slot carries, recapitulated in essay form inside the body. The Depends slot for NAT-card already attributes `<` and `≤` to NAT-order, `1 ∈ ℕ` and `0 < 1` to NAT-closure, `0 ∈ ℕ` to NAT-zero, and `#·` to T0, each with the specific site of use. The body paragraph is the "use-site inventory" pattern the instructions flag: prose explaining what foundations *are* already declared rather than advancing the axiom's content. The final sentence — "All four foundations are declared in the Depends slot" — is literally a pointer from the body to the Depends slot, which is the inversion of what the body should be doing.
**What needs resolving**: Remove the paragraph. If anything from it belongs in the body, it is a one-sentence statement of what `|S|` *is* (the cardinality operator, distinct from `#·`); the symbol-by-symbol attribution lives in Depends and should not also live in the body.

### NAT-order body's cross-reference to NAT-closure's clause list is inaccurate and out of scope
**Class**: REVISE
**Foundation**: —
**ASN**: NAT-order body (paragraph introducing the axiom register): "The axiom slot introduces `<` before constraining it … NAT-closure follows the same register for the arithmetic primitive, opening its axiom slot with the signature `+ : ℕ × ℕ → ℕ` before the unit-membership and left-identity clauses."
**Issue**: Two problems. First, NAT-order does not depend on NAT-closure, and the paragraph is a meta-comment comparing drafting registers across ASNs — content that belongs to a style guide rather than the specification of the strict-order axioms. Second, the description of NAT-closure's clause structure is factually wrong at the level of this very document: NAT-closure's axiom has signature, unit-membership `1 ∈ ℕ`, distinctness `0 < 1`, left identity, and right identity — five clauses, not "unit-membership and left-identity". A reader consulting the cross-reference finds it does not match the target. The paragraph therefore adds no argumentative content to NAT-order and actively misdescribes a sibling axiom.
**What needs resolving**: Delete the cross-reference to NAT-closure from NAT-order's body. If a remark about "introduce a symbol in its first clause before constraining it" is desired, keep only the NAT-order half of the sentence (which *does* describe NAT-order).

### NAT-zero Depends bullet re-derives the minimum consequence already derived in the body
**Class**: REVISE
**Foundation**: —
**ASN**: NAT-zero body: "Suppose some `n ∈ ℕ` did satisfy `n < 0`; the second clause forces `0 < n ∨ 0 = n`. In the first case, `0 < n` and `n < 0` together yield `0 < 0` by transitivity, contradicting irreflexivity. In the second case, `0 = n` rewrites `n < 0` to `0 < 0` by indiscernibility of `=` …, again contradicting irreflexivity." NAT-zero Depends, NAT-order bullet: "under the hypothesis `n < 0`, transitivity `m < n ∧ n < p ⟹ m < p` reduces the `0 < n` branch of the disjunction to `0 < 0`, and indiscernibility of `=` rewrites `n < 0` under `0 = n` to the same `0 < 0` in the equality branch; irreflexivity `¬(n < n)` then contradicts `0 < 0` in both branches, lifting the disjunction `0 < n ∨ 0 = n` to the *Consequence:* bullet `¬(n < 0)`."
**Issue**: The NAT-order Depends bullet's content is the same two-case derivation the body has already laid out. The Depends slot's job is to name the supplier and the supplied symbol/property, not to re-walk the proof already given in the body. The duplication is the "paragraph looks like a prior finding's content relocated rather than removed" pattern: either the body has the derivation, or the Depends has it; doing both makes a future edit to one quietly diverge from the other.
**What needs resolving**: Keep the case derivation in the body, where it reads as the justification for the Consequence bullet, and compress the Depends bullet for NAT-order to the grounded-symbols reading — e.g. "supplies `<` for the axiom's second clause and the irreflexivity + transitivity + `=`-indiscernibility used in the body's derivation of `¬(n < 0)`." The Depends bullet should not re-perform the derivation.

VERDICT: REVISE
