### Stale cross-references to "TA-Pos's Case `#z ≥ k`" precedent
**Foundation**: TA-Pos (PositiveTumbler) as currently defined in this ASN is purely definitional — it introduces the predicates `Pos(t)` and `Zero(t)` and contains no proof, no case analysis, and no variable `z`. Its own prose explicitly notes: "its proof is therefore carried out downstream by a separate ordering theorem (TA-PosDom) with its own Depends list, not stated here" and "Confining the definition layer to the predicate alone is what breaks the prior mutual-citation pattern between TA-Pos and ActionPoint. In earlier drafts the two cited each other at property granularity."
**ASN**: Multiple citations in ActionPoint's and TumblerAdd's Depends lists still invoke "TA-Pos's Case `#z ≥ k`" as a per-step precedent. ActionPoint's NAT-zero entry: "matching TA-Pos's Case `#z ≥ k` precedent." ActionPoint's NAT-closure entry: "This citation parallels TA-Pos's Case `#z ≥ k`, where NAT-closure's additive identity rewrites NAT-addcompat's strict-successor shape `0 < 0 + 1` to `0 < 1`." TumblerAdd's dominance proof and Depends similarly refer to "the per-step citation convention TA-Pos's Case `#z ≥ k` and ActionPoint's third postcondition establish for the structurally identical `0 ≤ t_⋅` step."
**Issue**: The cited precedent does not exist in the TA-Pos content actually present. The case analysis `#z ≥ k` / `#z < k` belongs to the ordering theorem (now TA-PosDom per TA-Pos's own pointer), not to TA-Pos. A reader auditing the per-step citation chain will follow the "TA-Pos's Case `#z ≥ k`" handle into TA-Pos and find no cases there — the pattern the convention is supposed to anchor cannot be located where the citations point.
**What needs resolving**: Either redirect every "TA-Pos's Case `#z ≥ k`" precedent reference in ActionPoint and TumblerAdd to TA-PosDom (or whichever downstream property retains the case split), or restore the Case-based content to TA-Pos so the referenced precedent exists where the citations say it does. Whichever route is taken, the label used in citations and the location of the cited material must agree.

### TA1-strict's universally-quantified statement lacks `∈ T` typing on the quantified variables
**Foundation**: The typing convention established elsewhere in the ASN types the quantified variables inside the universal. TA0 states `(A a, w ∈ T : Pos(w) ∧ actionPoint(w) ≤ #a : a ⊕ w ∈ T ∧ #(a ⊕ w) = #w)`, and T1's postconditions type their quantifiers `(A a ∈ T :: ¬(a < a))`, `(A a, b ∈ T :: ...)`, `(A a, b, c ∈ T : a < b ∧ b < c : a < c)`. Under this convention every universal over tumblers binds the type as part of the quantifier.
**ASN**: TA1-strict's stated property: "`(A a, b, w : a < b ∧ Pos(w) ∧ actionPoint(w) ≤ #a ∧ actionPoint(w) ≤ #b ∧ actionPoint(w) ≥ divergence(a, b) : a ⊕ w < b ⊕ w)`". The quantifier `A a, b, w` carries no `∈ T` annotation. The Formal Contract's precondition list does include `a ∈ T, b ∈ T, w ∈ T`, but the statement formula itself leaves the variables untyped.
**Issue**: The statement formula is ambiguous read in isolation: without `∈ T`, the quantifiers range over an unspecified domain, and the operators `#·`, `⊕`, `actionPoint(·)`, `divergence(·,·)`, `Pos(·)` are well-defined only for tumblers. A mechanical reader consuming the formula directly (as a translation target or as a citation by a downstream property quoting TA1-strict's statement form) must either re-infer the typing from the separate precondition list or treat the formula as ill-typed.
**What needs resolving**: Restate TA1-strict's property with `∈ T` on the quantifier — `(A a, b, w ∈ T : a < b ∧ Pos(w) ∧ ... : a ⊕ w < b ⊕ w)` — to match TA0's and T1's convention, or document elsewhere why TA1-strict's statement may omit the typing and have the preconditions list carry it alone. Either way, typing convention should be uniform across property statements in this ASN.
