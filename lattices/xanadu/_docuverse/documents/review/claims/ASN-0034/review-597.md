### Unnecessary T4a detour in Case k ≥ 3
**Class**: REVISE
**Foundation**: T4 (HierarchicalParsing), T4a (SyntacticEquivalence) — T4(ii) directly states no two zeros adjacent
**ASN**: TA5a, Case `k ≥ 3`: "Suppose for contradiction that T4 holds on `t'`. Then T4(i)'s `zeros(t') ≤ 3` places `t'` in T4a's domain, and T4a identifies T4's field-segment constraint — clauses (ii), (iii), (iv) — with the condition that every field segment of `t'` is non-empty. Under T4a's field-segment construction, the two positions `#t + 1` and `#t + 2` are consecutive zeros of `t'`, so the interior field segment they delimit spans the index range `[#t + 2, #t + 1]`, which has no indices and is therefore empty. T4a's Reverse-contrapositive — an empty field segment precludes the conjunction of T4's positional clauses — forces at least one of T4(ii), T4(iii), T4(iv) to fail on `t'`; the failing clause is T4(ii) instantiated at `i = #t + 1`, which yields `¬(0 = 0 ∧ 0 = 0)` and contradicts `t'_{#t+1} = t'_{#t+2} = 0`."
**Issue**: The proof establishes `t'_{#t+1} = 0` and `t'_{#t+2} = 0` (positions adjacent on ℕ since `#t+1 < #t+2 = (#t+1)+1`), then routes through T4a's biconditional and contrapositive to reach a failure of T4(ii) — but T4(ii) is *directly* the predicate "no two zeros adjacent," so `t'_{#t+1} = t'_{#t+2} = 0` already falsifies T4(ii) at `i = #t+1` without invoking T4a, "field segments," "Reverse-contrapositive," or "interior field segment of empty index range." The intermediate machinery serves no proof step: the contradiction's witness is the same `i = #t + 1` either way, and the assumption of T4 on `t'` is required only to invoke T4(ii) (not T4a). This is reviser-drift prose around an axiom — explaining why T4a *could* be applied here rather than what TA5a actually needs to discharge — and it propagates into the *Failure* clause ("under T4a's field-segment framing (applicable under the counterfactual `zeros(t') ≤ 3`)") and into the T4a entry of the Depends list. The "counterfactual `zeros(t') ≤ 3`" qualifier in the Failure slot is itself a symptom: with `zeros(t') = zeros(t) + (k − 1)` and `t` only constrained by `zeros(t) ≤ 3`, the condition `zeros(t') ≤ 3` need not hold, so the framing is contingent on a side condition the direct T4(ii) argument never needs.
**What needs resolving**: Either drop the T4a detour and replace Case `k ≥ 3` with the direct argument (positions `#t + 1` and `#t + 2` carry zero by TA5(d) given `k − 1 ≥ 2`; they are adjacent positions; T4(ii) at `i = #t + 1` fails), removing T4a from the Depends list and rewriting the Failure clause without the counterfactual T4a framing — or, if the T4a routing is intended to record a structural connection, justify why the indirect route is needed when a direct T4(ii) instantiation discharges the case, and resolve the awkward "counterfactual `zeros(t') ≤ 3`" qualifier (which is needed only because of the detour).
