### T4a's precondition presumes the field-segment constraint the theorem proves equivalent
**Foundation**: T4a (SyntacticEquivalence), Preconditions — "`t ∈ T` is T4-valid — in particular `zeros(t) ≤ 3`"; T4a body opening — "Let `t` be a T4-valid address tumbler"; T4's Axiom — T4-validity is the conjunction of `zeros(t) ≤ 3` *and* the field-segment constraint (no adjacent zeros, `t₁ ≠ 0`, `t_{#t} ≠ 0`).
**ASN**: T4a body, Forward direction — "Assume every field segment is non-empty. We derive each positional condition" (derives (i), (ii), (iii)); Reverse direction — "Assume conditions (i), (ii), and (iii) hold. We show every field segment is non-empty."
**Issue**: The stated precondition is T4-validity, which already includes the positional conditions (i), (ii), (iii). Under that precondition, the Forward direction is vacuous (its conclusion — the positional conditions — is already assumed true by hypothesis), and the Reverse direction is re-assuming what the precondition already gave. The actual work the proof performs (establishing the equivalence as a genuine bi-implication) requires the precondition to be strictly weaker than T4-validity — specifically `t ∈ T` with `zeros(t) ≤ 3` alone, so the field-segment constraint is unassumed. As written, T4a's formal contract over-conditions the claim in a way that formalization will read as a tautology on the antecedent side, even though the prose proof is doing substantive bidirectional work.
**What needs resolving**: Either weaken T4a's precondition so only `zeros(t) ≤ 3` (not the full field-segment constraint) is assumed, or restate the theorem as a conditional whose hypothesis is the zero-count bound alone, so the equivalence is a non-vacuous biconditional.

### T4a's Depends list cites T4c, but the proof body uses T4c only for external framing
**Foundation**: T4a, Depends — "T4c (LevelDetermination) — referenced in the opening framing … where T4c supplies the definitional zero-count classification that licenses the semantic reading; the equivalence proof itself is independent of T4c"; T4c itself is proved in a separate theorem object (`T4c.md`).
**ASN**: T4a body — T4c is mentioned in the opening framing paragraph ("Once T4c (LevelDetermination) classifies T4-valid tumblers into the four levels …, T4a reads as the semantic statement …") but is not invoked in the Forward, Reverse, or preconditions of the actual equivalence proof.
**Issue**: T4a's own justification of the T4c citation acknowledges the equivalence proof is "independent of T4c" — the citation is purely framing, not a load-bearing step. A Depends entry is supposed to enumerate what the proof actually consumes. Listing T4c here makes the dependency DAG over-connected: a downstream consumer or a DAG validator that treats Depends as the authoritative precondition/citation list will see a T4a→T4c edge that has no proof-internal counterpart, and a formalization pass that strips T4c from scope will (correctly) still discharge T4a's obligations. The asymmetry between "what the proof uses" and "what the contract declares" is exactly the kind of cross-cutting drift that per-property review misses.
**What needs resolving**: Either remove T4c from T4a's Depends list (since the proof is independent of it) and relocate the framing reference to non-contract prose, or restructure the opening framing so that it stops making a citation that the Depends entry itself describes as non-load-bearing.

**Foundation**: T6, Depends list — "T0 … T4 … T4a … T4b … T4c"; T6, Ingredient 1 — "The partial function `fields : T ⇀ … × Seq(ℕ⁺)` introduced in T4b"; T6, Ingredient 1 — "unambiguous because, by T4a, every *present* field segment is non-empty"; T6, Ingredient 2 — field-presence thresholds sourced from T4b's absence-pattern postcondition.

**Issue**: The closing prose characterises T6 as a corollary of T4 alone. But the proof body actually consumes T4b's partial-function signature and absence-pattern postcondition (for field extraction and presence thresholds), T4a's unambiguity-of-ε reading (to interpret the absence marker), and T0's ℕ⁺ subcarrier (for the value domain of projections). Calling T6 "a corollary of T4" — with the parenthetical that it "introduces no independent content beyond T4" — contradicts T6's own Depends list and its own proof, both of which make T4a and T4b load-bearing. A reader taking the closing prose at face value will infer a shallower dependency than the formal contract actually requires.
**What needs resolving**: Either reword T6's closing characterisation to reflect that it is a corollary of the T4-family (T4, T4a, T4b, and implicitly T4c via the level-label vocabulary) rather than of T4 alone, or reconcile the Depends list with the closing prose so both describe the same dependency set.
