### NAT-closure prose around `+` is use-site drift, not unpacking
**Class**: REVISE
**Foundation**: NAT-closure (NatArithmeticClosureAndIdentity) — Axiom clause `+ : ℕ × ℕ → ℕ`
**ASN**: NAT-closure body, paragraph 2: "The signature `+ : ℕ × ℕ → ℕ` carries two load-bearing commitments. Its domain `ℕ × ℕ` makes `+` total on the naturals — every pair of naturals has a sum — and its codomain `ℕ` closes the operation under addition, so compositional terms like `(m + n) + p` re-enter the signature without a side condition. Totality rules out partial addition and closure rules out sums that escape ℕ."
**Issue**: The paragraph is meta-justification of the signature ("load-bearing commitments"), reinforced by a forward-projected use-case ("`(m + n) + p` re-enter the signature without a side condition") that is not exhibited anywhere in this ASN. Neither T0 nor TA-Pos invokes `+` — the only consumer of NAT-closure in scope (TA-Pos) draws on `1 ∈ ℕ` for an index-range bound. The prose is therefore explaining why the axiom is helpful for hypothetical downstream consumers rather than stating what it says, which is the reviser-drift pattern flagged in the review instructions ("new prose around an axiom explains why the axiom is needed rather than what it says"). The companion paragraph about `1 ∈ ℕ` and `0 < 1` is correctly tight; the `+` paragraph is the asymmetric one.
**What needs resolving**: The body needs to either unpack `+ : ℕ × ℕ → ℕ` in terms of what the clause asserts (totality and codomain-closure as direct readings of the typed signature, not as "commitments" justified by a use-case that doesn't appear), or compress to a single short sentence. Forward-projecting downstream compositional uses of `+` does not belong in this body since no such uses are in scope here.
