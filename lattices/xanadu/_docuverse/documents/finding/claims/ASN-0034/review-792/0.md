### TA2 divergence-point step: `a ≠ w` asserted "by T3" from padded disagreement without the required intermediate argument
**Class**: REVISE
**Foundation**: T3 (CanonicalRepresentation) — `a = b ⟺ #a = #b ∧ (A i : 1 ≤ i ≤ #a : aᵢ = bᵢ)`, which speaks of native components and lengths only
**ASN**: TA2 (WellDefinedSubtraction) — proof body, *Divergence point* paragraph: "The padded sequences differ, so by T3 `a ≠ w`"; and the Formal Contract *Depends:* entry for T3: "used to conclude `a ≠ w` from the existence of a padded divergence"
**Issue**: T3's contrapositive fires on native-component inequality or length difference, not on padded disagreement directly. A padded disagreement at `k ≤ L` does imply `a ≠ w`, but only after an intermediate argument that the proof omits. Two complete routes exist:

*Reductio*: Assume `a = w`. T3 forward gives `#a = #w` and `aᵢ = wᵢ` for all `i ≤ #a`. Since `#a = #w`, NAT-order's trichotomy places `(#a, #w)` in case (α) with `L = #a = #w`, so the padded domain coincides with the native domain. For every `i ≤ L`: `âᵢ = aᵢ = wᵢ = ŵᵢ`. Padded sequences agree everywhere, contradicting the Case 2 assumption that zpd is defined.

*Direct case analysis*: The padded disagreement at `k ≤ L` falls in one of three zones — `k ≤ min(#a, #w)` (padded projection equality gives `aₖ ≠ wₖ`, firing T3's contrapositive on the existential disjunct); `k > #a` (padding forces `âₖ = 0` while `ŵₖ ≠ 0`, ruling out the padding zone for `ŵ` and yielding `#a < #w`, firing T3 on the length disjunct); `k > #w` (symmetric). Neither route is present in the proof body.

The formal contract's T3 Depends annotation compounds the error by describing the inference as T3 being "used to conclude `a ≠ w` from the existence of a padded divergence," which would require T3 to operate on padded sequences.

**What needs resolving**: The *Divergence point* paragraph must supply one of the two arguments above before asserting `a ≠ w`. The T3 Depends annotation in TA2's Formal Contract must describe the actual inference (reductio via T3's forward direction, or case-split reducing padded zones to native disagreement or length difference) rather than the imprecise "from padded divergence" shorthand.
