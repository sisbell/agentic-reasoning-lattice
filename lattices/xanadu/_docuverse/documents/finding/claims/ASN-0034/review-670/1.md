### T3: `≡` in the quantifier statement creates a parsing ambiguity
**Class**: OBSERVE
**Foundation**: T3 (CanonicalRepresentation)
**ASN**: Statement — `(A a, b ∈ T : #a = #b ∧ (A i : 1 ≤ i ≤ #a : aᵢ = bᵢ) ≡ a = b)`
**Issue**: The document uses two notational conventions: `(A x ∈ S :: body)` for the range-only form, and `(A x ∈ S : guard : body)` for the three-slot form. T3 uses a single `:` after `∈ T` with `≡` appearing where the second `:` or `::` would normally be. Under the three-slot reading, the guard would be `#a = #b ∧ ... ≡ a = b` with no body — which is ill-formed. Under the two-slot reading, the body is the biconditional `[#a = #b ∧ (...)] ≡ [a = b]`, which is what the proof establishes and what the postcondition restates. The proof and postcondition resolve the intent, but the statement itself departs from the document's own notation standard in a way that requires the reader to look past the statement to recover its meaning.
