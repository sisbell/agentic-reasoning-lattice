### T1 formal definition quantifies over ℕ without citing its carrier
**Class**: REVISE
**Foundation**: NAT-carrier (NatCarrierSet)
**ASN**: T1 (LexicographicOrder) — *Definition* `a < b` iff `∃ k ∈ ℕ` with `1 ≤ k` and `(A i ∈ ℕ : 1 ≤ i < k : aᵢ = bᵢ)` and either (i) or (ii). Formal Contract *Depends:* section.
**Issue**: The formal definition existentially quantifies `k ∈ ℕ` and universally quantifies `i ∈ ℕ`, using `ℕ` as a carrier set over which bound variables range. NAT-carrier is the sole provider of `ℕ` as a set in this ASN. Every other claim whose formal contract directly quantifies over `ℕ` — NAT-order, NAT-zero, NAT-closure, NAT-addcompat, NAT-cancel, NAT-discrete, NAT-wellorder, T0 — cites NAT-carrier in its Depends with an annotation identifying this use. T1's Depends does not list NAT-carrier. No listed dependency's annotation claims to supply `ℕ` as a set; NAT-order and NAT-closure supply relations and operations on ℕ, not the carrier itself. The witness variable `k` and range variable `i` in T1's definition are therefore ungrounded in the stated dependency chain.
**What needs resolving**: NAT-carrier must be added to T1's Depends, annotated to supply `ℕ` as the underlying set over which the bound variable `k` in `∃ k ∈ ℕ` and the bound variable `i` in `(A i ∈ ℕ : 1 ≤ i < k : aᵢ = bᵢ)` of the formal definition range.
