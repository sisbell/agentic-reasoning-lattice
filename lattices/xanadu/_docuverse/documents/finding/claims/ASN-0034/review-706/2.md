### T3 claim statement — single-colon GCL with `≡` is ambiguous
**Class**: OBSERVE
**Foundation**: N/A
**ASN**: T3 (CanonicalRepresentation) — claim statement `(A a, b ∈ T : #a = #b ∧ (A i : 1 ≤ i ≤ #a : aᵢ = bᵢ) ≡ a = b)`
**Issue**: In GCL, `(A x ∈ S : P : Q)` means "for all x in S with P, Q" — the single colon introduces a range then a body. Inserting `≡` makes the range boundary ambiguous: a reader cannot determine whether `#a = #b ∧ ...` is the range and `a = b` is the body (yielding only the forward direction), or whether `(#a = #b ∧ ...) ≡ a = b` is the body with no range restriction. The Postcondition in the Formal Contract uses `⟺` correctly. The double-colon form `(A a, b ∈ T :: (#a = #b ∧ ...) ≡ a = b)` is unambiguous.
