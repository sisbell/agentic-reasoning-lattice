### NAT-discrete derivation — contradicted case misdescribed in summary
**Class**: OBSERVE
**Foundation**: NAT-discrete (NatDiscreteness) — Consequence derivation, final sentence
**ASN**: NAT-discrete prose: "The case `m < n` is therefore impossible; it contributes nothing to the conclusion and is discharged by contradiction. In the case `m = n`, symmetry of `=` gives `n = m` directly. Both branches of the split terminate in `n = m`, closing the case analysis."
**Issue**: The final sentence contradicts the immediately preceding one. The `m < n` branch does not terminate in `n = m`; it is dismissed by contradiction. The `m = n` branch terminates in `n = m` by direct proof. Saying "both branches terminate in `n = m`" describes what the case analysis yields in aggregate, but attributes a direct proof route to a branch that was vacated. A reader tracing the proof path hits an inconsistency between the two sentences. The derivation is sound — ex falso licenses anything including `n = m` — but the summary misrepresents the case structure.

VERDICT: OBSERVE
