### Meta-prose in NAT-closure motivates axiom choice rather than advancing derivation
**Class**: OBSERVE
**Foundation**: NAT-closure (NatArithmeticClosureAndIdentity)
**ASN**: NAT-closure body — "This derivation pins down only the single sum `0 + 1`; without the universal clause, a model could satisfy the signature, the identity laws, and the bare `0 < 1` while still permitting `m + 1 = 0` at some `m ≥ 1`. The universal clause closes that gap uniformly across ℕ."
**Issue**: These two sentences explain why the universal form of the successor-positivity axiom was chosen — to exclude models permitting `m + 1 = 0` for `m ≥ 1`. This is motivation for the axiom's form, not a step in the derivation of the Consequence `0 < 1`. A reader following the formal proof — instantiate at `n := 0`, apply left-identity, substitute — passes through this passage without using it. It is prose around an axiom that explains why the axiom is needed rather than what the axiom says.
