### T4 canonical written form asserts segment structure without derivation
**Class**: REVISE
**Foundation**: n/a (internal)
**ASN**: T4 Definition / Canonical written form: for `k = 3`, "`t = N₁. ... .Nₐ . 0 . U₁. ... .Uᵦ . 0 . D₁. ... .Dᵧ . 0 . E₁. ... .Eδ`, with `0 < Nᵢ, 0 < Uⱼ, 0 < Dₖ, 0 < Eₗ` at every position present." Likewise for `k ∈ {0, 1, 2}`. T4 prose: "The field segments of `t` are the maximal contiguous sub-sequences of field-component positions — `k+1` of them when `zeros(t) = k`, separated by the field separators."
**Issue**: The canonical form is placed in a Definition slot but it makes a structural claim: that a T4-valid `t` with `zeros(t) = k` decomposes into exactly `k+1` nonempty contiguous segments of nonzero components separated by `k` zero-positions, with segment-length sums totalling `#t - k`. This decomposition is a derived consequence of the four-conjunct T4-valid predicate (no leading/trailing zero, no adjacent zeros, exactly `k` zeros), not a definitional stipulation about T (T already fixes what a sequence is). Asserting it as definition means downstream consumers cannot tell whether they may cite "k+1 nonempty segments" as established, or must re-derive it.
**What needs resolving**: Either prove the decomposition as a Consequence and let the canonical form sit in prose as illustration, or restrict the Definition slot to the four-conjunct predicate plus segment-naming notation that doesn't carry an implicit existence/structure claim.
