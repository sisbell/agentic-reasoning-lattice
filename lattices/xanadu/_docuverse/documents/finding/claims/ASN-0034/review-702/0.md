### T4a: Exhaustion Consequence cited but never consumed
**Class**: REVISE
**Foundation**: T4 (HierarchicalParsing) — Exhaustion Consequence `(A t ∈ T : zeros(t) ≤ 3 : zeros(t) ∈ {0, 1, 2, 3})`
**ASN**: T4a (SyntacticEquivalence) — proof body and Formal Contract Consequence annotation

**Issue**: The proof body declares: *"T4's Exhaustion Consequence… instantiates at the local t to pin k ∈ {0, 1, 2, 3} directly."* The Formal Contract then lists the Exhaustion Consequence as a consumed derivation dependency: *"derived from T4's field-segment clauses and Exhaustion Consequence…"*

Neither the Forward nor the Reverse derivation ever uses the upper bound k ≤ 3. Both proceed by a two-case split — k = 0 vs. k ≥ 1 — that is exhaustive for any k ∈ ℕ≥0 without needing the bound. The Exhaustion Consequence's output, k ∈ {0, 1, 2, 3}, is introduced in the setup paragraph and then silently dropped. The proof would be word-for-word identical with the Exhaustion invocation removed.

By contrast, T4b correctly invokes the Exhaustion Consequence: it performs a four-case analysis (k = 0, 1, 2, 3) and the Exhaustion Consequence is what makes that case presentation exhaustive over the T4-valid subdomain. T4a never performs that four-way split, yet claims the same dependency.

The Formal Contract's derivation annotation is a false claim: it names a foundation whose output is not consumed. A downstream verifier tracing the derivation chain will find no consumption site.

**What needs resolving**: Remove the Exhaustion Consequence from T4a's Formal Contract derivation citation, and remove or correct the setup prose that claims to use it. The two-case structure (k = 0 vs. k ≥ 1) stands on its own and is the actual derivation.
