### NAT-discrete prose implies equivalence where only one direction is proved
**Class**: OBSERVE
**Foundation**: NAT-discrete (NatDiscreteness)
**ASN**: Body — "The axiom has a familiar no-interval reformulation `(A m, n ∈ ℕ :: m ≤ n < m + 1 ⟹ n = m)`"
**Issue**: "Reformulation" implies the axiom and no-interval form are logically equivalent. The formal contract derives only one direction (axiom ⟹ no-interval). The reverse direction (no-interval ⟹ axiom) is neither proved nor claimed. A reader relying on the prose characterisation could mistake the consequence for a full equivalent and treat it as a substitute axiom in downstream reasoning.
