### T10a.3 postcondition admits the reflexive ancestor-descendant case, making the postcondition false
**Class**: REVISE
**Foundation**: T10a.1 (UniformSiblingLength) — all elements of a single allocator's domain have equal length; NAT-order (NatStrictTotalOrder) — irreflexivity `¬(n < n)`
**ASN**: T10a (AllocatorDiscipline), Formal Contract *Postconditions:*, T10a.3: "For any ancestor-descendant pair `(A, B)`, `∀b ∈ dom(B), ∀a ∈ dom(A) : #b > #a`"; and T10a.5 closure: "T10a.3 gives `#x ≥ #bₓ`"
**Issue**: The document defines "ancestor" reflexively: "X is an ancestor of Y iff X = Y or X is an ancestor of parent(Y)." Under this definition, (A, A) is an ancestor-descendant pair for every A ∈ 𝒯. The T10a.3 postcondition then asserts `∀b ∈ dom(A), ∀a ∈ dom(A) : #b > #a`. Taking `a = b` yields `#a > #a`, which NAT-order's irreflexivity refutes. The Consequence 3 proof establishes strict length separation only across a proper parent–child boundary; it never covers the reflexive case. The gap propagates to T10a.5's closure: X may equal Cₓ (when the path from P to X has length exactly one), making the pair (Cₓ, Cₓ) the reflexive case. For that case T10a.3 does not correctly supply `#x ≥ #bₓ`; T10a.1 is what gives `#x = #bₓ`. The conclusion `#x ≥ #bₓ` is correct in both the proper-descendant and base-case regimes, but the cited justification is incomplete for one of them.
**What needs resolving**: Restrict T10a.3's postcondition to proper ancestor-descendant pairs — pairs (A, B) with `depth(A) < depth(B)` (equivalently, A is an ancestor of B and A ≠ B). T10a.5's closure should cite T10a.1 alongside T10a.3 to cover both the proper-descendant case (`#x > #bₓ` from T10a.3, implying `#x ≥ #bₓ`) and the base case X = Cₓ (`#x = #bₓ` from T10a.1), establishing `#x ≥ #bₓ` across both.
