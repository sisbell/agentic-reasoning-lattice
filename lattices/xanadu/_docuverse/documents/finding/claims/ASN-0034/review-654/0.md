### Defensive meta-prose in T4c's Definition slot
**Class**: REVISE
**Foundation**: n/a (internal — T4c)
**ASN**: T4c's *Definition* body, after the universally quantified biconditional: "The four biconditionals stipulate the meaning of the label terms... they are not derived from independently characterised notions but introduce these terms by zero count. Outside the T4-valid subdomain T4c assigns no level; consumers must discharge T4-validity before reading a level off `zeros(t)`. No existence of a T4-valid tumbler at any given zero count is asserted — T4c is a pure definition of labels on whatever T4-valid tumblers exist."
**Issue**: The structural *Definition* slot is followed by three sentences of essay-style explanation about what is *not* being claimed (not derived from prior notions, not asserting existence, not assigning levels outside the subdomain). This is reviser drift: the prose defends the definition against hypothetical readings rather than stating what the definition says. The same pattern recurs in T4c's body ("Realisation — existence of a T4-valid tumbler at each zero count — is not claimed here") and in T4's preamble ("T4 is purely definitional... T4-validity is a precondition at the use-site, not derived here") and T4a's *Preconditions* note ("The field-segment constraint of full T4-validity is *not* assumed; it appears as the conclusion of the Forward direction and the local hypothesis of the Reverse direction"). A precise reader has to skip past these disclaimers to reach the actual definition or precondition.
**What needs resolving**: Strip the defensive what-is-not-claimed prose from structural slots. The Definition stands as the four biconditionals; the Preconditions stand as the listed constraints. If a reader needs to understand the subdomain restriction or the role of T4-validity, that belongs in the body's working prose, not as disclaimers attached to structural slots.
