### TA5-SIG: chain argument `1 ≤ i₀ < i₀ + 1 ≤ m → 1 < m` described but not walked
**Class**: OBSERVE
**Effective class**: REVISE (classifier override)
**Classifier rationale**: The proof claims a mechanism but does not execute the required four-case split, leaving the derivation of `1 < m` unjustified — the artifact is logically incomplete without the case analysis.
**Foundation**: NAT-order (NatStrictTotalOrder) — `≤`-defining clause and transitivity of `<`
**ASN**: TA5-SIG (LastSignificantPosition) — "the chain 1 ≤ i₀ < i₀ + 1 ≤ m — with the weak bounds dispatched into <-or-= cases by NAT-order's ≤-defining clause and the strict segments composed by NAT-order's transitivity of < — yields 1 < m"
**Issue**: The chain mixes two weak `≤` bounds and two strict `<` segments, producing four sub-cases. The proof names the mechanism but does not execute the case split: (1 < i₀ ∨ 1 = i₀) × (i₀ + 1 < m ∨ i₀ + 1 = m). Each case is trivial and all four terminate in 1 < m, but the case analysis is described rather than carried out.
