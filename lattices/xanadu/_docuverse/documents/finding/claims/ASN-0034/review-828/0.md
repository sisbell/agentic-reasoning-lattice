### T10a.5 (Case 2): inductive hypothesis assumed but never applied in the C_X positional-value induction
**Class**: OBSERVE
**Foundation**: T10a.4 (T4Preservation); TA5-SigValid
**ASN**: T10a.5 (CrossAllocatorIncomparability), "Outputs of C_X at position #s + 1", inductive step — "Assume `(tₙ)_{#s+1} ≥ 1`... T0 places `(tₙ)_{#s+1} ∈ ℕ`; NAT-closure places `(tₙ)_{#s+1} + 1 ∈ ℕ`; NAT-zero gives `0 ≤ (tₙ)_{#s+1}`..."
**Issue**: The inductive step derives `(tₙ₊₁)_{#s+1} ≥ 1` solely from `(tₙ)_{#s+1} ∈ ℕ` via NAT-addcompat's strict successor and NAT-discrete at `m = 0`. The assumed IH `(tₙ)_{#s+1} ≥ 1` is never referenced in this derivation. The conclusion is correct — the values at position `#s+1` in dom(C_X) are `1, 2, 3, …` — but the induction is formally structured as if the IH is load-bearing when it is not. A reader verifying the step will note the assumption is surplus.
