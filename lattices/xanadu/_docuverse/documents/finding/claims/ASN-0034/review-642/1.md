### Use-site narration in Depends entries
**Class**: REVISE
**Foundation**: T4, T4a, T4b, TA5-SIG
**ASN**: Depends slots throughout; e.g. T4's NAT-discrete entry ("at each `m ∈ {0, 1, 2}`, promotes `m < zeros(t)` to `m + 1 ≤ zeros(t)` in the Exhaustion Consequence's iterated case analysis so that..."), T4a's NAT-discrete entry (a four-clause inventory of every promotion site), TA5-SIG's NAT-addcompat entry (three lettered sub-instantiations).
**Issue**: Depends entries are accumulating use-site inventories — a list of every place in the proof where the cited dependency fires, with the local instantiation re-derived in the entry itself. The point of the Depends slot is to declare what the claim depends on; rebuilding the proof case-by-case inside the slot duplicates the body and doubles the surface area a reader must reconcile when checking soundness. T4a's NAT-discrete entry alone runs to four enumerated promotions plus a paragraph about why the `+1` form is preferred over a subtractive form.
**What needs resolving**: In each Depends entry, name the dependency and the specific axiom/Consequence consumed (one line, possibly two). Keep the use-site discussion in the prose, where the proof step is already in context. Do not re-walk instantiations in the structural slot.
