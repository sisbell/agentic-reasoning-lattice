### T4 clause label conflict between TA5a and GlobalUniqueness
**Class**: REVISE
**Foundation**: TA5a (IncrementPreservesT4) — proof body: "T4 requires: (i) zeros(t) ≤ 3, (ii) no two zeros adjacent, (iii) t₁ ≠ 0, (iv) t_{#t} ≠ 0"; Formal Contract Depends: "boundary clause t_{#t} ≠ 0 (iv) used at case k = 0"
**ASN**: GlobalUniqueness (GlobalUniqueness) — proof body: "by T4 clause (iii), p₂[#p₂] ≠ 0"; Formal Contract Depends: "T4 (HierarchicalParsing) — clause (iii) t_{#t} ≠ 0 on T4-valid addresses"
**Issue**: TA5a's proof body and Formal Contract Depends establish a consistent four-way enumeration of T4's conjuncts — T4(iii) = t₁ ≠ 0, T4(iv) = t_{#t} ≠ 0. GlobalUniqueness applies the label "clause (iii)" to the condition t_{#t} ≠ 0, which TA5a calls T4(iv). The mislabeling appears in both the proof body and the Formal Contract Depends of GlobalUniqueness. A reader who has absorbed TA5a's explicit enumeration will interpret "T4 clause (iii)" as the first-position condition t₁ ≠ 0 and will not understand how that clause justifies p₂[#p₂] ≠ 0.
**What needs resolving**: GlobalUniqueness's proof body and Formal Contract Depends must use the label T4(iv) — matching the convention established in TA5a's proof body and Formal Contract — when citing the last-position condition t_{#t} ≠ 0.
