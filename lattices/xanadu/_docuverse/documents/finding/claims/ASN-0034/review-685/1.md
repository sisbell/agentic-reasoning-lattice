### Self-contradictory closing sentence in NAT-discrete no-interval derivation
**Class**: OBSERVE
**Foundation**: NAT-order, NAT-discrete
**ASN**: NAT-discrete — "The case `m < n` is therefore impossible; it contributes nothing to the conclusion and is discharged by contradiction. In the case `m = n`, symmetry of `=` gives `n = m` directly. Both branches of the split terminate in `n = m`, closing the case analysis."
**Issue**: The prose first says the `m < n` branch "contributes nothing to the conclusion and is discharged by contradiction," then immediately says "Both branches terminate in `n = m`." These two statements are inconsistent: a branch that is discharged by contradiction does not terminate in `n = m` directly — it produces `n = m` only via ex falso. The proof is correct, but the closing sentence contradicts the preceding sentence.
