### Brief composition step for strict advancement
**Class**: OBSERVE
**Foundation**: NAT-order, NAT-addcompat
**ASN**: TumblerAdd — "From `wₖ ≥ 1`, NAT-addcompat's left order-compatibility gives `aₖ + wₖ ≥ aₖ + 1`, and its strict successor inequality gives `aₖ + 1 > aₖ`. NAT-order composes these into `aₖ + wₖ > aₖ`."
**Issue**: The composition `x < y ∧ y ≤ z ⟹ x < z` is not a named axiom of NAT-order — it requires unfolding `y ≤ z` into `y < z ∨ y = z` and dispatching each branch (transitivity for `<`, substitution for `=`). "NAT-order composes these" is shorthand for a two-case argument; a reader relying strictly on named axioms would need to supply it.
