### T10a.3 postcondition (T10a formal contract): depth inequality misrepresented as equivalent to proper ancestor-descendant relationship
**Class**: REVISE
**Foundation**: The allocator tree 𝒯 — a rooted tree in which distinct allocators at the same or different depths can exist in independent subtrees with no ancestor-descendant relationship
**ASN**: T10a (AllocatorDiscipline) — *Postconditions* section, T10a.3 entry: "For any proper ancestor-descendant pair `(A, B)` — pairs with `depth(A) < depth(B)` (equivalently, A is an ancestor of B and A ≠ B) — `∀b ∈ dom(B), ∀a ∈ dom(A) : #b > #a`"
**Issue**: The parenthetical asserts a logical equivalence between `depth(A) < depth(B)` and "A is an ancestor of B and A ≠ B." This equivalence is false. In any tree, a proper ancestor necessarily has strictly smaller depth, but the converse fails: two allocators in different subtrees can satisfy `depth(A) < depth(B)` with no ancestor-descendant relationship. A concrete instance: root R spawns children C₁ and C₂; C₁ spawns a child D. Then `depth(C₁) = 1 < 2 = depth(D)` but `depth(C₂) = 1 < 2 = depth(D)` as well, yet C₂ is not an ancestor of D. The claim `∀b ∈ dom(D), ∀a ∈ dom(C₂) : #b > #a` does not follow from T10a.3's proof — output lengths are determined by each allocator's own spawning-parameter sequence, not by depth ordering between unrelated allocators. The proof establishes the result only for actual ancestor-descendant pairs (along the unique tree path), but the "equivalently" formulation extends the stated scope to all pairs with differing depths, for which the result is unproved and may fail. T10a.3's own postcondition section correctly restricts to "ancestor A" and "descendant B on the same lineage" without this error.
**What needs resolving**: Replace or remove the "equivalently" characterization. The postcondition's scope must be restricted to pairs where A is an actual ancestor of B and A ≠ B. The depth inequality `depth(A) < depth(B)` is a consequence of this relationship in any tree, not an alternative sufficient condition. The corrected entry should state the scope as "pairs where A is a proper ancestor of B" and optionally note that `depth(A) < depth(B)` follows as a consequence, without asserting the converse.

VERDICT: REVISE
