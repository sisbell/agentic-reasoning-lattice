### NAT-addcompat body duplicates the Formal Contract without advancing reasoning
**Class**: REVISE
**Foundation**: NAT-addcompat (NatAdditionOrderAndSuccessor)
**ASN**: The three-bullet body of NAT-addcompat:
> "- Left order compatibility: `p ≤ n ⟹ m + p ≤ m + n` for every `m, n, p ∈ ℕ`.
> - Right order compatibility: `p ≤ n ⟹ p + m ≤ n + m` for every `m, n, p ∈ ℕ`.
> - Strict successor inequality: `n < n + 1` for every `n ∈ ℕ`."

**Issue**: These three bullets are a prose paraphrase of the three Axiom clauses in the Formal Contract (`(A m, n, p ∈ ℕ : p ≤ n : m + p ≤ m + n)`, etc.) with no added intuition, motivation, example, or derivation. The reader works through the body and arrives at the contract having learned nothing the contract did not state more precisely. Compare NAT-zero's body, which proves `¬(n < 0)` from the axiom clauses — that body advances reasoning. NAT-addcompat's body merely re-types the contract.
**What needs resolving**: Either delete the duplicate bullets and let the Formal Contract carry the axiom statement, or replace them with prose that advances something the contract does not — e.g., why both left and right compatibility are independent clauses (one does not derive from the other in the absence of commutativity, which is not yet posited), or why strict successor inequality is bundled with the compatibility clauses rather than placed in NAT-closure beside `0 < 1`.
