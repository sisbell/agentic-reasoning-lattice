### NAT-closure body paragraph on signature semantics is general-purpose meta-prose
**Class**: OBSERVE
**Foundation**: NAT-closure (NatArithmeticClosureAndIdentity)
**ASN**: > "The signature `+ : ℕ × ℕ → ℕ` carries two load-bearing commitments. Its domain `ℕ × ℕ` makes `+` total on the naturals — every pair of naturals has a sum — and its codomain `ℕ` closes the operation under addition, so compositional terms like `(m + n) + p` re-enter the signature without a side condition. Totality rules out partial addition and closure rules out sums that escape ℕ."

**Issue**: This paragraph teaches the reader what a function signature means in general — that domain implies totality, that codomain implies closure, that side conditions are absent — rather than stating what *this* axiom commits beyond the symbol `+ : ℕ × ℕ → ℕ` itself. The closing sentence ("Totality rules out partial addition and closure rules out sums that escape ℕ") is a contrastive paraphrase that adds nothing the signature did not already state. Per the rubric, "new prose around an axiom explains why the axiom is needed rather than what it says" — this is a borderline instance: it explains what signatures-in-general commit to, contrasted with hypothetical alternatives the axiom does not adopt. Sound; flagged for the reviser-drift pattern.
