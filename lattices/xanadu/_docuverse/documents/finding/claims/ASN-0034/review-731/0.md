### NAT-discrete derivation narrative is self-inconsistent about what the m < n branch establishes
**Class**: OBSERVE
**Foundation**: NAT-discrete (NatDiscreteness)
**ASN**: NAT-discrete — prose derivation of the no-interval consequence, specifically: "each of which we walk to `n = m`" … "The case `m < n` is therefore impossible; it contributes nothing to the conclusion and is discharged by contradiction. … Both branches of the split terminate in `n = m`, closing the case analysis."
**Issue**: The opening phrase "each of which we walk to `n = m`" commits to both branches producing `n = m` directly. The proof then shows the `m < n` branch is contradictory and "contributes nothing to the conclusion." The closing phrase "Both branches of the split terminate in `n = m`" reconciles them only via *ex falso* — the contradiction discharges to `n = m` vacuously. The narrative first promises a direct walk, then admits one branch is a contradiction, then claims both branches reach `n = m`. A precise reader must decide whether to interpret "terminate in `n = m`" as (a) direct derivation or (b) vacuous truth from contradiction; the text supports neither cleanly. The Formal Contract consequence is correct; the imprecision is confined to the prose derivation.

VERDICT: OBSERVE
