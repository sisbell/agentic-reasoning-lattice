### NAT-discrete no-interval proof: surviving-case conclusion attributed to both arms
**Class**: OBSERVE
**Foundation**: NAT-discrete (NatDiscreteness), no-interval Consequence derivation
**ASN**: NAT-discrete prose and Formal Contract Consequence
> "Both branches of the split terminate in `n = m`, closing the case analysis."

**Issue**: The `m < n` arm does not terminate in `n = m`. It terminates in ⊥: both disjuncts of `m + 1 ≤ n` (unfolded) are individually inconsistent with the hypothesis `n < m + 1`, so the entire arm is vacuous. The conclusion `n = m` comes exclusively from the surviving `m = n` arm. Saying both arms terminate in `n = m` obscures the proof structure: one arm is discharged by contradiction; the other yields the result directly. Under a discipline where each arm's exit condition is tracked, this matters for auditing the completeness of the case analysis.
