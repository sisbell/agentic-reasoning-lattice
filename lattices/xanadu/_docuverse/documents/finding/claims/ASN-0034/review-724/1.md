### OrdinalDisplacement mischaracterizes NAT-closure's Consequence `0 < 1` as a posited axiom
**Class**: REVISE
**Foundation**: NAT-closure (NatArithmeticClosureAndIdentity)
**ASN**: OrdinalDisplacement — derivation body: "NAT-closure posits `0 < 1` directly as the distinctness clause of its two named constants" and Depends annotation: "supplies the axiom `0 < 1` that anchors the `n ≥ 1 ⟹ n ≠ 0` promotion."
**Issue**: NAT-closure's Formal Contract lists `0 < 1` under *Consequence*, not *Axiom*. It is derived — from successor-positivity at `n := 0` composed with left-identity at `n := 1` via substitutivity — and depends on NAT-zero and NAT-order to run that derivation. The word "posits" and the label "the axiom" both misrepresent the claim's status. A downstream consumer tracing the dependency chain would look for `0 < 1` in NAT-closure's *Axiom* block and not find it.
**What needs resolving**: Both occurrences — the prose sentence and the Depends annotation — must correctly describe `0 < 1` as a derived *Consequence* of NAT-closure.
