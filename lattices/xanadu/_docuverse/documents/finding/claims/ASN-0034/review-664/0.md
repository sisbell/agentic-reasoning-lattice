### ≤ definition appears after the consequence that uses it in NAT-order's Formal Contract
**Class**: REVISE
**Foundation**: (foundation ASN — internal consistency only)
**ASN**: NAT-order (NatStrictTotalOrder), *Consequence:* ≤-transitivity bullet
**Issue**: The Formal Contract presents *Consequence* ≤-transitivity — which quantifies over `m ≤ n`, `n ≤ p`, and `m ≤ p` — before the *Definition* bullet that introduces `m ≤ n ⟺ m < n ∨ m = n`. A reader working through the Formal Contract top-to-bottom encounters `≤` as an ungrounded symbol in the consequence statement before its defining equivalence is stated. The *Axiom* section introduces only `<`; `≤` is not grounded until the *Definition* section that follows the consequence.
**What needs resolving**: The *Definition* items for `≤`, `≥`, and `>` must appear before the *Consequence* bullet that quantifies over them. The Formal Contract must be self-consistent in declaration order: defined symbols precede the theorems that use them.
