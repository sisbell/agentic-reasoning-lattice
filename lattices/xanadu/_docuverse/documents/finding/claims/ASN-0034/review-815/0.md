### T10a.3 Depends missing NAT-order
**Class**: REVISE
**Foundation**: NAT-order (NatStrictTotalOrder) — transitivity of `<`, `≤`-definition `m ≤ n ⟺ m < n ∨ m = n`, and the `>`, `≥` companion definitions
**ASN**: T10a.3 (LengthSeparation) — Formal Contract *Depends:* section
**Issue**: The proof invokes NAT-order's order-theoretic properties directly at three sites, none of which are discharged by the listed dependencies:

1. **Para 2 (child length)**: "NAT-addcompat's strict successor gives `γ < γ+1`, and left order-compatibility lifts `1 ≤ k'` to `γ+1 ≤ γ+k'`. Chaining: every child output has length `γ+k' > γ`." The chaining step `γ < γ+1 ∧ γ+1 ≤ γ+k' → γ < γ+k'` is mixed `<`-`≤` transitivity (NAT-order). The `>` notation is NAT-order's companion definition. Neither is provided by NAT-addcompat alone.

2. **Running-sum induction, inductive step**: "chaining yields `k'₁+…+k'_{i+1} ≥ i+1`" from `k'₁+…+k'_{i+1} ≥ i+k'_{i+1}` and `i+1 ≤ i+k'_{i+1}`. This is `≥`-`≤` transitivity, from NAT-order.

3. **Local monotonicity**: The step establishing `#output(A) < #output(A) + S` (where `S ≥ 1`) uses NAT-addcompat's strict successor and left order-compatibility to obtain `#output(A) < #output(A)+1 ≤ #output(A)+S → #output(A) < #output(A)+S`. The final chain is again mixed `<`-`≤` transitivity.

The pattern in this ASN is that every direct use of `<`, `>`, `≤`, `≥`, or transitivity of the ℕ-order requires an explicit NAT-order citation (T10a, T10a.5, NAT-sub, NAT-addbound, TA5a, T4, T1, Prefix, TA5-SIG, TA5-SigValid all cite it). T10a.3's Depends does not.
**What needs resolving**: NAT-order must be added to T10a.3's Depends, annotated as supplying the `>` and `≥` companion definitions (used in the child-length conclusion and local-monotonicity notation), transitivity of `<` and the mixed `<`-`≤` chain (used at the chaining step in paragraph 2 and in local monotonicity), and `≤`-transitivity (used in the running-sum induction's chaining step).
