### Divergence case (i) asserts uniqueness without establishing it
**Class**: OBSERVE
**Foundation**: NAT-wellorder (NatWellOrdering)
**ASN**: Divergence — Definition clause (i)
**Issue**: The definition states that `divergence(a, b)` in case (i) is "equivalently, the unique k satisfying [the full minimality condition]," but the equivalence between "least k in S" and "unique k satisfying the condition including the minimality universal" is not argued. The argument is short — if k and k' both satisfy the full condition and k < k', the minimality universal for k' at position i = k yields aₖ = bₖ, contradicting k ∈ S; symmetrically for k' < k — but it is not present. NAT-wellorder grounds existence of the minimum; uniqueness requires the separate step above.

VERDICT: OBSERVE
