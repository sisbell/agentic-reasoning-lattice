**TA5-SIG (LastSignificantPosition).** We define the *last significant position* of a tumbler `t ‚ąą T`, written `sig(t)`.

When `t` has at least one nonzero component ‚ÄĒ that is, `(E i : 1 ‚Č§ i ‚Č§ #t : tŠĶĘ ‚Č† 0)` ‚ÄĒ we set `sig(t)` to the rightmost index at which `t` is nonzero. Let `S = {i : 1 ‚Č§ i ‚Č§ #t ‚ąß tŠĶĘ ‚Č† 0}`; the existence hypothesis makes `S` nonempty, and every `i ‚ąą S` is bounded above by `#t`.

The maximum of a bounded nonempty ‚Ąē-subset is not delivered directly by NAT-wellorder, which states only the *least-element* principle; ‚Ąē has no greatest element, so the principle does not dualize unconditionally, and greatest elements are guaranteed only on subsets bounded above. We derive `max(S)` from the least-element principle with `#t` as the explicit boundedness witness. Form the upper-bound set `U = {u ‚ąą ‚Ąē : (A i ‚ąą S :: i ‚Č§ u)}`; every `i ‚ąą S` satisfies `i ‚Č§ #t`, so `#t ‚ąą U` and `U ‚Č† ‚ąÖ`. NAT-wellorder applied to `U` delivers a least upper bound `m`. We claim `m ‚ąą S`. Suppose otherwise: every `i ‚ąą S` satisfies `i ‚Č§ m` (since `m ‚ąą U`) and `i ‚Č† m` (since `m ‚ąČ S`), so NAT-order's `‚Č§`-defining clause `i ‚Č§ m ‚üļ i < m ‚ą® i = m` forces `i < m`, and NAT-discrete's forward direction then gives `i + 1 ‚Č§ m` for every `i ‚ąą S`. Fixing any `i‚āÄ ‚ąą S` ‚ÄĒ `S ‚Č† ‚ąÖ` supplies such a witness ‚ÄĒ we have `1 ‚Č§ i‚āÄ` (since `S ‚äÜ {1, ‚Ä¶, #t}`), and NAT-addcompat's strict successor inequality supplies `i‚āÄ < i‚āÄ + 1`; the chain `1 ‚Č§ i‚āÄ < i‚āÄ + 1 ‚Č§ m` ‚ÄĒ with the weak bounds dispatched into `<`-or-`=` cases by NAT-order's `‚Č§`-defining clause and the strict segments composed by NAT-order's transitivity of `<` ‚ÄĒ yields `1 < m`, hence `m ‚Č• 1`. NAT-sub's conditional closure at `m ‚Č• 1` supplies `m ‚ąí 1 ‚ąą ‚Ąē`. The value `m ‚ąí 1` is strictly below `m`: NAT-sub's right-inverse gives `(m ‚ąí 1) + 1 = m`, and NAT-addcompat's `(m ‚ąí 1) < (m ‚ąí 1) + 1` rewrites via that identity to `m ‚ąí 1 < m`. And `m ‚ąí 1` bounds `S`: each `i ‚ąą S` satisfies `i + 1 ‚Č§ m`, which splits via NAT-order's `‚Č§`-defining clause into `i + 1 = m` (then NAT-sub's right telescoping `(i + 1) ‚ąí 1 = i` gives `i = m ‚ąí 1`) or `i + 1 < m` (then NAT-sub's strict monotonicity at `p = 1` requires both `i + 1 ‚Č• 1` and `m ‚Č• 1`; `m ‚Č• 1` is available from the chain `1 ‚Č§ i‚āÄ < i‚āÄ + 1 ‚Č§ m` established above, and `i + 1 ‚Č• 1` is obtained for the running `i ‚ąą S` by chaining `1 ‚Č§ i` (since `S ‚äÜ {1, ‚Ä¶, #t}`) with NAT-addcompat's strict successor inequality `i < i + 1` ‚ÄĒ the weak bound `1 ‚Č§ i` dispatched into `<`-or-`=` cases by NAT-order's `‚Č§`-defining clause and the strict segment composed by NAT-order's transitivity of `<` ‚ÄĒ yielding `1 < i + 1`, hence `1 ‚Č§ i + 1` by the `‚Č§`-defining clause; strict monotonicity then gives `(i + 1) ‚ąí 1 < m ‚ąí 1`, i.e., `i < m ‚ąí 1`); in both cases `i ‚Č§ m ‚ąí 1`. So `m ‚ąí 1 ‚ąą U` with `m ‚ąí 1 < m`, contradicting minimality of `m` in `U`. Therefore `m ‚ąą S`, and we set `sig(t) = m = max(S)`.

When every component of `t` is zero ‚ÄĒ that is, `(A i : 1 ‚Č§ i ‚Č§ #t : tŠĶĘ = 0)` ‚ÄĒ we set `sig(t) = #t`.

In both cases `1 ‚Č§ sig(t) ‚Č§ #t`, since `#t ‚Č• 1` for every `t ‚ąą T`.

*Formal Contract:*
- *Preconditions:* `t ‚ąą T` (any tumbler with `#t ‚Č• 1`).
- *Definition:* `sig(t) = max({i : 1 ‚Č§ i ‚Č§ #t ‚ąß tŠĶĘ ‚Č† 0})` when `(E i : 1 ‚Č§ i ‚Č§ #t : tŠĶĘ ‚Č† 0)`; `sig(t) = #t` when `(A i : 1 ‚Č§ i ‚Č§ #t : tŠĶĘ = 0)`.
- *Depends:*
  - T0 (CarrierSetDefinition) ‚ÄĒ supplies `t ‚ąą T` as finite ‚Ąē-sequences with `#t ‚Č• 1`, component projection `tŠĶĘ`, and the length `#t`.
  - NAT-zero (NatZeroMinimum) ‚ÄĒ supplies `0 ‚ąą ‚Ąē` for the literal `0` appearing in the set-comprehension condition `tŠĶĘ ‚Č† 0` of the nonzero-case definition `sig(t) = max({i : 1 ‚Č§ i ‚Č§ #t ‚ąß tŠĶĘ ‚Č† 0})` and in the all-zero condition `(A i : 1 ‚Č§ i ‚Č§ #t : tŠĶĘ = 0)` of the all-zero-case definition.
  - NAT-closure (NatArithmeticClosureAndIdentity) ‚ÄĒ supplies `1 ‚ąą ‚Ąē` and closure of ‚Ąē under addition.
  - NAT-wellorder (NatWellOrdering) ‚ÄĒ supplies the least-element principle.
  - NAT-order (NatStrictTotalOrder) ‚ÄĒ supplies the `‚Č§`-defining clause `p ‚Č§ q ‚üļ p < q ‚ą® p = q` and transitivity of `<`.
  - NAT-discrete (NatDiscreteness) ‚ÄĒ supplies the forward direction `i < m ‚üĻ i + 1 ‚Č§ m`.
  - NAT-sub (NatPartialSubtraction) ‚ÄĒ supplies conditional closure `m ‚Č• 1 ‚üĻ m ‚ąí 1 ‚ąą ‚Ąē`, the right-inverse `(m ‚ąí 1) + 1 = m`, the right-telescoping clause `(i + 1) ‚ąí 1 = i`, and strict monotonicity at `p = 1`.
  - NAT-addcompat (NatAdditionOrderAndSuccessor) ‚ÄĒ supplies the strict successor inequality `(A n ‚ąą ‚Ąē :: n < n + 1)`.
- *Postconditions:* `1 ‚Č§ sig(t) ‚Č§ #t`.
