## Question 2 [nelson]

> What is the relationship between baptizing a position at one level of the hierarchy and the existence of a baptized position at the level above it?

### Nelson's Answer

Nelson is explicit that baptism flows through ownership of the parent:

> "The basic principle is that of owned numbers. Numbers are owned by individuals or companies, and subnumbers under them are bestowed on other individuals and companies on whatever basis the owners choose. Whoever owns a specific node, account, document or version may in turn designate (respectively) new nodes, accounts, documents and versions, by forking their integers. We often call this the 'baptism' of new numbers." (4/17)

> "The owner of a given item controls the allocation of the numbers under it." (4/20)

The prerequisite is ownership: to baptize beneath a position, you must own that position. Ownership implies the position exists — you cannot own something that was never baptized. The relationship is a strict chain: the parent must be a baptized, owned position before children can be baptized beneath it.

Nelson describes the hierarchy as a delegation chain:

> "Whoever owns a specific node, account, document or version may in turn designate (respectively) new nodes, accounts, documents and versions" (4/17)

Each level (node, account, document, version) is baptized by the owner of the level above. The chain starts at the root and extends downward through owned positions. No level can be skipped — you cannot baptize a document without an account to own it, or an account without a node to own it.
