**T4a (SyntacticEquivalence).** T4's field-segment constraint — stated positionally as (i) no two zeros are adjacent, (ii) `t₁ ≠ 0`, (iii) `t_{#t} ≠ 0` — is equivalent to the condition that every *field segment* of `t` is non-empty, where the field segments are the `zeros(t) + 1` maximal contiguous sub-sequences of non-zero positions delimited by the zeros. T4 asserts the positional form of this constraint directly; this theorem records the equivalent reading in terms of segment non-emptiness. Once T4c (LevelDetermination) classifies T4-valid tumblers into the four levels *node address*, *user address*, *document address*, *element address* by zero count, T4a reads as the semantic statement that every field segment present in an address has at least one component — but the equivalence itself is proven here without appeal to any such classification.

Let `t ∈ T` be a tumbler satisfying T4's zero-count bound `zeros(t) ≤ 3` — and *only* that bound; the field-segment constraint of full T4-validity (no adjacent zeros, `t₁ ≠ 0`, `t_{#t} ≠ 0`) is deliberately *not* presumed here, since establishing its equivalence with segment non-emptiness is the substantive work of this theorem and assuming it as a precondition would render the Forward direction vacuous (its conclusion would already be in hand by hypothesis) and the Reverse direction circular (it would re-derive what the precondition already supplied). Under that single bound the count `k = zeros(t)` lies in `{0, 1, 2, 3}` — with zeros at positions `p₁ < p₂ < … < pₖ`, where the strict increase of that enumeration is licensed by NAT-order (strict total order on ℕ). The bound `k ≤ 3` is exactly what makes the case analyses `k = 0, 1, 2, 3` carried out in the Forward and Reverse directions exhaustive over the stated domain; without it a residual branch `k ≥ 4` would be admitted, and the proof as written would leave that branch untreated. Set `p₀ = 0` and `p_{k+1} = #t + 1` as sentinels. The `k + 1` field segments of `t` are then the index ranges `(p_i, p_{i+1})` for `i = 0, 1, …, k` — i.e. the component sub-sequences `t[p_i + 1 .. p_{i+1} - 1]`. A segment is non-empty precisely when `p_{i+1} ≥ p_i + 2`. Every index not in `{p₁, …, pₖ}` is by definition a non-zero position of `t`, and since T0 fixes the carrier as ℕ, NAT-zero supplies `0 ≤ tᵢ` as a lower bound, and NAT-discrete at `m = 0` rules out the interval `0 ≤ tᵢ < 1` under `tᵢ ≠ 0`, such a position carries a strictly positive value — no supplementary positivity axiom on T4 is required.

*Forward.* Assume every field segment is non-empty. We derive each positional condition.

*Condition (ii): `t₁ ≠ 0`.* The first field segment is `t[1 .. p₁ - 1]` when `k ≥ 1`, or `t[1 .. #t]` when `k = 0`; in either case its first index is 1. Segment non-emptiness gives `p₁ ≥ 2` (when `k ≥ 1`) or `#t ≥ 1` (when `k = 0`), so index 1 lies in the segment and `t₁` is a non-zero component. Hence `t₁ ≠ 0`.

*Condition (iii): `t_{#t} ≠ 0`.* The last field segment is `t[p_k + 1 .. #t]` when `k ≥ 1`, or `t[1 .. #t]` when `k = 0`; in either case its last index is `#t`. Segment non-emptiness gives `p_k ≤ #t - 1` (when `k ≥ 1`) or `#t ≥ 1` (when `k = 0`), so index `#t` lies in the segment and `t_{#t}` is a non-zero component. Hence `t_{#t} ≠ 0`.

*Condition (i): no adjacent zeros.* Suppose for contradiction that `tᵢ = 0 ∧ tᵢ₊₁ = 0` for some `i` with `1 ≤ i < #t`. Then `i` and `i + 1` are consecutive zero positions — say `p_j = i` and `p_{j+1} = i + 1` — and the interior field segment `t[p_j + 1 .. p_{j+1} - 1] = t[i + 1 .. i]` has no indices, so it is empty. This contradicts segment non-emptiness. Hence no two zeros are adjacent.

*Reverse.* Assume conditions (i), (ii), and (iii) hold. We show every field segment is non-empty.

*First segment (`i = 0`).* The segment occupies indices 1 through `p₁ - 1` (or 1 through `#t` when `k = 0`). If `k = 0` the segment equals `t` itself, which has `#t ≥ 1` indices. If `k ≥ 1`, condition (ii) forces `t₁ ≠ 0`, so index 1 is not a zero position, and therefore `p₁ ≥ 2` — the segment has at least one index.

*Last segment (`i = k`).* The segment occupies indices `p_k + 1` through `#t` (when `k ≥ 1`). Condition (iii) forces `t_{#t} ≠ 0`, so index `#t` is not a zero position, and therefore `p_k ≤ #t - 1` — the segment has at least one index.

*Interior segments (`1 ≤ i < k`).* Each such segment occupies indices `p_i + 1` through `p_{i+1} - 1`. Condition (i) forbids `p_{i+1} = p_i + 1`, and since the zero positions are strictly increasing we already have `p_{i+1} ≥ p_i + 1`; together these give `p_{i+1} ≥ p_i + 2`, so the segment has at least one index.

All segments — first, interior, and last — are non-empty. ∎

*Formal Contract:*
- *Preconditions:* `t ∈ T` with `zeros(t) ≤ 3` (T4's zero-count bound) — strictly weaker than full T4-validity, since T4's field-segment constraint (no adjacent zeros, `t₁ ≠ 0`, `t_{#t} ≠ 0`) is *not* assumed as a hypothesis. The theorem proves the equivalence between that constraint (in its positional form (i), (ii), (iii)) and segment non-emptiness, so taking T4-validity as the precondition would presume the very positional conditions the Forward direction concludes and the Reverse direction discharges; the equivalence would then collapse to a tautology on the antecedent side and the proof would carry no genuine bidirectional content. Under the single bound `zeros(t) ≤ 3` the zero-position list `p₁ < p₂ < … < pₖ` is indexed by `k = zeros(t) ∈ {0, 1, 2, 3}` and the case analysis `k = 0, 1, 2, 3` in both the Forward and Reverse directions is exhaustive over the stated domain; without this bound a residual branch `k ≥ 4` would be admitted and left untreated. By T0 every component of `t` lies in ℕ, and by NAT-zero (`0 ≤ n` for every `n ∈ ℕ`) together with NAT-discrete (`m ≤ n < m + 1 ⟹ n = m`, at `m = 0`) a non-zero component is automatically strictly positive; no additional positivity assumption on T4 is invoked.
- *Depends:* T0 (CarrierSetDefinition) — invoked at the opening to fix the carrier as ℕ ("T0 fixes the carrier as ℕ"); T0 supplies the underlying set ℕ on which the NAT-* axioms speak, without which the lower-bound and discreteness citations below would have no carrier to quantify over. NAT-zero (NatZeroMinimum) — invoked at the opening ("NAT-zero supplies `0 ≤ tᵢ` as a lower bound") and in the precondition note that the "non-zero ⇒ strictly positive" step uses NAT-zero jointly with NAT-discrete rather than T0 alone; NAT-zero furnishes the lower-bound premise `0 ≤ tᵢ` required to instantiate NAT-discrete at `m = 0`, without which the interval `0 ≤ tᵢ < 1` could not be formed. NAT-discrete (NatDiscreteness) — invoked at the opening ("NAT-discrete at `m = 0` rules out the interval `0 ≤ tᵢ < 1` under `tᵢ ≠ 0`") and in the precondition note; NAT-discrete converts the lower bound `0 ≤ tᵢ` together with `tᵢ ≠ 0` into strict positivity `tᵢ ≥ 1`, and without it the equivalence "non-zero ⇔ strictly positive" on ℕ would fail (the reals ℝ admit `0 < r < 1` and satisfy NAT-zero but not NAT-discrete). NAT-order (NatStrictTotalOrder) — invoked throughout the zero-position analysis at `p₁ < p₂ < … < pₖ` (strict total order on ℕ licenses the strictly increasing enumeration) and at the boundary and spacing comparisons `p₁ ≥ 2`, `p_k ≤ #t - 1`, `p_{i+1} ≥ p_i + 2` used in the Forward and Reverse directions; without NAT-order neither the strictly increasing enumeration of zero positions nor the inequality manipulations on those positions would be licensed. T4 (HierarchicalParsing) — supplies the positional conditions (i), (ii), (iii) that the equivalence is proved *against* (these appear as the conclusion side of the Forward direction and the local hypothesis of the Reverse direction, not as standing preconditions of the theorem), the field-segment terminology used throughout, and the zero-count bound `zeros(t) ≤ 3` — the only T4 clause carried as a precondition — that licenses the restriction `k = zeros(t) ∈ {0, 1, 2, 3}` on the zero-position list `p₁ < p₂ < … < pₖ`; this bound is what makes the case analyses `k = 0, 1, 2, 3` of the Forward and Reverse directions exhaustive over the precondition domain, and without it the proof would leave a residual `k ≥ 4` branch untreated. The Forward and Reverse directions are both stated as derivations between T4's positional form and the segment-non-emptiness reading, with neither direction depending on the field-segment constraint as an assumption.
- *Postconditions:* The three positional conditions of T4's field-segment constraint — (i) no two zeros are adjacent, (ii) `t₁ ≠ 0`, (iii) `t_{#t} ≠ 0` — hold if and only if every field segment of `t` (a maximal contiguous sub-sequence of non-zero positions delimited by the zeros of `t`) is non-empty.
