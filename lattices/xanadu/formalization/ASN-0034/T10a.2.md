**T10a.2 (NonNestingSiblingPrefixes).** Distinct siblings from the same allocator are prefix-incomparable: `tᵢ ⋠ tⱼ ∧ tⱼ ⋠ tᵢ`.

Let `tᵢ` and `tⱼ` be distinct siblings from the same allocator with `i < j`. We must show neither is a prefix of the other.

First, `tᵢ ≠ tⱼ`. By TA5(a), each application of `inc(·, 0)` produces a strictly greater tumbler under T1, so `tₙ < tₙ₊₁` for every `0 ≤ n < j`. For adjacent siblings (`j = i + 1`) the conclusion `tᵢ < tⱼ` is immediate from this one-step increase. For non-adjacent siblings (`j > i + 1`), the chain `tᵢ < tᵢ₊₁ < ··· < tⱼ` requires `j − i − 1` applications of T1(c) (transitivity) to yield `tᵢ < tⱼ`. By T1(a) (irreflexivity), `tᵢ ≠ tⱼ`.

Second, `#tᵢ = #tⱼ` by T10a.1, since both are siblings of the same allocator.

Now suppose for contradiction that `tᵢ ≼ tⱼ`. By the Prefix definition, this yields `#tᵢ ≤ #tⱼ` together with the positional-agreement conjunct `(A k : 1 ≤ k ≤ #tᵢ : (tⱼ)ₖ = (tᵢ)ₖ)` on the shorter prefix's length — the sole content Prefix supplies here, component-wise matching across positions `1..#tᵢ` and nothing more. Combined with `#tᵢ = #tⱼ` established above, the range `1 ≤ k ≤ #tᵢ` exhausts every position of both tumblers. T3 (CanonicalRepresentation) — `#a = #b ∧ (A i : 1 ≤ i ≤ #a : aᵢ = bᵢ) ≡ a = b` — converts this equal-length positional agreement into identity `tᵢ = tⱼ`, contradicting `tᵢ ≠ tⱼ` established in the first paragraph. The symmetric argument excludes `tⱼ ≼ tᵢ`: Prefix yields `#tⱼ ≤ #tᵢ` with positional agreement `(A k : 1 ≤ k ≤ #tⱼ : (tᵢ)ₖ = (tⱼ)ₖ)` on `1..#tⱼ`; `#tⱼ = #tᵢ` promotes that range to exhaust both tumblers; and T3 again collapses equal-length positional agreement to identity `tⱼ = tᵢ`, once more contradicting `tᵢ ≠ tⱼ`.

Therefore `tᵢ ⋠ tⱼ ∧ tⱼ ⋠ tᵢ` — the sibling prefixes are non-nesting. The full tree-wide guarantee — that allocators not in an ancestor-descendant relationship also have prefix-incomparable output domains — is established in T10a.5 (CrossAllocatorIncomparability). ∎

*Formal Contract:*
- *Precondition:* `tᵢ`, `tⱼ` are distinct siblings from the same allocator (`i ≠ j`).
- *Postcondition:* `tᵢ ⋠ tⱼ ∧ tⱼ ⋠ tᵢ` — neither is a prefix of the other.
- *Depends:* T10a.1 (UniformSiblingLength) establishes `#tᵢ = #tⱼ`, the length equality that promotes Prefix's positional-agreement clause on `1..#tᵢ` (respectively `1..#tⱼ`) to agreement across every position of both tumblers — the precondition T3 needs for collapsing positional agreement to identity. Prefix (PrefixRelation) supplies the positional-agreement conjunct `(A k : 1 ≤ k ≤ #tᵢ : (tⱼ)ₖ = (tᵢ)ₖ)` of the assumed relation `tᵢ ≼ tⱼ` (and symmetrically for `tⱼ ≼ tᵢ`) — the definitional content of the prefix relation on the shorter tumbler's positions, nothing more; the step from equal-length positional agreement to identity is not a Prefix consequence and is discharged separately by T3. T3 (CanonicalRepresentation), postcondition `#a = #b ∧ (A i : 1 ≤ i ≤ #a : aᵢ = bᵢ) ≡ a = b` — the authority that converts the equal-length positional agreement obtained by combining T10a.1 with Prefix's conjunct into identity `tᵢ = tⱼ` (respectively `tⱼ = tᵢ`), supplying the contradiction that refutes the assumed prefix relation. TA5 (HierarchicalIncrement), postcondition (a) — `inc(t, 0) > t` — supplies the per-step strict increase `tₙ < tₙ₊₁` invoked for adjacent siblings (`j = i + 1`) and at every step of the chain for non-adjacent siblings. T1 (LexicographicOrder), postcondition (c) transitivity — lifts that per-step increase through the `j − i − 1` applications named explicitly in the proof to yield `tᵢ < tⱼ` for non-adjacent siblings; postcondition (a) irreflexivity — converts the strict inequality `tᵢ < tⱼ` into distinctness `tᵢ ≠ tⱼ`. Together: TA5(a) and T1(a), T1(c) deliver `tᵢ ≠ tⱼ`; T10a.1 forces `#tᵢ = #tⱼ`; Prefix's positional-agreement conjunct on `1..#tᵢ` extends under this equal length to exhaust both tumblers; and T3 collapses that equal-length positional agreement to identity, contradicting distinctness.
