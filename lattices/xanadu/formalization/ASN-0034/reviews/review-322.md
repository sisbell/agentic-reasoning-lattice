*2026-04-18 19:50*

### ReverseInverse's Depends omits T0 and NAT-zero despite per-step citation discipline elsewhere
**Foundation**: T0 (CarrierSetDefinition), NAT-zero (NatZeroMinimum)
**ASN**: ReverseInverse, Formal Contract Depends list. Preconditions write `a ∈ T`, `w ∈ T`, `k = #a`, `#w = k`, `(A i : 1 ≤ i < k : aᵢ = 0)`; Step 1 reads `aₖ`, `wₖ` and the zero-tumbler output; Step 3 writes `(y ⊕ w)ᵢ = yᵢ = 0 = wᵢ`, `0 ≤ yₖ`, `0 + wₖ = wₖ`, `0 < 0` (irreflexivity contradiction), and consumes `#(y ⊕ w) = #w`, `#w = k`, `k = #a`. The Depends list enumerates TumblerSub, TumblerAdd, TA-Pos, ActionPoint, TA4, T1, T3, TA3-strict, NAT-sub, NAT-addcompat, NAT-closure, NAT-cancel, NAT-order — but not T0 and not NAT-zero.
**Issue**: The ASN enforces a per-step convention — articulated at TA4's T0 entry ("Transitive availability through TumblerAdd, TumblerSub, or TA0 does not satisfy the convention") and mirrored in TA3-strict, TumblerAdd, TumblerSub, T1, T3, and ActionPoint — that properties writing T0's `∈ T` / `#·` / `·ᵢ` vocabulary directly must cite T0 themselves, and that the literal `0` in ℕ-valued (in)equalities must be discharged from NAT-zero rather than left implicit. ReverseInverse writes T0's vocabulary at its preconditions and at every component and length reference in Steps 1–3, and consumes the literal `0` in ℕ-valued comparisons (including `0 ≤ yₖ` opening the Step 3 strict-promotion chain, `0 + wₖ = wₖ` inside it, and the `0 < 0` irreflexivity refutation that NAT-cancel's equality-ruling-out step produces). Under the stated convention these citations cannot be satisfied transitively through TumblerSub, TumblerAdd, TA4, etc. — each of those is itself a direct T0/NAT-zero consumer and does not re-export the vocabulary as a handle.
**What needs resolving**: Either add T0 and NAT-zero to ReverseInverse's Depends with per-site entries paralleling TA4's (T0 for the precondition-list `∈ T`/`#`/component-typing and for the Step 3 references to `aₖ, wₖ, yₖ ∈ ℕ`; NAT-zero for the literal-`0` sites in Steps 1 and 3), or articulate a scope limit on the per-step convention that legitimately excuses ReverseInverse from the discipline its sibling properties enforce.
