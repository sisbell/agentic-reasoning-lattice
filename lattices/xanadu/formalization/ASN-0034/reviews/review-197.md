### Prefix reflexivity used in T10a.6 Case 2 but not exported by Prefix's Formal Contract
**Foundation**: Prefix (PrefixRelation) — its Formal Contract lists the *Definition* of `≼` / `≺` and one *Derived postcondition* (`p ≺ q ⟹ #p < #q`); it does not state reflexivity `p ≼ p` as a derived postcondition.
**ASN**: T10a.6 (Domain disjointness), Case 2: "cross-allocator prefix-incomparability (T10a.5) is contradicted by `t ≼ t` were `t` shared." The Depends list correspondingly cites "Prefix (reflexivity of ≼)" as if reflexivity were a Prefix export.
**Issue**: The ASN's per-step citation convention requires that a cited property's name resolve to one of that property's stated exports. Prefix's contract exports its Definition and the strict-prefix length postcondition, but not `p ≼ p`. The reflexivity step is derivable from the Definition together with reflexivity of `≤` from NAT-order (and the vacuous universal on indices), but T10a.6 cites it as a Prefix postcondition rather than unfolding the derivation. A future consumer auditing "what does Prefix export?" will not find reflexivity there and the citation will not terminate.
**What needs resolving**: Either add a reflexivity export to Prefix's Formal Contract (e.g., as a second derived postcondition sourced from NAT-order's `≤`-reflexivity and the vacuous-index argument), or rewrite T10a.6's Case 2 and its Depends to route the `t ≼ t` step through Prefix's Definition together with the NAT-order citation that supplies `#t ≤ #t`, matching the per-step sourcing the ASN applies to analogous steps elsewhere.

### T10a.8's "+1 preserves positivity" step bypasses the per-step NAT-citation convention
**Foundation**: (none — internal consistency with ASN's own citation convention, established at T4a's opening, T4c's exhaustion step, TA5-SigValid, TA5, and TA5a for the inferences "non-zero ⇒ strictly positive on ℕ" and "successor on ℕ preserves positivity")
**ASN**: T10a.8 (Uniform sibling zero count), proof: "T4's field-segment constraint (with T0's ℕ carrier) forces that position strictly positive; TA5(b)/TA5(c) modify only that position and preserve length, so the `+1` step keeps it positive and the zero set is unchanged." Depends lists T10a, T10a.4, T4, TA5, TA5-SigValid — no NAT-* axioms.
**Issue**: Two NAT-level inferences are used but not sourced. First, the "non-zero ⇒ strictly positive" step is attributed to "T4's field-segment constraint (with T0's ℕ carrier)" alone, whereas in TA5a's case-analysis preface and Depends the same step is explicitly sourced from T0 + NAT-zero + NAT-discrete (with the explicit argument that NAT-zero supplies `0 ≤ tᵢ` and NAT-discrete rules out `0 ≤ tᵢ < 1`). Second, the claim "the `+1` step keeps it positive" uses a successor-preserves-positivity inference that TA5a's case `k = 0` discharges from NAT-addcompat's strict successor inequality plus NAT-zero; T10a.8 names neither. Under the convention the ASN itself articulates ("matching the per-step citation convention established for T1, TA5, …"), T10a.8's proof and Depends understate its NAT-foundation consumption relative to the structurally identical steps proved under full sourcing in TA5a.
**What needs resolving**: Decide whether T10a.8's argument must route the "strictly positive" and "successor preserves positivity" steps through the same NAT-zero / NAT-discrete / NAT-addcompat citations TA5a uses, and if so add them to the proof text and to T10a.8's Depends; if T10a.8 is permitted to cite these inferences transitively through T10a.4 / TA5a rather than directly, state the delegation explicitly so the Depends list matches what the proof actually consumes.
