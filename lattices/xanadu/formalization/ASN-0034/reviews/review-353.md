Neither is primitive-from-nowhere: the ASN establishes `≤` in NAT-order and `1 ∈ ℕ` in NAT-closure, and T0's own enumeration advertises those axioms as "established as its own foundation object." T0's formal contract, however, has no dependency trace back to either. A consumer of T0 who reads the formal contract to learn what symbols are committed finds `≤` and `1` used with no cited licensor. Previous cycles tightened the `≤`/`0`/`1` traces for TA-Pos and ActionPoint; T0 remains the sole site using these symbols without a Depends entry. The carrier-definition slot publishes content resting on relations whose licensing claim is not acknowledged.
**What needs resolving**: Either add NAT-order and NAT-closure to T0's Depends (with pointer phrases for `≤` and for the numeral `1`), or rewrite T0's bounds without those symbols (e.g., "the set of nonempty finite sequences over ℕ" with no arithmetic predicate, and state the indexing domain by non-emptiness), so T0's contract uses only symbols it licenses or explicitly imports.
