### NAT-closure and NAT-sub are cited as dependencies but never stated
**Foundation**: structural — claims cited in Depends and invoked in proofs must have a stated axiom/definition somewhere in scope
**ASN**: T4 Depends: "NAT-closure (NatArithmeticClosureAndIdentity) — supplies `1 ∈ ℕ` and closure of ℕ under addition, grounding the numerals `2 := 1 + 1` and `3 := 2 + 1` in ℕ so that `zeros(t) ≤ 3` is a comparison of two ℕ-elements." T4b Depends: "NAT-closure (NatArithmeticClosureAndIdentity) — supplies `1 ∈ ℕ` together with closure of ℕ under addition, grounding the numeral `2 := 1 + 1 ∈ ℕ` (used in `s₁ ≥ 2` and `s_{j+1} ≥ s_j + 2`) and the sums `s_i + 1`, `s_i + 2`… NAT-sub (NatPartialSubtraction) — conditional-closure clause at the hypothesis `#t ≥ 1` (T0) gives `#t − 1 ∈ ℕ`…". The stated foundation claims in this ASN are T0, NAT-zero, NAT-discrete, NAT-order, and NAT-addcompat; neither NAT-closure nor NAT-sub appears as a named axiom block anywhere. Declared depends is empty, and the Foundation Statements block is "(none)".
**Issue**: Cycle-4's finding flagged the identical pattern for NAT-addcompat (cited but not stated) and was resolved by adding NAT-addcompat's axiom block. The revision introduces two new foundation claims (NAT-closure, NAT-sub) whose axioms are cited verbatim in multiple Depends and proof bodies — grounding `1 ∈ ℕ`, closure of `+`, the numerals `2` and `3`, and the partial `−` — but neither has an axiom statement in the ASN. Every `s_i + 2`, `s_i − 1`, `#t − 1`, `s₁ ≥ 2`, `s_{j+1} ≥ s_j + 2`, and `zeros(t) ≤ 3` in T4, T4a, T4b, T4c therefore rests on axioms no reader can consult.
**What needs resolving**: Either include NAT-closure (NatArithmeticClosureAndIdentity) and NAT-sub (NatPartialSubtraction) as stated foundation claims in this ASN with explicit axiom bodies, declare them as external dependencies with their statements quoted, or recast the proofs and Definition slots to avoid arithmetic that requires them.

### T4a has no standalone Formal Contract despite being called a "first-class numbered theorem"
**Foundation**: structural — T4b, T4c each appear as numbered theorems with their own *Preconditions/Definition/Depends/Postconditions* blocks; cycle 5's finding on T4c established that a promoted first-class theorem must carry a Formal Contract
**ASN**: T4 prose: "A direct consequence of T4, the first-class numbered theorem **T4a (SyntacticEquivalence)**, proves that the field-segment constraint is equivalent to the condition that every field segment of `t` is non-empty." T4 Postconditions: "T4a (SyntacticEquivalence), a direct consequence: the field-segment constraint is equivalent to every field segment of `t` being non-empty." T4b Depends: "T4a (SyntacticEquivalence) — supplies, via its reverse direction applied to T4's field-segment constraint, the conclusion that every field segment of `t` is non-empty; the position-inequalities `s₁ ≥ 2`, `s_k ≤ #t - 1`, and `s_{j+1} ≥ s_j + 2` that discharge non-emptiness of each extracted sub-sequence are a local re-expression of this conclusion."
**Issue**: T4a is described both as a "first-class numbered theorem" (T4 prose) and as "a direct consequence" postcondition (T4 Formal Contract). No standalone T4a section with its own Preconditions/Depends/Definition/Proof/Postconditions appears in the ASN — in contrast to T4b and T4c which each have full contract blocks. T4b's Depends cites "T4a's reverse direction" as a distinct object with directionality, which is coherent only if T4a is a biconditional claim with a standalone contract to cite; without that section, the "reverse direction" has no referent. The parallel cycle-5 finding required T4c to be given a standalone section; the same structural demand applies to T4a.
**What needs resolving**: Either add a standalone T4a section with its own Formal Contract (Preconditions, Depends, Postconditions including the biconditional whose reverse direction T4b relies on), or drop the "first-class numbered theorem" framing and rewrite T4b's citation to depend on T4 directly (with T4b performing the biconditional reasoning locally).

### T4c's "foundations underwrite neither a strictly positive numeral" caveat contradicts NAT-closure + NAT-addcompat now in scope
**Foundation**: NAT-closure (cited by T4c's own structure as supplying `1 ∈ ℕ`) and NAT-addcompat (`n < n + 1` for every `n ∈ ℕ`) — both referenced within T4c
**ASN**: T4 prose on T4c: "Realisation — existence of a T4-valid tumbler at each zero count — is not claimed: the foundations in scope underwrite neither a strictly positive numeral in ℕ nor tumblers of length ≥ 7, so T4c stands as a pure definition on whatever T4-valid tumblers exist." T4c body: "Realisation…would require the existence of a strictly positive numeral in ℕ and of tumblers of length up to 7, neither of which is underwritten by the foundations in scope (T0, NAT-zero, NAT-discrete, NAT-order, NAT-addcompat)." T4c Injectivity: "NAT-addcompat's strict successor inequality `n < n + 1`, instantiated at `n ∈ {0, 1, 2}`, gives `0 < 1`, `1 < 2`, and `2 < 3`".
**Issue**: The caveat claims no strictly positive numeral in ℕ is underwritten. But NAT-closure (cited in T4 and T4b Depends) supplies `1 ∈ ℕ`, and NAT-addcompat's `n < n + 1` at `n = 0` immediately yields `0 < 1`, so `1 ∈ ℕ⁺` is derivable from the very foundations T4c uses for Injectivity. Furthermore, T4c's own foundations list inside the caveat ("T0, NAT-zero, NAT-discrete, NAT-order, NAT-addcompat") omits NAT-closure — despite T4c depending on numerals `1, 2, 3 ∈ ℕ` in the chain `0 < 1 < 2 < 3`. The caveat's self-stated foundation list is inconsistent with what T4c actually uses, and the conclusion ("no strictly positive numeral") is refuted by the now-stated axioms.
**What needs resolving**: Either revise the realisation caveat to reflect the actual gap (e.g., the missing piece is tumblers of sufficient length, or arbitrary components beyond the numeral `1`), or drop the caveat and address realisation separately. Either way, reconcile T4c's stated foundation list with the axioms T4c actually invokes.

### T4c Depends omits NAT-closure despite invoking numerals 2 and 3
**Foundation**: NAT-closure (cited elsewhere in the ASN as the source of `1 ∈ ℕ` and closure of `+`, grounding `2 := 1 + 1` and `3 := 2 + 1`)
**ASN**: T4c Formal Contract Depends lists: T0, NAT-zero, NAT-discrete, NAT-order, NAT-addcompat, T4. T4c Injectivity: "The values `0, 1, 2, 3` are pairwise distinct in ℕ… `0 < 1`, `1 < 2`, and `2 < 3`". T4c Exhaustion: "`0 ≤ zeros(t) ≤ 3`" and chained increments via NAT-discrete at `m ∈ {0, 1, 2}` promoting `m < zeros(t)` to `m + 1 ≤ zeros(t)`.
**Issue**: T4c uses the numerals `1, 2, 3` and the comparisons `zeros(t) ≤ 3`, `m + 1 ≤ zeros(t)` at `m ∈ {1, 2}`. Without NAT-closure in its Depends, neither `2 ∈ ℕ` nor `3 ∈ ℕ` is licensed in T4c's local scope — the only foundation in T4c's list that asserts an ℕ-membership is NAT-zero's `0 ∈ ℕ`, and NAT-addcompat's `n < n + 1` does not by itself establish `1, 2, 3 ∈ ℕ`. Parallel claims (T4, T4b) do cite NAT-closure for exactly this reason; T4c's omission is a Depends-use mismatch within the same ASN.
**What needs resolving**: Either add NAT-closure to T4c's Depends with the role it plays (grounding `1, 2, 3 ∈ ℕ` for the chain `0 < 1 < 2 < 3` and the bound `zeros(t) ≤ 3`), or recast T4c's Exhaustion and Injectivity to avoid the numerals `2` and `3`.
