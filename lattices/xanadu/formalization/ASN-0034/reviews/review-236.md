# Cone Review — ASN-0034/T10a.8 (cycle 1)

*2026-04-18 04:45*

### T10a-N omits NAT-addcompat for `#a₂ > #a₁` strict length inequality
**Foundation**: TA5's Depends establishes the per-step convention that NAT-addcompat is cited explicitly for the strict successor inequality (`n < n + 1`) and order-compatibility of addition (`p ≤ n ⟹ m + p ≤ m + n`), "rather than [leaving] it as an implicit appeal to ℕ-arithmetic." Previous Findings 4, 6, and 7 have already flagged this for T10a.8, T10a.5, and T10a.3 respectively.
**ASN**: T10a-N (Necessity of sibling restriction) proof: "By TA5(b), since k' > 0, a₂ agrees with a₁ on every position 1 ≤ i ≤ #a₁. By TA5(d), #a₂ = #a₁ + k' > #a₁, so a₂ has components beyond position #a₁. These two facts — agreement on all positions of the shorter tumbler and strict length excess in the longer — are precisely the conditions of the Prefix definition: `a₁ ≼ a₂` with `#a₁ < #a₂`, so `a₁ ≺ a₂`." Depends lists TA5, Prefix, T10a.2, T10a.5, T10 — no NAT-addcompat.
**Issue**: TA5(d) delivers the length equation `#a₂ = #a₁ + k'`; the strict inequality `#a₁ + k' > #a₁` under `k' > 0` is exactly the NAT-addcompat strict-successor / order-compatibility inference that the ASN has committed to citing per-step. The Formal Contract also repeats this: "TA5(b): agreement on positions 1..#t; TA5(d): #inc(t, k') = #t + k'" — giving only equalities, not the strict inequality the necessity argument consumes. Without NAT-addcompat, the step from `#a₂ = #a₁ + k'` with `k' > 0` to `#a₁ < #a₂` is an implicit appeal to ℕ-arithmetic, the same pattern Findings 4, 6, and 7 flagged elsewhere.
**What needs resolving**: T10a-N's proof and Depends must cite NAT-addcompat at the step promoting TA5(d)'s length equation `#a₂ = #a₁ + k'` (with `k' ≥ 1`) to the strict length inequality `#a₁ < #a₂` that the Prefix definition consumes, matching the per-step convention TA5 applies and Previous Findings 4, 6, and 7 established for T10a.8, T10a.5, and T10a.3.
