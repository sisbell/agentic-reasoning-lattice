### Residual `0 < 1` rationale preserved as prose in T0, NAT-card, and NAT-closure
**Class**: REVISE
**Foundation**: NAT-closure Axiom `0 < 1`.
**ASN**: T0 prose: "That this inequality forces `#a ≥ 1` rather than the vacuous `#a ≥ 0` rests on NAT-closure's distinctness clause `0 < 1`: without `0 < 1`, a model interpreting `1` as `0` satisfies every other NAT-* clause and `1 ≤ #a` collapses to `0 ≤ #a`, dropping the 'at least one component' reading. With `0 < 1` in hand, NAT-order's `≤`-unfolding sends `1 ≤ #a` to `1 < #a ∨ 1 = #a`, and composition with `0 < 1` via transitivity (strict branch) or substitution of `=` (equality branch) lifts to `0 < #a`, excluding `#a = 0`." NAT-card prose: "The separation of the empty case … rests on NAT-closure's distinctness clause `0 < 1`: without `0 < 1`, a model interpreting `1` as `0` would admit `1 ∈ {1, …, 0}` and collapse the empty/nonempty anatomy …". NAT-closure Axiom bullet: "distinctness of the two named constants … securing downstream sites that rely on the strict inequality, in particular T0's nonemptiness clause … and NAT-card's initial-segment anatomy `{1, 2, …, n}` at `n = 0`". T0 Depends (NAT-closure bullet): "without `0 < 1`, a model interpreting `1` as `0` would reduce `1 ≤ #a` to the vacuous `0 ≤ #a` …".
**Issue**: This is the second reviser-drift pattern the instructions name: *a paragraph looks like a prior finding's content relocated rather than removed*. The prior "nondistinctness" finding prompted adding `0 < 1` to NAT-closure. The axiom is now in place. The model-theoretic argument showing why it was needed (trivial model `ℕ = {0}` interpreting `1` as `0`) now appears, in substantially the same form, in at least four sites — T0 prose, T0 Depends bullet, NAT-closure Axiom bullet, and NAT-card prose. A reader taking the current axiom at face value does not need to re-run the necessity argument, and a reader who does need it can read it once. As written, each ASN re-litigates the case against a counter-model that the contract now excludes by clause.
**What needs resolving**: Retain at most one short statement of necessity (e.g., one sentence in NAT-closure's body noting that `0 < 1` is the clause that separates `1` from `0`) and remove the trivial-model re-derivations from T0, NAT-card, and the Depends bullets. The downstream sites can cite `0 < 1` directly without re-explaining why it isn't redundant.

### NAT-closure's "foundation-wide pattern" paragraph anticipates axioms not present
**Class**: REVISE
**Foundation**: —
**ASN**: NAT-closure prose: "Left and right identity are stated as independent clauses because the NAT-* axioms of this ASN do not declare commutativity of addition on ℕ — without `m + n = n + m`, neither identity is derivable from the other. This is a foundation-wide pattern: every NAT-* axiom or theorem in this ASN involving `+` that admits a left and a right variant states each variant as an independent clause, for the same reason. The rationale is recorded here, at the site where `+` is introduced, and is not repeated at each subsequent occurrence."
**Issue**: The third reviser-drift pattern: *new prose around an axiom explains why the axiom is needed rather than what it says.* The two identity clauses say what they say; whether they are stated independently or together is a drafting choice the reader does not need to be briefed on. Worse, the "foundation-wide pattern" claim refers to "every NAT-* axiom or theorem in this ASN involving `+` that admits a left and a right variant" — but the only NAT-* axiom involving `+` in this content is NAT-closure itself. The paragraph is making a meta-commitment about future axioms not shown, and leaving a procedural note ("recorded here … not repeated at each subsequent occurrence") that belongs in a style guide rather than a specification.
**What needs resolving**: Remove the paragraph. If there is a genuine concern that commutativity is not assumed, state it once as a short factual note ("commutativity of `+` is not posited") at the point where it matters, not at the point where `+` is introduced.

### NAT-order's "Reading note" duplicates the disjointness-form explanation already given in prose
**Class**: REVISE
**Foundation**: —
**ASN**: NAT-order prose (paragraph following the exactly-one trichotomy derivation): "The familiar disjointness form `m < n ⟹ m ≠ n` is the contrapositive of the mutual-exclusion conjunct `¬(m < n ∧ m = n)` — a derivable restatement of that conjunct, not a separately exported Consequence; a consumer wanting the implicational form unfolds it from the exactly-one trichotomy bullet at the point of use. Stating it directly in the axiom slot would still launder the irreflexivity-plus-indiscernibility derivation through a non-minimal axiom clause, which is why the contract leaves it as a derivable restatement rather than naming it independently." NAT-order Consequence slot: "*Reading note.* The disjointness form `(A m, n ∈ ℕ : m < n : m ≠ n)` is the contrapositive of the conjunct `¬(m < n ∧ m = n)` and is therefore a derivable restatement of that conjunct, not a separately exported Consequence; downstream citations of the implicational form name this exactly-one trichotomy bullet."
**Issue**: The same content appears in two places — a body paragraph and a Reading-note inside the Consequence slot — with overlapping sentences. Both are meta-prose about what the contract does *not* export and why. The contract itself already communicates "not separately exported" by not exporting it; the Reading note is a structural slot carrying essay content about a thing that isn't there.
**What needs resolving**: Drop the Reading note inside the Consequence slot; if the information is useful at all, one sentence in body prose is enough. Alternatively, keep the Reading note as a minimal pointer ("the implicational form `m < n ⟹ m ≠ n` unfolds from this bullet") and drop the body paragraph's extended version.

### NAT-card Depends bullet cites "successor" as the construction for `{1, 2, …, n}`, but no successor operation is in NAT-closure
**Class**: REVISE
**Foundation**: NAT-closure Axiom (posits `+ : ℕ × ℕ → ℕ`, `1 ∈ ℕ`, `0 < 1`, left/right identity). NAT-order Definition (`≤`).
**ASN**: NAT-card Depends, NAT-closure bullet: "posits `1 ∈ ℕ` and closes ℕ under addition, so the initial segment `{1, 2, …, n}` — built from `1 ∈ ℕ` and successor — has ℕ-typed elements and `S ⊆ {1, 2, …, n} ⊆ ℕ` is grounded."
**Issue**: NAT-closure's axiom introduces `+`, `1`, `0 < 1`, and the two identity laws. It does not introduce a "successor" operation, name one, or characterise one. There is also no induction/recursion axiom that would let "built from `1 ∈ ℕ` and successor" construct the finite set `{1, 2, …, n}` as an enumerated union. The phrase conjures a construction the foundation does not supply. The initial segment `{1, 2, …, n}` is, however, grounded: it is the set `{k ∈ ℕ : 1 ≤ k ∧ k ≤ n}`, defined using `≤` from NAT-order and `1, n ∈ ℕ`. So the set exists; the Depends bullet just cites the wrong reason.
**What needs resolving**: Re-ground the initial-segment construction. The bullet should either (a) cite the `≤`-based definition `{k ∈ ℕ : 1 ≤ k ≤ n}` and route the dependency through NAT-order's `≤` plus `1 ∈ ℕ` from NAT-closure, or (b) explicitly posit a successor/initial-segment notation as an axiom somewhere, with the matching recursion characterisation. In either case the word "successor" should not appear in the Depends prose unless NAT-closure's axiom names it.

### NAT-card's codomain clause is entailed by the enumeration-characterisation clause
**Class**: REVISE
**Foundation**: —
**ASN**: NAT-card Axiom: "`(A n ∈ ℕ, S : S ⊆ {1, 2, …, n} :: |S| ∈ ℕ)` (conditional codomain); `|S|` is postulated as a `k ∈ ℕ` such that `S` admits a strictly increasing enumeration … (enumeration characterisation)".
**Issue**: The enumeration-characterisation clause postulates `|S|` as a `k ∈ ℕ`. That already says `|S| ∈ ℕ`. The first clause is therefore derivable from the second and duplicates it. Having both clauses also creates a drafting hazard: a future edit could tighten the codomain in one and not the other. Non-minimal axiom lists invite divergence.
**What needs resolving**: Either fold the codomain into the enumeration-characterisation clause as a single axiom ("`|S|` is postulated as the unique `k ∈ ℕ` such that S admits a strictly increasing enumeration …, and by this postulate `|S| ∈ ℕ`") or drop the standalone "conditional codomain" clause and let the enumeration characterisation do the work.

VERDICT: REVISE
