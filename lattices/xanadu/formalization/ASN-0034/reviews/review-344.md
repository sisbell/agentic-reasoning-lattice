# Regional Review — ASN-0034/TumblerAdd (cycle 7)

*2026-04-20 14:22*

Reading the ASN for new cross-claim issues not covered by Previous Findings.

### NAT-zero body contains a defensive ℤ/−1 counterexample essay in a formal-axiom slot
**Foundation**: NAT-zero (NatZeroMinimum) — a stated foundation axiom delivering `0 ∈ ℕ` and `(A n ∈ ℕ :: 0 ≤ n)`.
**ASN**: NAT-zero body, prose paragraph: "The minimum of a set must be both a member of that set and a lower bound of it — membership so that `0` is among the elements compared, and the inequality so that no element sits below it under `≤`. Either clause alone is strictly weaker: a non-member lower bound (e.g., `−1` bounding ℕ below in ℤ) satisfies the inequality without being an element, and a bare membership claim `0 ∈ ℕ` fixes no ordering of `0` against the other elements."
**Issue**: This is a defensive justification for why the axiom bundles two clauses, discharged via a ℤ counterexample (`−1 ∈ ℤ`) that lives outside the ASN's carrier domain. It mirrors exactly the pattern the cycle-2 regional finding flagged in NAT-wellorder's body (ℤ invoked to show well-ordering is independent of strict total order + discreteness). NAT-wellorder's body has since been pared back; NAT-zero's body now carries the same kind of counterexample-via-foreign-carrier essay in a formal-axiom slot. No claim in the ASN consumes "some non-member of ℕ could bound it below" — the paragraph defends the two-clause axiomatization to an imagined skeptic rather than advancing reasoning the ASN needs. A reader following the axiom must skip past this meta-commentary to reach the next claim.
**What needs resolving**: Either relocate the clause-independence observation out of NAT-zero's claim body (e.g., into a meta-note explaining axiomatization choices), or remove it — NAT-zero's formal-contract slot should carry only the axiom content, mirroring the trim that NAT-wellorder received.

### TA-Pos introduces the set Z as reified notation but no claim in the ASN consumes it
**Foundation**: TA-Pos (PositiveTumbler) — definitional claim introducing `Pos`, `Zero`, and the set-form `Z`.
**ASN**: TA-Pos body: "We additionally introduce the set-form **Z** = {t ∈ T : Zero(t)}, the reification of `Zero` on T, so that `t ∈ Z ⟺ Zero(t)`. `Z` is derived notation, not a new primitive." TA-Pos formal contract, Definition clause includes: "**Z** = {t ∈ T : Zero(t)}."
**Issue**: `Z` is declared in TA-Pos's formal contract and described as the "reification" of `Zero` on T, but no claim in the ASN references `Z` after its introduction — ActionPoint, TumblerAdd, and T3 all route through `Pos(w)` or `Zero(t)` predicates directly. TA-Pos's justification for `Z`'s presence (`the reification of Zero on T`) implies a use-site that expects membership language (`t ∈ Z`) rather than predicate language (`Zero(t)`); the ASN contains no such use-site. The prose hedge "derived notation, not a new primitive" deflects from the question the stated definition raises: why include the set-form in the formal contract of a foundation ASN if nothing stated here consumes it? The DeMorgan-duality paragraph that follows similarly describes how `Zero` *could* have been stated as `¬Pos`, which is commentary about alternative formulations rather than content any downstream claim in this ASN cites.
**What needs resolving**: Either drop `Z` from TA-Pos's formal contract (and the accompanying "reification" prose) until a consumer in this ASN actually uses set-membership language, or identify a stated claim here that consumes `t ∈ Z` and make that dependence explicit in its Depends list so `Z`'s presence in the formal contract is anchored to an in-ASN use-site.
