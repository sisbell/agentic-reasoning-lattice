### T12 Depends omits T0 despite writing `∈ T` directly in postconditions and proof
**Foundation**: T0 (CarrierSetDefinition) — introduces the carrier `T` and length operator `#`. The same direct-citation discipline articulated in the previous findings on Definition (Span), TA0, and TA-strict applies: an ASN that writes T0's vocabulary in its own statements cites T0 directly rather than inheriting it through a handle.
**ASN**: T12 (SpanWellDefinedness). Postcondition (a): "`s ⊕ ℓ ∈ T` (endpoint exists, by TA0)." Postcondition (c) quantifier: "for all `a, c ∈ span(s, ℓ)` and `b ∈ T`". Premise: "`s ∈ T`, `ℓ ∈ T`, `Pos(ℓ)`, and `actionPoint(ℓ) ≤ #s`". Proof writes `#s` (in `k ≤ #s`) and `∈ T` repeatedly (e.g., "the upper bound exists in `T`"). Depends list: "Definition (Span) … T1 … TA0 … TA-strict" — no T0.
**Issue**: T12 writes `∈ T` directly in its premise, in postcondition (a) on the left of `∈ T` (TA0's re-export covers this *as* a postcondition lookup, but T12 also asserts `b ∈ T` in postcondition (c) outside any TA0 handle), and in the premise's `#s` (which TA0 also writes but does not re-export). Under the discipline asserted in the prior findings, T12 needs a direct T0 citation for these; under an alternative transitive convention, the directness in T1, Definition (Span), and TA0 becomes unmotivated. T12 inherits the same unresolved discipline question that the prior findings raised but at a fourth site that the prior findings do not cover.
**What needs resolving**: Either add a T0 entry to T12's Depends naming the `∈ T` and `#` usages in the premise and postconditions (a)/(c), or extend the transitive-inheritance convention proposed in the prior findings' resolutions to cover T12 explicitly and audit whether the existing T0 citations elsewhere in the ASN should then be removed.

### T12 invokes "first nonzero position" semantics without citing ActionPoint
**Foundation**: ActionPoint — the property that defines `actionPoint(·)` (referenced as a Depends entry by Definition (Span), TA0, and TA-strict). Per Definition (Span)'s Depends, ActionPoint "supplies the action-point function `actionPoint(·)`" and "licenses `actionPoint(ℓ)` as a legal index into `ℓ`".
**ASN**: T12 (SpanWellDefinedness), proof opening: "We note that `k ≤ #s` is equivalent to requiring that the number of leading zeros in `ℓ` — which is `k − 1`, since the action point is the first nonzero position — be strictly less than `#s`: the two are restatements of the same arithmetic condition." T12 Depends list: "Definition (Span) … T1 … TA0 … TA-strict" — no ActionPoint entry.
**Issue**: The equivalence claim "the action point is the first nonzero position" and "the number of leading zeros … is `k − 1`" is a structural fact about ActionPoint's defining minimum-position formula, not derivable from any property T12 cites. Definition (Span), TA0, and TA-strict all use `actionPoint(ℓ)` as an opaque function symbol via their preconditions; none of them exposes the "first nonzero position" reading of `k`. T12's parenthetical observation is the only place in this slice that opens up ActionPoint's internal semantics, and ActionPoint is missing from Depends. Whether this aside is load-bearing or merely orienting, the citation discipline used elsewhere in the ASN requires it to be sourced.
**What needs resolving**: Either add an ActionPoint entry to T12's Depends naming the "first nonzero position" / "`k − 1` leading zeros" reading as the cited fact, or remove the parenthetical equivalence from the proof if it is not load-bearing for any subsequent step (the proof's actual invocations of TA0 and TA-strict in parts (a)/(b) use `k ≤ #s` directly and do not require the leading-zeros reformulation).

### T12 transitivity-of-`≤` proof says "the two strict cases" but only one combination is strict-strict
**Foundation**: T1 (LexicographicOrder), part (c) Transitivity — composes a pair of strict inequalities into a strict inequality. T1's Abbreviations introduce `≤` as `< ∨ =`, so `x ≤ y` and `y ≤ z` unfold to four combinations: (<,<), (<,=), (=,<), (=,=).
**ASN**: T12 (SpanWellDefinedness), proof part (c) Contiguity: "Transitivity of `≤` follows: if `x ≤ y` and `y ≤ z`, then by case analysis on the disjunctions (`x < y ∨ x = y` and `y < z ∨ y = z`), each combination yields `x ≤ z` — the two strict cases compose via T1(c), and the equality cases are immediate by substitution."
**Issue**: T1(c) composition applies to exactly one of the four combinations: (<,<) → `x < z`. The other three involve at least one equality and are discharged by substitution rather than by T1(c). The phrase "the two strict cases" has no referent — there is one strict-strict case, two mixed cases (one strict / one equality), and one pure-equality case. If "two strict cases" is intended to mean "the two cases of the first disjunction" or "the two strictness branches", the prose conceals which combinations actually invoke T1(c) versus substitution. A reviser auditing T1(c)'s use sites in this ASN cannot match the count.
**What needs resolving**: Replace "the two strict cases compose via T1(c), and the equality cases are immediate by substitution" with an exhaustive enumeration that names which of the four `(x ≤ y, y ≤ z)` combinations each justification handles — e.g., "the (<,<) case composes via T1(c); the (<,=), (=,<), and (=,=) cases follow by substitution of the equality" — so that each combination has exactly one rule and the T1(c) site count is unambiguous.
