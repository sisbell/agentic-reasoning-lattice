### Tumbler arithmetic preamble claims a TumblerSub construction that is not in the ASN
**Foundation**: The "Tumbler arithmetic" section introduces the operational inventory the rest of the section builds on.
**ASN**: Tumbler arithmetic opening paragraph: "These operations — tumbler addition (⊕, constructed in TumblerAdd) and subtraction (⊖, constructed in TumblerSub, § Tumbler subtraction above) — are not arithmetic on numbers but position-advance operations in a hierarchical address space."
**Issue**: The prose asserts two constructions with equal footing — ⊕ via TumblerAdd and ⊖ via a claim named TumblerSub in a "§ Tumbler subtraction above" section. Neither TumblerSub as a claim nor a "Tumbler subtraction" section exists anywhere in the ASN body as presented. The reference is directional ("above"), so it cannot be read as a forward deferral either. The "Definition of ⊕" subsection that immediately follows deepens the mismatch: the narrative framing ("operations that advance a position by a displacement … and that recover the displacement between two positions") presents ⊖ as a co-equal cornerstone of tumbler arithmetic, yet only ⊕ is ever formalised. A precise reader cannot resolve whether TumblerSub is (a) intended as a stated claim in this ASN that has been dropped, (b) a claim on a prior branch of the document that was relocated, or (c) a claim in a different ASN that the prose mis-classifies as internal.
**What needs resolving**: Either state TumblerSub with its own formal contract in a "Tumbler subtraction" section above Tumbler arithmetic (matching the prose's locational claim), or rewrite the preamble so it only commits to the ⊕/TumblerAdd construction actually present and describes ⊖ without asserting it is defined here.

### TA-Pos defers the Pos-dominates-Zero direction to a claim TA-PosDom that is absent
**Foundation**: TA-Pos (PositiveTumbler) — the definitional claim introducing Pos and Zero and the carrier Z.
**ASN**: TA-Pos body: "The predicate `Pos(t)` is not written `t > 0`, because `>` already denotes T1's lexicographic ordering, and the two notions diverge. … The converse direction — `Pos(t)` implies `t` is T1-greater than every zero tumbler — does hold, but its proof consumes ActionPoint and T1, and is carried out downstream by TA-PosDom."
**Issue**: TA-Pos defers a specific claim ("Pos(t) implies t is T1-greater than every zero tumbler") to a named downstream object, TA-PosDom. No TA-PosDom appears anywhere in the ASN body, neither as a stated claim nor as a declared foundation. TumblerAdd's Depends list mentions "TA-PosDom's Case `#z ≥ k`" in one of the regional-cycle parallels recorded in Previous Findings, treating TA-PosDom as an extant sibling. Because this is a foundation ASN (`Foundation statements: none`), "downstream" cannot refer to external foundation content — it must mean later content in this same ASN, which is not delivered. The divergence-between-Pos-and-T1 observation that motivates TA-Pos's notational choice is load-bearing (Z tumblers being T1-less than every positive tumbler underwrites the way spans, endsets, and containment are later consumed), so leaving the "converse direction" hanging in a named-but-absent claim is a concrete gap, not a bibliographic one.
**What needs resolving**: Either promote TA-PosDom to a stated claim in this ASN with a formal contract capturing the Pos-implies-T1-dominates-all-zeros direction, or rewrite TA-Pos's deferral so it no longer commits to the existence of a named object, and rewrite any companion prose (e.g. TumblerAdd's internal parallels cited in Previous Findings) that treats TA-PosDom as resolvable.

### ActionPoint's NAT-order Depends names trichotomy and irreflexivity but the proof uses neither
**Foundation**: NAT-order (NatStrictTotalOrder) — irreflexivity, transitivity, trichotomy, and the `≤` definition.
**ASN**: ActionPoint Depends entry: "NAT-order (NatStrictTotalOrder) — definition of ≤, trichotomy, irreflexivity." The proof invokes NAT-order exactly once in prose: "by NAT-order's definition `m ≤ n ⟺ m < n ∨ m = n`, this unfolds to `0 < w_{actionPoint(w)} ∨ 0 = w_{actionPoint(w)}`, and `w_{actionPoint(w)} ≠ 0` excludes the equality, leaving `0 < w_{actionPoint(w)}`."
**Issue**: Excluding the equality disjunct uses propositional contradiction with `w_{actionPoint(w)} ≠ 0`; it does not invoke NAT-order's trichotomy (which would be needed only if the mutual exclusivity of `0 < · ` and `0 = ·` had to be argued structurally). Irreflexivity has no citation site in the proof text at all — the minimality step "otherwise `i` would be a smaller member of the set, contradicting minimality" is stated without unpacking how `actionPoint(w) ≤ i` conjoined with `i < actionPoint(w)` is discharged. The Depends inventory therefore over-declares: two named NAT-order clauses are listed with no matching use-site in the proof. This is the mirror of the Previous Finding about T0's citation ledger attributing a NAT-addcompat use that ActionPoint does not make — here the mismatch is within ActionPoint's own Depends rather than T0's enumeration.
**What needs resolving**: Either extend ActionPoint's proof text so the minimality step explicitly discharges through NAT-order's trichotomy/irreflexivity (making both citations load-bearing), or trim the Depends entry to what the proof actually invokes — the `≤` defining clause, and whatever suffices for the minimality step.
