### PartitionMonotonicity's uniqueness argument invokes T3 without citing it
**Foundation**: (foundation ASN; internal consistency review)
**ASN**: PartitionMonotonicity (PartitionMonotonicity), *Total ordering* paragraph, *Uniqueness within a reach* clause: "distinct siblings `u, u' ∈ dom(cᵢ)` have `#u = #u'` (T10a.1, uniform sibling length) and `u ≠ u'` (TA5(a) gives the strictly increasing sibling stream `c_{i,0} < c_{i,1} < c_{i,2} < ...`); if some `s ∈ subtree(u) ∩ subtree(u')` existed, then `u ≼ s` and `u' ≼ s` together with `#u = #u'` would force `u = u'` (a tumbler is determined by its prefix at any given length, since two distinct tumblers of equal length cannot both equal `s` on positions `1..#u`), a contradiction."
**Issue**: The step "u and u' both agree with s on positions 1..#u = 1..#u' ⟹ u = u'" is a direct appeal to T3 (CanonicalRepresentation): two tumblers with equal length that agree on every component are identical. The narrative parenthetical even paraphrases T3's postcondition ("a tumbler is determined by its prefix at any given length"). But PartitionMonotonicity's Depends clause enumerates T5, T10a, T10a.1, T10a.4, TA5-SigValid, TA5, T1, Prefix, PrefixOrderingExtension, T9 — with no T3 citation. The per-step citation convention is enforced elsewhere in the ASN (T10, T10a.5, GlobalUniqueness, and TA5-SigValid each cite T3 directly when converting component-wise agreement into tumbler equality), and T3 is a foundation-tier property whose tightening should flag every consumer.
**What needs resolving**: Either add T3 to PartitionMonotonicity's Depends with a per-step citation pointing at the *Uniqueness within a reach* clause, or demonstrate that the same conclusion can be reached from the Prefix definition alone (noting that Prefix's own Depends cites T3 to derive `p ≺ q ⟹ #p < #q`, so the "ambient" T3-usage would still need an explicit audit). Without the citation, a reviser narrowing T3 has no Depends-backed signal that this proof's subtree-disjointness step consumes it.
