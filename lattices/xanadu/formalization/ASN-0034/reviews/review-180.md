### Projection signatures `N, U, D, E` disagree between T4b's body (total) and T6's Ingredient 1 (partial)
**Foundation**: T4b body — "We read `fields(t)` as the 4-tuple `(N(t), U(t), D(t), E(t))` and fix the projections `N, U, D, E : T → Seq(ℕ⁺)` accordingly"; T4b Definition/Postcondition fix `fields : T ⇀ Seq(ℕ⁺) × Seq(ℕ⁺) × Seq(ℕ⁺) × Seq(ℕ⁺)` as partial with domain the T4-valid subset.
**ASN**: T6, Ingredient 1 — "Write `N(t)`, `U(t)`, `D(t)`, `E(t)` for the node, user, document, and element projections of `fields` respectively — each a partial map `T ⇀ Seq(ℕ⁺)` defined on the same T4-valid subdomain."
**Issue**: Inside T4b itself, the projections `N, U, D, E` are introduced with total arrows `T → Seq(ℕ⁺)`, even though they are the projections of a function whose Definition and Postcondition both declare it partial (`T ⇀ ...`). The projections of a partial function cannot be total without the function itself being total, so T4b's body is internally inconsistent between its projection signatures and the `fields` signature. T6 then re-introduces these same projections as `T ⇀ Seq(ℕ⁺)` (partial), which is the correct reading but contradicts the arrow T4b's body established. The previous finding captured the `fields` signature mismatch; the projection-symbol signatures are a separate instance of the same drift that remains unreconciled.
**What needs resolving**: Restate the projection arrows `N, U, D, E` consistently as partial maps on the T4-valid subdomain wherever they are introduced, so T4b's body, T4b's Definition/Postcondition, and T6's Ingredient 1 agree on a single type signature for each projection.

**What needs resolving**: Either excise T4c from T6's declared dependency family (moving the label-vocabulary remark to non-contract framing), or have T6's proof body make load-bearing use of T4c's level-label biconditional so the Depends entry's self-disclaimer is no longer needed.

### T6(b)/(c)/(d) preamble says "share the same … fields" but Postcondition returns NO even when both tumblers symmetrically lack the field
**Foundation**: T6 preamble — "(b) Whether `a` and `b` share the same node and user fields. (c) Whether `a` and `b` share the same node, user, and document fields … (d) Whether the document field of `a` is a prefix of the document field of `b`"; T6 Formal Contract Postconditions — "(b) returns YES iff `zeros(a) ≥ 1 ∧ zeros(b) ≥ 1 ∧ …`; returns NO if either tumbler lacks a user field" (analogously for (c) and (d)); T4b Postcondition — `U(t) = ε` iff field `U` is absent (an unambiguous marker).
**ASN**: T6 proof body, (b) — "If either tumbler lacks a user field, the answer is *no*: they cannot share a field that one does not possess."
**Issue**: The English preamble of (b) asks about sharing user fields, and T4b licenses reading `ε` as a definite absence marker that is equal only to itself. Two user-absent tumblers (both node addresses) therefore both have `U = ε` and, by T4b's ε-as-unambiguous-marker clause, "share the same (absent) user field" in the straightforward set-equality sense. The Postcondition nevertheless mandates NO in the both-absent case by requiring `zeros(a) ≥ 1 ∧ zeros(b) ≥ 1`. The body's justification ("they cannot share a field that one does not possess") speaks only to asymmetric absence — it explains why the answer is NO when exactly one tumbler lacks the field, but it is silent on symmetric absence, where the preamble's natural reading and T4b's equality-of-ε marker both point at YES while the Postcondition hard-codes NO. The same silent choice recurs in (c) and (d). Under per-property review each of (b)/(c)/(d) looks internally consistent; the cross-cutting gap is that the preamble's informal question and T4b's ε-semantics jointly suggest a behaviour the Postcondition quietly refuses.
**What needs resolving**: Either re-state the preamble of (b)/(c)/(d) so it asks specifically about "sharing a *present* field of matching value" (aligning the English question with the Postcondition's threshold checks), or relax the Postcondition to admit symmetric absence as YES and have the proof body treat the both-ε case explicitly. Either way, the body justification must cover the symmetric-absence case rather than resting on the asymmetric phrase "a field that one does not possess".
