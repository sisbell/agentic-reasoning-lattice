### TS3 omits ‚Ąē-typing preconditions required by its callees
**Foundation**: OrdinalShift preconditions `v ‚ąą T, n ‚ąą ‚Ąē, n ‚Č• 1`; OrdinalDisplacement preconditions `n ‚ąą ‚Ąē, m ‚ąą ‚Ąē, n ‚Č• 1, m ‚Č• 1`.
**ASN**: TS3 (ShiftComposition), quantifier `(A v, n‚āĀ, n‚āā : n‚āĀ ‚Č• 1 ‚ąß n‚āā ‚Č• 1 ‚ąß #v = m : ...)` and Preconditions list `v ‚ąą T, n‚āĀ ‚Č• 1, n‚āā ‚Č• 1, #v = m`.
**Issue**: TS3 invokes OrdinalShift three times ‚ÄĒ `shift(v, n‚āĀ)`, `shift(u, n‚āā)`, `shift(v, n‚āĀ + n‚āā)` ‚ÄĒ and (transitively) OrdinalDisplacement at each, but never supplies `n‚āĀ ‚ąą ‚Ąē` or `n‚āā ‚ąą ‚Ąē`. OrdinalDisplacement's own proof explicitly articulates the convention that an order statement like `n ‚Č• 1` *presupposes rather than establishes* carrier membership and routes the m-th position's ‚Ąē-typing from the separate `n ‚ąą ‚Ąē` precondition. Under that convention, `n‚āĀ ‚Č• 1` and `n‚āā ‚Č• 1` alone do not discharge OrdinalShift's `n ‚ąą ‚Ąē` precondition. The chain from TS3 to the downstream callees therefore has a broken link at the very first step of each `shift(¬∑, ¬∑)` unfolding.
**What needs resolving**: TS3 must either (a) add `n‚āĀ ‚ąą ‚Ąē, n‚āā ‚ąą ‚Ąē` to its range predicate and Preconditions list so each shift invocation has a named `n ‚ąą ‚Ąē` source, or (b) justify internally why order-statement-only typing suffices here while OrdinalShift/OrdinalDisplacement route it separately. Option (a) is the convention already established in this ASN.

### TS3's right-side `n‚āĀ + n‚āā ‚Č• 1` discharge is unsourced
**Foundation**: OrdinalShift's precondition `n ‚ąą ‚Ąē, n ‚Č• 1`; NAT-closure (closure of ‚Ąē under addition); NAT-addcompat (order-compatibility / successor inequality); NAT-order (composition of `‚Č•`/`>`).
**ASN**: TS3 right-side preface: "Since n‚āĀ ‚Č• 1 and n‚āā ‚Č• 1, their sum n‚āĀ + n‚āā ‚Č• 2 ‚Č• 1, so őī(n‚āĀ + n‚āā, m) is a well-formed ordinal displacement with action point m."
**Issue**: Three sub-facts are asserted without citation: (i) `n‚āĀ + n‚āā ‚ąą ‚Ąē` (needed for OrdinalDisplacement's `n ‚ąą ‚Ąē` precondition on the sum), which requires NAT-closure; (ii) `n‚āĀ + n‚āā ‚Č• 2` from `n‚āĀ ‚Č• 1 ‚ąß n‚āā ‚Č• 1`, which requires NAT-addcompat's order-compatibility; (iii) `2 ‚Č• 1`, which requires NAT-addcompat's successor inequality and NAT-order's defining clause. The ASN's established per-step citation discipline (enforced explicitly inside TumblerAdd, TA-Pos, and OrdinalDisplacement's own `n ‚Č• 1 ‚üĻ n ‚Č† 0` promotion) requires each of these links to name a NAT-* source. TS3's Depends lists only OrdinalShift, OrdinalDisplacement, TA0, TumblerAdd, NAT-addassoc, T3 ‚ÄĒ none of NAT-closure, NAT-addcompat, or NAT-order appears, so the right-side preface rests on background arithmetic rather than the enumerated ‚Ąē axioms.
**What needs resolving**: TS3 must source the `n‚āĀ + n‚āā ‚ąą ‚Ąē` and `n‚āĀ + n‚āā ‚Č• 1` discharges from named NAT-* axioms (NAT-closure plus NAT-addcompat/NAT-order as appropriate) and extend its Depends list to match, or cite a sibling step in this ASN whose discharge it reuses.
