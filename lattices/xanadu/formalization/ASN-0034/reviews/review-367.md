### T4c's Depends list includes NAT-sub with explicit admission that T4c does not use it
**Foundation**: structural — a claim's Depends slot should list the foundations the claim actually invokes, so downstream consumers and reviewers can trace each use to its support
**ASN**: T4c Formal Contract *Depends*: "NAT-sub (NatPartialSubtraction) — supplies partial subtraction `m − n` on ℕ under `m ≥ n`. T4c's own proof does not invoke subtraction; NAT-sub is included here so that the ℕ-arithmetic palette — closure of `+` (NAT-closure), strict successor (NAT-addcompat), and partial `−` (NAT-sub) — is fully resolved in scope alongside T4, T4a, T4b (which do invoke partial subtraction), under which T4c's level assignment reads."
**Issue**: T4c's Depends entry for NAT-sub self-declares that T4c's proof does not invoke the dependency; its inclusion is justified only as a curatorial completeness gesture about the "ℕ-arithmetic palette" in scope across sibling claims. A Depends list is a local, claim-scoped statement of what the claim itself needs — T4a and T4b carry their own Depends lists, so T4c does not need to assemble a union on their behalf. The entry is defensive essay content filling a structural slot, and it undermines the contract reader's ability to assume every Depends entry is actually load-bearing for the claim whose contract they are reading.
**What needs resolving**: Drop NAT-sub from T4c's Depends (since T4c's own reasoning does not use it), or identify a step in T4c's proof that actually requires partial subtraction and restate the dependency in terms of that step.

### T4 introduces cardinality `|·|` as a set-level operator without a foundation axiom grounding it
**Foundation**: the stated foundations (T0, NAT-zero, NAT-discrete, NAT-order, NAT-addcompat) axiomatize ℕ and `#· : T → ℕ` but supply no set-theoretic cardinality primitive; NAT-closure and NAT-sub (even if they were stated) concern arithmetic, not sets
**ASN**: T4 main body: "Write `|S|` for the cardinality of a finite subset `S ⊆ ℕ`; `|·|` has codomain ℕ and is a set-level operator, distinct from T0's tumbler-length `#· : T → ℕ` (which acts on sequences, not sets). Define `zeros(t) = |{i : 1 ≤ i ≤ #t ∧ tᵢ = 0}|`; the argument … is a subset of the index domain `{1, …, #t} ⊆ ℕ` (T0), hence a finite subset of ℕ, so `zeros(t) ∈ ℕ`." T4c repeats: "`zeros(t) = |{i : 1 ≤ i ≤ #t ∧ tᵢ = 0}|` as in T4, with `|·|` the cardinality of a finite subset of ℕ (codomain ℕ, distinct from T0's tumbler-length `#·`)".
**Issue**: The ASN goes to considerable lengths to ground `+`, `−`, and each numeral `1, 2, 3` in named foundation claims (NAT-closure, NAT-sub, NAT-addcompat), including per-claim Depends citations for every arithmetic step. But `|·|`, which `zeros(t)` depends on at the same level of formality, is introduced inside T4's prose by declaration — no Foundation Statement supplies its type or its behaviour on finite subsets of ℕ, and no claim's Depends cites a source for it. The conclusion `zeros(t) ∈ ℕ` therefore rests on an operator whose axiom no reader can consult. Since T4's entire parsing machinery — the zero-count bound, the exhaustion step in T4c, the case split in T4b — is predicated on `zeros(t)` being a well-typed ℕ-valued quantity, this is the same class of gap as the earlier unstated-axiom findings, applied to set-level machinery rather than arithmetic.
**What needs resolving**: Either introduce a foundation claim axiomatizing `|·|` on finite subsets of ℕ (with codomain ℕ) and cite it in the Depends of each claim that uses `zeros(t)`, or replace the cardinality-based definition of `zeros(t)` with one expressed directly over the sequence `t` using machinery already in scope.
