### "Sibling" denotes two different relations across the ASN
**Foundation**: Self-review.
**ASN**: T10a.1 "All siblings produced by a single allocator"; T10a.2 "For all siblings a, b from the same allocator"; T10a.5 proof "Were Cₓ = Cᵧ, that child would be a lower common ancestor…"; GlobalUniqueness Case 5 "parent sibling"; T10a body "a param-1 child of `X` is a child `C` of `X`…".
**Issue**: The term *sibling* is used in two incompatible senses without formal disambiguation. (i) **Allocator-output sibling**: two distinct elements of one allocator's `dom(A)` — e.g., T10a.1/T10a.2/T10a.8, and "parent sibling" in Case 5 Case 3. (ii) **Allocator-tree sibling**: two allocators sharing a parent in 𝒯 — the relation implicit in T10a.5's "Cₓ ≠ Cᵧ both children of P" and in GlobalUniqueness's "allocators at the same depth but on different branches." The *Definitions* block of T10a formalizes *child*, *ancestor*, *descendant*, *depth*, *param-1/param-2 child* but not *sibling*. When a reader sees phrases like "uncle allocator and its nephew may share nesting prefixes if spawned from related siblings" (GlobalUniqueness Case 5 prose), the word "siblings" there is the tree-level sense, while "parent sibling" two paragraphs below is the output-level sense.
**What needs resolving**: Either formally define both senses with distinct terms (e.g., *domain element* vs *sibling allocator*), or pick one and rephrase occurrences of the other.
