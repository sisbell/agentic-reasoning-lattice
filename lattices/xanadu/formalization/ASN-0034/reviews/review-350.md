### T1 (LexicographicOrder) is cited in TA-Pos's Depends list but is not established anywhere in the ASN
**Foundation**: TA-Pos (PositiveTumbler) — formal contract Depends: "T1 (LexicographicOrder) — reserves `<` (hence `>`) for lexicographic order and supplies the prefix rule, both invoked to exhibit the divergence between `Pos` and `> 0`."
**ASN**: ASN Metadata: "Declared depends:" is empty. ASN Content comprises T0, TA-Pos, NAT-order, NAT-zero, NAT-discrete, NAT-closure, NAT-wellorder, ActionPoint. No claim with label `T1` or `LexicographicOrder` appears in the content, and it is not listed as an external foundation.
**Issue**: TA-Pos's Depends cites T1 as the supplier of the `<` reservation and the prefix rule that the divergence example `0 < 0.0` rests on. A `Depends:` entry is a formal contract commitment that the named claim exists and its postconditions are in scope. For this ASN, the named claim is neither established internally (no T1 body in the content) nor declared externally (Declared depends is empty). The repair that resolved the earlier cycle-1 finding — adding T1 to Depends to match the body's reliance — only succeeds if T1 is actually established somewhere the formal system can resolve it. As the ASN stands, the citation points to nothing; TA-Pos's divergence paragraph rests on an unlicensed dependency.
**What needs resolving**: Either include T1's claim body in the ASN content (with its own formal contract stating the ordering `<` and the prefix rule), add T1 to the Declared depends and supply it as a foundation statement, or remove the T1 dependency by relocating the divergence paragraph to a commentary/meta-note slot so TA-Pos no longer cites content the ASN does not supply.

### T0's enumeration names four NAT-* axioms (NAT-addcompat, NAT-sub, NAT-cancel, NAT-addassoc) whose claim bodies are absent from the ASN
**Foundation**: T0 (CarrierSetDefinition) — enumeration prose: "The standard properties of ℕ that downstream proofs cite are stated as separate axioms, each established as its own foundation object under both a hyphenated short label and a PascalCase canonical name: … NAT-addcompat (NatAdditionOrderAndSuccessor) …; NAT-sub (NatPartialSubtraction) …; NAT-cancel (NatAdditionCancellation) …; NAT-addassoc (NatAdditionAssociative) …."
**ASN**: ASN Content contains claim bodies for NAT-closure, NAT-order, NAT-discrete, NAT-zero, NAT-wellorder. No bodies for NAT-addcompat, NAT-sub, NAT-cancel, or NAT-addassoc appear anywhere in the content, and the trailing sentence "The hyphenated and PascalCase forms denote the same axiom; either resolves to the corresponding foundation object" extends the "established foundation object" claim to all nine.
**Issue**: T0 commits to nine NAT-* axioms being "established as its own foundation object" in this ASN. Five have bodies; four do not. With Declared depends empty, the four missing axioms cannot resolve externally either. T0's enumeration therefore advertises content the ASN does not deliver — the same class of defect as the ℕ⁺ and Z findings but at the claim level rather than the notation level. A reader following T0's enumeration to locate e.g. the associativity axiom finds no corresponding claim. This differs from the cycle-6 finding (which flagged a body present but mismatched): here the bodies are not present at all, so the scope clause T0 publishes over-commits without any body to reconcile against.
**What needs resolving**: Either trim T0's enumeration to the five NAT-* axioms that are actually stated in the ASN (removing the NAT-addcompat / NAT-sub / NAT-cancel / NAT-addassoc bullets, so T0 advertises only foundation objects this ASN establishes), or add the missing claim bodies so each enumerated axiom resolves to a stated foundation object matching T0's advertisement.
