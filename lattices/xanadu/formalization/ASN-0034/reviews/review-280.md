### TumblerSub's motivation invokes TumblerAdd's precondition and result-length postcondition without citing TumblerAdd in Depends

**Foundation**: TumblerAdd (TumblerAdd) — provides a precondition `actionPoint(w) ≤ #a` on the displacement operand, and a result-length postcondition that furnishes `#(a ⊕ w)` (here instantiated as "result-length identity" at `w = b ⊖ a` to give `#(a ⊕ (b ⊖ a)) = #a`). The ASN's per-step convention requires every property of another foundation object that a proof consumes — motivational or not — to appear in Depends with per-site accounting, as TA2's Depends does for T1, T3, and NAT-sub, and as TumblerSub's Depends does at two sites for T3.

**ASN**: TumblerSub, motivation paragraph. "…when `a` is a proper sequence-prefix of `b`, the action point of `b ⊖ a` satisfies `actionPoint(b ⊖ a) = zpd(b, a) ≥ #a + 1 > #a`, violating **TumblerAdd's precondition `actionPoint(w) ≤ #a`**. A second obstruction is independent of the prefix case: when `#a > #b` … the subtraction produces `#(b ⊖ a) = #a`; **TumblerAdd's result-length identity** gives `#(a ⊕ (b ⊖ a)) = #a > #b`, so `a ⊕ (b ⊖ a) ≠ b` by T3 — the round-trip fails on length alone even when the zpd constraint is satisfied." The Depends enumeration lists T0, T1, T3, Divergence, ZPD, TA-Pos, ActionPoint, NAT-sub, NAT-zero, NAT-order — no TumblerAdd entry.

**Issue**: The motivation's derivation that `#a > #b` suffices to break the round-trip is a chain of two formal steps: (a) TumblerAdd's result-length postcondition applied to `a` and `b ⊖ a` to produce `#(a ⊕ (b ⊖ a)) = #a`, then (b) T3's reverse direction to pass from the length inequality to `a ⊕ (b ⊖ a) ≠ b`. TumblerSub's Depends cites T3 for step (b) with explicit per-site accounting ("(ii) *Motivation's length-difference-to-`≠` step*"), but the TumblerAdd citation that furnishes step (a)'s input — the length identity `#(a ⊕ (b ⊖ a)) = #a` — has no Depends entry. The parallel citation for TumblerAdd's precondition `actionPoint(w) ≤ #a` in the same paragraph is likewise uncited. Under the ASN's convention — the same one invoked by T3's per-site accounting — these are missing discharges.

**What needs resolving**: TumblerSub must either (a) add TumblerAdd (TumblerAdd) to its Depends enumeration with per-site accounting for the two motivational consumptions (precondition citation at the prefix-obstruction step, result-length postcondition at the length-obstruction step), mirroring how T3 is already cited at two sites; or (b) demote the motivational paragraph to content that makes no formal property claims about TumblerAdd (removing the named precondition and result-length identity), so the per-step convention does not apply. If (a) raises a DAG concern (TumblerAdd may itself depend on TumblerSub for displacement construction), the ASN must establish which direction the dependency runs and restructure the motivation accordingly.
