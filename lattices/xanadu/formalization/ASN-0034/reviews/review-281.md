# Cone Review — ASN-0034/TA2 (cycle 5)

*2026-04-18 12:22*

### NAT-order's motivational trichotomy at length pair `(#b, #a)` is folded into the primary site, contrary to TumblerSub's per-site convention for TumblerAdd and T3

**Foundation**: T1's per-instance convention, declared in its own Depends ("branches of a case that instantiate an axiom at distinct symbols count as separate sites"). TumblerSub's own Depends follows this discipline for TumblerAdd (sites (i) and (ii) for two motivational citations at distinct operand pairs) and for T3 (site (ii) explicitly for the motivation's length-difference-to-`≠` step). NAT-zero is counted at four sites, T3 at two, TumblerAdd at two — per-site accounting is the norm throughout TumblerSub's Depends.

**ASN**: TumblerSub's Definition explicitly invokes NAT-order's trichotomy at *two* distinct length pairs. The Definition dispatches at `(#a, #w)`: "NAT-order's trichotomy on `(#a, #w)` selects exactly one of the sub-cases (α) ... (β) ... or (γ) ...". The motivation dispatches at `(#b, #a)`: "the Definition's NAT-order trichotomy dispatch (below) — applied to the length pair `(#b, #a)` of the operand pair of `b ⊖ a` — places the pair in its `#b < #a` sub-case, whose longer length `L = #a` furnishes the result length". TumblerSub's NAT-order Depends entry says "invoked at three distinct ℕ-pairs for three distinct roles" but absorbs the motivational `(#b, #a)` invocation into the first role: "The same length-pair dispatch also names the longer length `#a` in the motivational observation `#(b ⊖ a) = #a` (under `#a > #b`) applied to the reversed operand pair of `b ⊖ a`."

**Issue**: `(#a, #w)` and `(#b, #a)` are distinct-symbol instantiations of the same NAT-order axiom — the same relation the convention declares "count as separate sites". TumblerSub treats them as a single site for NAT-order, but applies the opposite discipline for TumblerAdd (counting the motivation's prefix-obstruction and length-obstruction citations as sites (i) and (ii), even though both are at the same `w = b ⊖ a`) and for T3 (counting the motivational length-difference-to-`≠` step as site (ii) distinct from the precondition-consequence step at site (i)). The accounting is therefore inconsistent across axioms within the same Depends block: motivational uses of TumblerAdd and T3 earn their own sites, but the motivational use of NAT-order does not.

**What needs resolving**: Either (a) split the NAT-order entry to count the motivational length-pair trichotomy at `(#b, #a)` as a fourth site (with its own role-description), mirroring the per-site treatment of TumblerAdd's motivational citations at sites (i) and (ii) and T3's motivational citation at site (ii); or (b) state an explicit co-operand-pair absorption convention for NAT-order that differs from the per-instance convention applied elsewhere, and explain why NAT-order qualifies for it while TumblerAdd and T3 do not. One accounting rule per Depends block.
