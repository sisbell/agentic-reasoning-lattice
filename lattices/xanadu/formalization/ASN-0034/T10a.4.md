**T10a.4 (T4Preservation).** The allocator discipline produces only T4-compliant addresses.

*Proof.* By induction on the depth of the allocator tree.

*Base case.* The root allocator's base address satisfies T4 by the initialization constraint of T10a.

*Inductive step.* Suppose an allocator at depth `d` has a base address `b` satisfying T4. The discipline permits sibling production via `inc(·, 0)` and child-spawning via `inc(·, k')` with `k' ∈ {1, 2}`.

By TA5a, `inc(b, 0)` preserves T4 unconditionally. For child-spawning, TA5a gives preservation iff `k' = 1` or `k' = 2 ∧ zeros(t) ≤ 2`. T10a restricts child-spawning to exactly these cases. Every `t` in the depth-`d` allocator's domain is T4-valid, since the sibling chain `t₀ = b, t₁ = inc(b, 0), …` propagates T4 by the unconditional `k = 0` branch of TA5a. Hence `inc(b, k')` satisfies T4 and serves as the child allocator's base at depth `d + 1`, closing the induction. ∎

*Formal Contract:*
- *Preconditions:* Allocator tree conforming to T10a; root base address satisfies T4.
- *Postconditions:* Every output at every depth satisfies T4.
- *Proof structure:* Induction on allocator tree depth. Base: T10a initialization. Step: TA5a preservation under `inc(·, 0)` and `inc(·, k')` with `k' ∈ {1, 2}`.
- *Depends:*
  - T10a (AllocatorDiscipline) — root-initialization constraint and runtime restriction of `k' = 2` to `zeros(t) ≤ 2`.
  - T4 (HierarchicalParsing) — invariant preserved by induction.
  - TA5a (IncrementPreservesT4) — per-step preservation: `inc(·, 0)` and `inc(·, 1)` preserve T4 unconditionally on T4-valid inputs; `inc(·, 2)` preserves T4 under `zeros(t) ≤ 2`.
