**NAT-zero (NatZeroMinimum).** `0` is the minimum of ℕ: `0 ∈ ℕ` and `(A n ∈ ℕ :: 0 ≤ n)`.

In words: `0` is itself a natural number, and every natural number is at least `0`. The two clauses together identify `0` as the minimum — membership places `0` among the elements compared, and the inequality ensures no element sits below it under `≤`.

*Formal Contract:*
- *Axiom:* `0 ∈ ℕ` (zero is a natural number); `(A n ∈ ℕ :: 0 ≤ n)` (zero is a lower bound of ℕ).
