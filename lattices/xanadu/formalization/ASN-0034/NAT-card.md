**NAT-card (NatFiniteSetCardinality).** For every `n ∈ ℕ` and every subset `S ⊆ {1, 2, …, n} ⊆ ℕ`, we postulate `|·|` as a primitive operator with `|S| ∈ ℕ` a value `k` for which `S` admits a strictly increasing enumeration `s₁ < s₂ < … < s_k` under `<` on ℕ with `S = {s₁, s₂, …, s_k}` (empty enumeration when `S = ∅`, so `|∅| = 0`), and `|S| ≤ n`. Existence of such an enumeration for every `S`, the postulate's selection of a single `k` per `S`, and the upper bound `|S| ≤ n` are all clauses of the axiom, not derivations: the available foundations (NAT-order, NAT-closure, NAT-zero) supply a strict total order `<` on ℕ and the constants `0, 1` closed under addition, but lack the induction principle or well-foundedness of `<` on ℕ that would be required to construct such an enumeration (for instance, by repeatedly extracting the minimum of `S \ {s₁, …, s_{i-1}}`), to show that two strictly increasing enumerations of the same finite set must agree element-by-element and share the same length, or to derive the upper bound `|S| ≤ n` from the enumeration by stepping the index bound `s_i ≥ i` across all `1 ≤ i ≤ k`. Uniqueness of `k` is therefore posited rather than derived: NAT-order's trichotomy `m < n ∨ m = n ∨ n < m` decides the order of any two naturals, which is enough to fix the minimum of a finite two-element subset (and hence `s₁ = t₁` for any two enumerations of `S`), but extending that agreement across all indices `1 ≤ i ≤ k` and forcing equal lengths would require an induction on `k` (or on `|S|`) that the Depends slot does not supply. The upper bound `|S| ≤ n` is similarly posited rather than derived: one would derive it from the enumeration characterisation by establishing `s_i ≥ i` for every `1 ≤ i ≤ k` — `s_1 ≥ 1` because `s_1 ∈ S ⊆ {1, …, n}`, and `s_{i+1} > s_i ≥ i ⟹ s_{i+1} ≥ i + 1` via a discrete-successor step on ℕ — and then reading off `k ≤ s_k ≤ n` from `s_k ∈ {1, …, n}`, but the induction on `i` together with the discrete-successor step on ℕ outruns the declared foundations, so `|S| ≤ n` is kept as an additional clause of the axiom, resting only on NAT-order's `≤` to render the inequality well-formed and on the axiom's range `S ⊆ {1, …, n}` to pin the right-hand side. Hence `|·|` is a well-defined total function on subsets of every initial segment `{1, …, n}` of ℕ with codomain ℕ solely by virtue of the postulate. The operator `|·|` is distinct from T0's tumbler-length `#· : T → ℕ`, which acts on sequences rather than sets.

The axiom body invokes symbols beyond ℕ's primitive membership. The strict order `<` appears in the strictly increasing enumeration `s₁ < s₂ < … < s_k` and the non-strict companion `≤` in the upper bound `|S| ≤ n` — both defined in NAT-order — and NAT-order's strict-total-order discipline (irreflexivity, transitivity, trichotomy `m < n ∨ m = n ∨ n < m`) is what makes "strictly increasing enumeration" a well-formed predicate on ℕ-indexed sequences. Neither trichotomy nor any other NAT-order property is appealed to in deriving uniqueness of `k` or the upper bound `|S| ≤ n`: carrying pairwise ordering across all indices of two enumerations, or stepping the bound `s_i ≥ i` across all indices of a single enumeration, are induction steps outside the reach of the declared foundations, so both uniqueness and the upper bound have been kept as clauses of the postulate rather than consequences of it. The initial segment `{1, 2, …, n}` is built from the `1 ∈ ℕ` posited by NAT-closure and the successor operation closed over ℕ by NAT-closure. The literal `0` appearing in the empty-enumeration case `k = 0` and the consequent `|∅| = 0` is the `0 ∈ ℕ` posited by NAT-zero. The disambiguation remark referencing T0's tumbler-length `#· : T → ℕ` pins `|·|` as a distinct operator. All four foundations are declared in the Depends slot so that the axiom body can be read without silently importing them.

*Formal Contract:*
- *Axiom:* `(A n ∈ ℕ : S ⊆ {1, 2, …, n} :: |S| ∈ ℕ)` (conditional codomain); `|S|` is postulated as a `k ∈ ℕ` such that `S` admits a strictly increasing enumeration `s₁ < s₂ < … < s_k` under `<` on ℕ with `S = {s₁, s₂, …, s_k}` (empty enumeration when `k = 0`, giving `|∅| = 0`) — both existence of the enumeration for every such `S` and the selection of a single `k` per `S` are clauses of the postulate, since the declared foundations supply no induction principle or well-foundedness of `<` on ℕ with which to derive either (enumeration characterisation); `(A n ∈ ℕ : S ⊆ {1, 2, …, n} :: |S| ≤ n)` (upper bound — posited as an independent clause of the axiom since deriving it from the enumeration characterisation would require an induction on `i` establishing `s_i ≥ i` across all indices `1 ≤ i ≤ k` plus a discrete-successor step on ℕ, neither supplied by the declared foundations; rests on NAT-order's `≤` to render the inequality well-formed and on the range `S ⊆ {1, …, n}` to pin the right-hand side). `|·|` acts on subsets of ℕ and is distinct from T0's tumbler-length `#·`, which acts on sequences.
- *Depends:*
  - NAT-order (NatStrictTotalOrder) — supplies the strict order `<` (used in the strictly increasing enumeration `s₁ < s₂ < … < s_k`) and the non-strict companion `≤` (used in the upper bound `|S| ≤ n`); the strict-total-order discipline (irreflexivity, transitivity, trichotomy `m < n ∨ m = n ∨ n < m`) makes "strictly increasing enumeration" a well-formed predicate on ℕ-indexed sequences. Neither trichotomy nor any other NAT-order property is appealed to in deriving uniqueness of `k` or the upper bound `|S| ≤ n`, since both steps would require induction on `k` (or on `|S|`) — and, for the upper bound, a discrete-successor step on ℕ — not among the declared foundations; both uniqueness and the upper bound are clauses of the postulate itself.
  - NAT-closure (NatArithmeticClosureAndIdentity) — posits `1 ∈ ℕ` and closes ℕ under addition, so the initial segment `{1, 2, …, n}` — built from `1 ∈ ℕ` and successor — has ℕ-typed elements and `S ⊆ {1, 2, …, n} ⊆ ℕ` is grounded.
  - NAT-zero (NatZeroMinimum) — supplies `0 ∈ ℕ` for the literal `0` appearing in the empty-enumeration case `k = 0` and in the empty-set cardinality `|∅| = 0`.
  - T0 (CarrierSetDefinition) — supplies the tumbler-length operator `#· : T → ℕ` referenced in the disambiguation remark distinguishing `|·|` from `#·`.
