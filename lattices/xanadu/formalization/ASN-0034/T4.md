## Hierarchical structure

Tumblers encode a containment hierarchy. Nelson uses zero-valued components as structural delimiters:

> "There are four major fields, each expandable indefinitely, with three major dividers between them. These fields are Server, User, Document and Contents."

> "A tumbler address may have at most three zero digits... These are interpreted as the major dividers, and have lexical significance as punctuation."

We formalize this. Define a *field separator* as a component with value zero. An address tumbler has the form:

`t = NвВБ. ... .NвВР . 0 . UвВБ. ... .Uбµ¶ . 0 . DвВБ. ... .DбµІ . 0 . EвВБ. ... .Eќі`

where each `NбµҐ, Uв±Љ, DвВЦ, EвВЧ > 0`. The four fields are:

- **Node field** `NвВБ. ... .NвВР`: identifies the server. "The server address always begins with the digit 1, since all other servers are descended from it."
- **User field** `UвВБ. ... .Uбµ¶`: identifies the account. "Typically, the user will have no control over the node address he, she or it is assigned; but once assigned a User account, the user will have full control over its subdivision forevermore."
- **Document field** `DвВБ. ... .DбµІ`: identifies the document and version. Nelson notes the boundary between base document and version is not syntactically marked вАФ "the version, or subdocument number is only an accidental extension of the document number, and strictly implies no specific relationship of derivation."
- **Element field** `EвВБ. ... .Eќі`: identifies the content element. T4 constrains only `EвВЧ > 0` at every element-field position; typed interpretations of `EвВБ` are the concern of downstream ASNs.

A tumbler with zero zeros addresses a node. One zero: a user account. Two zeros: a document. Three zeros: an element. The count of zero-valued components determines the specificity level.

**T4 (HierarchicalParsing).** Define `zeros(t) = #{i : 1 вЙ§ i вЙ§ #t вИІ tбµҐ = 0}`. We call the last three conditions collectively the *field-segment constraint*. T0 fixes the carrier as вДХ, and NAT-zero together with NAT-discrete (at `m = 0`) force every non-zero component to be strictly positive. The *field segments* of `t` are the maximal contiguous sub-sequences of non-zero positions вАФ `k+1` of them when `zeros(t) = k`, separated by the zeros вАФ and the field-segment constraint is exactly the requirement that all of them are non-empty. T4 stipulates that zero components are field separators and non-zero components are field components; the two roles are positionally decidable by inspecting `tбµҐ`.

Three consequences round out the parsing picture, each a first-class numbered theorem in this ASN:

  - **T4a (SyntacticEquivalence)** proves that the field-segment constraint is equivalent to the condition that every field segment of `t` is non-empty. Proved in `T4a.md` by case analysis on `k = zeros(t) вИИ {0, 1, 2, 3}`.
  - **T4b (UniqueParse)** introduces the partial function `fields(t)` with domain the T4-valid subset of `T` and codomain `Seq(вДХвБЇ) √Ч Seq(вДХвБЇ) √Ч Seq(вДХвБЇ) √Ч Seq(вДХвБЇ)`, splitting `t` at its zero positions, and proves it well-defined and uniquely determined by `t`.
  - **T4c (LevelDetermination)** assigns the four hierarchical level labels definitionally by zero count: `(A t вИИ T : t is T4-valid :: (zeros(t) = 0 вЖФ t is a node address) вИІ (zeros(t) = 1 вЖФ t is a user address) вИІ (zeros(t) = 2 вЖФ t is a document address) вИІ (zeros(t) = 3 вЖФ t is an element address))`. The induced mapping `{0, 1, 2, 3} вЖТ {node, user, document, element}` is a bijection. Outside the T4-valid subdomain no level is assigned.

A subtlety deserves emphasis: the hierarchy is *convention layered over flat arithmetic*, not enforcement by the algebra. The comparison, addition, subtraction, and increment operations treat every mantissa slot identically. There is no `isparent`, `isancestor`, or `ischild` primitive in the arithmetic layer. The hierarchical interpretation is projected onto flat sequences by the allocation machinery and by the field-parsing function `fields(t)`.

Hierarchy is constructed by the allocation machinery. The `.0.` separator is produced when the allocation `depth` parameter equals 2 вАФ creating a child at a *different hierarchical type* than its parent. When creating a same-type child (`depth = 1`), no zero separator is introduced. Gregory confirms: there was even a bug where the first document under an account failed to receive its `.0.` separator вАФ the convention had to be explicitly constructed by the allocator, not enforced by any algebraic invariant. вИО

**T4c (LevelDetermination).** Define `zeros(t) = #{i : 1 вЙ§ i вЙ§ #t вИІ tбµҐ = 0}`. On the T4-valid subset of `T`, T4c assigns the four hierarchical level labels definitionally by zero count: `t` is a *node address* iff `zeros(t) = 0`, a *user address* iff `zeros(t) = 1`, a *document address* iff `zeros(t) = 2`, an *element address* iff `zeros(t) = 3`. Outside this subdomain no level is assigned.

The proof obligation reduces to three elementary facts: exhaustion, injectivity, and surjectivity.

*Exhaustion.* By T4, `zeros(t) вЙ§ 3`; NAT-zero supplies `0 вЙ§ zeros(t)`; so `zeros(t) вИИ {0, 1, 2, 3}` on the T4-valid subdomain.

*Injectivity.* The values `0, 1, 2, 3` are pairwise distinct in вДХ (NAT-order trichotomy), and `zeros(t)` is single-valued. So distinct zero counts yield distinct labels.

*Surjectivity.* Each value is realised by a concrete T4-valid witness:

  - `(1)`: `zeros = 0`, *node address*.
  - `(1, 0, 1)`: `zeros = 1`, *user address*.
  - `(1, 0, 1, 0, 1)`: `zeros = 2`, *document address*.
  - `(1, 0, 1, 0, 1, 0, 1)`: `zeros = 3`, *element address*.

T4c is definitional: the four biconditionals name the zero counts. A field-segment characterisation вАФ e.g., *user address* as "`N(t)` and `U(t)` only non-empty in T4b's `fields(t)`" вАФ is consistent with T4c on the T4-valid subdomain, but the equivalence is a separate downstream theorem. вИО
