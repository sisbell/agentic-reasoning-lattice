## Hierarchical structure

Tumblers encode a containment hierarchy. Nelson uses zero-valued components as structural delimiters:

> "There are four major fields, each expandable indefinitely, with three major dividers between them. These fields are Server, User, Document and Contents."

> "A tumbler address may have at most three zero digits... These are interpreted as the major dividers, and have lexical significance as punctuation."

We formalize this, writing *node* for Nelson's *server* and *element* for his *contents* and retaining *user* and *document* verbatim; the renaming is notational. An address tumbler has up to four fields separated by zero-valued components; which fields are present in a given tumbler is determined by its zero count, and T4's Axiom below gives the per-`k` schema explicitly. The maximal written form вАФ the case with all four fields present, `zeros(t) = 3` вАФ is:

`t = NвВБ. ... .NвВР . 0 . UвВБ. ... .Uбµ¶ . 0 . DвВБ. ... .DбµІ . 0 . EвВБ. ... .Eќі`

where `0 < NбµҐ, 0 < Uв±Љ, 0 < DвВЦ, 0 < EвВЧ` at every position. The four fields вАФ each of which may be absent at lower zero counts вАФ are:

- **Node field** `NвВБ. ... .NвВР`: identifies the server. "The server address always begins with the digit 1, since all other servers are descended from it."
- **User field** `UвВБ. ... .Uбµ¶`: identifies the account. "Typically, the user will have no control over the node address he, she or it is assigned; but once assigned a User account, the user will have full control over its subdivision forevermore."
- **Document field** `DвВБ. ... .DбµІ`: identifies the document and version. Nelson notes the boundary between base document and version is not syntactically marked вАФ "the version, or subdocument number is only an accidental extension of the document number, and strictly implies no specific relationship of derivation."
- **Element field** `EвВБ. ... .Eќі`: identifies the content element. T4 constrains only `0 < EвВЧ` at every element-field position; typed interpretations of `EвВБ` are the concern of downstream ASNs.

The count of zero-valued components determines the specificity level вАФ for each `k вИИ {0, 1, 2, 3}` at which a T4-valid tumbler has `zeros(t) = k`, T4c is the single definitional site, assigning the label node address (at `k = 0`), user address (at `k = 1`), document address (at `k = 2`), or element address (at `k = 3`). The names appear here only as a forward reference to that definition; they are not defined by the present paragraph.

**T4 (HierarchicalParsing).** NAT-card axiomatizes the cardinality operator `|¬Ј|` as a well-defined total function on subsets of every initial segment `{1, 2, вА¶, n} вКЖ вДХ` (`n вИИ вДХ`), with codomain вДХ and with `|S|` characterised as the length of the unique strictly increasing enumeration of `S`; NAT-card further separates `|¬Ј|` from T0's tumbler-length `#¬Ј : T вЖТ вДХ`, which acts on sequences rather than sets. Define `zeros(t) = |{i : 1 вЙ§ i вЙ§ #t вИІ tбµҐ = 0}|`; the argument `{i : 1 вЙ§ i вЙ§ #t вИІ tбµҐ = 0}` is a subset of the index domain `{1, вА¶, #t} вКЖ вДХ` (T0), so NAT-card applies at `n = #t` and `zeros(t) вИИ вДХ` with `zeros(t) вЙ§ #t`. Every tumbler `t вИИ T` used as an address satisfies: `zeros(t) вЙ§ 3`; `(A i : 1 вЙ§ i < #t : ¬ђ(tбµҐ = 0 вИІ tбµҐвВКвВБ = 0))`; `tвВБ вЙ† 0`; `t_{#t} вЙ† 0`. We call the last three conditions collectively the *field-segment constraint*. T0 fixes the carrier as вДХ; NAT-order supplies the strict total order `<` on вДХ and its companion `вЙ§` (defined by `m вЙ§ n вЯЇ m < n вИ® m = n`), which support the zero-count bound `zeros(t) вЙ§ 3` and the adjacency clause's index range `1 вЙ§ i < #t`; NAT-closure supplies `1 вИИ вДХ` together with closure of вДХ under addition, grounding the numerals `2 := 1 + 1 вИИ вДХ` and `3 := 2 + 1 вИИ вДХ` so that the bound `zeros(t) вЙ§ 3` compares two elements of вДХ; and NAT-zero together with NAT-discrete (at `m = 0`) force every non-zero component to be strictly positive; write `вДХвБЇ = {n вИИ вДХ : 0 < n}` for the strictly positive naturals. The *field segments* of `t` are the maximal contiguous sub-sequences of non-zero positions вАФ `k+1` of them when `zeros(t) = k`, separated by the zeros. By the Axiom below, the zero-valued positions serve as *field separators* and the remaining positions carry *field components* вАФ strictly positive by the preceding argument; the two roles are decidable by inspecting `tбµҐ`.

Three first-class numbered theorems, each a downstream combination of T4 with additional foundations rather than a consequence of T4 alone, round out the parsing picture. **T4a (SyntacticEquivalence)** adds NAT-sub вАФ whose conditional-closure clause gives `#t вИТ 1 вИИ вДХ` under the locally unpacked `#t вЙ• 1` from T0's nonempty qualification of `t вИИ T` вАФ to prove that the field-segment constraint is equivalent to every field segment of `t` being non-empty. **T4b (UniqueParse)** is built on T4a: it introduces four partial functions `N, U, D, E : T вЗА T`, each with domain a subset of the T4-valid tumblers determined by `zeros(t)`, splitting `t` at its zero positions into the corresponding field sub-sequence (each in the all-`вДХвБЇ`-component subset of `T`), and proves each well-defined and uniquely determined by `t` вАФ using T4a's reverse direction to supply the conclusion that every field segment is non-empty, which T4b locally re-expresses as the segment-length inequalities that discharge non-emptiness of each extracted sub-sequence. Field absence is encoded by partiality of the corresponding projection, not by a distinguished empty-sequence marker. **T4c (LevelDetermination)** combines T4 with NAT-addcompat's strict successor inequality `n < n + 1` to assign the four hierarchical level labels definitionally by zero count вАФ `(A t вИИ T : t is T4-valid :: (zeros(t) = 0 вЖФ t is a node address) вИІ (zeros(t) = 1 вЖФ t is a user address) вИІ (zeros(t) = 2 вЖФ t is a document address) вИІ (zeros(t) = 3 вЖФ t is an element address))` вАФ and discharges exhaustion of the four zero-count values on the T4-valid subdomain together with pairwise distinctness of the labels. Realisation вАФ existence of a T4-valid tumbler at each zero count вАФ is not claimed; T4c stands as a pure definition on whatever T4-valid tumblers exist. Outside the T4-valid subdomain T4c assigns no level.

The canonical written form of a T4-valid address tumbler is given by the following schema, quantified per-`k` and not pre-committing to exhaustion: for each `k вИИ вДХ` with `0 вЙ§ k вЙ§ 3` at which `zeros(t) = k`, the form is вАФ `k = 0`: `t = NвВБ. ... .NвВР`; `k = 1`: `t = NвВБ. ... .NвВР . 0 . - *Depends:*
  - T0 (CarrierSetDefinition) вАФ supplies the carrier вДХ, the tumbler length `#¬Ј`, and the index domain `{1, вА¶, #t}` in which the separator positions and field-segment clauses are stated.

**T4a (SyntacticEquivalence).** T4's field-segment constraint вАФ stated positionally as (i) no two zeros are adjacent, (ii) `tвВБ вЙ† 0`, (iii) `t_{#t} вЙ† 0` вАФ is equivalent to the condition that every *field segment* of `t` is non-empty, where the field segments are the `zeros(t) + 1` maximal contiguous sub-sequences of non-zero positions delimited by the zeros.

Let `t вИИ T` be a tumbler with `zeros(t) вЙ§ 3`. Set `k = zeros(t) вИИ {0, 1, 2, 3}` with zeros at positions `sвВБ < sвВВ < вА¶ < s_k` (strict increase by NAT-order; the length `k` of this enumeration equals `zeros(t)` by NAT-card's enumeration characterisation of `|¬Ј|` applied at the zero-index subset `{i : 1 вЙ§ i вЙ§ #t вИІ tбµҐ = 0} вКЖ {1, вА¶, #t}`, whose cardinality T4 identifies with `zeros(t)`). *Forward.* Assume every field segment is non-empty. We derive each positional condition.

*Condition (ii): `tвВБ вЙ† 0`.* The first field segment is `t[1 .. sвВБ - 1]` when `k вЙ• 1`, or `t[1 .. #t]` when `k = 0`; in either case its first index is 1. *Condition (iii): `t_{#t} вЙ† 0`.* The last field segment is `t[s_k + 1 .. #t]` when `k вЙ• 1`, or `t[1 .. #t]` when `k = 0`; in either case its last index is `#t`. *Condition (i): no adjacent zeros.* Suppose for contradiction that `tбµҐ = 0 вИІ tбµҐвВКвВБ = 0` for some `i` with `1 вЙ§ i < #t`. Then `i` and `i + 1` are consecutive zero positions вАФ say `s_j = i` and `s_{j+1} = i + 1` вАФ and the interior field segment `t[s_j + 1 .. s_{j+1} - 1] = t[i + 1 .. i]` has no indices, so it is empty. This contradicts segment non-emptiness. Hence no two zeros are adjacent.

*Reverse.* Assume conditions (i), (ii), and (iii) hold. We show every field segment is non-empty.

*First segment (`i = 0`).* The segment occupies indices 1 through `sвВБ - 1` (or 1 through `#t` when `k = 0`). If `k = 0` the segment equals `t` itself, which has `#t вЙ• 1` indices. *Last segment (`i = k`).* The segment occupies indices `s_k + 1` through `#t` (when `k вЙ• 1`). Condition (iii) forces `t_{#t} вЙ† 0`, so index `#t` is not a zero position, and therefore `s_k вЙ§ #t - 1` вАФ the segment has at least one index.

*Interior segments (`1 вЙ§ i < k`).* Each such segment occupies indices `s_i + 1` through `s_{i+1} - 1`. Condition (i) forbids `s_{i+1} = s_i + 1`, and since the zero positions are strictly increasing we already have `s_{i+1} вЙ• s_i + 1`; together these give `s_{i+1} вЙ• s_i + 2`, so the segment has at least one index.

All segments вАФ first, interior, and last вАФ are non-empty. вИО

**T4b (UniqueParse).** Under the constraints of T4, the four partial functions

 `N, U, D, E : T вЗА T`

that extract the node, user, document, and element sub-sequences of `t` are well-defined and uniquely determined by `t`. Writing `вДХвБЇ = {n вИИ вДХ : 0 < n}`, each projection's image lies in the subset of `T` whose every component is in `вДХвБЇ`: a nonempty finite sequence over `вДХ` (by T0) with every component strictly positive by NAT-zero and NAT-discrete at `m = 0`.   - `zeros(t) = 0`: only `N(t)` defined, with `N(t) = (tвВБ, ..., t_{#t})`.
  - `zeros(t) = 1`: `N(t)` and `U(t)` defined.

By T0, each `tбµҐ` is the value of the `i`-th component of `t` вАФ a function of `t` and `i` вАФ so the set `{i : 1 вЙ§ i вЙ§ #t вИІ tбµҐ = 0}` of separator positions is determined by `t`, and with it the field boundaries and the sub-sequences. Therefore each projection is well-defined and unique on its stated domain. вИО

*Formal Contract:*
- *Preconditions:* `t` satisfies the T4 constraints (`zeros(t) вЙ§ 3`, no two zeros adjacent, `tвВБ вЙ† 0`, `t_{#t} вЙ† 0`).
