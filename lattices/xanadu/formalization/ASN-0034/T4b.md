  - `N` is defined on the whole T4-valid subdomain (`N` is never absent).
  - `U` is defined iff `t` is T4-valid and `zeros(t) ‚Č• 1`.
  - `D` is defined iff `t` is T4-valid and `zeros(t) ‚Č• 2`.
  - `E` is defined iff `t` is T4-valid and `zeros(t) = 3`.

T4 exports as a Consequence that `zeros(t) ‚ąą {0, 1, 2, 3}` for every T4-valid `t`, so ranging `k` over `{0, 1, 2, 3}` below collectively covers every `t` in the T4-valid subdomain.   - `zeros(t) = 1`: `N(t)` and `U(t)` defined.
  - `zeros(t) = 2`: `N(t)`, `U(t)`, `D(t)` defined.
  - `zeros(t) = 3`: all four projections defined.

To access individual components of a field we introduce the notation `t.X‚āĀ`, grounded in T0's projection as follows. Whenever `X(t)` is defined, `X(t)` is a nonempty finite sequence over `‚Ąē‚Āļ ‚äÜ ‚Ąē`, so `X(t) ‚ąą T` with `#(X(t)) ‚Č• 1` by T0, and T0's component projection `¬∑ŠĶĘ` is defined at every `i ‚ąą {1, ‚Ä¶, #(X(t))}` ‚ÄĒ in particular at `i = 1`. Since `t` is T4-valid, the field-segment constraint (i)‚Äď(iii) holds, and T4a's reverse direction yields its exported conclusion: every field segment of `t` is non-empty. We unpack this conclusion locally into the three position-inequalities used in the case analysis below.
