**T4b (UniqueParse).** Write `Seq(X)` for the set of finite, possibly empty sequences over `X`, and `őĶ` for its unique zero-length element. T0 fixes `T` as the *nonempty* finite sequences over `‚Ąē` ‚ÄĒ by the stipulation `#a ‚Č• 1` for every `a ‚ąą T` ‚ÄĒ so `T` strictly excludes `őĶ`; `Seq(¬∑)` differs from T's construction only by adjoining `őĶ`, and we use `Seq(‚Ąē‚Āļ)` (with `‚Ąē‚Āļ = {n ‚ąą ‚Ąē : 0 < n}`) precisely because field absence demands a value outside `T`. Under the constraints of T4 ‚ÄĒ at most three zero-valued components, no two zeros adjacent, `t‚āĀ ‚Č† 0`, `t_{#t} ‚Č† 0` ‚ÄĒ the partial function

that decomposes a tumbler into its node, user, document, and element sub-sequences is well-defined and uniquely determined by `t` on the T4-valid subdomain, with absent fields represented by `őĶ`.

We read `fields(t)` as the 4-tuple `(N(t), U(t), D(t), E(t))` and fix the projections `N, U, D, E : T ‚áÄ Seq(‚Ąē‚Āļ)` accordingly, each sharing the T4-valid subdomain of `fields`. Each projection returns a finite sequence over `‚Ąē‚Āļ` on its domain: NAT-zero gives `0 ‚Č§ tŠĶĘ` for every component of `t` (in ‚Ąē by T0), NAT-discrete at `m = 0` rules out `0 ‚Č§ tŠĶĘ < 1` when `tŠĶĘ ‚Č† 0`, and T4 assigns zero the role of separator, so every field-content position is strictly positive. *Field absence* is defined independently of the projections as a direct predicate on `zeros(t)`: `N` is never absent on the T4-valid subdomain; `U` is *absent in `t`* iff `zeros(t) = 0`; `D` is *absent in `t`* iff `zeros(t) ‚Č§ 1`; `E` is *absent in `t`* iff `zeros(t) ‚Č§ 2`. The biconditional `X(t) = őĶ` iff field `X` is absent in `t` is then discharged by inspection of the case construction below: in each of the four cases on `zeros(t)`, the projections that the construction sets to `őĶ` are exactly those that the predicate marks absent.

The absence pattern of `fields(t)` is fixed by case analysis on `zeros(t) ‚ąą {0, 1, 2, 3}`:

  - `zeros(t) = 0`: `N(t) = (t‚āĀ, ..., t_{#t})`, `U(t) = D(t) = E(t) = őĶ`.
  - `zeros(t) = 1`: `N(t)` and `U(t)` non-empty, `D(t) = E(t) = őĶ`.
  - `zeros(t) = 2`: `N(t), U(t), D(t)` non-empty, `E(t) = őĶ`.
  - `zeros(t) = 3`: all four non-empty.

To access individual components of a field we introduce the notation `t.X‚āĀ`, grounded in T0's projection as follows. Since `‚Ąē‚Āļ = {n ‚ąą ‚Ąē : 0 < n} ‚äÜ ‚Ąē`, a finite sequence over `‚Ąē‚Āļ` is a finite sequence over `‚Ąē`; if nonempty, it is therefore an element of `T` by T0's stipulation that `T` is the set of nonempty finite sequences over `‚Ąē`. *Derivation.* By T4's stipulation, the separator positions of `t` are exactly `{i : 1 ‚Č§ i ‚Č§ #t ‚ąß tŠĶĘ = 0}`, and every non-separator position carries a field component ‚ÄĒ strictly positive by NAT-zero and NAT-discrete at `m = 0` on T0's carrier ‚Ąē.
