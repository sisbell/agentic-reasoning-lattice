**TA5-SIG (LastSignificantPosition).** We define the *last significant position* of a tumbler `t вИИ T`, written `sig(t)`.

When `t` has at least one nonzero component вАФ that is, `(E i : 1 вЙ§ i вЙ§ #t : tбµҐ вЙ† 0)` вАФ we set `sig(t)` to the rightmost index at which `t` is nonzero. Let `S = {i : 1 вЙ§ i вЙ§ #t вИІ tбµҐ вЙ† 0}`; the existence hypothesis makes `S` nonempty, and every `i вИИ S` is bounded above by `#t`.

The maximum of a bounded nonempty вДХ-subset is not delivered directly by NAT-wellorder, which states only the *least-element* principle; вДХ has no greatest element, so the principle does not dualize unconditionally, and greatest elements are guaranteed only on subsets bounded above. We derive `max(S)` from the least-element principle with `#t` as the explicit boundedness witness. Form the upper-bound set `U = {u вИИ вДХ : (A i вИИ S :: i вЙ§ u)}`; every `i вИИ S` satisfies `i вЙ§ #t`, so `#t вИИ U` and `U вЙ† вИЕ`. NAT-wellorder applied to `U` delivers a least upper bound `m`. We claim `m вИИ S`. Suppose otherwise: every `i вИИ S` satisfies `i вЙ§ m` (since `m вИИ U`) and `i вЙ† m` (since `m вИЙ S`), so NAT-order's `вЙ§`-defining clause `i вЙ§ m вЯЇ i < m вИ® i = m` forces `i < m`, and NAT-discrete's forward direction then gives `i + 1 вЙ§ m` for every `i вИИ S`. Fixing any `iвВА вИИ S` вАФ `S вЙ† вИЕ` supplies such a witness вАФ we have `1 вЙ§ iвВА` (since `S вКЖ {1, вА¶, #t}`), and NAT-addcompat's strict successor inequality supplies `iвВА < iвВА + 1`; the chain `1 вЙ§ iвВА < iвВА + 1 вЙ§ m` вАФ with the weak bounds dispatched into `<`-or-`=` cases by NAT-order's `вЙ§`-defining clause and the strict segments composed by NAT-order's transitivity of `<` вАФ yields `1 < m`, hence `m вЙ• 1`. NAT-sub's conditional closure at `m вЙ• 1` supplies `m вИТ 1 вИИ вДХ`. The value `m вИТ 1` is strictly below `m`: NAT-sub's right-inverse gives `(m вИТ 1) + 1 = m`, and NAT-addcompat's `(m вИТ 1) < (m вИТ 1) + 1` rewrites via that identity to `m вИТ 1 < m`. And `m вИТ 1` bounds `S`: each `i вИИ S` satisfies `i + 1 вЙ§ m`, which splits via NAT-order's `вЙ§`-defining clause into `i + 1 = m` (then NAT-sub's right telescoping `(i + 1) вИТ 1 = i` gives `i = m вИТ 1`) or `i + 1 < m` (then NAT-sub's strict monotonicity at `p = 1`, with both `i + 1 вЙ• 1` and `m вЙ• 1`, gives `(i + 1) вИТ 1 < m вИТ 1`, i.e., `i < m вИТ 1`); in both cases `i вЙ§ m вИТ 1`. So `m вИТ 1 вИИ U` with `m вИТ 1 < m`, contradicting minimality of `m` in `U`. Therefore `m вИИ S`, and we set `sig(t) = m = max(S)`.

When every component of `t` is zero вАФ that is, `(A i : 1 вЙ§ i вЙ§ #t : tбµҐ = 0)` вАФ we set `sig(t) = #t`.

In both cases `1 вЙ§ sig(t) вЙ§ #t`, since `#t вЙ• 1` for every `t вИИ T`.

*Formal Contract:*
- *Preconditions:* `t вИИ T` (any tumbler with `#t вЙ• 1`).
- *Definition:* `sig(t) = max({i : 1 вЙ§ i вЙ§ #t вИІ tбµҐ вЙ† 0})` when `(E i : 1 вЙ§ i вЙ§ #t : tбµҐ вЙ† 0)`; `sig(t) = #t` when `(A i : 1 вЙ§ i вЙ§ #t : tбµҐ = 0)`.
- *Depends:*

  - NAT-wellorder (NatWellOrdering) вАФ least-element principle, applied to the upper-bound set `U = {u вИИ вДХ : (A i вИИ S :: i вЙ§ u)}` (nonempty via `#t вИИ U`) to produce the least upper bound `m` of `S = {i : 1 вЙ§ i вЙ§ #t вИІ tбµҐ вЙ† 0}`; the contradiction argument then shows `m вИИ S`, making `m = max(S)`.
