**T2 (IntrinsicComparison).** The order relation T1 is computable from the two tumblers alone, without consulting any external data structure. The number of component pairs the comparison examines is bounded by both `#a` and `#b` ‚Äî at most `#a` pairs and at most `#b` pairs.

*Proof.* We must establish two claims: (1) the comparison requires no data beyond the components and lengths of the two tumblers, and (2) the number of component pairs examined is at most `#a` and at most `#b`.

Let `a = a‚ÇÅ. ... .a‚Çò` and `b = b‚ÇÅ. ... .b‚Çô`. The definition of `<` in T1 asks for the existence of a witness position `k ‚â• 1` satisfying two conditions: agreement at all positions before `k`, and either a component divergence at `k` or prefix exhaustion at `k`. We trace exactly what data this examination consults and how many comparisons it performs.

The comparison proceeds by scanning positions `i = 1, 2, ...` and comparing the pair `(a·µ¢, b·µ¢)` at each position. Two outcomes are possible.

*Case 1: divergence at some position `k` with `k ‚â§ m ‚àß k ‚â§ n`.* The scan finds `a‚Çñ ‚âÝ b‚Çñ` after verifying `a·µ¢ = b·µ¢` for all `1 ‚â§ i < k` ‚Äî that is, `k` is the *first* position at which the two sequences disagree, the least element of the nonempty subset `{i ‚àà ‚Ñï : 1 ‚â§ i ‚àß i ‚â§ m ‚àß i ‚â§ n ‚àß a·µ¢ ‚âÝ b·µ¢}` of shared-range divergence positions, and the well-definedness of this least element is discharged from NAT-wellorder applied to that subset. The minimality of `k` is what licenses the count of *exactly* `k` examined pairs: were some smaller position `i < k` also a divergence, the agreement premise `a·µ¢ = b·µ¢` for `1 ‚â§ i < k` would fail, contradicting the minimality clause supplied by NAT-wellorder, so the scan cannot have skipped past an earlier divergence and the examined positions are precisely `{1, 2, ..., k}`. Exactly `k` component pairs are examined. The case hypothesis's first conjunct `k ‚â§ m` gives `k ‚â§ #a`, and its second conjunct `k ‚â§ n` gives `k ‚â§ #b`, so the count `k` is bounded by both `#a` and `#b`. NAT-order's trichotomy at the component pair `(a‚Çñ, b‚Çñ)` resolves `a‚Çñ ‚âÝ b‚Çñ` into the two-way split `a‚Çñ < b‚Çñ ‚à® b‚Çñ < a‚Çñ` ‚Äî its remaining disjunct `a‚Çñ = b‚Çñ` is excluded by the case hypothesis, and the exactly-one clause guarantees one of the strict comparisons holds. Whichever holds decides the ordering via T1 case (i): `a‚Çñ < b‚Çñ` yields `a < b`, and `b‚Çñ < a‚Çñ` yields `b < a`. Both outcomes are comparisons of two natural numbers intrinsic to the tumblers.

*Case 2: no divergence within the shared range.* The scan exhausts every position `i` with `1 ‚â§ i ‚àß i ‚â§ m ‚àß i ‚â§ n` ‚Äî the shared range ‚Äî without finding `a·µ¢ ‚âÝ b·µ¢`. The count of component pairs examined and the ordering decision are together determined by NAT-order's trichotomy at the length pair `(m, n)`, which partitions the outcome exhaustively into three branches. *Sub-case `m < n`.* The shared range is exactly `{i : 1 ‚â§ i ‚â§ m}` ‚Äî every position of `a`, each of which satisfies `i ‚â§ n` via NAT-order's transitivity composing the `a`-position bound `i ‚â§ m` with the case hypothesis `m < n` in the mixed strict-and-nonstrict form `i ‚â§ m ‚àß m < n ‚üπ i ‚â§ n` ‚Äî so exactly `m` component pairs are examined; that count satisfies `m ‚â§ m` reflexively and `m ‚â§ n` from the case hypothesis's strict comparison read as the strict disjunct of `‚â§`. Then `a` is a proper prefix of `b` and T1 case (ii) gives `a < b`. *Sub-case `n < m`.* The shared range is exactly `{i : 1 ‚â§ i ‚â§ n}` ‚Äî every position of `b`, each of which satisfies `i ‚â§ m` via NAT-order's transitivity composing the `b`-position bound `i ‚â§ n` with the case hypothesis `n < m` in the mirrored mixed form `i ‚â§ n ‚àß n < m ‚üπ i ‚â§ m` ‚Äî so exactly `n` component pairs are examined; that count satisfies `n ‚â§ n` reflexively and `n ‚â§ m` from the case hypothesis's strict comparison read as the strict disjunct of `‚â§`. Then `b` is a proper prefix of `a` and T1 case (ii) with roles swapped gives `b < a`. *Sub-case `m = n`.* The shared range covers every position of either tumbler, so exactly `m` component pairs are examined; that count satisfies `m ‚â§ m` reflexively and `m ‚â§ n` by substitution of `m = n`. Componentwise agreement on the shared range together with `m = n` gives `a = b` by T3. In every sub-case the count of pairs examined is at most `m` and at most `n`. The exhaustiveness of this three-way partition ‚Äî the "exactly one" clause of NAT-order's trichotomy ‚Äî is what underwrites postcondition (a) for this branch: some ordering among `a` and `b` is always decided, because no fourth outcome is possible for the length pair `(m, n)`.

In both cases, the number of component pairs examined is at most `m` and at most `n` ‚Äî equivalently, at most `#a` and at most `#b` ‚Äî establishing claim (2).

For claim (1), we observe that every value consulted ‚Äî the components `a·µ¢` and `b·µ¢` at each position, and the lengths `m` and `n` ‚Äî belongs to the two tumblers themselves. The definition of T1 is expressed entirely in terms of these values. No tree structure, no index, no auxiliary mapping, and no external state participates in the decision. ‚àé

The importance of T2 is operational: span containment tests, link search, and index traversal all reduce to tumbler comparison. If comparison required a lookup, these operations would depend on auxiliary state, and the system's decentralization guarantee would collapse ‚Äî one could not determine whether an address falls within a span without access to the index that manages that span.

Gregory's implementation confirms T2. The comparison function `tumblercmp` delegates to `abscmp`, which performs a purely positional comparison: exponent first (a proxy for the number of leading zeros), then lexicographic mantissa slot-by-slot. No tree structure, no index, no external state is consulted.

*Formal Contract:*
- *Preconditions:* `a, b ‚àà T` ‚Äî two well-formed tumblers (finite sequences over ‚Ñï with `#a ‚â• 1` and `#b ‚â• 1`, per T0).
- *Depends:* T0 (CarrierSetDefinition) ‚Äî the proof's bound on component pairs examined ‚Äî that the count is at most `#a` and at most `#b` ‚Äî is expressed in terms of the length operator `#¬∑` that T0 introduces, and the componentwise scan at positions `i = 1, 2, ...` consults the component-projection `¬∑·µ¢` that T0 also introduces; postcondition (c) enumerates the consulted values as `{a·µ¢ : 1 ‚â§ i ‚â§ #a}`, `{b·µ¢ : 1 ‚â§ i ‚â§ #b}`, `#a`, and `#b`, each of which is a T0 operator applied to a T0 element. T1 (LexicographicOrder) ‚Äî T2's claim is that the order relation defined by T1 is computable from the two tumblers alone, so T1 is the subject of the claim; the proof unfolds T1's witness structure (existence of `k ‚â• 1` with `a·µ¢ = b·µ¢` for all `1 ‚â§ i < k` and either case (i) component divergence or case (ii) prefix exhaustion) and dispatches the two outcomes of the scan to T1 case (i) ‚Äî Case 1 (divergence at `k` with `k ‚â§ m ‚àß k ‚â§ n` and the ordering decided by `a‚Çñ < b‚Çñ` vs. `b‚Çñ < a‚Çñ`) ‚Äî and T1 case (ii) ‚Äî Case 2 with `m < n`, which gives `a < b` as a proper prefix (the symmetric `n < m` branch of Case 2 invokes T1 case (ii) with the roles swapped to conclude `b < a`). T3 (CanonicalRepresentation) ‚Äî postcondition (a) requires T3 for the equality case: when all shared-range component pairs agree ‚Äî i.e., `a·µ¢ = b·µ¢` for every `i` with `1 ‚â§ i ‚àß i ‚â§ m ‚àß i ‚â§ n` ‚Äî and `m = n`, T3 bridges component-level agreement to `a = b`. NAT-wellorder (NatWellOrdering) ‚Äî Case 1's count of *exactly* `k` examined component pairs requires `k` to be the *first* (least) position at which `a` and `b` disagree, not merely *some* position of disagreement; the well-definedness of this least element is discharged from NAT-wellorder applied to the nonempty subset `{i ‚àà ‚Ñï : 1 ‚â§ i ‚àß i ‚â§ m ‚àß i ‚â§ n ‚àß a·µ¢ ‚âÝ b·µ¢}` of shared-range divergence positions (nonempty in Case 1 by the case hypothesis `a‚Çñ ‚âÝ b‚Çñ` together with `k ‚â§ m ‚àß k ‚â§ n`, which exhibits `k` as a member). The minimality clause supplied by NAT-wellorder is what makes the agreement premise `a·µ¢ = b·µ¢` for all `1 ‚â§ i < k` consistent with the case hypothesis ‚Äî were some smaller position also a divergence, the premise would fail ‚Äî and therefore underwrites the count "exactly `k`" rather than "some count ‚â• k", which is the count that postcondition (b)'s bound `‚â§ #a` and `‚â§ #b` is then checked against via the case hypothesis's conjuncts `k ‚â§ m` and `k ‚â§ n`. NAT-order (NatStrictTotalOrder) ‚Äî the proof invokes NAT-order at four distinct sites: two trichotomy invocations (on ‚Ñï, each dispatching a case split into T1's clauses) and two transitivity invocations (in the mixed strict-and-nonstrict form `i ‚â§ p ‚àß p < q ‚üπ i ‚â§ q`, each lifting a position bound across a strict length comparison). Trichotomy is invoked twice, each invocation necessary for its case split to be exhaustive and to dispatch unambiguously into T1's clauses. Case 1 invokes trichotomy at the component pair `(a‚Çñ, b‚Çñ)` to resolve the case hypothesis `a‚Çñ ‚âÝ b‚Çñ` into the two-way strict dichotomy `a‚Çñ < b‚Çñ ‚à® b‚Çñ < a‚Çñ`: trichotomy's three disjuncts `a‚Çñ < b‚Çñ`, `a‚Çñ = b‚Çñ`, `b‚Çñ < a‚Çñ` are exhaustive, and the case hypothesis excludes the middle disjunct, while the exactly-one clause guarantees that one of the remaining two strict comparisons holds. Whichever holds dispatches to T1 case (i) ‚Äî `a‚Çñ < b‚Çñ` witnessing `a < b` and `b‚Çñ < a‚Çñ` witnessing `b < a` ‚Äî so postcondition (a) is decided in this case exactly because trichotomy supplies the strict comparison. Case 2 invokes trichotomy at the length pair `(m, n)` to partition the outcome of exhausting the shared range into three branches ‚Äî `m < n`, `n < m`, `m = n` ‚Äî which dispatch respectively to T1 case (ii) (`a < b` as a proper prefix), its symmetric instance (`b < a`), and T3 (`a = b` from componentwise agreement on the shared range extended to agreement everywhere by `m = n`); the exhaustiveness of this three-way partition ‚Äî the claim that no fourth outcome is possible for the length pair ‚Äî is what underwrites postcondition (a) in this branch, since without trichotomy's exactly-one clause at `(m, n)` the "no divergence within the shared range" branch could fail to produce any conclusion. Transitivity is invoked twice, both in Case 2's dispatch following the length-pair trichotomy, in its mixed strict-and-nonstrict form. Case 2's `m < n` sub-case invokes transitivity to compose each `a`-position bound `i ‚â§ m` (holding for every `i` in `{i : 1 ‚â§ i ‚â§ m}`) with the case hypothesis `m < n` into `i ‚â§ n`; this is what identifies the shared range `{i : 1 ‚â§ i ‚àß i ‚â§ m ‚àß i ‚â§ n}` with `{i : 1 ‚â§ i ‚â§ m}` ‚Äî every position of `a` satisfies the second shared-range conjunct `i ‚â§ n` exactly because transitivity lifts the `a`-position bound across the strict length comparison ‚Äî so the count of examined pairs collapses to `m` (the full length of `a`) and T1 case (ii)'s proper-prefix premise is discharged. Case 2's `n < m` sub-case invokes the mirrored instance to compose each `b`-position bound `i ‚â§ n` with the case hypothesis `n < m` into `i ‚â§ m`; this identifies the shared range with `{i : 1 ‚â§ i ‚â§ n}` ‚Äî every position of `b` satisfies the first shared-range conjunct `i ‚â§ m` exactly because the mirrored transitivity lifts the `b`-position bound across the mirrored strict length comparison ‚Äî so the count collapses to `n` (the full length of `b`) and T1 case (ii) with roles swapped discharges its proper-prefix premise. Case 2's `m = n` sub-case does not invoke transitivity: the shared-range identification there passes through substitution of the equality `m = n` rather than composition across a strict comparison, and so earns no transitivity site.
