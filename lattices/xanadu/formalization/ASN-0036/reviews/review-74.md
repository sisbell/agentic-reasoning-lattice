# Cone Review — ASN-0036/D-CTG-depth (cycle 5)

*2026-04-14 21:59*

### D-CTG-depth's contiguity reduction postcondition is stated informally and requires an unproved lemma about T1
**Foundation**: T1 (LexicographicOrder, ASN-0034): `a < b` iff first differing component satisfies `aₖ < bₖ`, or `a` is a proper prefix of `b`
**ASN**: D-CTG-depth postcondition: "`(A v₁, v₂ : v₁ ∈ V_S(d) ∧ v₂ ∈ V_S(d) : (A j : 2 ≤ j ≤ #v₁ − 1 : (v₁)ⱼ = (v₂)ⱼ))`. Contiguity of V_S(d) reduces to contiguity of the m-th (last) component."
**Issue**: D-CTG-depth's formal postcondition establishes that all positions in V_S(d) share components 2 through m − 1. The second sentence — "contiguity reduces to contiguity of the last component" — is the property's entire payoff (it is the "Reduction" in "SharedPrefixReduction"), yet it appears as informal prose within the postcondition block rather than as a formalized claim. The reduction does not follow from the shared-prefix result alone. It requires an intermediate lemma about T1: that no tumbler with a different value at any component j ∈ [2, m − 1] can lie between two tumblers that agree at components 1 through m − 1. Specifically, if u and q share prefix (S, P₂, …, P_{m−1}) and w differs from this prefix at component j (with 2 ≤ j ≤ m − 1), then T1(i) forces either w < u or w > q — the two inequalities u < w and w < q require Pⱼ < Qⱼ and Qⱼ < Pⱼ simultaneously, which is impossible. Without this step, the claim that D-CTG's full-sequence contiguity collapses to last-component contiguity is an unjustified leap. Any downstream consumer (particularly operation ASNs proving contiguity preservation at depth ≥ 3) that cites D-CTG-depth's reduction must independently derive this T1 lemma, since the postcondition they'd be citing is informal.
**What needs resolving**: Either formalize the reduction as a second postcondition with its own quantified statement and derivation from the shared-prefix result plus T1, or extract the T1 lemma ("same-prefix tumblers admit no different-prefix intermediates under lexicographic order") as a citable property and reference it in the postcondition.
