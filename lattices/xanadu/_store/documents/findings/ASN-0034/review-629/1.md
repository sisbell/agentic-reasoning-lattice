### Redundant T0 invocation in S ⊆ ℕ justification
**Class**: OBSERVE
**Foundation**: ActionPoint
**ASN**: ActionPoint, Derivation: "The set S = {i ∈ ℕ : 1 ≤ i ≤ #w ∧ wᵢ ≠ 0} is a nonempty subset of ℕ: nonempty by TA-Pos, and a subset of ℕ by construction (the carrier `i ∈ ℕ` is licensed by T0's commitment that the index domain `{1, …, #w}` of w lies in ℕ)."
**Issue**: S is a set-comprehension with the explicit carrier restriction `i ∈ ℕ` in front of the colon — it is a subset of ℕ by the form of the comprehension itself, with no further justification needed. The parenthetical invoking T0's "index domain ⊆ ℕ" commitment is justifying something that is not the load-bearing fact (which is just that the comprehension restricts to i ∈ ℕ). T0's index-domain commitment is what licenses `wᵢ ≠ 0` to be a meaningful clause for indices in range, not what makes S a subset of ℕ. The phrasing reads like reviser drift — meta-prose about why we're allowed to write `i ∈ ℕ` rather than the direct read of the comprehension.
