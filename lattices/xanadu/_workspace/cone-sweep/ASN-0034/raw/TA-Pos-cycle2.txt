### NAT-closure body justifies axiom form rather than stating it
**Class**: OBSERVE
**Foundation**: NAT-closure (NatArithmeticClosureAndIdentity) — successor-positivity axiom `(A n ∈ ℕ :: 0 < n + 1)`
**ASN**: NAT-closure body — "This derivation pins down only the single sum `0 + 1`; without the universal clause, a model could satisfy the signature, the identity laws, and the bare `0 < 1` while still permitting `m + 1 = 0` at some `m ≥ 1`. **Issue**: This passage explains why the universal form of the axiom was chosen over a weaker alternative. The content — "here is a model that would invalidate a weaker version" — is editorial motivation, not mathematical advance. The precise reader working through the body must skip it to follow the argument from axioms to the `0 < 1` consequence.
