### Conditional-closure clause is entailed by signature
**Class**: OBSERVE
**Foundation**: NAT-sub (NatPartialSubtraction)
**ASN**: Axiom clauses `− : {(m, n) ∈ ℕ × ℕ : m ≥ n} → ℕ` (signature) and `(A m, n ∈ ℕ : m ≥ n : m − n ∈ ℕ)` (conditional closure).
**Issue**: The signature's codomain `→ ℕ` already commits that for every `(m, n)` in the domain of definition (i.e., `m ≥ n`), `m − n ∈ ℕ`. The conditional-closure clause is therefore a restatement of what the signature posits, not an independent axiom. Author's body prose acknowledges this implicitly when it derives single-valuedness from "positing − as a function" but lists conditional closure as a separate clause anyway. Sound as written; flagged for the record only.
