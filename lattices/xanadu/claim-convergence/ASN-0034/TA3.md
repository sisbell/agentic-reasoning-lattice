**TA3 (OrderPreservationUnderSubtractionWeak).** `(A a, b, w : a < b ‚ąß a ‚Č• w ‚ąß b ‚Č• w : a ‚äĖ w ‚Č§ b ‚äĖ w)`.

*Proof.* We show that for all `a, b, w ‚ąą T` with `a < b`, `a ‚Č• w`, `b ‚Č• w`, we have `a ‚äĖ w ‚Č§ b ‚äĖ w`.

By TA2, `a ‚äĖ w` and `b ‚äĖ w` are well-formed tumblers in `T`. Recall TumblerSub: given `x ‚Č• w`, zero-pad both operands to `L_{x,w}` (the longer of `#x`, `#w`, dispatched by NAT-order's trichotomy on `(#x, #w)`) and scan for the first disagreement. If none exists, `x ‚äĖ w` is the zero tumbler of length `L_{x,w}`. Otherwise, let `d` be the first divergence; then `(x ‚äĖ w)ŠĶĘ = 0` for `i < d`, `(x ‚äĖ w)_d = x_d - w_d`, and `(x ‚äĖ w)ŠĶĘ = xŠĶĘ` for `i > d`, with result length `L_{x,w}`.

Since `a < b`, T1 provides two cases: (i) there exists a first position `j` with `j ‚Č§ #a ‚ąß j ‚Č§ #b` where `a‚Īľ < b‚Īľ`, or (ii) `a` is a proper prefix of `b`.

**Case A: `a` is a proper prefix of `b`** (T1 case (ii)). Then `#a < #b` and `aŠĶĘ = bŠĶĘ` for all `i ‚Č§ #a`.

*Sub-case A1: `a = w`.* NAT-order's trichotomy on `(#a, #w)` gives `L_{a,w} = #a`; `a ‚äĖ w` is the zero tumbler of that length. Since `a` is a proper prefix of `b` and `a = w`, `bŠĶĘ = wŠĶĘ` for all `i ‚Č§ #w = #a`. If some `bŠĶĘ` with `i > #w` is nonzero, `(b, w)` diverges beyond `#w`, making `b ‚äĖ w` positive; by TA-PosDom, `a ‚äĖ w < b ‚äĖ w`. Otherwise, the zero-padded sequences of `b` and `w` agree everywhere, and under `#w = #a < #b` NAT-order places `(#b, #w)` in sub-case (ő≥), giving `L_{b,w} = #b`; `b ‚äĖ w` is the zero tumbler of length `#b`. Both results are zero, agreeing on positions `1, ..., #a`; the strict length inequality `#a < #b` is converted by NAT-discrete into `#a + 1 ‚Č§ #b` (with `#a + 1 ‚ąą ‚Ąē` by NAT-closure), supplying T1 case (ii)'s witness. Thus `a ‚äĖ w < b ‚äĖ w` by T1 case (ii).

*Sub-case A2: `a > w` with divergence.* Let `d‚āź = zpd(a, w)`. We claim `d‚āź ‚Č§ #a`: if `a > w` by T1 case (i), it supplies `d‚āź ‚Č§ #a ‚ąß d‚āź ‚Č§ #w`; if by T1 case (ii), `w` is a proper prefix of `a` and `d‚āź` is the first `i > #w` with `aŠĶĘ > 0`, so `d‚āź ‚Č§ #a`. Since `#a < #b`, also `d‚āź ‚Č§ #b`. Case A's prefix agreement `bŠĶĘ = aŠĶĘ` for `i ‚Č§ #a` lifts through ZPD's padded-projection equality on the native domain (`√ĘŠĶĘ = aŠĶĘ` from `i ‚Č§ #a`, `bŐāŠĶĘ = bŠĶĘ` from `i ‚Č§ #a ‚Č§ #b`) to give `√ĘŠĶĘ = bŐāŠĶĘ` for `1 ‚Č§ i ‚Č§ #a`. Chaining at `i < d‚āź` with ZPD's pre-divergence agreement `√ĘŠĶĘ = ŇĶŠĶĘ` for `(a, w)` yields `bŐāŠĶĘ = ŇĶŠĶĘ` for `i < d‚āź`; at `i = d‚āź`, ZPD's first-disagreement `√Ę_{d‚āź} ‚Č† ŇĶ_{d‚āź}` combined with `√Ę_{d‚āź} = bŐā_{d‚āź}` gives `bŐā_{d‚āź} ‚Č† ŇĶ_{d‚āź}`. By ZPD's minimality, `d_b = d‚āź`; write `d = d‚āź = d_b`.

At positions `i < d`, TumblerSub's Definition gives `(a ‚äĖ w)ŠĶĘ = (b ‚äĖ w)ŠĶĘ = 0`. At position `d`: TumblerSub's Definition gives `(a ‚äĖ w)_d = √Ę_d - ŇĶ_d` and `(b ‚äĖ w)_d = bŐā_d - ŇĶ_d` ‚ÄĒ well-typed on ZPD's padded projections regardless of whether `d` exceeds `#w` in the prefix-divergence sub-case. Both differences are in ‚Ąē by NAT-sub's conditional closure under `√Ę_d ‚Č• ŇĶ_d` and `bŐā_d ‚Č• ŇĶ_d`, supplied by TumblerSub's exported postconditions `√Ę_d > ŇĶ_d` (applied to `(a, w)` under `a ‚Č• w`) and `bŐā_d > ŇĶ_d` (applied to `(b, w)` under `b ‚Č• w`) via NAT-order. Equality of the two divergence-point components follows from `√Ę_d = bŐā_d` (just established). At positions `d < i ‚Č§ #a`: TumblerSub's Definition gives `(a ‚äĖ w)ŠĶĘ = √ĘŠĶĘ` and `(b ‚äĖ w)ŠĶĘ = bŐāŠĶĘ`; with `i ‚Č§ #a ‚Č§ #b`, ZPD's padded-projection equality lifts to native (`√ĘŠĶĘ = aŠĶĘ`, `bŐāŠĶĘ = bŠĶĘ`), and Case A's prefix agreement gives `aŠĶĘ = bŠĶĘ`, hence `(a ‚äĖ w)ŠĶĘ = (b ‚äĖ w)ŠĶĘ`. The results agree on `1, ..., #a`.

Denote result lengths `L_{a,w}`, `L_{b,w}`. We establish `L_{a,w} ‚Č§ L_{b,w}` by enumerating NAT-order's trichotomy on `(#a, #w)`. In (őĪ) or (ő≥), `L_{a,w} = #a`; with `#a < #b`, `(#b, #w)` is in (ő≥), giving `L_{b,w} = #b > #a`. In (ő≤) `#a < #w`, `L_{a,w} = #w`; NAT-order on `(#b, #w)` gives `L_{b,w} ‚ąą {#b, #w}` with `L_{b,w} ‚Č• #w = L_{a,w}`.

At positions `#a < i ‚Č§ L_{a,w}` (only in sub-case (ő≤), where `L_{a,w} = #w`): TumblerSub's Definition gives `(a ‚äĖ w)ŠĶĘ = √ĘŠĶĘ`, and ZPD's padding clause for `√Ę` at `i > #a` gives `√ĘŠĶĘ = 0`; likewise `(b ‚äĖ w)ŠĶĘ = bŐāŠĶĘ`, which equals `bŠĶĘ` if `i ‚Č§ #b` (ZPD's padded-projection equality) and `0` if `i > #b` (ZPD's padding clause) ‚ÄĒ in either case, NAT-zero's lower bound `0 ‚Č§ bŐāŠĶĘ` gives `√ĘŠĶĘ ‚Č§ bŐāŠĶĘ`, hence `(a ‚äĖ w)ŠĶĘ ‚Č§ (b ‚äĖ w)ŠĶĘ`.

If a first disagreement exists at `p ‚ąą 1, ..., L_{a,w}`, then `p > #a` and `(a ‚äĖ w)_p = 0`, `(b ‚äĖ w)_p ‚Č† 0`. By NAT-zero's lower bound and NAT-order's defining clause, `(b ‚äĖ w)_p > 0`. Position `p` satisfies `p ‚Č§ L_{a,w} ‚ąß p ‚Č§ L_{b,w}`, so T1 case (i) yields `a ‚äĖ w < b ‚äĖ w`. If no disagreement exists, NAT-order's trichotomy at `(L_{a,w}, L_{b,w})` with `L_{a,w} ‚Č§ L_{b,w}` fixes either `L_{a,w} < L_{b,w}` or `L_{a,w} = L_{b,w}`. In the strict case, NAT-discrete gives `L_{a,w} + 1 ‚Č§ L_{b,w}` (with closure by NAT-closure), so `a ‚äĖ w` is a proper prefix of `b ‚äĖ w` by T1 case (ii). In the equal case, T3 yields `a ‚äĖ w = b ‚äĖ w`.

*Sub-case A3: `a > w` without divergence (zero-padded equality).* The only possibility is T1 case (ii): `w` is a proper prefix of `a` with `aŠĶĘ = 0` for all `i > #w`. NAT-order on `(#a, #w)` is sub-case (ő≥), giving `L_{a,w} = #a`; `a ‚äĖ w` is the zero tumbler of that length. Since `#w < #a < #b`, NAT-order on `(#b, #w)` is sub-case (ő≥), giving `L_{b,w} = #b`. By TA-Pos's complementarity, `b ‚äĖ w` is either positive or a zero tumbler. If positive, `a ‚äĖ w < b ‚äĖ w` by TA-PosDom. If a zero tumbler, both results are zero; NAT-discrete converts `#a < #b` into `#a + 1 ‚Č§ #b`, supplying T1 case (ii)'s witness. Thus `a ‚äĖ w < b ‚äĖ w`.

**Case B: Component divergence at `j`** (T1 case (i)). There exists a first position `j` with `j ‚Č§ #a ‚ąß j ‚Č§ #b`, `a‚Īľ < b‚Īľ`, and `aŠĶĘ = bŠĶĘ` for all `i < j`.

*Sub-case B1: `a` is zero-padded-equal to `w`.* TumblerSub's no-divergence branch fixes `a ‚äĖ w = [0, ‚Ä¶, 0]` of length `L_{a,w}`, hence `Zero(a ‚äĖ w)` by TA-Pos's Definition. We show `(b, w)` is not zero-padded-equal, supplying TumblerSub's exported `Pos` postcondition for `b ‚äĖ w`. NAT-order's trichotomy on `(#a, #w)` places `L_{a,w}` in `{#a, #w}` with `L_{a,w} ‚Č• #a` in each branch ((ő≤) supplies `#a < #w = L_{a,w}`); analogously, `L_{b,w} ‚Č• #b`. Case B's witness `j ‚Č§ #a ‚ąß j ‚Č§ #b ‚ąß a‚Īľ < b‚Īľ` therefore gives `j ‚Č§ L_{a,w}` and `j ‚Č§ L_{b,w}`. ZPD's padded-projection equality (legal at `j ‚Č§ #a`) gives `√Ę‚Īľ = a‚Īľ`; a's zero-padded-equality instantiated at `j ‚Č§ L_{a,w}` yields `√Ę‚Īľ = ŇĶ‚Īľ`, hence `a‚Īľ = ŇĶ‚Īľ`. NAT-order's trichotomy at `(j, #w)` splits into two cases. (i) `j ‚Č§ #w`: ZPD's padded-projection equality at `j ‚Č§ #w` gives `ŇĶ‚Īľ = w‚Īľ`, so `a‚Īľ = w‚Īľ`; substituting into `a‚Īľ < b‚Īľ` yields `w‚Īľ < b‚Īľ`, and NAT-order's disjointness-of-`<`-and-`=` at `(w‚Īľ, b‚Īľ)` gives `w‚Īľ ‚Č† b‚Īľ`. ZPD's padded-projection equalities `bŐā‚Īľ = b‚Īľ` (from `j ‚Č§ #b`) and `ŇĶ‚Īľ = w‚Īľ` lift this to `bŐā‚Īľ ‚Č† ŇĶ‚Īľ`. (ii) `j > #w`: ZPD's padding clause for `ŇĶ` in the `(a, w)` context (legal since `#w < j ‚Č§ L_{a,w}`) gives `ŇĶ‚Īľ = 0`, so `a‚Īľ = 0`; substituting into `a‚Īľ < b‚Īľ` yields `0 < b‚Īľ`, and NAT-order's disjointness-of-`<`-and-`=` at `(0, b‚Īľ)` gives `b‚Īľ ‚Č† 0`. ZPD's padded-projection equality `bŐā‚Īľ = b‚Īľ` (from `j ‚Č§ #b`) together with ZPD's padding clause for `ŇĶ` in the `(b, w)` context (legal since `#w < j ‚Č§ L_{b,w}`) gives `bŐā‚Īľ = b‚Īľ ‚Č† 0 = ŇĶ‚Īľ`. In either case, `bŐā‚Īľ ‚Č† ŇĶ‚Īľ` at `1 ‚Č§ j ‚Č§ L_{b,w}`, so `(b, w)` is not zero-padded-equal (ZPD's existence biconditional, contrapositive); hence `zpd(b, w)` is defined, and TumblerSub's exported `Pos` postcondition under `b ‚Č• w` gives `Pos(b ‚äĖ w)`. By TA-PosDom, `a ‚äĖ w < b ‚äĖ w`.

For the remaining sub-cases, `d‚āź = zpd(a, w)` is well-defined, so TumblerSub's exported postcondition applied to `(a, w)` under `a ‚Č• w` gives `√Ę_{d‚āź} > ŇĶ_{d‚āź}` (on ZPD's padded projections, since `d‚āź` may exceed `#w` in the prefix-divergence sub-case). T1's case analysis of `a > w` (which holds since `a ‚Č• w` and `d‚āź` is defined) gives `d‚āź ‚Č§ #a` hypothesis-free: T1 case (i) for `(a, w)` supplies `d‚āź ‚Č§ #a ‚ąß d‚āź ‚Č§ #w`; T1 case (ii) places `w` as a proper prefix of `a` with `d‚āź` the first `i > #w` where `aŠĶĘ ‚Č† 0`, hence `#w < d‚āź ‚Č§ #a`.

The zero-padded divergence `d_b = zpd(b, w)` is also well-defined: were `b` zero-padded-equal to `w`, ZPD's padded equality `(A i : 1 ‚Č§ i ‚Č§ L_{b,w} : bŐāŠĶĘ = ŇĶŠĶĘ)` would hold throughout `1..L_{b,w}`. NAT-order's trichotomy on `(#b, #w)` places `L_{b,w}` in `{#b, #w}` with `L_{b,w} ‚Č• #b` in each branch (in (ő≤), `#b < #w = L_{b,w}`); Case B's witness `j ‚Č§ #b` therefore gives `j ‚Č§ L_{b,w}`, so the hypothesised equality at `j` yields `bŐā‚Īľ = ŇĶ‚Īľ`. ZPD's padded-projection equality at `j ‚Č§ #b` gives `bŐā‚Īľ = b‚Īľ`, hence `b‚Īľ = ŇĶ‚Īľ`. NAT-order's trichotomy at `(j, #w)` splits into two cases. (i) `j ‚Č§ #w`: ZPD's padded-projection equality at `j ‚Č§ #w` gives `ŇĶ‚Īľ = w‚Īľ`, so `b‚Īľ = w‚Īľ`; substituting into Case B's witness `a‚Īľ < b‚Īľ` yields `a‚Īľ < w‚Īľ`. For `i < j`, the hypothesised equality at `i ‚Č§ L_{b,w}` (legal since `i < j ‚Č§ L_{b,w}`) gives `bŐāŠĶĘ = ŇĶŠĶĘ`; ZPD's padded-projection equalities at `i < j ‚Č§ #b` and `i < j ‚Č§ #w` lift this to `bŠĶĘ = wŠĶĘ`, and Case B's pre-`j` agreement `aŠĶĘ = bŠĶĘ` chains to `aŠĶĘ = wŠĶĘ`. Thus `j ‚Č§ #a ‚ąß j ‚Č§ #w ‚ąß a‚Īľ < w‚Īľ` together with `aŠĶĘ = wŠĶĘ` for `i < j` is T1 case (i)'s witness for `a < w` ‚ÄĒ contradicting `a ‚Č• w` via T1's trichotomy clause `¬¨(a < w ‚ąß w ‚Č§ a)`. (ii) `j > #w`: ZPD's padding clause for `ŇĶ` at `#w < j ‚Č§ L_{b,w}` gives `ŇĶ‚Īľ = 0`, so `b‚Īľ = 0`; substituting into Case B's witness `a‚Īľ < b‚Īľ` yields `a‚Īľ < 0`, contradicting NAT-zero's lower bound `0 ‚Č§ a‚Īľ`. In either case the hypothesis is refuted, so `(b, w)` is not zero-padded-equal; ZPD's existence biconditional then makes `d_b = zpd(b, w)` well-defined.

By the symmetric T1 case analysis of `b > w` (`b ‚Č• w` with `d_b` now defined), `d_b ‚Č§ #b` hypothesis-free: T1 case (i) for `(b, w)` supplies `d_b ‚Č§ #b ‚ąß d_b ‚Č§ #w`; T1 case (ii) places `w` as a proper prefix of `b` with `d_b` the first `i > #w` where `bŠĶĘ ‚Č† 0`, hence `#w < d_b ‚Č§ #b`.

NAT-order's trichotomy on `(d‚āź, d_b)` partitions into three sub-cases.

*Sub-case B2: `d‚āź = d_b = d`.* TumblerSub's exported postcondition applied to `(a, w)` and `(b, w)` gives `√Ę_d > ŇĶ_d` and `bŐā_d > ŇĶ_d`, hence `√Ę_d, bŐā_d ‚Č• ŇĶ_d` by NAT-order. The preamble's hypothesis-free `d‚āź ‚Č§ #a` and `d_b ‚Č§ #b`, with `d‚āź = d_b = d`, give `d ‚Č§ #a ‚ąß d ‚Č§ #b`, letting padded projections coincide with native at `d` and earlier (`√Ę_d = a_d`, `bŐā_d = b_d`, and `√ĘŠĶĘ = aŠĶĘ`, `bŐāŠĶĘ = bŠĶĘ` for `i ‚Č§ d`). For `i < d`, both results are zero. ZPD's pre-divergence agreements give `√ĘŠĶĘ = ŇĶŠĶĘ = bŐāŠĶĘ` for `i < d`, lifting to `aŠĶĘ = bŠĶĘ`; combined with Case B's witness `a‚Īľ < b‚Īľ`, this forces `j ‚Č• d`. If `j = d`: NAT-sub's strict monotonicity at `√Ę_d < bŐā_d` (from `a_d < b_d`) with both `‚Č• ŇĶ_d` yields `√Ę_d - ŇĶ_d < bŐā_d - ŇĶ_d`, so `(a ‚äĖ w)_d < (b ‚äĖ w)_d`; with both results zero on `i < d`, T1 case (i) gives `a ‚äĖ w < b ‚äĖ w`. If `j > d`: Case B's pre-`j` agreement gives `a_d = b_d`, so the divergence-point components `√Ę_d - ŇĶ_d` and `bŐā_d - ŇĶ_d` are identical; at `d < i < j`, with `i < j ‚Č§ #a ‚ąß ‚Č§ #b`, both copy matching minuend components (`√ĘŠĶĘ = aŠĶĘ = bŠĶĘ = bŐāŠĶĘ`); at `j`, `(a ‚äĖ w)‚Īľ = √Ę‚Īľ = a‚Īľ < b‚Īľ = bŐā‚Īľ = (b ‚äĖ w)‚Īľ`. By T1 case (i), `a ‚äĖ w < b ‚äĖ w`.

*Sub-case B3: `d‚āź < d_b`.* ZPD's first-disagreement characterisation gives `√Ę_{d‚āź} ‚Č† ŇĶ_{d‚āź}`; ZPD's pre-divergence agreement for `(b, w)` at `d‚āź < d_b` gives `bŐā_{d‚āź} = ŇĶ_{d‚āź}`; chaining, `√Ę_{d‚āź} ‚Č† bŐā_{d‚āź}`. The preamble's `d‚āź ‚Č§ #a` together with `d‚āź < d_b ‚Č§ #b` (NAT-order's transitivity composing `d‚āź < d_b` with the preamble's `d_b ‚Č§ #b`) give `d‚āź ‚Č§ #a ‚ąß d‚āź ‚Č§ #b`, letting padded projections coincide with native at `d‚āź` and earlier; pre-divergence agreement for `(a, w)` chained through `ŇĶ` gives `√ĘŠĶĘ = bŐāŠĶĘ` for `i < d‚āź`, lifting to `aŠĶĘ = bŠĶĘ`, and `√Ę_{d‚āź} ‚Č† bŐā_{d‚āź}` lifts to `a_{d‚āź} ‚Č† b_{d‚āź}`. Case B's first-disagreement witness `a‚Īľ < b‚Īľ` then fixes `j = d‚āź` with `a_{d‚āź} < b_{d‚āź}`, hence `√Ę_{d‚āź} < bŐā_{d‚āź} = ŇĶ_{d‚āź}`. But TumblerSub's exported postcondition applied to `(a, w)` under `a ‚Č• w` gives `√Ę_{d‚āź} > ŇĶ_{d‚āź}` ‚ÄĒ contradicting `√Ę_{d‚āź} < ŇĶ_{d‚āź}`. This case is impossible.

*Sub-case B4: `d‚āź > d_b`.* ZPD's pre-divergence agreement for `(a, w)` at `d_b < d‚āź` gives `√Ę_{d_b} = ŇĶ_{d_b}`; ZPD's first-disagreement characterisation for `(b, w)` gives `bŐā_{d_b} ‚Č† ŇĶ_{d_b}`; chaining, `√Ę_{d_b} ‚Č† bŐā_{d_b}`. The preamble's `d_b ‚Č§ #b` together with `d_b < d‚āź ‚Č§ #a` (NAT-order's transitivity composing `d_b < d‚āź` with the preamble's `d‚āź ‚Č§ #a`) give `d_b ‚Č§ #a ‚ąß d_b ‚Č§ #b`, so padded projections coincide with native at `d_b` and earlier, giving `aŠĶĘ = bŠĶĘ` for `i < d_b` (chained through `ŇĶŠĶĘ`) and `a_{d_b} ‚Č† b_{d_b}`. Case B's first-disagreement witness `a‚Īľ < b‚Īľ` fixes `j = d_b` with `a_{d_b} < b_{d_b}`. TumblerSub's exported postcondition applied to `(b, w)` under `b ‚Č• w` gives `bŐā_{d_b} > ŇĶ_{d_b}`, so `(b ‚äĖ w)_{d_b} = bŐā_{d_b} - ŇĶ_{d_b} > 0` by NAT-sub's strict positivity. Then `(a ‚äĖ w)_{d_b} = 0` since `d_b < d‚āź` (Definition's `rŠĶĘ = 0` for `i < k`), and at `i < d_b`, both results are zero. By T1 case (i), `a ‚äĖ w < b ‚äĖ w`.

In every case, `a ‚äĖ w ‚Č§ b ‚äĖ w`. ‚ąé
