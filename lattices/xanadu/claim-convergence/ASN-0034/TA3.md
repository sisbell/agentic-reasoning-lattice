**TA3 (OrderPreservationUnderSubtractionWeak).** `(A a, b, w : a < b ‚àß a ‚â• w ‚àß b ‚â• w : a ‚äñ w ‚â§ b ‚äñ w)`.

*Proof.* We show that for all `a, b, w ‚àà T` with `a < b`, `a ‚â• w`, `b ‚â• w`, we have `a ‚äñ w ‚â§ b ‚äñ w`.

By TA2, `a ‚äñ w` and `b ‚äñ w` are well-formed tumblers in `T`. Recall TumblerSub: given `x ‚â• w`, zero-pad both operands to `L_{x,w}` (the longer of `#x`, `#w`, dispatched by NAT-order's trichotomy on `(#x, #w)`) and scan for the first disagreement. If none exists, `x ‚äñ w` is the zero tumbler of length `L_{x,w}`. Otherwise, let `d` be the first divergence; then `(x ‚äñ w)·µ¢ = 0` for `i < d`, `(x ‚äñ w)_d = x_d - w_d`, and `(x ‚äñ w)·µ¢ = x·µ¢` for `i > d`, with result length `L_{x,w}`.

Since `a < b`, T1 provides two cases: (i) there exists a first position `j` with `j ‚â§ #a ‚àß j ‚â§ #b` where `a‚±º < b‚±º`, or (ii) `a` is a proper prefix of `b`.

**Case A: `a` is a proper prefix of `b`** (T1 case (ii)). Then `#a < #b` and `a·µ¢ = b·µ¢` for all `i ‚â§ #a`.

*Sub-case A1: `a = w`.* NAT-order's trichotomy on `(#a, #w)` gives `L_{a,w} = #a`; `a ‚äñ w` is the zero tumbler of that length. Since `a` is a proper prefix of `b` and `a = w`, `b·µ¢ = w·µ¢` for all `i ‚â§ #w = #a`. If some `b·µ¢` with `i > #w` is nonzero, `(b, w)` diverges beyond `#w`, making `b ‚äñ w` positive; by TA-PosDom, `a ‚äñ w < b ‚äñ w`. Otherwise, the zero-padded sequences of `b` and `w` agree everywhere, and under `#w = #a < #b` NAT-order places `(#b, #w)` in sub-case (Œ≥), giving `L_{b,w} = #b`; `b ‚äñ w` is the zero tumbler of length `#b`. Both results are zero, agreeing on positions `1, ..., #a`; the strict length inequality `#a < #b` is converted by NAT-discrete into `#a + 1 ‚â§ #b` (with `#a + 1 ‚àà ‚Ñï` by NAT-closure), supplying T1 case (ii)'s witness. Thus `a ‚äñ w < b ‚äñ w` by T1 case (ii).

*Sub-case A2: `a > w` with divergence.* Let `d‚Çê` be the first position where the zero-padded sequences of `a` and `w` disagree. We claim `d‚Çê ‚â§ #a`: if `a > w` by T1 case (i), it supplies `d‚Çê ‚â§ #a ‚àß d‚Çê ‚â§ #w`; if by T1 case (ii), `w` is a proper prefix of `a` and `d‚Çê` is the first `i > #w` with `a·µ¢ > 0`, so `d‚Çê ‚â§ #a`. Since `b·µ¢ = a·µ¢` for all `i ‚â§ #a` and `d‚Çê ‚â§ #a`, `d_b = d‚Çê = d`.

At positions `i < d`: both results are zero. At position `d`: both compute `a_d - w_d = b_d - w_d`, each in ‚Ñï by NAT-sub's conditional closure under `a_d, b_d ‚â• w_d` (from the divergence-point inequalities via NAT-order), and equal because `a_d = b_d`. At positions `d < i ‚â§ #a`: both copy their minuends, giving `a·µ¢ = b·µ¢`. The results agree on `1, ..., #a`.

Denote result lengths `L_{a,w}`, `L_{b,w}`. We establish `L_{a,w} ‚â§ L_{b,w}` by enumerating NAT-order's trichotomy on `(#a, #w)`. In (Œ±) or (Œ≥), `L_{a,w} = #a`; with `#a < #b`, `(#b, #w)` is in (Œ≥), giving `L_{b,w} = #b > #a`. In (Œ≤) `#a < #w`, `L_{a,w} = #w`; NAT-order on `(#b, #w)` gives `L_{b,w} ‚àà {#b, #w}` with `L_{b,w} ‚â• #w = L_{a,w}`.

At positions `#a < i ‚â§ L_{a,w}` (only in sub-case (Œ≤)): `(a ‚äñ w)·µ¢ = 0` from `a`'s zero-padding, while `(b ‚äñ w)·µ¢ = b·µ¢` or `0` ‚Äî in either case `(a ‚äñ w)·µ¢ ‚â§ (b ‚äñ w)·µ¢`.

If a first disagreement exists at `p ‚àà 1, ..., L_{a,w}`, then `p > #a` and `(a ‚äñ w)_p = 0`, `(b ‚äñ w)_p ‚âÝ 0`. By NAT-zero's lower bound and NAT-order's defining clause, `(b ‚äñ w)_p > 0`. Position `p` satisfies `p ‚â§ L_{a,w} ‚àß p ‚â§ L_{b,w}`, so T1 case (i) yields `a ‚äñ w < b ‚äñ w`. If no disagreement exists, NAT-order's trichotomy at `(L_{a,w}, L_{b,w})` with `L_{a,w} ‚â§ L_{b,w}` fixes either `L_{a,w} < L_{b,w}` or `L_{a,w} = L_{b,w}`. In the strict case, NAT-discrete gives `L_{a,w} + 1 ‚â§ L_{b,w}` (with closure by NAT-closure), so `a ‚äñ w` is a proper prefix of `b ‚äñ w` by T1 case (ii). In the equal case, T3 yields `a ‚äñ w = b ‚äñ w`.

*Sub-case A3: `a > w` without divergence (zero-padded equality).* The only possibility is T1 case (ii): `w` is a proper prefix of `a` with `a·µ¢ = 0` for all `i > #w`. NAT-order on `(#a, #w)` is sub-case (Œ≥), giving `L_{a,w} = #a`; `a ‚äñ w` is the zero tumbler of that length. Since `#w < #a < #b`, `b` agrees with `w` on `1, ..., #a`, and NAT-order on `(#b, #w)` gives `L_{b,w} = #b`. If `b ‚äñ w` has a positive component, `a ‚äñ w < b ‚äñ w` by TA-PosDom. If `b ‚äñ w` is also a zero tumbler, both results are zero; NAT-discrete converts `#a < #b` into `#a + 1 ‚â§ #b`, supplying T1 case (ii)'s witness. Thus `a ‚äñ w < b ‚äñ w`.

**Case B: Component divergence at `j`** (T1 case (i)). There exists a first position `j` with `j ‚â§ #a ‚àß j ‚â§ #b`, `a‚±º < b‚±º`, and `a·µ¢ = b·µ¢` for all `i < j`.

*Sub-case B1: `a` is zero-padded-equal to `w`.* Then `a ‚äñ w` is the zero tumbler of length `L_{a,w}`. At position `j`, `w‚±º = a‚±º`, so `b‚±º > w‚±º`. The pair `(b, w)` diverges at or before `j`, making `b ‚äñ w` positive. By TA-PosDom, `a ‚äñ w < b ‚äñ w`.

For the remaining sub-cases, `d‚Çê = zpd(a, w)` is well-defined, so TumblerSub's exported postcondition applied to `(a, w)` under `a ‚â• w` gives `√¢_{d‚Çê} > ≈µ_{d‚Çê}` (on ZPD's padded projections, since `d‚Çê` may exceed `#w` in the prefix-divergence sub-case). The zero-padded divergence `d_b = zpd(b, w)` is also well-defined: were `b` zero-padded-equal to `w`, ZPD's padded equality would give `bÃÇ_{d‚Çê} = ≈µ_{d‚Çê}`, hence `√¢_{d‚Çê} > bÃÇ_{d‚Çê}`; chaining ZPD's pre-divergence agreement `√¢·µ¢ = ≈µ·µ¢` for `i < d‚Çê` with the hypothesised `bÃÇ·µ¢ = ≈µ·µ¢` gives `√¢·µ¢ = bÃÇ·µ¢` for `i < d‚Çê`. Case B's first-disagreement witness `a‚±º < b‚±º` (with `j ‚â§ #a ‚àß j ‚â§ #b`) forces `d‚Çê ‚â§ j`: were `d‚Çê > j`, padded projections at `j ‚â§ #a ‚àß ‚â§ #b` would coincide with native (`√¢‚±º = a‚±º`, `bÃÇ‚±º = b‚±º`), and `√¢‚±º = bÃÇ‚±º` from `j < d‚Çê` would give `a‚±º = b‚±º`, contradicting `a‚±º < b‚±º`. Hence `d‚Çê ‚â§ j`, giving `d‚Çê ‚â§ #a ‚àß d‚Çê ‚â§ #b`; padded coincides with native at `d‚Çê` and earlier (`√¢_{d‚Çê} = a_{d‚Çê}`, `bÃÇ_{d‚Çê} = b_{d‚Çê}`, and `a·µ¢ = b·µ¢` for `i < d‚Çê`), making `d‚Çê` a T1 case (i) witness for `a > b` ‚Äî contradicting `a < b`.

NAT-order's trichotomy on `(d‚Çê, d_b)` partitions into three sub-cases.

*Sub-case B2: `d‚Çê = d_b = d`.* TumblerSub's exported postcondition applied to `(a, w)` and `(b, w)` gives `√¢_d > ≈µ_d` and `bÃÇ_d > ≈µ_d`, hence `√¢_d, bÃÇ_d ‚â• ≈µ_d` by NAT-order. The preamble's `d ‚â§ #a ‚àß d ‚â§ #b` lets padded projections coincide with native at `d` and earlier (`√¢_d = a_d`, `bÃÇ_d = b_d`, and `√¢·µ¢ = a·µ¢`, `bÃÇ·µ¢ = b·µ¢` for `i ‚â§ d`). For `i < d`, both results are zero. ZPD's pre-divergence agreements give `√¢·µ¢ = ≈µ·µ¢ = bÃÇ·µ¢` for `i < d`, lifting to `a·µ¢ = b·µ¢`; combined with Case B's witness `a‚±º < b‚±º`, this forces `j ‚â• d`. If `j = d`: NAT-sub's strict monotonicity at `√¢_d < bÃÇ_d` (from `a_d < b_d`) with both `‚â• ≈µ_d` yields `√¢_d - ≈µ_d < bÃÇ_d - ≈µ_d`, so `(a ‚äñ w)_d < (b ‚äñ w)_d`; with both results zero on `i < d`, T1 case (i) gives `a ‚äñ w < b ‚äñ w`. If `j > d`: Case B's pre-`j` agreement gives `a_d = b_d`, so the divergence-point components `√¢_d - ≈µ_d` and `bÃÇ_d - ≈µ_d` are identical; at `d < i < j`, with `i < j ‚â§ #a ‚àß ‚â§ #b`, both copy matching minuend components (`√¢·µ¢ = a·µ¢ = b·µ¢ = bÃÇ·µ¢`); at `j`, `(a ‚äñ w)‚±º = √¢‚±º = a‚±º < b‚±º = bÃÇ‚±º = (b ‚äñ w)‚±º`. By T1 case (i), `a ‚äñ w < b ‚äñ w`.

*Sub-case B3: `d‚Çê < d_b`.* ZPD's first-disagreement characterisation gives `√¢_{d‚Çê} ‚âÝ ≈µ_{d‚Çê}`; ZPD's pre-divergence agreement for `(b, w)` at `d‚Çê < d_b` gives `bÃÇ_{d‚Çê} = ≈µ_{d‚Çê}`; chaining, `√¢_{d‚Çê} ‚âÝ bÃÇ_{d‚Çê}`. The preamble's `d‚Çê ‚â§ #a ‚àß d‚Çê ‚â§ #b` lets padded projections coincide with native at `d‚Çê` and earlier; pre-divergence agreement for `(a, w)` chained through `≈µ` gives `√¢·µ¢ = bÃÇ·µ¢` for `i < d‚Çê`, lifting to `a·µ¢ = b·µ¢`, and `√¢_{d‚Çê} ‚âÝ bÃÇ_{d‚Çê}` lifts to `a_{d‚Çê} ‚âÝ b_{d‚Çê}`. Case B's first-disagreement witness `a‚±º < b‚±º` then fixes `j = d‚Çê` with `a_{d‚Çê} < b_{d‚Çê}`, hence `√¢_{d‚Çê} < bÃÇ_{d‚Çê} = ≈µ_{d‚Çê}`. But TumblerSub's exported postcondition applied to `(a, w)` under `a ‚â• w` gives `√¢_{d‚Çê} > ≈µ_{d‚Çê}` ‚Äî contradicting `√¢_{d‚Çê} < ≈µ_{d‚Çê}`. This case is impossible.

*Sub-case B4: `d‚Çê > d_b`.* ZPD's pre-divergence agreement for `(a, w)` at `d_b < d‚Çê` gives `√¢_{d_b} = ≈µ_{d_b}`; ZPD's first-disagreement characterisation for `(b, w)` gives `bÃÇ_{d_b} ‚âÝ ≈µ_{d_b}`; chaining, `√¢_{d_b} ‚âÝ bÃÇ_{d_b}`. With `d_b < d‚Çê ‚â§ #a ‚àß ‚â§ #b` (preamble), padded projections coincide with native at `d_b` and earlier, so `a·µ¢ = b·µ¢` for `i < d_b` (chained through `≈µ·µ¢`) and `a_{d_b} ‚âÝ b_{d_b}`. Case B's first-disagreement witness `a‚±º < b‚±º` fixes `j = d_b` with `a_{d_b} < b_{d_b}`. TumblerSub's exported postcondition applied to `(b, w)` under `b ‚â• w` gives `bÃÇ_{d_b} > ≈µ_{d_b}`, so `(b ‚äñ w)_{d_b} = bÃÇ_{d_b} - ≈µ_{d_b} > 0` by NAT-sub's strict positivity. Then `(a ‚äñ w)_{d_b} = 0` since `d_b < d‚Çê` (Definition's `r·µ¢ = 0` for `i < k`), and at `i < d_b`, both results are zero. By T1 case (i), `a ‚äñ w < b ‚äñ w`.

In every case, `a ‚äñ w ‚â§ b ‚äñ w`. ‚àé
