# Cone Review — ASN-0034/TA-strict (cycle 1)

*2026-04-18 00:01*

### TA-strict duplicates TumblerAdd's `a ⊕ w > a` postcondition but with weaker citation discipline
**Foundation**: TumblerAdd (this ASN) — Postconditions: "a ⊕ w ∈ T, #(a ⊕ w) = #w, **a ⊕ w > a (T1)**, a ⊕ w ≥ w (T1, T3)"
**ASN**: TA-strict (StrictIncrease) — "`(A a ∈ T, Pos(w) : a ⊕ w > a)`"
**Issue**: The exact claim `a ⊕ w > a` is already established as a postcondition of TumblerAdd — the paragraph labelled "*ordering guarantee*" inside TumblerAdd discharges it with explicit per-step NAT-* sourcing (NAT-addcompat left order-compatibility + strict successor inequality, then NAT-order composition of `≥`/`>` into strict). TA-strict reproves the same fact with a different, looser argument: "Therefore `rₖ = aₖ + wₖ > aₖ`, since adding a positive natural number to a non-negative one yields a strictly larger result" — no axiom cited for the `≥ → >` promotion. Correspondingly, TA-strict's *Depends* clause cites only TA-Pos, ActionPoint, TA0, TumblerAdd, T1 — it omits NAT-addcompat, NAT-order, NAT-closure, and NAT-zero, all of which TumblerAdd explicitly invokes at the identical strict-advancement step. A reviser tightening the NAT-* chain in TumblerAdd's ordering-guarantee paragraph has no Depends-backed visibility into TA-strict; conversely, a reviser auditing TA-strict sees a hand-waved arithmetic step that every sister property of this ASN discharges mechanically.
**What needs resolving**: TA-strict must either (a) delegate to TumblerAdd's postcondition `a ⊕ w > a` in a one-line proof and cite TumblerAdd as the sole arithmetic source (making the Depends omission correct by construction), or (b) maintain its separate proof and source the strict-through-addition step at TumblerAdd's granularity, adding NAT-addcompat, NAT-order, and NAT-zero to Depends. The ASN must also clarify why TA-strict exists as a distinct property if TumblerAdd already exports the same claim — the motivational paragraph ("Without TA-strict, the axioms admit a degenerate model…") reads as if TA-strict is an independent axiom ruling out a no-op model, but in the current construction TumblerAdd's definition is itself non-degenerate and TA-strict is a theorem, not an axiom.

### TA-strict's stated quantifier omits `w ∈ T` and the well-definedness precondition
**Foundation**: TA0 (this ASN) — preconditions `a ∈ T, w ∈ T, Pos(w), actionPoint(w) ≤ #a`
**ASN**: TA-strict — statement `(A a ∈ T, Pos(w) : a ⊕ w > a)` with parenthetical "(where `a ⊕ w` is well-defined, i.e., `k ≤ #a` per TA0)"; formal contract Preconditions list separately enumerates `a ∈ T, w ∈ T, Pos(w), k ≤ #a`.
**Issue**: The property statement's universal binds only `a ∈ T` and `Pos(w)`; `w ∈ T` and `actionPoint(w) ≤ #a` are relegated to a parenthetical. The formal contract's Preconditions list then adds them back. For a property whose whole purpose is to rule out a degenerate model, the statement of what is being asserted should match the preconditions under which it is asserted, without the reader having to reconcile a quantifier with a parenthetical remark. Compare TumblerAdd, which states its preconditions uniformly in the Formal Contract and lets the property body reference them directly.
**What needs resolving**: Align the stated universal in TA-strict with its formal-contract preconditions — either `(A a, w ∈ T : Pos(w) ∧ actionPoint(w) ≤ #a : a ⊕ w > a)` or an explicit statement that the preconditions are those of TA0, not a parenthetical aside.
