### T3 referenced under two different names across the ASN
**Foundation**: The authoritative name for T3 as defined in the Foundation section is "T3 (CanonicalRepresentation)." This matches the Justification paragraph of T10a, which writes "T3 (CanonicalRepresentation)," and T10a.5's Depends, which cites "T3 (divergence of distinct tumblers)" against that same property.
**ASN**: T10a.3's Formal Contract Depends clause: "T3 (TumblerDistinguishability) — tumblers of different lengths are distinct — excludes collisions between parent siblings and child outputs and between outputs at different nesting depths along any lineage."
**Issue**: T10a.3 is the only postcondition in the ASN that renames T3 — everywhere else (T10a Justification, T10a.5 body and Depends, T10a.3's own proof body "By T3, tumblers that differ in length are distinct") T3 is either unqualified or attached to the canonical label CanonicalRepresentation. A reader cross-checking dependencies by name — or a dependency extractor consuming the Depends list — sees "TumblerDistinguishability" and cannot match it to the CanonicalRepresentation foundation without extra-textual judgment. One symbol must carry one name throughout.
**What needs resolving**: T10a.3's Depends must reference T3 under the same name used by the foundation statement and by the rest of the ASN (CanonicalRepresentation), so every citation of T3 in this document resolves to a single lexical identifier.

### T10a.5 inductive step asserts "strictly positive value" under iterated +1 without citing NAT-arithmetic
**Foundation**: The ASN's per-step convention, established in TA5's Depends and reinforced in Previous Findings about T10a.8, is that "the `+1` step keeps it positive" / "non-zero ⇒ strictly positive on ℕ" / "`n < n + 1`" inferences are discharged explicitly from NAT-zero + NAT-discrete and/or NAT-addcompat, "rather than leaving them as implicit appeals to ℕ-arithmetic."
**ASN**: T10a.5 Consequence 5, inductive step, case `j = #bₓ`: "successive elements of `dom(Cₓ)` increment position j (since `sig(bₓ) = #bₓ = j`), producing values `1, 2, 3, …` starting from `bₓ[j] = 1` (TA5(c)). Every element of `dom(Cₓ)` therefore carries a strictly positive value at position j." T10a.5's Depends lists T10a, T10a.1, T10a.3, T10a.4, T4, TA5, TA5-SigValid, T3, and Prefix — no NAT-zero, NAT-discrete, or NAT-addcompat.
**Issue**: The claim that the sequence `1, 2, 3, …` produced by iterated `+1` on ℕ stays strictly positive is exactly the "+1 preserves strict positivity" inference that Previous Finding 4 flagged in T10a.8, and that TA5's Depends handles by citing NAT-addcompat. T10a.5's proof needs the same ℕ-arithmetic step to carry `bₓ[j] = 1` forward through all descendants of Cₓ — TA5(c) delivers the `+1` advance at the sig position but does not, on its own, establish `1 + n ≠ 0` or `1 + n > 0` for arbitrary `n ∈ ℕ`. The step is load-bearing for the divergence argument's closure (contrasted against `bᵧ[j] = 0` on the other side), and the Depends list omits every lemma the document's own convention names for it.
**What needs resolving**: T10a.5's proof and Depends must cite the NAT-arithmetic authority (matching the T0 + NAT-zero + NAT-discrete + NAT-addcompat per-step convention TA5 applies and Previous Findings 1/4 flag for T10a.8) at the step sharpening the iterated `+1` sequence from "sequence of ℕ-values" to "strictly positive at every step."
