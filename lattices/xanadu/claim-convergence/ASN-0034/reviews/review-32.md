### TumblerSub produces displacements that violate TumblerAdd's precondition
**Foundation**: TumblerAdd (TumblerAdd) — precondition `actionPoint(w) ≤ #a`
**ASN**: TumblerSub (TumblerSub) — "The primary downstream use is computing displacements between positions — given positions a and b, the subtraction b ⊖ a yields the displacement from a to b (see D0)."
**Issue**: When `a` is a proper sequence-prefix of `b` (which includes same-level addresses where one extends the other, e.g. user address `[1,0,3]` and sub-account `[1,0,3,5]`), the displacement `d = b ⊖ a` has `actionPoint(d) = zpd(b,a) ≥ #a + 1 > #a`. This violates TumblerAdd's precondition `actionPoint(w) ≤ #a` for the roundtrip `a ⊕ d`. Concretely: `b = [1,0,3,5]`, `a = [1,0,3]`. Zero-pad `a` to `[1,0,3,0]`. zpd at position 4 (`5 ≠ 0`). `d = [0,0,0,5]`. `actionPoint(d) = 4 > 3 = #a`. TumblerAdd cannot consume this displacement with start position `a`. The formal contracts of each operation are individually sound, but TumblerSub's motivating description asserts that it "yields the displacement from a to b" without noting that the result may be incompatible with TumblerAdd when the subtrahend is a prefix of the minuend. The parenthetical "(see D0)" defers to an unstated property, but no constraint is mentioned that would resolve the incompatibility.
**What needs resolving**: Either TumblerSub's motivating description must qualify which operand pairs produce TumblerAdd-compatible displacements (specifically: `actionPoint(b ⊖ a) ≤ #a`, equivalently `zpd(b,a) ≤ #a`, equivalently `a` is not a proper sequence-prefix of `b`), or the ASN must acknowledge that "displacement" as produced by TumblerSub is a broader concept than "displacement consumable by TumblerAdd," and that the roundtrip relationship holds only under additional constraints to be established by D0.

### T8 proof claims exhaustiveness from NoDeallocation but requires an unstated allocation-completeness axiom
**Foundation**: NoDeallocation (NoDeallocation) — "The system's operation vocabulary contains no operation that removes an element from the allocated set."
**ASN**: T8 (AllocationPermanence) — "These three cases are exhaustive... The exhaustiveness of this case analysis rests on the NoDeallocation axiom: the system's operation vocabulary admits no removal operation, so every state transition falls into one of the three classes above."
**Issue**: The proof's case analysis lists three operation classes: (1) read-only (T1, T2, T4), (2) pure arithmetic (⊕, ⊖), (3) allocation via T10a. It then claims exhaustiveness "rests on the NoDeallocation axiom." But NoDeallocation rules out removal — it says nothing about whether T10a's two mechanisms (sibling and child-spawning) are the *only* operations that insert into the allocated set. A hypothetical non-T10a insertion operation would not violate NoDeallocation yet would fall outside all three cases. The proof silently assumes "the only operations that add to the allocated set are those governed by T10a," an assumption not provided by any stated axiom. The monotonicity *conclusion* is nonetheless valid via a simpler argument: any transition either preserves the allocated set (inclusion trivially) or modifies it; NoDeallocation rules out removal; therefore any modification can only be addition, giving `allocated(s) ⊆ allocated(s')`. This two-step argument needs only NoDeallocation and requires no enumeration of operations. The gap is in the proof structure (the case analysis claims more than its premises warrant), not in the theorem.
**What needs resolving**: Either (a) add an explicit axiom that T10a characterizes all allocation operations (making the case analysis genuinely exhaustive), or (b) simplify the proof to derive monotonicity from NoDeallocation alone without the operation-enumerating case analysis, or (c) weaken the exhaustiveness claim to acknowledge that the case analysis covers all *currently defined* operations while the monotonicity conclusion holds for *any* future operation by the NoDeallocation argument.

### T10 (PartitionIndependence) is load-bearing throughout T10a but absent from the ASN
**Foundation**: T10 (PartitionIndependence) — referenced but not defined anywhere in the presented ASN content
**ASN**: T10a (AllocatorDiscipline) — justification section: "T10 (PartitionIndependence), whose precondition — non-nesting prefixes — is the guarantee that T10a's discipline is designed to establish"; T10a.2: "satisfying the precondition of T10"; T10a-N: "This violates the T10 precondition. The axiom is therefore both sufficient... and necessary for prefix-incomparability of sibling outputs."
**Issue**: T10a's justification explicitly lists T10 among its six foundations. T10a.2 asserts it satisfies T10's precondition. T10a-N proves the discipline is necessary by showing relaxation violates T10's precondition. Yet T10's formal statement — what it assumes, what it guarantees, what "PartitionIndependence" means precisely — is not present. We know informally that T10 has a "non-nesting prefixes" precondition, but: (1) we cannot verify T10a.2 actually satisfies T10's precondition as formally stated, since T10 is not formally stated; (2) we cannot verify T10a-N's claim that the relaxed rule "violates the T10 precondition" without knowing what that precondition requires; (3) we cannot assess whether T10a's consequences are sufficient for T10's needs or merely necessary. T10a is constructed entirely in service of T10, yet T10 is a hole in the dependency graph.
**What needs resolving**: T10 must be formally stated in the ASN with its preconditions, postconditions, and formal contract, so that T10a.2's sufficiency claim, T10a-N's necessity claim, and the six-foundation justification can all be verified against T10's actual requirements.
