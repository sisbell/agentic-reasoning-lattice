### Unaxiomatized `min(#a, #b)` operator in TA1-strict preconditions
**Foundation**: Divergence's definition and Depends list explicitly avoid `min` — "This length-pair invocation replaces any implicit reliance on a primitive binary-minimum operator on ℕ — the shorter of `#a` and `#b` is named by the selected sub-case rather than by an un-axiomatized `min(#a, #b)`, matching the convention that every operator in the Definition must be discharged by a Depends entry." The NAT-* enumeration (NAT-order, NAT-wellorder, NAT-closure, NAT-addcompat, NAT-cancel, NAT-sub, NAT-discrete, NAT-zero) contains no `min` operator.
**ASN**: TA1-strict's Formal Contract: *Preconditions:* `actionPoint(w) ≤ min(#a, #b) ∧ actionPoint(w) ≥ divergence(a, b)`, and the proof's opening: "The preconditions give `k ≥ j` and `k ≤ min(#a, #b)`. From these bounds, `j ≤ min(#a, #b)`, which rules out Divergence case (ii) — prefix divergence requires `j = min(#a, #b) + 1`". TumblerAdd's strict-advancement paragraph also uses `min`: "so `k ≤ min(#a, #(a ⊕ w))`".
**Issue**: The convention Divergence establishes — that every operator in a Definition must be discharged by a Depends entry, with the shorter-of-two-lengths selected via NAT-order's trichotomy sub-cases rather than a primitive `min` — is violated in TA1-strict (where `min` appears in the formal precondition, not merely as narrative shorthand) and in TumblerAdd (where it appears in the proof aside). TA1-strict's Depends cites no axiom for `min`, and no NAT-min axiom exists. A downstream formalizer cannot discharge the precondition without either axiomatizing `min` or rewriting it.
**What needs resolving**: Either (a) rephrase TA1-strict's precondition and proof (and TumblerAdd's aside) using the conjunction `actionPoint(w) ≤ #a ∧ actionPoint(w) ≤ #b` and a NAT-order trichotomy sub-case split where the shorter length is required — the same pattern Divergence case (ii) uses; or (b) add an explicit NAT-min axiom (or derive `min` as a defined symbol) and update Divergence's and TumblerAdd's min-avoidance prose to reflect the new policy. Whichever route is taken, Divergence, TumblerAdd, and TA1-strict must treat `min` consistently.
