# Cone Review — ASN-0034/T1 (cycle 1)

*2026-04-17 09:09*

### NAT-addcompat's right-compatibility clause cannot deliver strict promotion as its own prose claims
**Foundation**: NAT-addcompat (NatAdditionOrderCompatibility)
**ASN**: NAT-addcompat's motivating paragraph: *"TA1's sub-case `j = k` adds the fixed increment `wₖ` on the right of the variable summands `aₖ`, `bₖ` to promote `aₖ < bₖ` into `aₖ + wₖ < bₖ + wₖ`."* The axiom itself states only: *"`(A m, n, p ∈ ℕ : n ≥ p : n + m ≥ p + m)` (right order compatibility)."*
**Issue**: The motivating prose offers TA1's strict-promotion step as the *reason* NAT-addcompat must include right compatibility as a separate clause, but the clause as formalized is stated only for non-strict `≥`. Non-strict right compatibility gives `aₖ ≤ bₖ ⟹ aₖ + wₖ ≤ bₖ + wₖ`; it does not give `aₖ < bₖ ⟹ aₖ + wₖ < bₖ + wₖ`. Lifting from the non-strict conclusion to strict requires an additional appeal to NAT-cancel's right-cancellation clause (via its contrapositive: `aₖ ≠ bₖ ⟹ aₖ + wₖ ≠ bₖ + wₖ`). As stated, the axiom's prose advertises a capability — strict promotion on either side via NAT-addcompat alone — that the axiom does not deliver without a companion citation. The symmetric concern applies to the left-compatibility clause if TumblerAdd, TA5, T10a-N, or T0(a) also promote strict inequalities through addition. If downstream proofs follow the prose and cite only NAT-addcompat at their strict-promotion sites, those proofs will have missing dependencies on NAT-cancel.
**What needs resolving**: Either extend NAT-addcompat with explicit strict-compatibility clauses on both sides so that the motivating prose matches the axiom, or correct the prose to describe the non-strict step only and require downstream strict-promotion sites (TA1 on the right; TumblerAdd/TA5/T10a-N/T0(a) on the left if strict) to cite NAT-cancel alongside NAT-addcompat in their Depends lists. The audit must cover every downstream proof whose Depends currently attributes a strict-inequality-through-addition step to NAT-addcompat alone.
