# Cone Review — ASN-0034/GlobalUniqueness (cycle 4)

### Several T0-family and NAT-* axioms cited as load-bearing are never stated in the ASN
**Foundation**: Self-review (foundation ASN); no external foundations declared ("this is a foundation ASN; review internal consistency only").
**ASN**: T0 (CarrierSetDefinition) is cited dozens of times — e.g. T1's Depends ("the definition uses length `#a` and component projection `aₖ`… which T0 introduces"), T4a's opening ("T0 fixes the carrier as ℕ"), T10a's Consequence 8, GlobalUniqueness Case 5, AllocatedSet ("T0, T0(a), T0(b) size the ambient tumbler space"), and TA5's `t' ∈ T` conclusion. NAT-zero, NAT-discrete, NAT-wellorder, NAT-sub, and NAT-closure are likewise cited per-step throughout: NAT-zero/NAT-discrete in nearly every "non-zero ⇒ strictly positive on ℕ" inference (T4a opening, TA5-SigValid, TA5 Case `k>0`, T10a.5, T10a.8, T10a.3, T10a-N), NAT-wellorder in T1's Trichotomy least-element construction and in T10's `min{j : …}`, NAT-sub in T10a.3's local-monotonicity subtraction-form chain, NAT-closure in TA5 and T10a.8's `+1` membership step. NAT-addassoc's body even names this list explicitly: "T0 lists `NAT-addassoc` alongside the eight other NAT-* axioms — NAT-closure, NAT-order, NAT-discrete, NAT-addcompat, NAT-wellorder, NAT-zero, NAT-sub, NAT-cancel — and declares the combined list exhaustive."
**Issue**: Of those nine NAT-* axioms, only four (NAT-addcompat, NAT-cancel, NAT-order, NAT-addassoc) are stated in the ASN. T0 itself, T0(a), T0(b), NAT-closure, NAT-discrete, NAT-wellorder, NAT-zero, and NAT-sub have no statement in the content. The ASN's stated convention is per-step citation: every load-bearing ℕ-arithmetic step is supposed to terminate at a stated axiom rather than appeal to background arithmetic. With the T0/NAT-* family absent, every "T0 fixes ℕ", every "NAT-zero supplies `0 ≤ n`", every "NAT-discrete rules out `0 ≤ n < 1`", every "NAT-wellorder licenses `min{…}`", every "NAT-sub places `m − n ∈ ℕ`", and every "NAT-closure places `n + 1 ∈ ℕ`" reaches for a proposition the reader cannot consult. This is structurally parallel to PF2 (T8/NoDeallocation) and PF7 (TA5a/TA5-SIG) but covers a distinct family with much wider downstream consumption.
**What needs resolving**: Either state T0 and the remaining five NAT-* axioms inline as part of this ASN (consistent with NAT-addcompat/NAT-cancel/NAT-order/NAT-addassoc being stated here and with the "review internal consistency only" header), or declare them as external foundations and record the dependency edges in the ASN metadata so the per-step citations terminate at a stated source.

### T4b and T4c are promised as standalone first-class theorems but their statements are absent
**Foundation**: Self-review (foundation ASN).
**ASN**: T4's body: "Each of the three postconditions below is stated and proved as a separate first-class numbered theorem in this ASN — T4a in `T4a.md`, T4b in `T4b.md`, T4c in `T4c.md`, each with its own formal contract." T4b is consumed in T4a's framing ("once T4c (LevelDetermination) classifies T4-valid tumblers into the four levels"), in T4's Preconditions block as a precondition consumer of T3 and T4a, and downstream the body promises that "T7's Depends list" cites T4a and T4b. T4c is consumed in T4's Postconditions ("T4c (LevelDetermination): … `(A t ∈ T : t is T4-valid :: (zeros(t) = 0 ↔ t is a node address) ∧ …)`") and in T4a's framing reference.
**Issue**: Of T4a/T4b/T4c, only T4a is stated in the ASN content. T4b's `fields(t)` partial function — whose Definition clause is supposed to fix domain, codomain, and action in T4b's own Formal Contract — is referenced in T4's Postconditions and consumed by the framing of T4a, but the actual theorem and contract are absent. T4c's four definitional biconditionals are stated as a *postcondition of T4* but the standalone theorem T4c (with its own proof of bijectivity onto `{node, user, document, element}`) is not present. This means consumers downstream of T4 — including the ASN's own GlobalUniqueness Case 5 (which routes through T4a) and the framing claims that T7 cites T4a and T4b — rely on theorem objects that have no statement in scope. Distinct from PF7 (TA5a, TA5-SIG): those concern the increment family; T4b/T4c concern the parsing family.
**What needs resolving**: State T4b and T4c inline (consistent with the "stated and proved as a separate first-class numbered theorem in this ASN" claim) or change T4's body and the downstream framing to stop describing them as in-scope theorems with their own contracts.

### AllocatedSet's State definition references TA7a/Vocabulary, which is not in this ASN
**Foundation**: Self-review (AllocatedSet definition block).
**ASN**: AllocatedSet, *Definitions → State*: "s ∈ 𝒮 is a configuration of the allocator tree — the set of activated allocators and, for each, the count nₛ(A) of sibling increments performed. Here 𝒮 denotes the state space of the allocation system, distinct from the symbol S used in TA7a/Vocabulary for positive-component ordinals."
**Issue**: AllocatedSet's State definition contains a disambiguation note tying its own symbol `𝒮` against another symbol `S` "used in TA7a/Vocabulary for positive-component ordinals." Neither TA7a nor Vocabulary appears anywhere in the ASN content — they're not stated, not cited in any Depends list, and not mentioned in any other property's body. The disambiguation parenthesis presumes the reader has TA7a/Vocabulary in scope to disambiguate against, but those notations are simply absent here. The header declares no external foundations either, so a reader cannot tell whether TA7a/Vocabulary is an external dependency, an in-ASN sibling that has been deleted, or a leftover reference from a prior draft.
**What needs resolving**: Either remove the disambiguation note (no `S`-vs-`𝒮` collision exists within the present ASN's vocabulary), or state TA7a/Vocabulary in the ASN, or declare TA7a/Vocabulary as an external foundation and record the edge in the metadata so the parenthetical refers to a defined source.
