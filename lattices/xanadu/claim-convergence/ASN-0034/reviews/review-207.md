### TA-assoc: Pos(b ⊕ c) not explicitly discharged for right-side well-definedness
**Foundation**: TumblerAdd, TA0 (sister properties in this ASN; both require `Pos(w)` as a precondition on the displacement)
**ASN**: TA-assoc (AdditionAssociative). Preconditions list `Pos(b)`, `Pos(c)`; the proof *Action point of `s`* paragraph writes "We must determine `actionPoint(s)` to expand the right side `a ⊕ s`" and concludes "`actionPoint(s) = min(k_b, k_c)`". The formal contract's postconditions include `actionPoint(b ⊕ c) = min(k_b, k_c)`.
**Issue**: The right-side operation `a ⊕ (b ⊕ c)` (and the inner `b ⊕ c`) can be formed only under TumblerAdd's precondition `Pos(b ⊕ c)`. The proof never discharges `Pos(b ⊕ c)` as a named step — it is implicit in "the first nonzero component of `s` occurs at position `min(k_b, k_c)`", but that is the conclusion of the action-point computation, not a separately-established positivity that licenses applying ActionPoint/TumblerAdd to `s`. Symmetrically, the postcondition `actionPoint(b ⊕ c) = min(k_b, k_c)` presupposes `Pos(b ⊕ c)` in order for `actionPoint(·)` to be defined on its argument. ActionPoint requires `Pos(·)` as an input precondition, so the precondition chain from `Pos(b), Pos(c)` → `Pos(b ⊕ c)` → `actionPoint(b ⊕ c)` has an unstated middle link.
**What needs resolving**: Either explicitly establish `Pos(b ⊕ c)` as a lemma from `Pos(b), Pos(c)` before invoking ActionPoint on `b ⊕ c`, or make `Pos(b ⊕ c)` a stated intermediate conclusion of the *Action point of `s`* paragraph that the right-side expansion and the `actionPoint(b ⊕ c) = min(k_b, k_c)` postcondition then consume.

### NAT-addassoc introduced outside T0's "exhaustive NAT-* enumeration"
**Foundation**: T0 (CarrierSetDefinition) — referenced as enumerating the available NAT-* axioms; NAT-addassoc (NatAdditionAssociative, this ASN) — new axiom stated in this ASN
**ASN**: NAT-addassoc's rationale says "This is a standard property of ℕ, stated here as an axiom so downstream proofs can cite it directly." TumblerAdd's dominance-proof rationale asserts "T0's exhaustive NAT-* enumeration does not include commutativity of addition on ℕ" (and uses this exhaustiveness to rule out a symmetric proof route).
**Issue**: Two uses of the word "enumeration" pull against each other. TumblerAdd treats T0's NAT-* list as *exhaustive* — a closed set that justifies ruling out commutativity as unavailable. NAT-addassoc introduces a NAT-* axiom outside that enumeration. If T0's enumeration is exhaustive, NAT-addassoc cannot sit outside it (and TumblerAdd's negative argument becomes fragile: whatever mechanism admits NAT-addassoc as an add-on could also admit commutativity). If T0's enumeration is not exhaustive, TumblerAdd's rationale overstates what T0 provides. The ASN needs one consistent story about which axioms over ℕ are in scope and where they are registered.
**What needs resolving**: Either integrate NAT-addassoc into T0's enumeration (so downstream proofs cite a single exhaustive list) or soften TumblerAdd's "exhaustive NAT-* enumeration" language to reflect that additional ℕ axioms may be introduced alongside T0 in this ASN, and articulate the criterion that admits NAT-addassoc but excludes commutativity.
