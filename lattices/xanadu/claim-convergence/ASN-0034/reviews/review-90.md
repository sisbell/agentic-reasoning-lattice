### TumblerSub (⊖) referenced as a constructed sibling operation but never defined
**Foundation**: § Tumbler arithmetic intro paragraph — parallels TumblerAdd (⊕) with TumblerSub (⊖).
**ASN**: Opening of § Tumbler arithmetic: "These operations — tumbler addition (⊕, constructed in TumblerAdd) and subtraction (⊖, constructed in TumblerSub) — are not arithmetic on numbers but position-advance operations in a hierarchical address space." Further down: "The system requires operations that advance a position by a displacement ... and that recover the displacement between two positions (for computing span widths)."
**Issue**: The ASN asserts that two operations are constructed in this section (⊕ via TumblerAdd, ⊖ via TumblerSub) and motivates both in the span-width prose, but no property named TumblerSub or symbol ⊖ is ever defined in this ASN. Neither the TumblerAdd block nor any subsequent section carries a subtraction construction, and no forward-reference annotation (analogous to T3 in T1) declares TumblerSub as promised-in-a-later-document. This is the same species of dangling reference flagged in cycles 1 and 3 for TA0 and TA6: a prose assertion that a sibling property exists, with no corresponding text or forward-reference annotation. A formalizer reading the Tumbler arithmetic section will find an unresolved "⊖, constructed in TumblerSub" pointer and no target.
**What needs resolving**: Either introduce TumblerSub explicitly (or annotate it as a forward reference to a later section/ASN using the same pattern T3 receives in T1), rewrite the intro to scope the section to ⊕ alone, or delete the subtraction phrases from both the intro paragraph and the "recover the displacement between two positions" motivation.

### T3's classification as "axiom" vs derived "Postcondition" is inconsistent across the ASN
**Foundation**: T0 (CarrierSetDefinition) Formal Contract — uses the field name `Axiom` for the carrier-set postulate. T3 (CanonicalRepresentation) Formal Contract — uses the field name `Postcondition`.
**ASN**: T1's proof prelude states: "T3 is an axiom that holds by the definition of T as a set of finite sequences (T0); its content is paraphrased at each point of use so that the proof is self-contained despite the forward dependency." T3's own Formal Contract, however, reads: "*Postcondition:* Tumbler equality is sequence equality: ..." — parallel to T1's Postconditions, not to T0's Axiom. T3's prose block also carries a full *Proof.* section that derives the biconditional from T0's extensional sequence equality ("Both directions are immediate consequences of what it means for two finite sequences to be equal"), and its Depends list cites T0.
**Issue**: The same property is classified two ways. T1's proof load-bears on T3 being an independent axiom (separately postulated alongside T0); T3's own structure (Postcondition field + Proof from T0 + Depends: T0) treats T3 as a theorem derived from T0. If T3 is an axiom, its Formal Contract should use the `Axiom` field-name convention established by T0, and the "proof" is at most a motivational sketch — the independence from T0 must be explicit. If T3 is a derived theorem, T1's prose "T3 is an axiom" is incorrect and should instead describe T3 as a consequence of T0. A formalizer pulling T3 into TLA+ needs to know which it is: an AXIOM declaration or a THEOREM whose proof must discharge.
**What needs resolving**: Pick one classification and make it consistent. Either (i) reclassify T3 as an axiom — change the Formal Contract field from `Postcondition` to `Axiom`, drop T0 from Depends (since an axiom is not a consequence), and demote the Proof section to a motivational remark; or (ii) keep T3 as a theorem — remove the phrase "T3 is an axiom" from T1's proof prelude and describe T3 as a consequence of T0's extensional sequence equality.
