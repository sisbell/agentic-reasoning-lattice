### T6's precondition chain drops T3 while invoking T4b, whose contract lists T3 as a precondition
**Foundation**: T4b (UniqueParse), Preconditions — "`t` satisfies T3 (CanonicalRepresentation): the component sequence of `t` is fixed by sequence identity, with no alternative encoding yielding different component values. `t` satisfies the T4 constraints..."; T4b body — "The canonical representation of T3 ensures that the components of `t` are fixed ... the separator positions are therefore uniquely determined, the field boundaries are uniquely determined, and the sub-sequences assigned to each field are uniquely determined"; T4 Formal Contract — "T4b (UniqueParse) requires T3 (CanonicalRepresentation) — canonical representation fixes the component sequence of `t`, ensuring separator positions computed by scanning for zeros are uniquely determined."
**ASN**: T6, Preconditions — "`a, b ∈ T` are valid tumblers satisfying T4 (at most three zeros, no adjacent zeros, no leading or trailing zero). Positivity of non-zero components is supplied by T0's carrier ℕ"; T6 Depends — enumerates only "T0 ... T4 ... T4a ... T4b"; T6 Ingredient 1 — "The T6 precondition requires `a, b` to satisfy T4, so both `a` and `b` lie in the domain of `fields` and each invocation below is well-defined; every consumer of a projection in cases (a)–(d) inherits this discharge."
**Issue**: T4b's contract fixes T3 as a per-`t` precondition, and its body relies on T3 precisely for the uniqueness half of well-definedness — domain membership alone (T4-validity) places `t` in the domain of `fields`, but what makes `fields(t)` a *unique* value is T3. T6 invokes `fields(a)` and `fields(b)` and asserts "each invocation below is well-defined" on T4-validity alone, never mentioning T3 in Preconditions, Depends, or in the Ingredient 1 discharge. The precondition chain T6 → T4b → T3 is therefore unbroken only if T3 is silently treated as universal over `T` — but T4b itself refuses to make that treatment, listing T3 in both its own Preconditions and in the Depends line that T4 repeats. Under per-property review, T4b's contract looks consistent and T6's contract looks consistent; the cross-cutting gap is that the same dependency (T3) is per-tumbler at T4b and apparently implicit-in-`T` at T6.
**What needs resolving**: Either carry T3 through T6 explicitly (add it to T6's Preconditions and Depends so the chain to T4b is complete), or reconcile the treatment of T3 at T4b so the canonical-representation assumption is discharged once as an ambient property of `T` rather than as a precondition each caller must rediscover.
