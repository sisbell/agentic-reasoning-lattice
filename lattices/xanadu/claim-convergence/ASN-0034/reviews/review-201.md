### TumblerSub uses `min`/`max` primitives that the ASN convention forbids
**Foundation**: Divergence and ZPD (sister properties of this ASN). Both go to substantial lengths to discharge length-pair operators via NAT-order trichotomy at `(#a, #b)` / `(#a, #w)`, explicitly stating that "no primitive binary-minimum operator on ℕ" and "no primitive binary-maximum operator on ℕ" is invoked. The *Depends* enumeration convention requires every operator in a Definition to be discharged by a Depends entry.
**ASN**: TumblerSub. Quoted: "the result is the zero tumbler of length `max(#a, #w)`"; "The result has length `max(#a, #w)`"; "#(b ⊖ a) = max(#a, #b) = #a"; "#(a ⊖ w) = max(#a, #w)" (postcondition); and in the precondition-consequence proof, "Component divergence at position `k ≤ min(#w, #a)`".
**Issue**: `max(#a, #w)` and `min(#w, #a)` appear in TumblerSub's Definition, postconditions, and proof without being dispatched through NAT-order's trichotomy at the length pair `(#a, #w)`. Divergence's Depends rationale explicitly notes the length-pair invocation "replaces any implicit reliance on a primitive binary-minimum operator on ℕ — the shorter of `#a` and `#w` is named by the selected sub-case rather than by an un-axiomatized `min`." TumblerSub adopts neither this dispatch nor the shared-bound conjunction `k ≤ #a ∧ k ≤ #w` that the same convention substitutes for `min`. TumblerSub's Depends does not list NAT-order at the length pair `(#a, #w)` for max-selection (its sole NAT-order entry is for the `>`-to-`≥` weakening of `aₖ > wₖ`), so the `max(·)` and `min(·)` operators are unsourced.
**What needs resolving**: TumblerSub must either (a) dispatch the length-pair selection through NAT-order's trichotomy at `(#a, #w)` along the lines of ZPD's three-sub-case structure (so `#(a ⊖ w)` is named per sub-case, equivalently via ZPD's already-discharged `L`), and restate the precondition-consequence case (i) bound as the conjunction `k ≤ #a ∧ k ≤ #w` matching Divergence's case (i); or (b) declare a primitive `max`/`min` operator on ℕ in an additional NAT-* axiom and cite it. Without one of these, TumblerSub diverges from the per-step citation discipline its sister properties enforce.

### D1 inherits TumblerSub's `min`/`max` and collapses Divergence sub-cases
**Foundation**: Divergence's case (ii) is split into sub-cases (ii-a) `#a < #b` with `divergence(a, b) = #a + 1` and (ii-b) `#b < #a` with `divergence(a, b) = #b + 1`. TumblerSub's length is `max(#a, #w)`.
**ASN**: D1, proof. Quoted: "so in particular k ≤ #a ≤ #b, whence `k ≤ min(#a, #b)`. This rules out Divergence case (ii) — which would require `k = min(#a, #b) + 1 > #a`"; later "The result has length `#w = max(#b, #a) = #b`".
**Issue**: D1 uses `min(#a, #b)` and `max(#b, #a)` directly, without the dispatch convention Divergence and ZPD enforce; this is the same gap as the TumblerSub finding propagated downward. Separately, the case-(ii) elimination conflates sub-cases (ii-a) and (ii-b) into a single `min(#a, #b) + 1` formulation that does not match Divergence's actual case structure: Divergence case (ii) requires `#a ≠ #b`, but D1's preconditions allow `#a = #b`, in which case neither sub-case (ii-a) nor (ii-b) can fire — an exclusion D1 should note explicitly. The `> #a` claim under sub-case (ii-b) (where the index would be `#b + 1` with `#b < #a`) does not hold without further argument.
**What needs resolving**: D1 must (a) dispatch the length-pair operators through NAT-order trichotomy on `(#a, #b)` (or otherwise source them); and (b) eliminate Divergence case (ii) by addressing each sub-case as Divergence states it — sub-case (ii-a) excluded by `k ≤ #a` together with `k = #a + 1`, sub-case (ii-b) excluded by `#a ≤ #b` (which forecloses `#b < #a`), and the `#a = #b` situation excluded from case (ii) at the case-hypothesis level — rather than by the conflated `k = min + 1 > #a` step.

### D1's ZPD–Divergence appeal silently swaps operands
**Foundation**: ZPD's *Postconditions (Relationship to Divergence)* is stated for the ordered pair `(a, w)`, giving `zpd(a, w) = divergence(a, w)` in case (i). ZPD's *Postconditions (Symmetry)* gives `zpd(a, w) = zpd(w, a)`, and Divergence's postcondition gives `divergence(a, b) = divergence(b, a)`.
**ASN**: D1, proof. Quoted: "Let `k = divergence(a, b)`. … the ZPD–Divergence relationship (ZPD) gives `zpd(b, a) = divergence(a, b) = k`."
**Issue**: The ZPD–Divergence relationship in scope is `zpd(a, w) = divergence(a, w)`, with both sides taking the *same* ordered pair. D1 instantiates the relationship at the operand pair `(b, a)` on the ZPD side and `(a, b)` on the Divergence side and equates them. Two operand swaps are required — `zpd(b, a) = zpd(a, b)` (ZPD symmetry) and `divergence(a, b) = divergence(b, a)` (Divergence symmetry) — to bridge `zpd(b, a)` and `divergence(a, b)`. Neither symmetry is invoked at this step, and Divergence's symmetry postcondition is not in D1's Depends.
**What needs resolving**: D1 must either (a) instantiate the ZPD–Divergence relationship at a single ordered pair throughout (e.g., compute `divergence(b, a)` and use `zpd(b, a) = divergence(b, a)` directly, then appeal to Divergence symmetry only where needed for the case-i strict-inequality direction at the divergence point), or (b) cite both ZPD symmetry and Divergence symmetry explicitly to justify the operand swap, and add Divergence's symmetry postcondition to D1's Depends.
