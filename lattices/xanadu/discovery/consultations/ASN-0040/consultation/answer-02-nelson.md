## Question 2 [nelson]

> What is the relationship between baptizing a position at one level of the hierarchy and the existence of a baptized position at the level above it?

### Nelson's Answer

Nelson describes baptism as hierarchical — each level is produced by forking from the level above:

> "Whoever owns a specific node, account, document or version may in turn designate (respectively) new nodes, accounts, documents and versions, by forking their integers. We often call this the 'baptism' of new numbers." (4/17)

The structural implication: a child position is created by forking from a parent position. The parent must exist as a position on the tumbler line before it can be forked.

> "A server node, or station, has ancestors and may have possible descendant nodes. An account, too, and a document, all have possible descendants." (4/19)

Descendants are defined in relation to ancestors — the ancestor must be present for the descendant to be created. The sequence is explicit: nodes spawn accounts, accounts spawn documents, documents spawn versions and elements. Nelson lists these in order:

> "The entire tumbler works like that: nodes can spin off nodes; accounts can spin off accounts; nodes can spin off accounts; and so on. Thus all numeration in the docuverse is compressed into a single mechanism." (4/19)

The "spin off" metaphor implies the parent is the source of the child. The child is produced from the parent — not independently alongside it. The baptismal chain flows from root through successive levels, each level forked from the one above.
