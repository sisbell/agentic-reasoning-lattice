## Question 20 [evidence]

> Are heat capacities reported for substances in different physical states (solid vs. liquid vs. gas) for the same substance?

No. The corpus does not report heat capacities for the same substance across different physical states.

Dulong and Petit's own measurements, presented in the table, cover only solid elements (bismuth, lead, gold, platinum, tin, silver, zinc, tellurium, copper, nickel, iron, cobalt, sulphur) measured as fine powder via a cooling method `[1819-dulong-petit]`.

Gases (oxygen, azote, hydrogen) are mentioned only in passing, citing Laroche and Berard's separate work as corroborating the law — these are different substances from the solids in the table, not the same substances measured in a different state `[1819-dulong-petit]`.

No substance appears in the corpus in more than one physical state.
