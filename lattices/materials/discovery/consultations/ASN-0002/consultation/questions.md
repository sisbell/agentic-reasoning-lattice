# Sub-Questions — Constitution and the Nature of Heat

**Inquiry:** What does the theory commit to about the constitution of bodies and the nature of heat?

1. [theory] What does the theory commit to regarding the nature of heat as a state of motion of molecules rather than a material substance?
2. [theory] What does the theory predict about the relationship between temperature and the translational vis viva of molecules constituting a body?
3. [theory] How does the theory characterize the internal constitution of a molecule such that vis viva may reside in modes beyond translational motion?
4. [theory] What must hold about the ratio β between total vis viva and translational vis viva for bodies whose molecules possess internal structure?
5. [theory] What does elastic collision require of the constituent bodies, and what does this commitment say about their fundamental nature?
6. [theory] How does the theory distinguish between the vis viva of molecular translation and the vis viva associated with motion internal to each molecule at equilibrium?
7. [theory] What does the theory commit to about the persistence and conservation of total vis viva across encounters between molecules constituting a body?
8. [theory] How does the theory treat the equilibrium condition between the translational and internal vis viva of a single species of molecule?
9. [theory] What does the theory predict about the sharing of vis viva between two bodies of different constitution brought into thermal contact?
10. [theory] What must hold about β for a system of perfectly smooth elastic spheres, and what does this reveal about the constitution such a model assumes?
11. [evidence] For the thirteen simple bodies in the table, how closely do the products of atomic weight and specific heat cluster, and what is the numerical spread between the extremes (bismuth's 0.3830 versus cobalt's 0.3685)?
12. [evidence] Which specific bodies in the corpus are grouped as "simple" or elementary, and what distinguishes them from the compound bodies the authors set aside for later treatment?
13. [evidence] What specific heat values does the corpus record for the densest metals — gold (0.0298), platinum (0.0314), lead (0.0293), bismuth (0.0288) — and how do these compare to the lighter metals like zinc (0.0927) and iron (0.1100)?
14. [evidence] What relative atomic weights do the authors assign to each of the thirteen simple bodies, and on what empirical basis (chemical combining proportions) are these weights fixed?
15. [evidence] Under what measurement conditions (temperature interval above ambient, pressure of residual air, mass of sample, duration of cooling) were the cooling-rate observations performed, and how uniform were these conditions across substances?
16. [evidence] What does the corpus report about specific heats of gases (oxygen, azotic gas, hydrogen) from Laroche and Berard's measurements, and how do these values relate numerically to the pattern seen in solids?
17. [evidence] What empirical evidence does the corpus cite for the claim that compound bodies show "a very simple ratio" between their capacity and that of their elementary constituents?
18. [evidence] What does the corpus report about whether specific heat depends on the physical form or state of aggregation of a body, and what measurements support or qualify this?
19. [evidence] What quantitative discrepancies does the corpus identify between its own measurements and those from earlier tables, and by what factor do the authors say the worst earlier values err?
20. [evidence] What does the corpus record about the relationship between interatomic distances, density, and dilatability — specifically the Gay-Lussac observation on carburet of sulphur and alcohol at their boiling points?
