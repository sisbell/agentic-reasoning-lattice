## Question 16 [evidence]

> What does the corpus report about specific heats of gases (oxygen, azotic gas, hydrogen) from Laroche and Berard's measurements, and how do these values relate numerically to the pattern seen in solids?

The corpus does not report numerical specific heat values for the gases from Laroche and Berard. It makes only qualitative statements about them.

**What the corpus actually says** `[1819-dulong-petit]`:

- **Oxygen and azotic (nitrogen) gas**: Their specific heats "do not differ from what they ought to be to agree accurately with our law, except by a quantity less than the probable errors of such experiments." No numerical values are given.

- **Hydrogen**: The value is "rather too small" to fit the law, but Dulong and Petit attribute this to a measurement artifact — hydrogen cools so rapidly compared to other elastic fluids that the corrections Laroche and Berard applied introduced an inaccuracy they "did not attempt to free it" from.

**Relation to the solid pattern**: The corpus asserts that oxygen and azotic gas are consistent with the equal-atomic-heat-capacity law established for the 13 solid elements (products ranging 0.3675–0.3830 in the table). Hydrogen is described as a discrepant outlier explained by experimental error, not a genuine exception. No atom weights or product values are given for the gases, so no numerical comparison to the solid column can be made from this source.

The corpus provides no exact gas specific heat figures — only conformity or non-conformity judgments relative to the law.
