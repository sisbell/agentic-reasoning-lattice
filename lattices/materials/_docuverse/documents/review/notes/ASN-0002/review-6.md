# Review of ASN-0002

## REVISE

### Issue 1: "Law of equivalent volumes follows from P.equipartition" overstates what this ASN can derive
**ASN-0002, §"The primacy of motion"**: "The law of equivalent volumes follows from the equality of mean translational vis viva per molecule at common temperature (P.equipartition, developed above)."
**Problem**: Deriving the law of equivalent volumes (Avogadro's hypothesis) from P.equipartition requires the pressure–volume–temperature machinery that this ASN explicitly defers — §"The internal compartment and the ratio β" states "We defer the derivation of that relation to a later specification that develops the requisite pressure–volume–temperature machinery." The words "follows from" claim a derivational chain this ASN cannot discharge. This is also inconsistent with the ASN's own posture elsewhere (where the β–specific-heat relation is carefully flagged as requiring deferred apparatus) and sits uncomfortably against the scope exclusion of "Pressure-volume-temperature relations and gas laws."
**Required**: Soften the claim so it does not assert a derivation this ASN cannot produce. Either (a) note parenthetically that the deduction runs through the deferred PVT apparatus, or (b) drop the specific clause and list the law of equivalent volumes alongside the other items ("accounts for…") without a "follows from" verb that commits to a derivation.

### Issue 2: "Only kinematic invariant" of cross-species encounters omits momentum
**ASN-0002, §"Sharing of translational vis viva between species"**: "…under encounters whose only kinematic invariant is M₁a² + M₂b² = M₁a'² + M₂b'², which forces Maxwellian forms with equal moduli M₁α₁² = M₂α₂²…"
**Problem**: Under the encounter premises (i)–(iv), cross-species encounters conserve total translational energy *and* total momentum (P.enc_momentum derives the latter strictly). The equal-modulus conclusion does follow from the energy constraint, but calling the energy equation "the only kinematic invariant" is literally incorrect. In a sketch of an outside-scope derivation this is a small slip, but the claim as written contradicts P.enc_momentum.
**Required**: Replace "only kinematic invariant" with wording that acknowledges both momentum and energy are invariants and then points to the energy constraint as the one that forces equal moduli. E.g. "…encounters with total momentum and total translational energy as kinematic invariants; the equal-modulus conclusion flows from the latter."

VERDICT: REVISE
