# Review of ASN-0002

## REVISE

### Issue 1: P.motion_primacy — "relativity of motion" ground does not discriminate against the statical picture
**ASN-0002, §"The primacy of motion"**: "Conceptually, a theory of hard-body collision in which the outcome of an impact depends on the *absolute* motions of the bodies is ruled out by the relativity of motion: the outcome of a local interaction cannot depend on the frame in which it is observed."
**Problem**: The section lists this as one of the "grounds [that] force the commitment" to P.motion_primacy (dynamical over statical). But the argument rules out a specific variant of the *dynamical* picture — a hard-body-collision theory whose outcome depends on absolute motion — not the statical picture. A statical theory has no moving bodies for frame-invariance to bear on; this conceptual point does not bear on choosing dynamical over statical. Its logical role is a consistency constraint on the form any dynamical picture must take, not a discriminator against the rival.
**Required**: Either (a) reframe the paragraph as a consistency constraint on the form of the dynamical picture (distinct from the enumeration of grounds for preferring it to statics), (b) replace it with a ground that genuinely discriminates against the statical picture, or (c) drop it, leaving the empirical-fluid and explanatory-reach grounds to discharge the commitment. As currently positioned it reads as evidence for P.motion_primacy that it cannot supply.

### Issue 2: P.beta_unity_if_no_internal — regime not carried in claim statement
**ASN-0002, §"The internal compartment and the ratio β"**: "If species k carries no internal degree of freedom capable of exchanging energy with translation through encounters, then β_k = 1."
**Problem**: The consequent "β_k = 1" presupposes that β_k is defined as a species-characteristic value — a status that per P.beta_species_invariant holds only "within the regime in which the equilibrium apportionment holds." The claim's antecedent admits the decoupled-mode sub-case, which the surrounding text addresses by pointing out that β would there depend on the state condition Σ m.eint = 0, not on the species. Outside the equilibrium-apportionment regime, β as computed from the defining formula is simply not species-characteristic, so the consequent is either ill-defined or false depending on state. The claim statement omits the regime qualifier that other claims (P.beta_species_invariant, P.temp_functional, P.enc_ratio_equilibration) carry.
**Required**: Either add the regime qualifier to P.beta_unity_if_no_internal — matching P.beta_species_invariant's "within the regime in which the equilibrium apportionment holds" — or narrow the antecedent to the case the ASN actually grounds the implication on (the pure-centre-of-force realization, where m.eint ≡ 0 makes β = 1 universally and no regime qualifier is required). Leave the claim statement congruent with the grounding given in the body.

VERDICT: REVISE
