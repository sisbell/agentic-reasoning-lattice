## Question 16 [evidence]

> Which compounds (as distinct from pure elements) appear in the corpus with measured heat capacities, and what values are reported?

The corpus reports no numerical heat capacity values for compounds.

The table in `[1819-dulong-petit]` lists only "simple bodies" (pure elements): Bismuth, Lead, Gold, Platinum, Tin, Silver, Zinc, Tellurium, Copper, Nickel, Iron, Cobalt, and Sulphur — with specific heats ranging from 0.0288 (Bismuth) to 0.1880 (Sulphur).

The authors acknowledge they did measure compound bodies ("we had it in our power to subject to experiment a great many bodies whose proportions may be considered as fixed") and mention compounds by name — water, sulphuret of lead, oxides of copper/lead/silver, carburet of sulphur — but they explicitly withhold the numerical results, stating they "choose rather to wait" until enough cases are gathered to draw decisive conclusions. No compound heat capacity values appear in this paper.
