## Question 18 [evidence]

> What atomic weights are assigned to the elements in the corpus, and from which source or convention?

From `[1819-dulong-petit]`, the table lists "Relative weights of the atoms" for 13 elements:

| Element   | Relative atomic weight |
|-----------|------------------------|
| Bismuth   | 13.30                  |
| Lead      | 12.95                  |
| Gold      | 12.43                  |
| Platinum  | 11.16                  |
| Tin       | 7.35                   |
| Silver    | 6.75                   |
| Zinc      | 4.03                   |
| Tellurium | 4.03                   |
| Copper    | 3.957                  |
| Nickel    | 3.69                   |
| Iron      | 3.392                  |
| Cobalt    | 2.46                   |
| Sulphur   | 2.011                  |

No units are stated; these are dimensionless ratios relative to an implicit reference.

**Source/convention:** The authors state the weights are "deduced from the ratios observed between the weights of the elementary substances that unite together" — that is, from chemical combining proportions (gravimetric stoichiometry). No external authority (Dalton, Berzelius, etc.) is cited as the source. The authors explicitly acknowledge arbitrariness: where multiple integer-ratio choices are equally consistent with chemical data, they chose the one producing "the most simple relation" — which in practice means the choice that makes the product (specific heat × atomic weight) most nearly constant across all 13 elements.
