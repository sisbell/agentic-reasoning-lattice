## Question 12 [evidence]

> For which elements are molar heat capacities (specific heat × atomic weight) reported, and what numerical values do they take?

From `[1819-dulong-petit]`, the table reports specific heat × atomic weight ("Products of the weight of each atom by the corresponding capacity") for 13 elements:

| Element | Specific heat | Atomic weight | Product |
|---------|--------------|---------------|---------|
| Bismuth | 0.0288 | 13.30 | 0.3830 |
| Lead | 0.0293 | 12.95 | 0.3794 |
| Gold | 0.0298 | 12.43 | 0.3704 |
| Platinum | 0.0314 | 11.16 | 0.3740 |
| Tin | 0.0514 | 7.35 | 0.3779 |
| Silver | 0.0557 | 6.75 | 0.3759 |
| Zinc | 0.0927 | 4.03 | 0.3736 |
| Tellurium | 0.0912 | 4.03 | 0.3675 |
| Copper | 0.0949 | 3.957 | 0.3755 |
| Nickel | 0.1035 | 3.69 | 0.3819 |
| Iron | 0.1100 | 3.392 | 0.3731 |
| Cobalt | 0.1498 | 2.46 | 0.3685 |
| Sulphur | 0.1880 | 2.011 | 0.3780 |

**Units:** The specific heats are dimensionless ratios relative to water (convention of the era); atomic weights are relative (not g/mol). The products are therefore also dimensionless in this system. No absolute unit system is stated in the paper.

**Range:** Products span 0.3675 (Tellurium) to 0.3830 (Bismuth). The corpus contains no molar heat capacity data outside this table.
