### Issue 1: β_k = 1 discharge claim contradicts the case the ASN itself admits earlier

**Category:** THEORY
**Reason:** The choice between fix-options (a) and (b) depends on what the theory actually commits to about internal DOFs coupled to translation under force laws outside (iv)'s centres-of-force restriction — whether such a DOF must carry strictly positive equilibrium share or may have zero share.

**Theory question:** Does the theory commit that under apparatus (i)–(iv) an exchangeable internal DOF (one coupled to translation through the encounter law) must carry a strictly positive equilibrium share of energy, so that β_k = 1 ≡ no exchangeable DOF under (i)–(iv); or does the theory admit species for which an exchangeable DOF is present but its equilibrium share is zero?

**Theory's Answer:**

## Answer

The corpus does not formally commit to the positivity of an exchangeable DOF's equilibrium share. Maxwell's treatment assumes the final ratio rather than deriving it.

**What the corpus does say:**

On the β = 1 case — it is equated with the absence of internal motion, not established as the only way to have zero internal share:

> "If the molecules are pure centres of force, there can be no energy of rotation, and the whole energy is reduced to that of translation; but in all other cases the whole energy of the molecule may be represented by ½Mv²β, where β is the ratio of the total energy to the energy of translation." [1867-maxwell-dynamical-theory-of-gases]

On the β > 1 case — the ratio 1 : (β − 1) is *supposed*, not derived from encounter dynamics:

> "We have no means of determining such intricate actions in the present state of our knowledge of molecules, so that we must content ourselves with the assumption that the value of θ is, on an average, the same as for pure centres of force, and that the final velocities differ from the initial velocities only by quantities which may in each collision be neglected, although in a great many encounters the energy of translation and the internal energy of the molecules arrive, by repeated small exchanges, at a final ratio, which we shall suppose to be that of 1 to β − 1." [1867-maxwell-dynamical-theory-of-gases]

Restated later as a Clausius hypothesis:

> "If we assume with Clausius that the ratio of the mean energy of internal motion to that of agitation tends continually towards a definite value (β − 1), we may conclude that, except in very violent disturbances, this ratio is always preserved, so that Ē = (β − 1)(ξ̄² + η̄² + ζ̄²)." [1867-maxwell-dynamical-theory-of-gases]

And β is fixed empirically, not from the encounter law:

> "The value of β can be determined if we know either of the specific heats of the gas, or the ratio between them." [1867-maxwell-dynamical-theory-of-gases]

**What the corpus commits to vs. what it leaves open:**

- *Committed:* If the molecule is a pure centre of force (no internal DOF at all), then β = 1.
- *Committed:* Translational equipartition between species: M₁v̄₁² = M₂v̄₂² (eq. 34), derived from the encounter law itself via the functional equation f₁(a)f₂(b) = f₁(a′)f₂(b′) constrained only by M₁a² + M₂b² = M₁a′² + M₂b′².
- *Not committed:* That an exchangeable internal DOF must carry strictly positive equilibrium share. The derivation of the final velocity distribution uses only translational energy conservation (eq. 23); internal DOFs enter solely through Clausius's posited ratio, which is then pinned by specific-heat data.

In short: the theory narratively treats "β = 1" as synonymous with "no internal DOF," but the encounter calculation does not prove the converse. A species with a present-but-vanishing internal share is not derived to be impossible within what Maxwell establishes — it is simply outside the framework Maxwell imports from Clausius. The positivity is an assumption, inherited and then calibrated from experiment, not a consequence of (i)–(iv).
