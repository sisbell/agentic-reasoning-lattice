**Clustering:** All thirteen values fall within the narrow band 0.3675–0.3830. The simple mean is approximately 0.3754.

**Spread correction:** The question identifies cobalt (0.3685) as the lower extreme, but the actual minimum in the table is tellurium at **0.3675**. The true range is therefore:

> 0.3830 (bismuth) − 0.3675 (tellurium) = **0.0155**

The bismuth–cobalt spread the question cites is 0.3830 − 0.3685 = **0.0145**, which understates the full range by 0.0010.

No units are assigned to these products in the paper; specific heats are dimensionless ratios and atomic weights are relative weights, so the products are dimensionless. The authors attribute the residual spread to measurement error in the specific heats and imprecision in the chemical analyses [1819-dulong-petit].
