## Question 19 [evidence]

> What quantitative discrepancies does the corpus identify between its own measurements and those from earlier tables, and by what factor do the authors say the worst earlier values err?

The corpus gives one explicit quantitative claim about earlier measurement error:

> "It is not uncommon, for example, to meet with numbers in the best tables **three or four times as great** as they ought to be." [1819-dulong-petit]

No specific numerical values are given for individual substances where the discrepancy occurs — the authors do not tabulate their results against prior results side by side. The statement is a general characterization of the prior literature's worst cases.

They name the exceptions: Lavoisier and Laplace's values, and Laroche and Bérard's measurements on elastic fluids, are treated as reliable. All other prior measurements are described as "extremely inaccurate," with the 3–4× factor being the stated upper bound of error magnitude. No units are attached to this ratio; it is a dimensionless multiplier on specific heat values.
